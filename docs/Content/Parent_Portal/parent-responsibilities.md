# Parent Responsibilities

## Parent Expectations at Smart Academy

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 11, 2026

---

## Overview

At Smart Academy, we believe that education is a partnership between home and school. As parents, you play a crucial role in your child's educational and character development. This document outlines the responsibilities and expectations we have of our parents.

---

## Islamic Foundation

### Parents as Primary Educators

**The Prophet (peace be upon him) said:**
"Each of you is a shepherd and each of you is responsible for his flock." (Bukhari and Muslim)

**Your Sacred Trust (Amanah):**
- Children are trust (Amanah) from Allah
- Parents are primarily responsible for their upbringing
- School supports parents in this responsibility
- You will be asked about this trust on Day of Judgment

**Partnership with School:**
- School extends and supports home education
- Consistency between home and school is essential
- Mutual respect and communication required
- Shared goals for child's development

## Core Parental Responsibilities

### 1. Islamic Development at Home

**Daily Islamic Practices:**
- **Prayer:** Ensure children pray at home (teach and model)
- **Quran:** Encourage Quran recitation and memorization review
- **Dua:** Teach morning and evening duas
- **Islamic Environment:** Create Islamic atmosphere at home
- **Media Monitoring:** Ensure halal entertainment and media

**Modeling Islamic Character:**
- Children learn by watching parents
- Model honesty, kindness, patience, and humility
- Show respect to spouse and family members
- Pray regularly in front of children
- Speak kindly and avoid harsh language

**Teaching Islamic Values:**
- Explain why we do what we do as Muslims
- Share stories of Prophets and righteous people
- Discuss Islamic character in daily situations
- Encourage love for Allah and His Messenger
- Teach Islamic etiquette and manners

### 2. Educational Support

**Ensuring Regular Attendance:**
- Children should attend school every day unless ill
- Schedule appointments outside school hours when possible
- Plan family vacations during school breaks
- Communicate absences promptly
- Understand that attendance affects learning

**Punctuality:**
- Ensure children arrive on time (by 8:00 AM)
- Late arrivals disrupt learning and miss important activities
- Model punctuality and responsibility
- Plan morning routine to avoid rushing

**Homework Support:**
- Provide quiet, designated space for studying
- Establish regular homework time
- Be available to help, but don't do work for child
- Check that homework is completed
- Communicate with teacher if child struggles

**Learning Environment at Home:**
- Limit screen time and television
- Provide books and educational materials
- Show interest in what child learns
- Discuss school learning at home
- Encourage curiosity and questions

### 3. Communication with School

**Regular Communication:**
- Check parent portal at least weekly
- Read all school communications
- Respond to messages and inquiries promptly
- Update contact information when it changes
- Inform school of significant home events

**Attending Meetings:**
- Parent-teacher conferences (mandatory)
- School meetings and events
- PTA meetings when possible
- Workshops and training sessions
- Scheduled appointments

**Two-Way Communication:**
- Share concerns early, not wait for crisis
- Provide information about home situations affecting child
- Ask questions when unsure
- Provide constructive feedback
- Communicate respectfully and Islamically

### 4. Financial Responsibilities

**Timely Fee Payment:**
- Pay fees by due date each month/quarter
- Plan ahead for fee payments
- Contact accounts office if problem arises
- Understand that fees support school operations
- Late fees may apply for late payments

**Providing Materials:**
- Purchase required textbooks and supplies
- Replace lost or damaged materials
- Ensure child has proper uniform
- Provide materials for projects and activities
- Support fee-based activities when participating

### 5. Character and Behavior Support

**Reinforcing School Rules:**
- Support school discipline and expectations
- Discuss behavior expectations with child
- Cooperate with school when behavior issues arise
- Support consequences for inappropriate behavior
- Work collaboratively on behavior improvement

**Character Development:**
- Emphasize importance of good character (Akhlaq)
- Teach Islamic manners and etiquette
- Model respectful behavior
- Address inappropriate behavior at home
- Celebrate character growth and improvements

**Social Responsibility:**
- Teach responsibility for actions
- Encourage helping others
- Model service to community
- Teach respect for all people
- Discourage arrogance and pride

## Day-to-Day Responsibilities

### Morning Routine

**Before School:**
- Ensure child gets adequate sleep (8-10 hours)
- Provide healthy breakfast
- Ensure proper uniform and cleanliness
- Check that child has all materials (books, homework, etc.)
- Make dua for child before leaving
- Arrive on time (by 8:00 AM)

**Islamic Morning Practices:**
- Morning Adhkar and dua
- Fajr prayer (when age-appropriate)
- Model starting day with Bismillah
- Remind child of good behavior intention

### After School Routine

**When Child Returns Home:**
- Ask about their day
- Check backpack for communications
- Provide healthy lunch/snack
- Designate time for rest and play
- Establish homework time

**Supporting Learning:**
- Review homework assignments
- Check what was learned in school
- Help with difficult concepts (without doing work)
- Encourage reading time
- Limit television and screen time

**Islamic After-School:**
- Asr prayer (when age-appropriate)
- Quran review or memorization
- Islamic character discussions
- Reminders of Islamic behavior

### Evening Routine

**Before Bed:**
- Review next day's preparations
- Pack backpack
- Ensure uniform is ready
- Establish reasonable bedtime
- Evening Adhkar and dua

**Creating Islamic Environment:**
- Family prayer time (when appropriate)
- Islamic story or discussion
- Night prayer reminders
- Make dua for child's success in this world and hereafter

## Academic Year Responsibilities

### Beginning of Year

**Enrollment and Registration:**
- Complete all required paperwork
- Provide accurate information
- Pay registration and admission fees
- Attend orientation programs
- Purchase required materials

**Orientation:**
- Attend parent orientation
- Learn about school policies and expectations
- Meet teachers and administration
- Understand curriculum and expectations
- Ask questions

### Throughout the Year

**Ongoing Monitoring:**
- Monitor academic progress via portal
- Review grades and feedback
- Check attendance records
- Communicate concerns promptly
- Attend parent-teacher conferences

**Supporting School Activities:**
- Attend school events and programs
- Volunteer when able
- Support fundraising initiatives
- Participate in parent community
- Provide constructive feedback

**Responding to Communications:**
- Read all notices and newsletters
- Respond to requests for meetings
- Address concerns raised by teachers
- Complete surveys and feedback forms
- Stay informed about school news

### End of Year

**Year-End Activities:**
- Attend final parent-teacher conference
- Collect report cards and student work
- Return school materials
- Clear any outstanding fees
- Provide feedback on year

**Planning for Next Year:**
- Re-enrollment if required
- Purchase materials for next grade
- Attend orientation if moving to new level
- Discuss any concerns about next year
- Plan summer learning activities

## Specific Responsibilities by Child's Age

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup-KG)

**Developmental Support:**
- Toilet training (for PlayGroup/Nursery)
- Self-care skills (dressing, eating)
- Social skills (sharing, taking turns)
- Language development (talking, listening)
- Emotional regulation

**School Readiness:**
- Establish morning and bedtime routines
- Practice separating from parents
- Teach basic manners (please, thank you)
- Encourage independence
- Read to child daily

**Home Learning:**
- Read together regularly
- Play educational games
- Teach Islamic basics (names of Allah, simple duas)
- Encourage exploration and curiosity
- Limit screen time significantly

### Primary Years (Class 1-5)

**Academic Support:**
- Establish homework routine
- Monitor reading and mathematics skills
- Practice basic facts (multiplication tables, etc.)
- Encourage writing and creativity
- Discuss school learning

**Growing Independence:**
- Teach time management
- Encourage responsibility for belongings
- Support problem-solving skills
- Allow appropriate choices
- Teach organizational skills

**Islamic Development:**
- Ensure prayer practice when age-appropriate
- Teach wudu procedure
- Encourage Quran memorization review
- Discuss Islamic character daily
- Model Islamic behavior consistently

### Secondary Years (Class 6-9)

**Academic Accountability:**
- Monitor but don't hover
- Encourage independence in learning
- Support time management for multiple subjects
- Discuss future goals and aspirations
- Provide resources for challenging subjects

**Social and Emotional:**
- Discuss peer relationships
- Guide social media use (age-appropriate)
- Support growing independence
- Discuss Islamic identity in modern world
- Maintain open communication

**Islamic Identity:**
- Discuss Islamic perspective on contemporary issues
- Support prayer and religious practice
- Discuss modesty and Islamic behavior
- Encourage Islamic friendships
- Prepare for Islamic responsibilities (fasting, etc.)

## Challenges and How to Address Them

### Common Challenges

**Time Constraints:**
- **Challenge:** Busy schedules make involvement difficult
- **Solution:** Prioritize key involvement (conferences, portal checks)
- **Strategy:** Use portal efficiently for updates

**Multiple Children:**
- **Challenge:** Balancing needs of multiple children
- **Solution:** Establish family routines
- **Strategy:** Older children help younger

**Language Barriers:**
- **Challenge:** Difficulty understanding English-medium education
- **Solution:** School provides translations when possible
- **Strategy:** Learn alongside child; ask questions

**Financial Constraints:**
- **Challenge:** Affording fees and materials
- **Solution:** Scholarship programs available
- **Strategy:** Contact administration early; discuss payment plans

**Understanding Modern Education:**
- **Challenge:** Teaching methods different from your experience
- **Solution:** Attend workshops; read materials provided
- **Strategy:** Ask teachers to explain approaches
