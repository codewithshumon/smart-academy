## About Smart Academy

### Our Story

**Established:** 2020
**Location:** Rural Bangladesh
**Type:** English Medium Islamic School
**Students:** 500+ enrolled
**Scholarship Program:** 25% of students on scholarship

Smart Academy was founded with a vision to bridge the gap between urban and rural educational opportunities while maintaining Islamic identity and values. We believe every child deserves access to quality education that prepares them for both this world and the hereafter.

### FAITH in ACTION Framework

Our educational philosophy is built on FAITH in ACTION:

**F - Foundation of Knowledge:**
- Strong academic foundations in core subjects
- Islamic studies as the foundation for all learning
- Critical thinking and inquiry skills
- Love for lifelong learning

**A - Academic Excellence:**
- Rigorous standards and high expectations
- Differentiated instruction to meet diverse needs
- STEAM integration for modern skills
- Preparation for higher education and careers

**I - Islamic Character:**
- Moral development alongside academic growth
- Practical application of Islamic teachings
- Service to community and humanity
- Sense of responsibility to Allah and society

**T - Transformative Learning:**
- Education that transforms hearts and minds
- Connecting learning to real-life contexts
- Reflective practice and self-improvement
- Empowering students as agents of positive change

**H - Holistic Development:**
- Intellectual, spiritual, emotional, social growth
- Multiple intelligences and diverse talents
- Physical health and well-being
- Creative and artistic expression

**in ACTION:**
- Practical application of knowledge
- Project-based and experiential learning
- Community service and social responsibility
- Problem-solving for real-world challenges

### What Makes Us Unique

## School Operations

### School Hours and Schedule

**Academic Year:**
- **April to March:** Academic year
- **Terms:** Two main terms
- **Breaks:** Summer, Winter, Islamic holidays

**School Hours:**
- **Sunday - Thursday:** 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM
- **Friday - Saturday:** Closed (Islamic weekend)
- **Ramadan:** Reduced hours (announced)

**Daily Schedule:**
- **8:00 AM:** School opens, students arrive
- **8:30 - 8:50 AM:** Morning assembly
- **8:50 AM - 2:30 PM:** Classes
- **10:30 - 10:45 AM:** Break
- **12:15 - 12:45 PM:** Lunch and Dhuhr prayer
- **2:30 PM:** Dismissal
- **4:00 PM:** School closes

### Attendance Policy

**Importance of Attendance:**
- Regular attendance is crucial for academic success
- Students should attend school every day unless ill
- Attendance affects grades and promotion
- Islamic obligation to seek knowledge

**Attendance Requirements:**
- Minimum 90% attendance required (in most cases)
- Excessive absences may affect promotion
- Pattern of absences will be addressed
- Attendance recorded daily

**Reporting Absence:**
- Notify school on first day of absence
- Provide written explanation upon return
- Medical certificate required for extended illness (3+ days)
- Planned absences require prior approval

**Late Arrival:**
- Students should arrive by 8:00 AM
- Late arrivals disrupt learning
- Repeated lateness will be addressed
- Students late after 8:45 AM may not be admitted

**Dress Code Principles:**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my child is having academic difficulties?**
A: Contact the teacher immediately. Early intervention is key. We have support services available.

**Q: How can I monitor my child's progress?**
A: Use the parent portal regularly for attendance, grades, and assignments. Attend conferences and communicate with teachers.

**Q: What is the policy on birthday celebrations?**
A: Simple celebrations allowed with prior permission. No elaborate parties. Halal treats only.

**Q: Can I visit my child's classroom?**
A: Scheduled visits only. Contact teacher to arrange. Observations must not disrupt learning.

**Q: What if I have a concern about my child's teacher?**
A: First, speak directly to the teacher. If unresolved, contact department head or administration.

**Q: How are behavioral issues handled?**
A: We use positive approaches, Islamic guidance, and character development. Serious issues involve parents.

**Q: What should I do if my child is being bullied?**
A: Report immediately to teacher or administration. We have zero tolerance for bullying.

**Q: Can I take my child out of school for vacation?**
A: Not recommended. It affects learning. If necessary, inform school in advance and understand academic consequences.

**Q: How does the school ensure Islamic character development?**
A: Through Islamic studies, modeling by staff, character education, Islamic environment, and partnership with parents.
