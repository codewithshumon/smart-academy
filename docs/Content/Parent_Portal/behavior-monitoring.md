## Overview

Behavior monitoring at Smart Academy focuses on developing Islamic character, positive social skills, and responsible behavior. This guide explains how behavior is monitored, supported, and communicated to parents.

---

## Philosophy of Behavior Development

### Islamic Foundation

**Character as Priority:**
- "The best among you are those who have the best manners and character" (Bukhari)
- Islamic character (Akhlaq) central to education at Smart Academy
- Behavior reflects inner state and relationship with Allah
- Goal: Develop Muslims who embody Islamic values in all settings

**Prophetic Model:**
- Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) as perfect example
- Teach through love, patience, and wisdom
- Balance between compassion and accountability
- Focus on teaching what to do, not just what not to do
- Positive reinforcement preferred over punishment

**Comprehensive Approach:**
- Behavior education integrated throughout day
- All settings are opportunities for character development
- Mistakes as opportunities for learning and growth
- Focus on intention (Niyyah) alongside behavior

### Positive Behavior Approach

**Proactive, Not Reactive:**
- Teach expected behaviors explicitly
- Model positive behavior
- Create environment that encourages good behavior
- Recognize and reinforce positive choices
- Prevent problems before they occur

**Restorative, Not Punitive:**
- Focus on repairing harm when mistakes occur
- Teach appropriate replacement behaviors
- Maintain relationships while correcting behavior
- Problem-solve together, don't just punish
- Forgive and move forward (Islamic principle)

## Addressing Behavioral Concerns

### Minor Concerns

**What They Are:**
- First occurrences of minor misbehavior
- Forgetfulness or carelessness
- Developmentally typical behavior
- Learning mistakes

**How Addressed:**
- Gentle reminder
- Redirection to appropriate behavior
- Teaching alternative behavior
- Natural consequences
- Opportunity to try again

**Examples:**
- Talking out of turn
- Minor disruption
- Forgetfulness about materials
- Minor messiness
- Playing when should be working

### Moderate Concerns

**What They Are:**
- Repeated minor behavior issues
- Behavior that affects others
- More serious single incidents
- Defiance or disrespect

**How Addressed:**
- Private conversation with student
- Problem-solving together
- Contact with parents
- Loss of privilege
- Reflection activity
- Action plan developed

**Examples:**
- Repeated disruption
- Disrespect to teacher/peer
- Not completing work repeatedly
- Minor physical incident (pushing, etc.)
- Refusal to follow directions

### Serious Concerns

**What They Are:**
- Physical aggression or fighting
- Bullying or harassment
- Vandalism or property damage
- Severe disrespect or defiance
- Repeated moderate concerns despite intervention

**How Addressed:**
- Immediate intervention
- Parent notification and meeting
- Behavior contract
- Counseling involvement
- Specific consequences
- Action plan with monitoring

**Possible Consequences:**
- Loss of privileges
- Restorative activities (fixing harm done)
- Community service at school
- Reflection assignments
- In-school suspension (rare)
- Suspension from school (very rare, for serious incidents)

## Behavioral Challenges and Support

### Common Challenges by Age

**Early Childhood (PlayGroup-KG):**
- **Sharing and Taking Turns:** Learning to share toys, attention
- **Impulse Control:** Difficulty controlling behavior
- **Separation:** Difficulty leaving parents
- **Following Directions:** Remembering and following rules
- **Emotional Regulation:** Managing big emotions

**Primary Years (Class 1-5):**
- **Peer Relationships:** Friendship conflicts, exclusion
- **Attention and Focus:** Difficulty staying on task
- **Responsibility:** Forgetting materials, assignments
- **Honesty:** Telling lies to avoid trouble
- **Defiance:** Testing boundaries

**Secondary Years (Class 6-9):**
- **Peer Pressure:** Influence of friends on behavior
- **Identity Formation:** Experimenting with behaviors
- **Independence vs. Rules:** Resisting authority
- **Social Media:** Online behavior issues
- **Academic Pressure:** Acting out due to stress

### Support Strategies

**At School:**
- Clear, consistent expectations
- Positive behavior support
- Counseling services
- Behavior intervention plans
- Social skills groups
- Mentorship programs

**At Home:**
- Consistent expectations and routines
- Clear rules and consequences
- Positive reinforcement
- Teaching alternative behaviors
- Modeling positive behavior
- Communication with school

**When Professional Help Needed:**
- Chronic behavior problems despite intervention
- Sudden behavior changes
- Emotional or behavioral regulation concerns
- Trauma or significant life events
- Mental health concerns
- School can provide referrals

## Bullying Prevention and Intervention

### Definition of Bullying

**Bullying Is:**
- Repeated, intentional behavior
- Power imbalance (real or perceived)
- Physical, verbal, social, or online
- Intent to harm, intimidate, or exclude

**Types of Bullying:**
- **Physical:** Hitting, kicking, destroying property
- **Verbal:** Name-calling, teasing, threats
- **Social:** Exclusion, rumors, manipulation
- **Cyber:** Online harassment, exclusion, spreading rumors

### Islamic Perspective on Bullying

**Prohibited:**
- "And do not insult one another..." (Quran 49:11)
- Backbiting, gossip, mockery prohibited
- Harming others is major sin
- Rights of Muslims to be protected from harm

**Responsibility:**
- Bystanders have responsibility to help
- Not reporting is complicity
- Defending victims is obligation
- Kindness to all, even those different from us

### School Response to Bullying

**Immediate Action:**
- Stop the behavior
- Ensure safety of all students
- Separate students involved
- Get adult help immediately

**Investigation:**
- Talk to all students involved
- Gather facts and evidence
- Determine if bullying occurred
- Document incident

**Intervention:**
- Support for victim (counseling, safety planning)
- Consequences for bully (education, restorative actions, disciplinary)
- Education for bystanders
- Parent notification
- Monitoring and follow-up

**Prevention:**
- Bullying prevention education
- Islamic character education
- Positive school climate
- Reporting systems
- Supervision in common areas

### Parent Actions

**If Child Is Bullied:**
- Listen and believe child
- Report to school immediately
- Document incidents
- Support child emotionally
- Teach safety strategies
- Pray and make dua
- Monitor for signs of distress

**If Child Is Bullying:**
- Take concern seriously
- Listen to school's perspective
- Support school's intervention
- Teach alternative behaviors
- Monitor behavior at home
- Provide consequences if needed
- Seek counseling if needed
- Make dua for child's guidance

## Restorative Practices

### Philosophy

**Focus on Repairing Harm:**
- When behavior harms others, focus on fixing harm
- Accountability through making amends
- Restoring relationships
- Learning from mistakes
- Forgiveness and moving forward

**Restorative Questions:**
- What happened?
- What were you thinking at the time?
- What have you thought about since?
- Who has been affected and how?
- What needs to happen to make things right?
- How can we prevent this in the future?

### Practices

**Restorative Conversations:**
- Facilitated discussion between those involved
- Each person shares perspective
- Listen to understand, not just respond
- Decide together how to repair harm
- Agree on plan for moving forward

**Restorative Circles:**
- Group process for addressing incidents
- Everyone affected has voice
- Collective problem-solving
- Community building

**Restorative Consequences:**
- Apology (verbal or written)
- Repair or replace damaged property
- Community service
- Teaching what was learned
- Action plan for improvement
