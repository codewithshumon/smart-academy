## Overview

Islamic Studies is central to Smart Academy's mission, integrating religious education with character development. This guide explains how Islamic Studies progress is monitored, assessed, and supported, and how parents can reinforce Islamic learning at home.

---

## Philosophy of Islamic Education

### Purpose of Islamic Studies

**Knowledge:**
- Understanding Quran and Sunnah
- Learning Islamic beliefs and practices
- Knowledge of Islamic history and civilization
- Understanding Fiqh (Islamic jurisprudence) for daily life
- Arabic language for Quran understanding

**Practice:**
- Implementing Islamic teachings in daily life
- Performing acts of worship correctly
- Developing Islamic character and manners
- Living according to Islamic values
- Being a Muslim in all aspects of life

**Character:**
- Developing beautiful Islamic character (Akhlaq)
- Loving Allah and His Messenger
- Fearing Allah (Taqwa)
- Sincerity (Ikhlas) in all actions
- Moral excellence

### Islamic Perspective on Assessment

**Intention (Niyyah) Matters:**
- Allah knows intentions
- Sincerity in seeking religious knowledge
- Learning to please Allah, not for grades
- Effort alongside achievement valued

**Knowledge as Worship:**
- Seeking Islamic knowledge is act of worship
- Learning brings reward even if not mastered
- Journey of learning is valuable
- Every step toward Allah rewarded

**Character Development:**
- Inner transformation more important than rote memorization
- Application of knowledge more important than grades
- Islamic behavior in daily life ultimate goal
- Progress in character as important as academic progress

## Islamic Studies Curriculum Components

### Quranic Studies

**Areas of Study:**
- **Recitation (Tajweed):** Proper pronunciation and recitation rules
- **Memorization (Hifz):** Surahs and portions of Quran
- **Understanding (Tafsir):** Basic meaning of verses
- **Application:** How to implement Quranic teachings

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Recitation Accuracy:** Proper Tajweed applied
- **Memorization Retention:** Surahs memorized and remembered
- **Understanding:** Comprehension of basic meanings
- **Application:** Applying Quranic teachings in life

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Oral Recitation:** Teacher listens to recitation
- **Memorization Checks:** Regular review of memorized portions
- **Written Assessments:** For understanding and theory (where age-appropriate)
- **Observation:** Teacher observation of application
- **Recitation Competitions:** Opportunities to demonstrate skills

### Hadith Studies

**Areas of Study:**
- **Selected Hadith:** From authentic sources (Bukhari, Muslim, etc.)
- **Understanding:** Meaning and context of Hadith
- **Application:** How to implement Hadith teachings
- **Love for Prophet:** Developing love and respect for Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him)

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Memorization:** Hadith memorized
- **Understanding:** Comprehension of meaning
- **Application:** Implementing Hadith teachings
- **Narration:** Ability to share Hadith with others

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Oral Recitation:** Reciting Hadith accurately
- **Written Assessments:** Testing understanding (age-appropriate)
- **Application Observations:** Living according to Hadith
- **Presentations:** Sharing Hadith and lessons with class

### Fiqh (Islamic Jurisprudence)

**Areas of Study:**
- **Purity (Taharah):** Wudu, Ghusl, Tayammum
- **Prayer (Salah):** Performance of daily prayers, Friday prayer, Eid prayers
- **Fasting (Sawm):** Rules of fasting in Ramadan
- **Zakat and Charity:** Rules of giving Zakat, general charity
- **Hajj and Umrah:** Basic rules (for older students)
- **Daily Life Fiqh:** Eating, sleeping, dress, interactions according to Sunnah

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Knowledge:** Understanding of rules
- **Practice:** Correct performance of acts of worship
- **Understanding:** Knowing reasons and wisdom
- **Application:** Applying rulings in daily life

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Written Tests:** Knowledge of Fiqh rulings
- **Practical Assessment:** Observation of prayer performance
- **Oral Questioning:** Checking understanding
- **Practical Application:** Living according to learned rulings

### Islamic History

**Areas of Study:**
- **Prophets' Stories:** Lessons from lives of Prophets
- **Companions:** Lives of Sahabah
- **Islamic Civilization:** Contributions of Muslims
- **Prophet's Life (Seerah):** Life of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him)
- **Muslim Heroes:** Inspiring figures from Islamic history

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Knowledge:** Facts and events from history
- **Understanding:** Lessons and morals from stories
- **Connection:** Relating historical events to current life
- **Love for Figures:** Developing love for Prophets, Sahabah, righteous people

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Written Assessments:** Tests on historical knowledge
- **Projects:** Presentations, posters, creative work
- **Oral Presentations:** Sharing stories and lessons
- **Written Narratives:** Writing about historical events

### Arabic Language

**Areas of Study:**
- **Reading:** Reading Arabic text (Quranic and basic Arabic)
- **Writing:** Writing Arabic letters and words
- **Vocabulary:** Basic Arabic vocabulary
- **Grammar (Nahw):** Basic Arabic grammar (for older students)
- **Understanding:** Comprehending basic Arabic

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Reading Fluency:** Reading Arabic text correctly
- **Writing Skills:** Writing Arabic legibly
- **Vocabulary Retention:** Remembering and using vocabulary
- **Comprehension:** Understanding basic Arabic

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Reading Checks:** Reading passages aloud
- **Written Work:** Writing exercises
- **Vocabulary Tests:** Testing vocabulary retention
- **Comprehension Questions:** Checking understanding

### Islamic Character and Morals (Akhlaq)

**Areas of Study:**
- **Islamic Manners (Adab):** Greetings, respect, etiquette
- **Character Traits:** Honesty, patience, kindness, generosity, etc.
- **Prophetic Character:** Learning from Prophet's example
- **Islamic Values:** Justice, compassion, humility, etc.
- **Avoiding Negative Traits:** Lying, backbiting, anger, etc.

**Progress Indicators:**
- **Knowledge:** Knowing what is right and wrong
- **Behavior:** Demonstrating Islamic character
- **Consistency:** Showing character across settings
- **Growth:** Improving in character over time

**How Progress Is Assessed:**
- **Observation:** Teacher observation of behavior and interactions
- **Self-Assessment:** Student reflecting on own character
- **Peer Feedback:** (Appropriately) Peers noting character
- **Practical Application:** Living Islamic values
- **Projects:** Creating work about Islamic character

## Assessment Methods

### Formative Assessment

**Ongoing Assessment During Learning:**
- **Daily Practice:** Regular recitation, review
- **Class Participation:** Engagement in discussions
- **Homework:** Memorization practice, written work
- **Observation:** Teacher watching for Islamic behavior
- **Question and Answer:** Checking understanding during lessons

**Purpose:**
- Provide feedback for improvement
- Identify areas needing more work
- Guide instruction
- Encourage continuous effort

### Summative Assessment

**End of Unit/Term Assessment:**
- **Written Tests:** On knowledge and understanding (age-appropriate)
- **Oral Tests:** Recitation, memorization checks
- **Projects:** Presentations, creative work
- **Practical Assessment:** Demonstrating skills (prayer performance, etc.)
- **Portfolio:** Collection of best work

**Purpose:**
- Measure achievement at point in time
- Determine grades for reporting
- Identify strengths and weaknesses
- Document progress

## Islamic Studies Grading

### Grade Components

**Knowledge (40-50%):**
- Understanding of Islamic concepts
- Knowledge of Quran, Hadith, Fiqh, history
- Arabic language knowledge
- Recall and retention

**Practice and Application (30-40%):**
- Performance of acts of worship (prayer, etc.)
- Application of Islamic teachings in daily life
- Islamic character and behavior
- Consistency in religious practices

**Skills and Participation (10-20%):**
- Quran recitation skills
- Arabic reading and writing
- Class participation and engagement

**Special Consideration:**
- Effort and intention (Niyyah) considered

- **Religious Practice:** Observance of Islamic practices
- **Teacher Comments:** Specific feedback on progress

**Islamic Character Section:**
- Prayer attendance and performance
- Islamic manners and behavior
- Respect for Islamic values
- Integration of Islam in daily life
- Areas of strength and improvement

## Monitoring Progress at Home

### What Parents Can Observe

**Religious Practices:**
- **Prayer:** Does child pray regularly and correctly?
- **Quran:** Does child recite Quran daily?
- **Islamic Manners:** Does child display Islamic manners at home?
- **Religious Knowledge:** Can child share what they learned?

**Character Development:**
- **Honesty:** Truthfulness in speech and actions
- **Respect:** Respect for parents, siblings, elders
- **Kindness:** Treatment of family members and others
- **Patience:** Ability to handle frustration
- **Generosity:** Willingness to share and give

**Islamic Identity:**
- **Pride in Islam:** Positive attitude toward being Muslim
- **Islamic Practices:** Choosing Islamic practices willingly
- **Islamic Dress:** Modesty in dress (according to family practice)
- **Islamic Friends:** Choosing friends who support Islamic values

### Questions to Ask Child

**About Learning:**
- "What did you learn in Islamic Studies today?"
- "Which Surah/Hadith are you working on?"
- "Can you teach me what you learned?"
- "What Islamic value did you discuss today?"
- "Did you memorize anything new?"

**About Practice:**
- "Did you pray Dhuhr prayer at school today?"
- "How can we apply what you learned at home?"
- "Can you show me what you learned?"
- "What questions do you have about what you learned?"

**About Character:**
- "How did you show [Islamic character trait] today?"
- "How did you handle [situation] in Islamic way?"
- "What can we improve in our home based on what you learned?"

## Supporting Islamic Learning at Home

### Creating Islamic Environment

**Home Environment:**
- **Prayer Space:** Designated area for prayer
- **Islamic Visuals:** Quranic verses, Islamic art (avoiding images of living beings)
- **Islamic Books:** Accessible Islamic books for children
- **Quran Access:** Quran available for reading
- **Islamic Media:** Islamic nasheeds, lectures, videos (appropriate for children)

**Daily Routines:**
- **Morning Adkar:** Remembrances after waking
- **Prayer Times:** Praying together when possible
- **Mealtime Etiquette:** Islamic manners for eating
- **Bedtime Routine:** Evening Adkar, Quran recitation
- **Family Islamic Time:** Regular time for Islamic learning together

### Supporting Specific Areas

**Quran:**
- **Daily Recitation:** Even 5-10 minutes daily
- **Listen to Child:** Have child recite to you
- **Listen Together:** Listen to Quran recitation (car, home)
- **Review Memorization:** Help child review memorized Surahs
- **Discuss Meaning:** Talk about meanings of verses

**Islamic Knowledge:**
- **Learn Together:** Learn Islamic topics as family
- **Ask Questions:** Encourage curiosity about Islam
- **Islamic Stories:** Share stories from Quran and Islamic history
- **Answer Questions:** Help child find answers to Islamic questions
- **Admit Uncertainty:** Say "I don't know, let's find out" when unsure

**Islamic Practice:**
- **Model Islamic Behavior:** Child learns from watching you
- **Pray Together:** When possible, pray as family
- **Make Ramadan Special:** Special activities in Ramadan
- **Celebrate Eid:** Make Eid celebrations meaningful
- **Islamic Etiquette:** Practice Islamic manners consistently

**Islamic Character:**
- **Praise Islamic Behavior:** Notice and praise Islamic character
- **Correct Gently:** Guide child to better behavior Islamicly
- **Forgive and Move On:** Islamic approach to mistakes
- **Discuss Islamic Values:** Talk about why we do what we do
- **Connect to Quran/Sunnah:** Reference Islamic sources for behavior

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenges in Quran Memorization

**Challenge:** Difficulty memorizing
**Solutions:**
- Break into smaller portions
- Consistent daily review
- Listen to recitation repeatedly
- Understand meaning (helps memorization)
- Make dua for ease
- Be patient and encouraging
- Use repetition and consistency

**Challenge:** Forgetting previously memorized portions
**Solutions:**
- Regular review schedule
- Review in prayer
- Consistent daily recitation
- Don't move to new until old solid
- Use in daily life (recite as Adhkar, etc.)

### Challenges in Prayer

**Challenge:** Reluctance to pray
**Solutions:**
- Make prayer normal family routine
- Pray together when possible
- Explain why we pray (not just "have to")
- Make prayer area comfortable and inviting
- Praise effort in praying
- Be patient, it takes time
- Make dua for child's guidance

**Challenge:** Difficulty with prayer performance
**Solutions:**
- Practice prayer steps together
- Use visual guides (posters, books)
- Correct gently and kindly
- Praise improvement
- Regular practice
- Remember: learning takes time

### Challenges in Islamic Knowledge

**Challenge:** Lack of interest in Islamic Studies
**Solutions:**
- Make it relevant to child's life
- Use stories and examples
- Connect to child's interests
- Learn together as family
- Make it interactive and engaging
- Show enthusiasm yourself
- Make dua for interest

**Challenge:** Limited parental Islamic knowledge
**Solutions:**
- Learn alongside child
- Use resources (books, videos, apps)
- Ask Islamic Studies teacher for guidance
- Attend Islamic classes together
- It's never too late to learn
- Sincerity and effort rewarded by Allah

## Age-Appropriate Expectations

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

**Focus:**
- **Love for Islam:** Positive association with Islamic practices
- **Basic Practices:** Short Surahs, basic Duas, beginning prayer movements
- **Islamic Manners:** Basic greetings, please/thank you
- **Prophetic Stories:** Simple stories from Quran

**What Progress Looks Like:**
- Knows short Surahs (Fatiha, short Surahs from Juz 30)
- Knows basic Duas (before eating, sleeping, etc.)
- Shows beginning Islamic manners (Assalamu Alaikum)
- Enjoys Islamic stories and activities
- Begins to understand prayer concepts

### Primary Years (Class 1-5)

**Focus:**
- **Building Foundations:** Memorizing longer Surahs, more Duas
- **Understanding:** Basic understanding of Islamic concepts
- **Practice:** Regular prayer, fasting practice (for older children)
- **Character:** Developing Islamic character traits

**What Progress Looks Like:**
- Memorizing Surahs from Juz 30
- Learning to pray correctly
- Understanding basic Islamic beliefs
- Learning Hadith and applying lessons
- Developing Islamic character
- Showing interest in Islamic learning

### Secondary Years (Class 6-9)

**Focus:**
- **Deepening Knowledge:** More detailed understanding of Fiqh, Islamic beliefs
- **Application:** Applying Islam in more complex situations
- **Identity:** Developing Muslim identity in challenging world
- **Leadership:** Leading Islamic activities, being role model

**What Progress Looks Like:**
- Deeper understanding of Islamic rulings
- Application of Islam in daily decisions
- Regular and consistent prayer
- Fasting Ramadan fully
- Islamic character in all settings
- Understanding Islam in modern context
- Explaining Islam to others

## Communicating with Islamic Studies Teachers

### When to Contact Islamic Studies Teacher

**Academic Concerns:**
- Struggling with Quran memorization
- Difficulty understanding Islamic concepts
- Not making expected progress
- Want to know how to support at home

**Character and Practice Concerns:**
- Not practicing Islamic teachings at home
- Questions about Islamic practices
- Behavioral concerns from Islamic perspective
- Guidance on religious matters

**How to Contact:**
- Send message via parent portal
- Email Islamic Studies teacher
- Request meeting for more in-depth discussion
- Attend parent-teacher conferences

### Effective Communication

**Best Practices:**
- Share observations from home
- Ask for guidance on supporting child
- Communicate respectfully
- Share child's religious background and practice
- Ask questions about Islamic topics

**Sample Questions:**
- "How can I help [child] with Quran memorization at home?"
- "What Surahs/Hadith is [child] working on currently?"
- "How is [child]'s Islamic character developing in school?"
- "What can we do at home to support what you're teaching?"
- "I've noticed [concern], do you see this at school?"
