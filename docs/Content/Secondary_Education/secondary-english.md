# Secondary English Language & Literature Curriculum

**Smart Academy | Mastering English for SSC Excellence and Beyond**

## Document Overview

This comprehensive guide details the English Language and Literature curriculum for Classes 6-10 at Smart Academy, designed to develop advanced English proficiency for SSC 2026 success and future academic/professional excellence. English is the primary medium of instruction at Smart Academy and a critical subject for university admission, career opportunities, and global communication.

## Class 6 English Curriculum

### English 1st Paper

**Reading Comprehension:**

*Seen Passages (from textbook):*
- Narrative texts: Stories with plot, characters, setting
- Informational texts: Articles, explanations, descriptions
- Functional texts: Instructions, advertisements, notices
- Comprehension questions: Literal, inferential, evaluative
- Vocabulary from context
- Main idea and supporting details

*Unseen Passages (new texts):*
- Similar genres to seen passages
- Testing reading strategies
- Time management practice
- Application of skills

**Vocabulary:**
- Synonyms and antonyms
- Word meanings from context
- Word formation: Prefixes, suffixes, root words
- Common idioms and phrases
- Homonyms and homophones
- Completing sentences with appropriate words

**Grammar in Context:**
- Parts of speech: All types reviewed
- Tenses: Present, past, future (simple, continuous, perfect)
- Articles: A, an, the (usage rules)
- Prepositions: Time, place, direction
- Subject-verb agreement
- Singular and plural
- Correct forms of verbs

**Guided Writing:**
- Paragraph writing: Topic sentence, supporting details, conclusion
- Completing stories: Given beginning, write ending
- Describing pictures: What you see
- Simple email/letter writing
- Rearranging sentences to make paragraph

### English 2nd Paper

**Grammar:**

*Articles:*
- Definite article: The
- Indefinite articles: A, an
- When to use and not use articles
- Common errors and practice

*Prepositions:*
- Prepositions of time: At, on, in, since, for
- Prepositions of place: At, on, in, under, above, beside
- Prepositions of movement: To, from, into, out of
- Fixed prepositions: Depend on, listen to, etc.

*Tenses:*
- 12 tenses overview
- Present: Simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous
- Past: Simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous
- Future: Simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous
- Usage in context
- Common errors

*Right Forms of Verbs:*
- Identifying correct verb form for context
- Gerund vs. infinitive
- Participles: Present and past
- Subject-verb agreement
- Auxiliary verbs: Do, does, did, will, shall, etc.

*Voice:*
- Active voice
- Passive voice
- Converting between active and passive
- When to use each

*Narration:*
- Direct speech
- Indirect speech
- Converting between direct and indirect
- Tense changes in narration

*Sentences:*
- Simple, compound, complex sentences
- Sentence connectors: And, but, or, because, although, etc.
- Combining sentences
- Sentence transformation

**Composition:**

*Formal Writing:*
- Formal letter writing: Structure, language, format
- Application writing: Job, admission, complaint
- Email writing: Professional format

*Informal Writing:*
- Informal letters: To friends, family
- Personal emails
- Friendly tone and language

*Paragraph Writing:*
- Structure: Topic sentence, body, conclusion
- Unity and coherence
- Transitional words
- Various topics: Descriptive, narrative, expository

*Essay Writing:*
- Introduction to 5-paragraph essay
- Introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion
- Developing main idea with support
- Practice with various topics

*Creative Writing:*
- Story writing: Plot, characters, setting, dialogue
- Completing given stories
- Descriptive writing: Using sensory details
- Narrative writing: Personal experiences

**Speaking & Listening:**
- Classroom discussions and participation
- Oral reading: Fluency, pronunciation, intonation
- Short presentations: Show and tell, book reports
- Listening to stories and lectures
- Following oral instructions
- Role plays and dialogues

## Class 9 English (SSC Curriculum)

### English 1st Paper - 100 Marks

**Structure:**
- Reading: 60 marks
- Vocabulary: 10 marks
- Grammar: 10 marks
- Writing: 20 marks

**Reading Comprehension:**

*Seen Passages (40 marks):*
- From textbook (English for Today)
- Unit-based questions
- Multiple choice questions (MCQ): 10 marks
- Short questions: 10 marks
- True/False: 5 marks
- Fill in blanks: 5 marks
- Matching: 5 marks
- Summary/Main idea: 5 marks

*Unseen Passage (20 marks):*
- Passage not from textbook
- Reading comprehension questions
- MCQ: 5 marks
- Short answer questions: 10 marks
- Information transfer: Flow chart/table: 5 marks

**Vocabulary (10 marks):**
- Fill in gaps with clues: 5 marks
- Fill in gaps without clues: 5 marks
- Contextual meaning
- Word formation
- Synonyms/Antonyms

**Grammar (10 marks):**
- Gap filling with appropriate words
- Articles: 2-3 marks
- Prepositions: 2-3 marks
- Right forms of verbs: 2-3 marks
- Other grammatical items: 2-3 marks

**Writing (20 marks):**
- Paragraph: 10 marks
  - Topics: School, environment, technology, sports, etc.
  - Structure: Introduction, body, conclusion
  - 200-250 words
- Writing emails/letters: 10 marks
  - Email to friend/formal email
  - Format and appropriate language

### English 2nd Paper - 100 Marks

**Structure:**
- Grammar: 60 marks
- Composition: 40 marks

**Grammar (60 marks):**

*Gap Filling (15 marks):*
- Articles: A, an, the (5 marks)
  - Rules for usage
  - Common contexts
  - Practice passages
- Prepositions (5 marks)
  - Time, place, movement prepositions
  - Fixed prepositions
  - Common combinations
- Right forms of verbs (5 marks)
  - Tense identification
  - Gerund vs. infinitive
  - Subject-verb agreement
  - Participles

*Changing Sentences (15 marks):*
- Voice: Active to passive, passive to active (3 marks)
- Narration: Direct to indirect, indirect to direct (3 marks)
- Degrees: Positive, comparative, superlative (3 marks)
- Affirmative to negative, vice versa (3 marks)
- Simple, compound, complex (3 marks)

*Sentence Connectors (5 marks):*
- Using: And, but, or, because, although, however, therefore, etc.
- Logical connections between ideas
- Cohesion in writing

*Punctuation & Capitalization (5 marks):*
- Correct use of punctuation marks
- Capital letters: Rules and practice
- Given unpunctuated passage to punctuate

*Use of Modifiers (5 marks):*
- Pre-modifiers and post-modifiers
- Correct placement
- Avoiding danglers

*Synonyms & Antonyms (5 marks):*
- Identifying synonyms
- Identifying antonyms
- Contextual usage

*Phrases & Idioms (5 marks):*
- Common phrases and their meanings
- Idiomatic expressions
- Using in sentences

*Translation (5 marks):*
- Bengali to English translation
- Focus on grammar and vocabulary
- Maintaining meaning

**Composition (40 marks):**

*Formal Letter/Application (10 marks):*
- Job application
- Admission application
- Complaint letter
- Permission letter
- Format: Address, date, salutation, body, closing
- Formal language and tone

*Paragraph (10 marks):*
- Various topics: Social, environmental, educational
- Structure: Topic sentence, supporting details, conclusion
- Coherence and unity
- 200-250 words

*Composition/Essay (15 marks):*
- Narrative: Personal experience, story
- Descriptive: Place, person, event
- Argumentative: Express opinion with reasons
- Expository: Explain a topic
- 250-300 words
- Introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion

*Dialogue/Conversation (5 marks):*
- Between two persons
- On given situation
- Minimum 5-6 exchanges
- Natural and appropriate language

## Class 10 English (SSC Intensive Preparation)

### Overview
Class 10 focuses on intensive SSC preparation: Complete syllabus, comprehensive revision, strategic practice, mock examinations.

**Same Syllabus as Class 9 with:**
- **Additional Depth**: All topics covered more thoroughly
- **Extensive Practice**: Previous years' questions (10+ years)
- **Mock Examinations**: Monthly full-length tests
- **Error Analysis**: Identifying and correcting weaknesses
- **Time Management**: Completing paper in 3 hours
- **Scoring Strategies**: Maximizing marks in each section

### SSC Preparation Strategies

**English 1st Paper Strategies:**

*Reading:*
- Read textbook thoroughly: All units multiple times
- Practice unseen passages: Various sources
- Improve reading speed
- Understand question types
- Answer completely but concisely
- Time management: 2 hours for reading section

*Vocabulary:*
- Build word bank: Textbook + additional
- Practice contextual meaning
- Use vocabulary in writing

*Grammar:*
- Master all rules
- Practice extensively
- Common errors to avoid

*Writing:*
- Practice paragraph writing: Various topics
- Email/Letter formats memorized
- Time management: 30 min paragraph, 15 min email

**English 2nd Paper Strategies:**

*Grammar:*
- All rules mastered thoroughly
- Practice each type extensively
- Previous years' questions
- Time management: 1.5 hours for grammar

*Composition:*
- All formats practiced
- Multiple drafts of common topics
- Formal language mastery
- Time management: 1.5 hours for composition

**General Strategies:**
- Read questions carefully
- Plan time for each section
- Start with easier questions
- Write neatly and clearly
- Check answers if time permits
- Stay calm and focused
- No blank answers: Write something relevant

### Exam Technique

**Day Before Exam:**
- Light revision: Notes and key points
- No new topics
- Early sleep
- Prepare materials: Pens, admit card, etc.
- Make du'a and relax

**Exam Day:**
- Arrive early at center
- Read instructions carefully
- Allocate time per section
- Start with confident sections
- Manage time strictly
- Check answers if possible

**Answer Writing:**
- Write clear, complete answers
- Use proper grammar and vocabulary
- Organize answers with structure
- Write neatly and legibly
- Use appropriate length: Not too short or too long
- Stick to question asked

## Assessment System

**Formative (Ongoing):**
- Daily: Participation, oral reading, discussions
- Weekly: Quizzes, homework, short writes
- Bi-weekly: Tests, presentations
- Purpose: Guide instruction, provide feedback

**Interim (Periodic):**
- Monthly: Unit tests (both papers)
- Quarterly: Comprehensive exams
- Projects and assignments
- Presentations

**Summative (Terminal):**
- Mid-year exam (December): Full SSC format
- Final exam (June): Full SSC format
- Purpose: Measure mastery, SSC preparation

**Distribution:**
- Continuous Assessment: 40%
- Written Examinations: 50%
- Oral/Listening: 10%

**SSC Mock Tests (Class 10):**
- Monthly mock tests: Full SSC format
- Timed examinations
- SSC marking scheme
