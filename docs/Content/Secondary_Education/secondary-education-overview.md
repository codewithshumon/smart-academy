## Program Introduction

Welcome to Smart Academy's Secondary Education Program, where we guide students aged 11-16 through the transformative journey from adolescence to young adulthood. Our Class 6-10 program represents the critical bridge between foundational primary education and higher education, combining rigorous academic preparation for SSC examinations with comprehensive Islamic education to develop confident, capable, and principled young Muslims ready to excel in higher education and contribute meaningfully to society.

The secondary years are pivotal for intellectual development, character formation, and career preparation. At Smart Academy, we recognize that these five years determine whether students will achieve their full potential academically, spiritually, and personally. Our program prepares students not only for SSC success but for lifelong learning, ethical leadership, and service to humanity under Islamic guidance.

## SSC 2026: Critical Information

### Bangladesh SSC 2026 Curriculum Changes

**IMPORTANT CONTEXT:**
The Bangladesh government has reverted to the National Curriculum 2012 for SSC 2026 examinations, abandoning the 2021 experimental curriculum. This decision affects all current Class 10 students.

**Key Changes:**
- Return to National Curriculum 2012 standards
- Shortened syllabus for SSC 2026 batch
- Traditional division system restored (Science/Humanities/Business)
- Familiar examination structure returned
- Class 10 students complete entire syllabus in 1 year (accelerated pace)
- 32 subjects available across all streams

**Examination Structure:**
- **Non-Practical Subjects**: 70 marks theory + 30 marks MCQ = 100 marks
- **Practical Subjects**: 75 marks theory + 25 marks practical = 100 marks
- **ICT**: 50 marks total (special structure)
- **Examination Period**: February 2026
- **Duration**: Approximately 2-3 weeks

**Subject Categories:**

**Compulsory Subjects (All Streams):**
1. Bangla (Bangla 1st Paper & Bangla 2nd Paper) - 200 marks total
2. English (English 1st Paper & English 2nd Paper) - 200 marks total
3. Mathematics - 100 marks
4. Bangladesh & Global Studies - 100 marks
5. Information & Communication Technology (ICT) - 50 marks
6. Islamic Studies (Religion) - 100 marks

**Science Group Additional Subjects:**
7. Physics - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
8. Chemistry - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
9. Biology - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
10. Higher Mathematics - 100 marks (optional but recommended)

**Total for Science Group**: 950-1050 marks

**Smart Academy's Response:**
Our experienced faculty has successfully adapted to National Curriculum 2012, ensuring:
- Comprehensive coverage of shortened syllabus
- Strategic focus on high-yield topics
- Regular assessment and progress monitoring
- Intensive revision and practice
- Mock examinations mimicking actual SSC format
- Individual student support and remediation
- Stress management and exam strategies

## Assessment & Evaluation

### Comprehensive Assessment System

We use multiple methods to monitor learning and guide instruction:

**Formative Assessment** (Ongoing):
- **Daily**: Class discussions, questioning, observations, exit tickets
- **Weekly**: Quizzes, work samples, homework review
- **Bi-weekly**: Short tests, projects, presentations
- **Purpose**: Guide instruction, provide feedback, support learning
- **Weight**: Contributes to continuous assessment

**Interim Assessment** (Periodic):
- **Monthly**: Unit tests in all major subjects
- **Quarterly**: Comprehensive examinations
- **Progress Monitoring**: Track mastery of learning objectives
- **Diagnostic**: Identify strengths and areas for improvement
- **Weight**: Major component of continuous assessment

**Summative Assessment** (Terminal):
- **Mid-Year Examination** (December): Comprehensive test covering first semester
- **Final Examination** (June): Comprehensive test covering entire year
- **SSC Mock Examinations** (Classes 9-10): Full-length practice tests
- **Purpose**: Measure mastery, prepare for SSC, determine promotion
- **Weight**: Major component of final grade

**SSC Preparation Assessment** (Classes 9-10):
- **Monthly Test Series**: Chapter-wise tests
- **Mock Examinations**: Full SSC-format tests (minimum 4 per year)
- **Board Question Practice**: Previous years' questions
- **Time-Based Tests**: Exam condition practice
- **Subject-Wise Analysis**: Identify weak areas
- **Strategy Development**: Test-taking strategies

**Laboratory Assessment** (Science Subjects):
- **Practical Skills**: Experimental techniques, safety protocols
- **Laboratory Reports**: Documentation, analysis, conclusions
- **Practical Examinations**: Hands-on assessment
- **Weight**: 25% of subject grade (practical subjects)

**Assessment Distribution:**
- **Continuous Assessment**: 40%
  - Daily work, homework, class participation: 10%
  - Quizzes and short tests: 10%
  - Monthly tests: 10%
  - Projects and assignments: 10%
- **Written Examinations**: 50%
  - Mid-year examination: 20%
  - Final examination: 30%
- **Practical/Oral**: 10%
  - Laboratory work (Science): 5%
  - Presentations and oral exams: 5%

**Grading Scale:**
- **A+** (80-100): Outstanding - Exceptional achievement
- **A** (70-79): Excellent - High achievement
- **A-** (60-69): Very Good - Strong achievement
- **B** (50-59): Good - Satisfactory achievement
- **C** (40-49): Satisfactory - Acceptable achievement
- **D** (33-39): Pass - Minimum acceptable achievement
- **F** (Below 33): Fail - Does not meet minimum standards

**Grade Point Average (GPA):**
- A+ = 5.00
- A = 4.00
- A- = 3.50
- B = 3.00
- C = 2.00
- D = 1.00
- F = 0.00

## Stream Selection Guidance

### Choosing the Right Path (End of Class 8)

One of the most important decisions is stream selection for SSC:

**Three Streams Available:**

**1. Science Stream**
- **Best For**: Students interested in engineering, medicine, technology, research
- **Core Subjects**: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Higher Mathematics
- **Career Paths**: Doctor, Engineer, Scientist, Researcher, Pharmacist, Technology
- **Requirements**: Strong performance in mathematics and science (Class 6-8)
- **Recommended GPA**: Minimum 4.0 in Class 8

**2. Humanities Stream**
- **Best For**: Students interested in law, social sciences, literature, education, civil service
- **Core Subjects**: History, Geography, Civics, Economics, Sociology (various combinations)
- **Career Paths**: Lawyer, Teacher, Civil Servant, Journalist, Social Worker, Diplomat
- **Requirements**: Strong language and analytical skills
- **Recommended GPA**: Minimum 3.5 in Class 8

**3. Business Studies Stream**
- **Best For**: Students interested in business, finance, management, accounting
- **Core Subjects**: Accounting, Business Organization, Finance, Marketing
- **Career Paths**: Accountant, Business Manager, Banker, Entrepreneur, Financial Analyst
- **Requirements**: Strong mathematical and analytical skills
- **Recommended GPA**: Minimum 3.5 in Class 8

**Selection Process:**
- **Class 8 Performance Review**: Academic record analysis
- **Interest Assessment**: Career interest inventories
