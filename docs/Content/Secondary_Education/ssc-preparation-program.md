## Introduction

### Welcome to SSC Preparation

The Secondary School Certificate (SSC) examination is a crucial milestone in every Bangladeshi student's academic journey. It's the culmination of ten years of school education and opens doors to higher secondary education and beyond. At Smart Academy, we understand the significance of this examination and have designed a comprehensive preparation program to ensure our students not only succeed but excel.

**Conducted by:** Eight regional education boards in Bangladesh
- Dhaka Board
- Rajshahi Board
- Chittagong Board
- Comilla Board
- Jessore Board
- Barishal Board
- Sylhet Board
- Dinajpur Board
- Madrasah Board
- Technical Board

- Foundation for HSC (Higher Secondary Certificate)

## SSC 2026 Examination Structure

### General Information

**Total Subjects:** 10-11 subjects
- 9 compulsory subjects (common to all streams)
- 1-2 optional subjects (stream-specific)

**Assessment Components:**

1. **Written Examination:** 70% (varies by subject)
2. **Practical/Project:** 20-30% (varies by subject)
3. **Continuous Assessment:** 10% (varies by subject)

**Examination Duration:**
- Theory exams: 2-3 hours per subject
- Practical exams: Subject-specific
- Spread over 2-3 weeks

**Grading System:**

| Marks Range | Letter Grade | Grade Point | Description |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| 80-100 | A+ | 5.00 | Outstanding |
| 70-79 | A | 4.00 | Excellent |
| 60-69 | A- | 3.50 | Very Good |
| 50-59 | B | 3.00 | Good |
| 40-49 | C | 2.00 | Satisfactory |
| 33-39 | D | 1.00 | Pass |
| 0-32 | F | 0.00 | Fail |

**GPA Calculation:**
- Sum of grade points of best 8 subjects (excluding optional subjects with lower grades)
- Divided by 8
- GPA: 0.00 to 5.00
- Golden A+: A+ in all subjects including optional
- Regular A+: GPA 5.00 (may have A in one or two optional subjects)

### Question Patterns

**Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ):**
- Usually 25-30 questions
- 1 mark each
- Total: 25-30 marks
- Duration: 25-30 minutes
- No negative marking (usually)

**Creative Questions/Broad Questions:**
- 6-8 questions provided
- Answer 4-6 (varies by subject)
- Each question has multiple parts
- Total: 40-45 marks
- Requires understanding, application, analysis

**Short Questions:**
- 8-10 questions provided
- Answer 6-8
- Brief answers required
- Total: 15-20 marks

**Practical/Project:**
- Conducted before or during exam period
- Laboratory work (Science subjects)
- Project work (Social Sciences, Languages)
- Practical application (ICT, Accounting)

## Revision Techniques

### Effective Revision Methods

#### 1. Spaced Repetition

**Concept:**
- Review material at increasing intervals
- Strengthens long-term memory
- More effective than cramming

**Schedule:**
- Day 1: Learn new topic
- Day 2: Revise once
- Day 4: Revise again
- Day 7: Revise again
- Day 14: Revise again
- Day 30: Revise again

**Application:**
- Use for memorization (formulas, definitions)
- Vocabulary learning
- Facts and dates

#### 2. Active Recall

**Method:**
- Try to remember without looking at notes
- Test yourself
- More effective than passive re-reading

**Techniques:**
- Flashcards
- Self-quizzing
- Explain to someone
- Write from memory

**Benefits:**
- Identifies gaps
- Strengthens memory
- Builds confidence

#### 3. The Feynman Technique

**Steps:**
1. Choose a topic
2. Teach it to someone (or pretend to)
3. Identify gaps in your understanding
4. Review and simplify
5. Teach again

**Why it works:**
- True understanding shown by ability to teach
- Simplification aids memory
- Identifies weak areas

#### 4. Mind Mapping

**Process:**
- Central topic in center
- Main ideas as branches
- Sub-topics as smaller branches
- Use colors, images

**Benefits:**
- Visual learning
- See connections
- Whole-picture understanding
- Creative and engaging

**Best for:**
- Essay subjects
- Interconnected topics
- Revision overview

#### 5. SQ3R Method

**Steps:**
1. **Survey:** Quick scan of material
2. **Question:** Create questions about content
3. **Read:** Read actively to answer questions
4. **Recite:** Say answers in own words
5. **Review:** Go over material again

**Effective for:**
- Textbook reading
- New topic learning
- Deep understanding

#### 6. Pomodoro Technique

**Process:**
- Study for 25 minutes (focused)
- Break for 5 minutes
- Repeat 4 times
- Long break (15-30 minutes)

**Benefits:**
- Maintains focus
- Prevents burnout
- Productivity boost
- Time management

#### 7. Past Paper Practice

**Strategy:**
- Solve past 10 years' papers
- Time yourself
- Mark honestly
- Analyze mistakes

**Benefits:**
- Understand question patterns
- Time management
- Identify important topics
- Build exam confidence
