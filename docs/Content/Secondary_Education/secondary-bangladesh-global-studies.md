## Introduction

### Welcome to Bangladesh & Global Studies

Bangladesh & Global Studies is a comprehensive subject that helps students understand their own country, its rich heritage, and its place in the global community. At Smart Academy, we believe that knowledge of history, geography, civics, and culture is essential for developing informed, responsible, and globally-aware citizens.

### Subject Importance

**Why Study Bangladesh & Global Studies?**

- Develop national identity and patriotic values
- Understand Bangladesh's journey from ancient times to modern nation
- Learn about global diversity and interconnections
- Prepare for SSC examinations
- Build foundation for higher studies in social sciences
- Develop critical thinking and analytical skills
- Foster civic responsibility and active citizenship

### Islamic Perspective

In Islam, seeking knowledge about the world and human societies is highly encouraged. The Quran repeatedly asks believers to reflect on creation, civilizations, and the rise and fall of nations:

> "Say, 'Travel through the land and observe how He began creation.'" (Quran 29:20)

Understanding history, geography, and social systems helps us:
- Recognize Allah's wisdom in creating diverse peoples and lands
- Learn from past civilizations and their moral lessons
- Appreciate the interconnectedness of humanity
- Fulfill our role as khalifa (stewards) on Earth

## Program Philosophy

### Educational Approach

Smart Academy's Bangladesh & Global Studies program is built on these principles:

**1. Student-Centered Learning**
- Active participation in discussions and debates
- Hands-on projects and field experiences
- Inquiry-based exploration
- Critical analysis of sources

**2. Integrated Knowledge**
- Connecting history, geography, economics, and civics
- Linking local and global perspectives
- Relating past events to present situations
- Understanding cause-and-effect relationships

**3. Islamic Values Integration**
- Examining historical events through ethical lenses
- Understanding Islamic contributions to civilization
- Developing moral judgment based on Islamic principles
- Recognizing human rights and social justice in Islam

**4. Practical Application**
- Developing map-reading and research skills
- Preparing students for civic participation
- Building awareness of current events
- Encouraging community engagement

### Learning Methodology

**Diverse Teaching Methods:**

1. **Interactive Lectures**: Engaging presentations with multimedia
2. **Group Discussions**: Facilitated debates on historical and current issues
3. **Project Work**: Research projects, presentations, exhibitions
4. **Field Trips**: Visits to museums, historical sites, government offices
5. **Guest Speakers**: Historians, geographers, government officials
6. **Map Work**: Practical geography exercises
7. **Documentary Analysis**: Critical viewing of historical documentaries
8. **Timeline Activities**: Creating chronological understanding
9. **Role Playing**: Historical simulations and debates
10. **Current Events Analysis**: Weekly news discussions

## Bangladesh Studies

### Section 1: Ancient Bangladesh (Pre-Historic to 1204 CE)

#### Pre-Historic Period

**Stone Age in Bengal:**
- Evidence of human habitation 100,000 years ago
- Archaeological sites: Mahasthangarh, Wari-Bateshwar
- Tools, pottery, and early settlements
- Transition from hunter-gatherers to agricultural communities

**Early Civilizations:**
- Development of agriculture in the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta
- Rice cultivation and settled communities
- Early trade networks
- Religious practices and burial customs

#### Ancient Kingdoms (500 BCE - 750 CE)

**Gangaridai and Prasioi:**
- Powerful kingdoms mentioned in Greek sources
- Resisted Alexander the Great's expansion
- Advanced military organization
- Trade with Roman Empire

**Gupta Period (320-550 CE):**
- Golden age of Indian culture
- Buddhism and Hinduism flourishing in Bengal
- Educational centers at Paharpur and Mainamati
- Art, literature, and architecture

**Shashanka's Kingdom (590-625 CE):**
- First independent Bengali king
- Capital at Karnasuvarna (modern Murshidabad)
- Promoted Shaivism
- Conflict with Harsha and Buddhist kings

#### The Pala Dynasty (750-1161 CE)

**Foundation and Expansion:**
- Gopala established the dynasty (750 CE)
- Democratic election of the first king
- Control over Bengal and Bihar
- Dharmapala and Devapala expanded empire

**Buddhist Golden Age:**
- Patronage of Buddhism
- Vikramashila and Somapura Mahavihara universities
- International Buddhist learning centers
- Scholars like Atisa Dipankara

**Art and Architecture:**
- Paharpur Buddhist Vihara (UNESCO World Heritage Site)
- Terracotta temples
- Bronze sculptures
- Illuminated manuscripts

**Decline:**
- Invasions by Chola and Chalukya dynasties
- Internal conflicts and weak rulers
- Rise of Sena dynasty

#### The Sena Dynasty (1161-1204 CE)

**Establishment:**
- Vijayasena overthrew the Palas
- Hindu Brahmanical revival
- Capital at Nabadwip

**Cultural Achievements:**
- Sanskrit literature flourished
- Jayadeva composed Gita Govinda
- Development of Bengali language
- Social and religious reforms

**End of Ancient Period:**
- Turkish invasion by Bakhtiar Khilji (1204 CE)
- Beginning of Islamic rule in Bengal
- Transformation of political and cultural landscape

### Section 2: Medieval Bangladesh (1204-1757 CE)

#### Sultanate Period (1204-1576 CE)

**Establishment of Muslim Rule:**
- Bakhtiar Khilji's conquest (1204 CE)
- Integration into Delhi Sultanate
- Spread of Islam in Bengal
- Development of syncretic culture

**Independent Bengal Sultanate (1352-1576 CE):**

**Major Dynasties:**
1. Ilyas Shahi Dynasty (1352-1487)
   - Shamsuddin Ilyas Shah founded independent sultanate
   - Expanded territory
   - Built mosques and madrasas
   - Promoted Persian and Bengali languages

2. Hussain Shahi Dynasty (1494-1538)
   - Golden age of Bengal Sultanate
   - Alauddin Hussain Shah (1494-1519) - greatest sultan
   - Religious tolerance and cultural synthesis
   - Chaitanya Mahaprabhu and Bhakti movement flourished
   - Promoted Bengali literature

3. Karrani Dynasty (1564-1576)
   - Last independent Afghan dynasty
   - Battle of Tukaroi (1575)
   - Battle of Rajmahal (1576)
   - Integration into Mughal Empire

**Cultural and Economic Development:**
- Growth of cities: Gaur, Pandua, Sonargaon
- Magnificent mosques: Sixty Dome Mosque (Bagerhat)
- Development of Bengali literature
- Maritime trade with Southeast Asia, China, Middle East
- Agricultural prosperity
- Cloth production (muslin)

#### Mughal Period (1576-1757 CE)

**Conquest and Administration:**
- Akbar's conquest completed by 1576
- Bengal became Mughal province (Subah)
- Provincial capital at Dhaka (1608)
- Efficient revenue system

**Economic Prosperity:**
- Bengal became richest Mughal province
- Famous for muslin cloth, silk, rice
- European trading companies established
- Dhaka emerged as major city (population 1 million)

**Major Developments:**

**Under Shah Jahan (1628-1658):**
- Construction of Lalbagh Fort (Dhaka)
- Star Mosque and other architectural monuments
- Peak of Mughal architecture in Bengal

**Under Aurangzeb (1658-1707):**
- Shaista Khan as governor (1664-1688)
- Expelled Portuguese pirates
- Economic and administrative reforms
- Infrastructure development

**Under Murshid Quli Khan (1717-1727):**
- Virtually independent nawab
- Capital moved to Murshidabad
- Efficient tax collection
- Promoted trade and commerce

**Cultural Synthesis:**
- Development of Bengali Islamic literature
- Sufi saints and their influence
- Integration of Hindu and Muslim traditions
- Growth of syncretic practices

**Decline of Mughal Power:**
- Weak successors after Murshid Quli Khan
- Siraj-ud-Daulah became Nawab (1756)
- Conflict with British East India Company
- Battle of Plassey (1757) - turning point

### Section 3: British Colonial Period (1757-1947)

#### Early British Rule (1757-1857)

**Battle of Plassey (1757):**
- Conflict between Siraj-ud-Daulah and British
- Robert Clive's conspiracy with Mir Jafar
- Betrayal led to British victory
- Beginning of British political control

**Consolidation of Power:**
- Battle of Buxar (1764)
- Dual government system (1765-1772)
- Company rule established
- Exploitation and economic drain

**Economic Exploitation:**
- Destruction of muslin and cottage industries
- Forced cultivation of indigo and opium
- Great Bengal Famine (1770) - 10 million died
- Permanent Settlement (1793) - created zamindari system
- Drain of wealth to Britain

**Social and Cultural Changes:**
- Introduction of English education
- Bengal Renaissance
- Religious reform movements
- Rise of Western-educated middle class

**Resistance Movements:**
- Fakir-Sannyasi Rebellion (1763-1800)
- Titumir's Bamboo Fort Rebellion (1831)
- Indigo Revolt (1859-1860)
- Peasant uprisings

#### British Crown Rule (1858-1947)

**After Sepoy Mutiny:**
- Direct British Crown rule from 1858
- Queen Victoria's proclamation
- Administrative reorganization
- Infrastructure development (railways, telegraphs)

**Partition of Bengal (1905):**
- Lord Curzon's decision to divide Bengal
- East Bengal and Assam province created (capital: Dhaka)
- Muslim support, Hindu opposition
- Swadeshi movement and boycott of British goods
- Annulment in 1911

**Political Awakening:**

**Formation of Political Parties:**
- Indian National Congress (1885)
- All India Muslim League (1906) - founded in Dhaka
- Communist Party, Socialist groups
- Various nationalist organizations

**Major Movements:**
1. Swadeshi Movement (1905-1911)
   - Boycott of British goods
   - Promotion of indigenous industries
   - National education movement
   - Cultural nationalism

2. Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922)
   - Gandhi's leadership
   - Boycott of British institutions
   - Hindu-Muslim unity
   - Mass participation

3. Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-1934)
   - Salt March
   - Non-payment of taxes
   - Women's participation
   - Severe repression

4. Quit India Movement (1942)
   - Demand for immediate independence
   - "Do or Die" call
   - Mass arrests and violence
   - Underground resistance

**Path to Partition:**

**Key Events:**
- Lahore Resolution (1940) - demand for Muslim homelands
- Cripps Mission (1942)
- Cabinet Mission Plan (1946)
- Direct Action Day (1946)
- Communal riots
- Mountbatten Plan (1947)

**Creation of Pakistan (August 14, 1947):**
- Two-nation theory
- East Bengal became East Pakistan
- Massive population transfers
- Communal violence
- Refugee crisis

### Section 4: Pakistan Period (1947-1971)

#### Early Years (1947-1952)

**Political Structure:**
- East Pakistan as part of Pakistan
- Capital in West Pakistan (Karachi, later Islamabad)
- Underrepresentation of Bengalis
- Military and bureaucratic dominance by West Pakistan

**Language Movement:**

**Background:**
- Urdu imposed as sole state language (1948)
- Bengali spoken by 56% of Pakistan's population
- Demand for Bengali as state language
- Student and intellectual protests

**21st February 1952:**
- Police firing on student protesters in Dhaka
- Martyrdom of Salam, Barkat, Rafiq, Jabbar, Shafiur
- Mass uprising across East Pakistan
- International Mother Language Day (UNESCO recognition)
- Victory: Bengali recognized as state language (1956)

**Significance:**
- Birth of Bengali nationalism
- Foundation of independence movement
- Cultural and linguistic identity assertion
- Symbol of resistance against oppression

#### Growing Discontent (1952-1971)

**Economic Exploitation:**
- East Pakistan earned 60% of foreign exchange (jute)
- West Pakistan received 70% of development budget
- Disparity in per capita income
- Lack of industrialization in East
- Brain drain to West Pakistan

**Political Discrimination:**
- Military and civil service dominated by West
- 1956 Constitution delayed and inadequate
- One Unit scheme in West vs. East
- Political instability and military coups
- Martial law periods (1958-1962, 1969-1971)

**Natural Disasters:**
- Cyclone of 1970 - 500,000 deaths
- Inadequate response from central government
- Increased alienation of Bengalis

**Six-Point Movement (1966):**

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Six Points:
1. Federal parliamentary system
2. Federal government limited to defense and foreign affairs
3. Separate currency or fiscal accounts for each province
4. Provincial taxation powers
5. Separate foreign exchange accounts
6. Provincial militia or paramilitary forces

**Significance:**
- Charter of Bengali autonomy
- Massive public support
- Agartala Conspiracy Case (1968)
- Mass Movement (1969)

**1970 Elections:**
- First general election in Pakistan
- Awami League won 160 of 162 seats in East Pakistan
- Clear mandate for Six Points
- Majority in Pakistan's National Assembly
- West Pakistan refused to transfer power
- Constitutional crisis

#### Liberation War (1971)

**March 1971 - Crisis Deepens:**

**Key Events:**
- March 1-25: Non-cooperation movement
- March 7: Historic speech by Sheikh Mujibur Rahman
  - "This time the struggle is for our freedom"
  - "This time the struggle is for our independence"
- March 25: Operation Searchlight - Pakistan military crackdown
- March 26: Declaration of Independence

**Independence Declaration:**

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's declaration (transmitted):
> "This may be my last message. From today Bangladesh is independent. I call upon the people of Bangladesh, wherever you are and with whatever you have, to resist the army of occupation. Your fight must go on until the last soldier of the Pakistan occupation army is expelled from the soil of Bangladesh. Final victory is ours."

**The Nine-Month War:**

**Phase 1 (March-May 1971):**
- Spontaneous resistance across Bangladesh
- Formation of Mukti Bahini (Freedom Fighters)
- Genocide by Pakistan Army
- Massacre of intellectuals, minorities, civilians
- 10 million refugees fled to India
- International awareness growing

**Phase 2 (June-September 1971):**
- Organized guerrilla warfare
- Mukti Bahini training in India
- Formation of Bangladesh Government in Exile (April 17)
- 11 sectors of war
- Control of rural areas
- International diplomacy

**Phase 3 (October-December 1971):**
- Intensified guerrilla attacks
- Pakistan Air Force attack on India (December 3)
- India's official entry into war
- Joint Bangladesh-India operations
- Liberation of major cities
- Surrender on December 16, 1971

**Victory Day (December 16, 1971):**
- Pakistan Army surrendered in Dhaka
- 93,000 Pakistani soldiers became POWs
- Birth of independent Bangladesh
- Sheikh Mujibur Rahman released (January 8, 1972)
- Triumphant return to Dhaka (January 10, 1972)

**Cost of Freedom:**
- 3 million martyrs
- 200,000 women violated
- Millions displaced
- Massive destruction of infrastructure
- Economic devastation

### Section 5: Independent Bangladesh (1971-Present)

#### Reconstruction Period (1971-1975)

**Immediate Challenges:**
- War-ravaged economy
- Destroyed infrastructure
- Millions of refugees to rehabilitate
- Food shortage and famine risk
- International recognition needed

**Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Leadership:**

**Major Achievements (1972-1975):**
1. Constitution adopted (December 16, 1972)
   - Parliamentary democracy
   - Four pillars: Nationalism, Socialism, Democracy, Secularism
   - Bengali as state language

2. International Recognition
   - UN membership (1974)
   - OIC membership
   - Recognition by 140+ countries

3. Reconstruction Efforts
   - Infrastructure rebuilding
   - Nationalization of industries
   - Land reforms
   - Educational expansion

**Challenges:**
- Famine of 1974
- Political instability
- Economic difficulties
- Law and order problems
- International conspiracies

**Tragic End:**
- August 15, 1975: Military coup
- Assassination of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and family
- Brutal murder of Bangabandhu and most family members
- Sheikh Hasina and Sheikh Rehana survived (abroad)
- Dark chapter in Bangladesh history

#### Military Rule Period (1975-1990)

**Ziaur Rahman Era (1975-1981):**
- Military takeover
- Constitutional amendments
- Multi-party system introduced
- Formation of BNP
- Economic liberalization
- "Bangladesh Nationalism" vs. "Bengali Nationalism"
- Assassination (May 30, 1981)

**Ershad Era (1982-1990):**
- Another military coup
- Martial law imposed
- Formation of Jatiya Party
- Declared Islam as state religion (1988)
- Economic development programs
- Massive corruption
- Popular uprising (1990)
- Resigned and democracy restored

#### Democratic Period (1991-Present)

**Parliamentary Democracy:**
- 1991: Free and fair elections
- BNP government (1991-1996)
- Awami League government (1996-2001)
- BNP-Jamaat coalition (2001-2006)
- Caretaker government (2007-2008)
- Awami League government (2009-present)

**Major Developments:**

**Economic Progress:**
- GDP growth averaging 6-7% annually
- Garment industry growth (80% of exports)
- Remittances from overseas workers
- Poverty reduction (from 57% to 20%)
- Food self-sufficiency
- Digital Bangladesh initiatives

**Infrastructure Development:**
- Padma Bridge (own financing)
- Metro Rail in Dhaka
- Elevated expressways
- Power generation increased
- Digital connectivity

**Social Indicators:**
- Female education enrollment increased
- Maternal mortality reduced
- Life expectancy increased to 73 years
- Literacy rate above 75%
- Women's empowerment

**International Relations:**
- Active UN peacekeeping participation
- Climate change leadership
- Rohingya refugee crisis management
- Regional cooperation (SAARC, BIMSTEC)

**Challenges:**
- Political polarization
- Corruption
- Traffic congestion
- Environmental degradation
- Rohingya crisis
- Climate change vulnerability

**Recent Achievements:**
- Graduation from LDC status (2026)
- Bangabandhu Satellite
- Nuclear power plant (Rooppur)
- COVID-19 management
- Vaccine production capacity

### Section 6: Bangladesh Geography

#### Physical Geography

**Location and Boundaries:**
- South Asia, northeastern part
- Coordinates: 20°34'N to 26°38'N, 88°01'E to 92°41'E
- Borders: India (4,096 km), Myanmar (193 km)
- Coastline: 580 km (Bay of Bengal)
- Total area: 147,570 sq km
- World's 94th largest country

**Physiographic Regions:**

**1. Tertiary Hills (12%):**
- Chittagong Hill Tracts
- Sylhet Hills
- Highest point: Keokradong or Tahjingdong (1,064 m)
- Steep slopes and valleys
- Dense forests
- Indigenous communities

**2. Pleistocene Terraces (8%):**
- Barind Tract (Rajshahi)
- Madhupur Tract (Dhaka-Mymensingh)
- Lalmai Hills (Comilla)
- Higher elevation
- Red clay soil
- Less fertile

**3. Floodplains (80%):**
- Ganges-Brahmaputra-Meghna delta
- World's largest delta
- Flat and fertile
- Annual flooding
- Most populated region
- Major agricultural area

**Rivers and Water Systems:**

**Major Rivers:**
1. Padma (Ganges) - 366 km in Bangladesh
2. Brahmaputra (Jamuna) - 292 km
3. Meghna - 272 km
4. Surma-Kushiyara
5. Karnaphuli
6. Sangu
7. Total: 700+ rivers

**River Systems:**
- Three major river systems converge
- Annual sediment deposition
- Navigable waterways
- Fishing resources
- Flood and erosion challenges

**Haors and Wetlands:**
- Haor basin in northeast
- Hakaluki Haor (largest)
- Tanguar Haor (Ramsar site)
- Important for biodiversity
- Fish production
- Migratory bird sanctuary

**Coastal Zone:**
- 19 coastal districts
- Sundarbans mangrove forest (World Heritage Site)
- Home to Royal Bengal Tiger
- Coastal islands and chars
- Vulnerable to cyclones and sea-level rise

**Climate:**

**Type:** Tropical monsoon climate

**Seasons:**
1. Winter (December-February)
   - Temperature: 10-20°C
   - Dry and pleasant
   - Northwestern fog

2. Pre-monsoon/Summer (March-May)
   - Temperature: 25-38°C
   - Hot and humid
   - Nor'westers (Kalbaishakhi)

3. Monsoon (June-September)
   - Temperature: 25-31°C
   - Heavy rainfall
   - 80% of annual rain
   - Floods

4. Post-monsoon (October-November)
   - Temperature: 20-30°C
   - Mild and pleasant
   - Occasional cyclones

**Rainfall:**
- Average: 2,200 mm annually
- Highest: Sylhet region (4,000+ mm)
- Lowest: Northwest (1,400 mm)
- Cherrapunji effect in northeast

**Natural Disasters:**
- Floods (annual occurrence)
- Cyclones (Bay of Bengal)
- Tornadoes
- River erosion
- Earthquakes (seismic zone)
- Droughts (occasional)

### Section 7: Culture and Traditions

#### Bengali Culture

**Language and Literature:**

**Bangla Language:**
- 7th most spoken language globally
- 230+ million speakers
- Rich literary heritage
- Emerged from Magadhi Prakrit
- Modern Bangla standardized in 19th century

**Classical Literature:**
- Charyapada (8th-12th century) - earliest Bengali text
- Medieval poetry: Chandidas, Vidyapati
- Mangal Kavya tradition
- Folk literature: Maimansingha Gitika

**Modern Literature:**
- Rabindranath Tagore (Nobel Prize 1913)
  - Author of national anthems of Bangladesh and India
  - Gitanjali, poems, novels, plays
- Kazi Nazrul Islam (National Poet)
  - Rebel poet
  - Songs of equality and revolution
- Michael Madhusudan Dutt
- Sarat Chandra Chattopadhyay
- Begum Rokeya (feminist pioneer)

**Contemporary Literature:**
- Jasimuddin (folk poet)
- Shamsur Rahman
- Al Mahmud
- Humayun Ahmed
- Taslima Nasrin
- Many others

**Language Movement Legacy:**
- International Mother Language Day (21 February)
- UNESCO recognition (1999)
- Shaheed Minar monument
- Ekushey Book Fair (month-long)
- Protection of linguistic diversity

#### Music and Performing Arts

**Traditional Music:**

**1. Rabindra Sangeet:**
- Songs composed by Tagore
- 2,230+ songs
- Various themes and styles
- National anthem: "Amar Sonar Bangla"

**2. Nazrul Geeti:**
- Songs by Nazrul Islam
- Revolutionary and devotional
- Islamic themes prominent

**3. Folk Music:**
- Baul (spiritual folk tradition)
  - Lalon Shah (greatest Baul philosopher)
  - UNESCO Intangible Heritage
- Bhatiali (boatmen's songs)
- Bhawaiya (northern Bengal)
- Jari, Sari, Murshidi
- Gambhira (Rajshahi)

#### Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and Early Islam

**Birth and Early Life (570-610 CE):**
- Born in Mecca
- Orphaned early
- Raised by grandfather and uncle
- Known as "Al-Amin" (The Trustworthy)
- Married Khadijah (RA)
- Contemplation in Cave Hira

**Prophethood (610-632 CE):**

**First Revelation (610 CE):**
- Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) appeared
- First verses of Quran revealed (Surah Al-Alaq)
- "Read in the name of your Lord who created"
- Beginning of 23-year revelation period

**Meccan Period (610-622 CE):**
- Initial secret preaching
- Public declaration after 3 years
- Core message: Tawhid (Oneness of Allah)
- Opposition from Quraysh tribe
- Persecution of early Muslims
- Boycott of Banu Hashim
- Year of Sorrow (619 CE) - deaths of Khadijah and Abu Talib
- Isra and Mi'raj (Night Journey)
- Pledges of Aqabah

**Hijrah (622 CE):**
- Migration to Medina
- Islamic calendar begins
- Significance: Sacrifice for faith
- Establishment of Muslim community

**Medinan Period (622-632 CE):**

**State Building:**
- Constitution of Medina
- Multi-religious society
- Muslims, Jews, pagans coexisted
- Islamic governance established
- Mosque as community center

**Major Events:**
- Battle of Badr (624 CE) - decisive victory
- Battle of Uhud (625 CE) - lessons in discipline
- Battle of Trench (627 CE) - defensive strategy
- Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (628 CE) - peace treaty
- Conquest of Mecca (630 CE) - peaceful
- Farewell Pilgrimage (632 CE)
- Farewell Sermon (human equality, rights)

**Death (632 CE):**
- Passed away in Medina
- Age 63
- Buried in Medina
- Legacy: Complete religion, transformed society

**Quranic Revelation:**
- 23 years of revelation
- 114 Surahs (chapters)
- 6,236 verses
- Final revelation: Surah Al-Ma'idah 5:3
- Preserved in original Arabic
- Memorized by thousands
- Written compilation under Abu Bakr and Uthman (RA)

- First Caliph
- Close companion of Prophet
- Compiled Quran
- Fought apostasy (Ridda wars)
- Expanded beyond Arabia
- Humble and pious leader

- Islamic calendar
- Justice and accountability
- Simple lifestyle despite empire
- Assassinated by Persian slave

**Uthman ibn Affan (RA) (644-656 CE):**
- Third Caliph
- Standardized Quran
- Naval expansion
- Further territorial growth
- Internal dissent arose
- Martyred during siege

**Ali ibn Abi Talib (RA) (656-661 CE):**
- Fourth Caliph
- Cousin and son-in-law of Prophet
- Known for wisdom and justice
- Civil wars: Battle of Camel, Siffin
- Khawarij conflict
- Martyred in Kufa
- Shia-Sunni split origins

### Appendix F: Glossary of Terms

**Important Terms:**

- **Autonomy:** Self-governance within a larger state
- **Civilization:** Complex society with cities, government, culture
- **Delta:** Landform at river mouth where it deposits sediment
- **Democracy:** Government by the people
- **Dynasty:** Series of rulers from same family
- **Genocide:** Systematic killing of ethnic/national/religious group
- **Haor:** Wetland ecosystem in Bangladesh
- **Khalifa:** Steward, vicegerent (Islamic concept)
- **Liberation:** Freedom from occupation or oppression
- **Monsoon:** Seasonal wind bringing rain
- **Partition:** Division of territory
- **Peninsula:** Land surrounded by water on three sides
- **Sovereignty:** Supreme power or authority
- **Tawhid:** Oneness of Allah (Islamic monotheism)
