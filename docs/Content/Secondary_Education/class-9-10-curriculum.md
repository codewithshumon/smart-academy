## Document Overview

This comprehensive guide details the Class 9-10 curriculum at Smart Academy, specifically designed for SSC 2026 examination preparation under the National Curriculum 2012. These two critical years represent the culmination of secondary education, requiring intensive study, strategic exam preparation, and comprehensive mastery of all subjects for SSC success.

The SSC (Secondary School Certificate) examination is one of the most important milestones in a Bangladeshi student's academic journey, determining university admission opportunities and future career paths. Smart Academy's SSC program combines rigorous academic preparation with Islamic character development, ensuring students achieve excellence while maintaining strong faith and values.

## SSC 2026: Critical Context

### National Curriculum 2012 Return

**IMPORTANT BACKGROUND:**
The Bangladesh government reverted to National Curriculum 2012 for SSC 2026, abandoning the experimental 2021 curriculum. This change affects all current Class 9-10 students.

**Key Features of SSC 2026:**

**Curriculum Structure:**
- National Curriculum 2012 standards (traditional syllabus)
- Shortened syllabus for SSC 2026 batch
- Division/Stream system: Science, Humanities, Business Studies
- 32 subjects available across streams
- Class 10 accelerated: Complete syllabus in 1 year

**Examination Format:**
- **Non-Practical Subjects**: 70 marks theory + 30 marks MCQ = 100 marks
- **Practical Subjects** (Physics, Chemistry, Biology): 75 marks theory + 25 marks practical = 100 marks
- **ICT**: 50 marks total (25 theory + 25 practical)
- **Examination Period**: February 2026
- **Duration**: Approximately 2-3 weeks
- **Grading**: A+ to F scale, GPA system

**Subject Structure:**

**Compulsory (All Streams):**
1. Bangla 1st Paper - 100 marks
2. Bangla 2nd Paper - 100 marks
3. English 1st Paper - 100 marks
4. English 2nd Paper - 100 marks
5. Mathematics - 100 marks
6. Bangladesh & Global Studies - 100 marks
7. Information & Communication Technology (ICT) - 50 marks
8. Religion (Islamic Studies) - 100 marks

**Science Stream Additional:**
9. Physics - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
10. Chemistry - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
11. Biology - 100 marks (75 theory + 25 practical)
12. Higher Mathematics - 100 marks (optional but highly recommended)

**Total Marks (Science with Higher Math):** 1050 marks

- Simulate actual SSC examination
- Practice time management
- Identify weak areas

- Reduce test anxiety

### Study Techniques

**Time Management:**
- Create study schedule: Subject-wise daily plan
- Prioritize weak subjects
- Break study into manageable chunks (Pomodoro: 45 min study, 15 min break)
- Include revision time
- Balance all subjects

**Active Learning:**
- Don't just read - write, summarize, teach
- Make mind maps and flowcharts
- Create formula sheets and quick notes
- Practice problems actively
- Self-testing regularly

**Revision Strategies:**
- Multiple revisions: 3-4 times for all subjects
- Spaced repetition: Review at intervals
- Focus on weak topics
- Quick revision notes
- Group study (occasionally) for discussions

**Exam Techniques:**
- Read questions carefully
- Plan time per section
- Start with easier questions
- Show all work (math/science)
- Check answers if time permits
- Stay calm and focused

## Parent Partnership in SSC Preparation

### How Parents Can Support

**Academic Support:**
- Provide quiet, comfortable study space
- Ensure proper lighting and resources
- Minimize distractions (TV, social media)
- Encourage consistent study routine
- Monitor progress without excessive pressure
- Hire tutors if needed
- Stay in communication with teachers

**Emotional Support:**
- Believe in your child's abilities
- Provide encouragement and motivation
- Avoid excessive pressure or comparison
- Listen to concerns and worries
- Celebrate efforts and improvements
- Be present and available
- Maintain positive attitude

**Practical Support:**
- Healthy, nutritious meals
- Adequate sleep schedule
- Regular breaks and recreation
- Reduced household responsibilities during exams
- Financial support for resources
- Transportation to school/tutoring
- Attend parent meetings

**Spiritual Support:**
- Pray together as family
- Make du'a for child's success
- Encourage Islamic practices
- Remind of Allah's help and mercy
- Teach trust in Allah (Tawakkul)
- Maintain perspective on success/failure

**Communication:**
- Regular check-ins with child
- Attend parent-teacher meetings
- Contact teachers with concerns
- Join parent groups
- Stay informed about exam schedule
- Be available for school activities

### Signs of Stress (Watch For):**
- Changes in sleep or appetite
- Withdrawal or irritability
- Declining performance
- Physical symptoms (headaches, etc.)
- Excessive worry or anxiety
- Loss of interest

**If Stressed:** Talk, listen, support, consult counselor, reduce pressure, seek help.
