## Engineering Education Philosophy

### What is Engineering?

Engineering is the application of scientific and mathematical principles to design and build machines, structures, systems, and processes that solve problems and meet human needs.

**Engineering vs. Science:**
- **Science** asks "Why?" and seeks to understand the natural world
- **Engineering** asks "How can we solve this?" and creates solutions

Both are interconnected—science informs engineering, and engineering advances science.

### Our Approach to Engineering Education

**Hands-On, Project-Based Learning**

Students learn engineering by doing engineering:
- Real design challenges, not just worksheets
- Building and creating physical products
- Testing and refining designs
- Learning from failures and successes

**Engineering Design Process as Framework**

All engineering activities follow the engineering design process:
1. Ask: Define the problem
2. Imagine: Brainstorm solutions
3. Plan: Select and plan a solution
4. Create: Build a prototype
5. Test: Test and evaluate
6. Improve: Refine based on results

This cyclical process mirrors how professional engineers work.

**Failure is Part of Learning**

In engineering, failure provides valuable information:
- Failed designs teach what doesn't work
- Testing reveals weaknesses to address
- Iteration leads to better solutions
- Resilience and persistence are developed

We create a safe environment where students are encouraged to take risks, fail, learn, and try again.

**Collaboration and Teamwork**

Engineering is collaborative:
- Students work in teams on challenges
- Learn to communicate ideas and listen to others
- Divide tasks and combine strengths
- Navigate disagreements constructively
- Prepare for real-world engineering teams

**Constraints and Criteria**

Real engineering involves working within constraints:
- **Criteria:** What the solution must accomplish (functional requirements)
- **Constraints:** Limitations (budget, materials, time, size, etc.)

Students learn to optimize designs within given parameters.

**Interdisciplinary Connections**

Engineering integrates:
- **Science:** Applying scientific principles and knowledge
- **Technology:** Using tools and technology in design
- **Mathematics:** Calculating, measuring, analyzing data
- **Arts:** Creative design, aesthetics, user experience

Engineering naturally brings STEAM together.

### Learning Principles

**Constructionist Learning**

Students construct knowledge by creating artifacts (Seymour Papert):
- Building deepens understanding
- Creating makes learning personal and meaningful
- Sharing creations promotes communication

**Inquiry-Based Learning**

Students investigate and discover:
- Ask questions about problems and solutions
- Research and explore possibilities
- Test hypotheses through prototypes
- Draw conclusions from evidence

**Authentic Problem-Based Learning**

Engineering challenges connect to real world:
- Solve problems that matter
- Consider actual constraints and needs
- Apply learning to genuine contexts
- Develop relevant skills

**Iterative Design**

Design is not linear but cyclical:
- Initial designs are rarely perfect
- Testing reveals areas for improvement
- Refinement leads to better solutions
- Multiple iterations are expected and encouraged

## Engineering Design Process

### The Engineering Design Process Framework

Smart Academy teaches and uses the Engineering Design Process (EDP) as the foundation for all engineering activities. While various models exist, we use a clear, student-friendly version:

### 1. ASK: Define the Problem

**Objective:** Clearly understand the problem or need

**Activities:**
- Identify what problem needs solving
- Research the background and context
- Define who will use the solution (users)
- Clarify criteria (what solution must do)
- Identify constraints (limitations: materials, cost, time, size, etc.)
- Ask questions to deepen understanding

**Student Questions:**
- What problem are we trying to solve?
- Who has this problem?
- Why is it a problem?
- What does a solution need to accomplish?
- What are the limitations?

**Islamic Connection:**
- Identify problems that benefit humanity
- Consider needs of underserved populations
- Think about environmental impact from the start

### 2. IMAGINE: Brainstorm Solutions

**Objective:** Generate creative ideas for possible solutions

**Activities:**
- Brainstorm many different ideas
- Encourage wild, creative thinking
- Defer judgment (no bad ideas in brainstorming)
- Sketch rough ideas
- Research what solutions already exist
- Combine and build on ideas

**Brainstorming Rules:**
1. Generate as many ideas as possible (quantity over quality)
2. Encourage unusual and creative ideas
3. Build on others' ideas
4. No criticism during brainstorming

**Student Questions:**
- What are many different ways to solve this?
- What do existing solutions do well or poorly?
- How can we think outside the box?
- What if we combine ideas?

**Islamic Connection:**
- Learn from Islamic engineering heritage
- Consider solutions that are ethical and beneficial
- Think about sustainability and stewardship

### 3. PLAN: Select and Design a Solution

**Objective:** Choose the most promising idea and plan how to build it

**Activities:**
- Evaluate ideas against criteria and constraints
- Select the best idea (or combination of ideas)
- Create detailed plans and drawings
- List materials needed
- Consider how to build it
- Anticipate challenges
- Assign roles (if team)

**Planning Tools:**
- Sketches and diagrams
- Labeled drawings
- Materials lists
- Step-by-step procedures
- CAD designs (older students)

**Student Questions:**
- Which idea best meets criteria and constraints?
- How will we build this?
- What materials do we need?
- What could go wrong, and how can we prepare?

**Islamic Connection:**
- Plan for safety and quality (ihsan)
- Minimize waste of materials (no israf)
- Consider ethical implications

### 4. CREATE: Build a Prototype

**Objective:** Construct a model or prototype of the solution

**Activities:**
- Gather materials and tools
- Follow safety procedures
- Build according to plan
- Adapt plan as needed during building
- Solve problems that arise
- Document construction process
- Work collaboratively

**Prototype:** Early model to test concept (doesn't have to be perfect)

**Student Actions:**
- Measure, cut, assemble, attach
- Use tools safely and correctly
- Follow plan but remain flexible
- Help team members
- Stay organized

**Islamic Connection:**
- Work with excellence and care
- Use materials responsibly
- Practice patience
- Support team members (cooperation)

### 5. TEST: Test and Evaluate

**Objective:** Determine how well the solution works

**Activities:**
- Test prototype against criteria
- Collect data (measurements, observations)
- Document results (photos, videos, data tables)
- Evaluate what worked and what didn't
- Identify problems and weaknesses
- Be honest about failures
- Celebrate successes

**Testing Methods:**
- Functional tests (Does it do what it should?)
- Durability tests (How long does it last?)
- User tests (Is it easy to use?)
- Measurements (strength, speed, capacity, etc.)

**Student Questions:**
- Does our solution meet the criteria?
- How well does it work?
- What works well?
- What doesn't work or could be better?
- Why did this happen?

**Islamic Connection:**
- Honesty in reporting results (sidq)
- Thoroughness in testing (ihsan)
- Learning from failures (patience and persistence)

### 6. IMPROVE: Redesign and Refine

**Objective:** Use test results to make the solution better

**Activities:**
- Analyze test results
- Identify specific improvements needed
- Brainstorm ways to improve
- Make changes to design
- Rebuild or modify prototype
- Test again
- Repeat cycle as needed

**Iteration:** The process of making successive improvements

**Student Questions:**
- What changes will make it better?
- How can we fix problems?
- What did we learn from testing?
- Should we try a different approach?

**Improvement Strategies:**
- Modify existing design
- Add or remove features
- Try different materials
- Change dimensions or proportions
- Redesign specific components

**Islamic Connection:**
- Strive for continuous improvement (ihsan)
- Persistence and not giving up
- Learning from mistakes
- Gratitude for lessons learned

### Communication Throughout

**Share and Present:**
- Explain design process and reasoning
- Present final solution to audience
- Create posters, presentations, or videos
- Reflect on learning and challenges
- Receive and give feedback

**Documentation:**
- Engineering notebooks: record ideas, plans, tests, results, reflections
- Photos and videos of process
- Drawings and diagrams
- Data tables and graphs

## Early Childhood Engineering (PlayGroup - KG)

### Program Philosophy

Engineering in early childhood focuses on building, exploring, and simple problem-solving through play. Activities develop spatial reasoning, fine motor skills, creativity, and beginning understanding of cause-and-effect.

### Learning Goals

**Cognitive:**
- Explore how things work
- Understand cause and effect
- Develop spatial awareness
- Begin problem-solving

**Skills:**
- Building and constructing
- Using simple tools
- Following basic instructions
- Trying different approaches

**Social-Emotional:**
- Working together
- Sharing materials
- Handling frustration when structures fall
- Celebrating successes

**Islamic Integration:**
- Caring for materials (amanah)
- Helping friends
- Building with purpose

### Engineering Activities

**1. Building Blocks and Construction**

**Materials:**
- Large blocks (foam, wood, plastic)
- LEGO Duplo
- Magna-Tiles
- Cardboard boxes

**Activities:**
- Free building and exploration
- Building tall towers
- Creating enclosures and spaces
- Bridges between two points
- Simple representations (house, car, etc.)

**Learning:**
- Stability and balance
- Shapes and how they fit together
- Height and length
- Trial and error

**2. Simple Machines Exploration**

**Concepts (very basic):**
- Ramps (inclined planes)
- Wheels and rolling
- Levers (seesaws)
- Pulleys (simple)

**Activities:**
- Rolling cars down ramps (adjusting height)
- Using toy tools
- Playground simple machines (seesaw, slide, swing)
- Pulley to lift toy bucket

**Learning:**
- Things move in different ways
- Ramps make things go down
- Wheels help things roll

**3. Engineering Challenges (Guided)**

**Examples:**
- Build a tall tower
- Build a bridge for a toy car
- Create a house for a stuffed animal
- Build a ramp for a ball
- Make something that floats

**Approach:**
- Teacher presents challenge
- Students explore and attempt
- Teacher assists and encourages
- Celebrate all attempts

**4. Taking Things Apart (Safely)**

**Activity:**
- Provide broken or old items (toys, small appliances) that are safe
- Students explore parts and how they fit together
- Discuss what parts might do

**Safety:**
- Only approved items (no sharp edges, electrical hazards, small parts)
- Close teacher supervision

**Learning:**
- Things are made of parts
- Parts work together
- Curiosity about how things work

**5. Outdoor Engineering**

**Activities:**
- Building with sand and water
- Creating structures with sticks and stones
- Digging and constructing
- Nature building (fairy houses)

**Learning:**
- Properties of natural materials
- Effects of water on structures
- Working with irregular materials

### Assessment

- Observation of engagement and exploration
- Ability to build simple structures
- Willingness to try and experiment
- Persistence when challenges arise
- Sharing and cooperating

## Primary Engineering Program (Class 1-5)

### Program Overview

Primary engineering program introduces the engineering design process and provides hands-on challenges appropriate to developmental level. Students build foundational skills and understanding while having fun creating and testing solutions.

---

### Class 1-2 Engineering

**Integration:** Engineering activities primarily integrated into science and math classes, plus special STEAM units

**Focus:** Simple engineering challenges, building activities, exploring materials

#### Sample Engineering Challenges

**1. Paper Airplane Challenge (Class 1)**

**Problem:** Design a paper airplane that flies far

**Criteria:** Fly at least 3 meters

**Constraints:** One sheet of paper, no tape or other materials

**Process:**
- Learn basic paper airplane fold
- Test flight distance
- Try different designs
- Measure distances
- Identify best design

**Skills:** Following instructions, testing, measuring, comparing

**2. Strong Shape Challenge (Class 1)**

**Problem:** Which shape is strongest?

**Activity:**
- Create columns from paper: cylinder, square, triangle
- Test by placing books on top
- Count books before collapse

**Learning:** Triangles and cylinders are strong shapes in engineering

**3. Bridge Building (Class 2)**

**Problem:** Build a bridge that spans a gap and holds weight

**Criteria:** Span 20 cm, hold toy car

**Constraints:** 20 popsicle sticks, glue, tape

**Process:**
- Imagine different bridge designs
- Plan and draw
- Build
- Test with toy car, then add weight
- Improve

**Skills:** Designing, building, testing, measuring

**4. Cup Tower Challenge (Class 2)**

**Problem:** Build tallest tower from cups

**Constraints:** 20 plastic cups, 5 minutes

**Variations:** Must support ball on top, withstand fan wind

**Learning:** Stable base, balance, speed vs. quality

#### Skills Development (Class 1-2)

- Using simple tools (rulers, tape, scissors) safely
- Measuring with non-standard and standard units
- Drawing simple plans
- Following step-by-step instructions
- Testing and observing results
- Explaining what worked or didn't

### Class 3-4 Engineering

**Time:** Increased engineering activities; some dedicated engineering units

**Focus:** Systematic design process, diverse engineering challenges, beginning documentation

#### Learning Objectives

- Follow engineering design process with guidance
- Create plans before building
- Test designs and collect data
- Iterate based on results
- Work collaboratively
- Explain engineering thinking

#### Sample Engineering Challenges

**1. Earthquake-Resistant Structure (Class 3)**

**Problem:** Design structure to withstand shaking (simulated earthquake)

**Background:** Learn about earthquakes and building design

**Criteria:** At least 30 cm tall, withstand 30 seconds of shaking

**Constraints:** Materials provided (toothpicks, marshmallows, or straws and tape), 40 minutes build time

**Process:**
- Research earthquake-resistant design
- Brainstorm and sketch ideas
- Build structure
- Test on shake table (cookie sheet on top of marbles)
- Measure survival time and height
- Analyze failures
- Redesign and rebuild

**Learning:** Base-to-height ratio, cross-bracing, flexibility, connections

**2. Water Filtration System (Class 3)**

**Problem:** Design system to filter dirty water

**Background:** Clean water access, filtration methods

**Criteria:** Water clearer than initial sample, safe materials

**Constraints:** Plastic bottle, coffee filters, sand, gravel, cotton balls, charcoal, time

**Process:**
- Learn about filtration layers
- Plan filter design (layers)
- Build filter in bottle
- Test with dirty water (soil, debris)
- Observe cleanliness of output
- Iterate to improve

**Learning:** Filtration, layers, particle size, water challenges

**Islamic Connection:** Access to clean water as human right, stewardship of water resources

**3. Catapult Engineering (Class 4)**

**Problem:** Build catapult to launch projectile accurately and far

**Criteria:** Hit target 2 meters away, launch at least 3 meters

**Constraints:** Popsicle sticks, rubber bands, spoon, tape, small projectile (marshmallow or pom-pom)

**Process:**
- Learn about catapults and levers
- Design catapult
- Build and assemble
- Test distance and accuracy
- Adjust for better performance
- Competition: distance and accuracy contests

**Learning:** Levers, fulcrum, force, trajectory, precision

**4. Solar Oven Design (Class 4)**

**Problem:** Design oven that uses sun to heat food

**Background:** Solar energy, heat absorption and reflection

**Criteria:** Heat water or melt chocolate, safe to handle

**Constraints:** Pizza box, aluminum foil, plastic wrap, black paper, tape, time

**Process:**
- Research solar ovens
- Plan design (reflector, insulation, window)
- Build oven
- Test by placing in sun with thermometer or food item
- Measure temperature increase
- Improve insulation or reflector

**Learning:** Solar energy, reflection, insulation, angles, renewable energy

**Islamic Connection:** Stewardship of environment, renewable energy, using Allah's blessings (sunlight)

#### Skills Development (Class 3-4)

- Engineering design process independence
- Detailed planning and sketching
- Measuring accurately (cm, grams)
- Recording data in tables
- Analyzing test results
- Identifying variables
- Written explanations of designs
- Presenting to peers

### Class 5 Engineering

**Time:** Regular engineering units and projects

**Focus:** Independent application of design process, complex challenges, documentation, competitions

#### Learning Objectives

- Apply engineering design process independently
- Research and apply engineering principles
- Create detailed plans and CAD models (introduction)
- Conduct systematic testing and analysis
- Iterate multiple times
- Work effectively in teams
- Communicate engineering work formally

#### Sample Engineering Challenges

**1. Prosthetic Hand Design (Class 5)**

**Problem:** Design functional prosthetic hand for person with limb difference

**Background:** Assistive technology, prosthetics, empathy

**Criteria:** Able to pick up small objects, durable, under budget

**Constraints:** Cardboard, straws, string, tape, scissors, time, cost limit

**Process:**
- Research prosthetics and hand function
- Analyze human hand movements
- Brainstorm designs
- Create detailed plan
- Build prototype
- Test picking up various objects
- Refine based on tests
- Present to class

**Learning:** Human anatomy, assistive technology, engineering for accessibility, mechanisms

**Islamic Connection:** Helping those with disabilities, accessibility, using skills to serve others

**2. Marble Roller Coaster (Class 5)**

**Problem:** Design roller coaster that is exciting but safe

**Criteria:** Marble completes course without flying off, at least 2 hills, 1 loop or turn, minimum length 1 meter

**Constraints:** Foam tubes (pipe insulation), tape, cardboard supports, marble, time

**Process:**
- Learn about potential and kinetic energy, loops, slopes
- Design course on paper
- Build track with supports
- Test marble run
- Analyze failures (where does marble fall off or stop?)
- Adjust heights, curves, supports
- Iterate to complete successful run

**Learning:** Energy transformation, friction, momentum, slope, precision

**3. Bridge Engineering Competition (Class 5)**

**Problem:** Build strongest bridge

**Criteria:** Span 50 cm gap, hold maximum weight

**Constraints:** 100 popsicle sticks, glue (limited amount), time

**Process:**
- Research bridge types (beam, arch, truss, suspension)
- Analyze strengths and weaknesses
- Choose design and plan
- Calculate approximately how many sticks for each part
- Build carefully with precise measurements
- Test by adding weight incrementally until failure
- Record maximum weight held
- Class competition for strongest bridge

**Learning:** Bridge engineering, trusses, tension and compression, geometric shapes, structural strength

**4. Rube Goldberg Machine (Class 5, Team Project)**

**Problem:** Create complex chain reaction machine to accomplish simple task

**Task Examples:** Pop a balloon, ring a bell, turn on a light, drop ball in cup

**Criteria:** At least 5 steps, uses 3 different simple machines, completes task consistently

**Constraints:** Variety of materials available, time, space

**Process:**
- Learn about Rube Goldberg machines and simple machines
- Work in teams to plan elaborate sequence
- Assign sections to team members
- Build and connect sections
- Test entire sequence (troubleshoot failures)
- Refine timing and connections
- Video successful run
- Present and demonstrate

**Learning:** Chain reactions, cause and effect, simple machines, timing, troubleshooting, teamwork, persistence

**5. App Design Project (Class 5, if technology available)**

**Problem:** Design app to solve a problem

**Process:**
- Identify problem that app could solve
- Research existing apps
- Design user interface (screens, buttons, flow)
- Build simple prototype using MIT App Inventor or similar
- Test with users
- Improve based on feedback

**Learning:** User-centered design, app development, problem-solving, iteration

#### Engineering Notebook

Class 5 students maintain engineering notebooks for projects:
- Problem definition
- Research notes
- Brainstorming sketches
- Final plan with labels and dimensions
- Materials list
- Build process notes and photos
- Test data and observations
- Analysis of results
- Iterations and improvements
- Reflection

#### Skills Development (Class 5)

- Independent design process
- Research and application of engineering concepts
- CAD design (Tinkercad introduction)
- Precise measurement and calculation
- Systematic testing protocols
- Data analysis and graphing
- Engineering communication (reports, presentations)
- Teamwork and project management

### Class 6 Engineering

#### Learning Objectives

- Apply engineering design process systematically
- Explore multiple engineering disciplines
- Use CAD software for design
- Apply mathematical and scientific principles to engineering
- Build complex functional prototypes
- Analyze designs quantitatively
- Collaborate effectively in engineering teams

#### Engineering Challenges and Projects

**1. Hydraulic Arm**

**Discipline:** Mechanical Engineering

**Problem:** Design robotic arm powered by hydraulics (syringes and water)

**Concepts:** Hydraulics, pressure, mechanical advantage, pneumatics vs. hydraulics

**Materials:** Syringes, plastic tubing, cardboard, tape, fasteners

**Process:**
- Learn about hydraulic systems
- Design arm with at least 2 joints
- Build and connect hydraulic system
- Test grip strength and range of motion
- Iterate for better performance

**Applications:** Construction equipment, robotics, medical devices

**2. Wind Turbine Design**

**Discipline:** Energy Engineering

**Problem:** Design wind turbine to generate maximum electricity

**Concepts:** Renewable energy, blade design, gear ratios, electrical generation

**Materials:** Small motor (as generator), blades (cardboard, plastic), dowels, wires, LED

**Process:**
- Research wind turbine design
- Design blade shape and number
- Build turbine
- Test with fan at standard distance
- Measure electricity (light brightness, multimeter if available)
- Optimize blade design

**Islamic Connection:** Renewable energy, sustainability, using Allah's blessings (wind)

**3. Biomimicry Engineering Challenge**

**Discipline:** Biomedical/Biomimicry Engineering

**Problem:** Design solution inspired by nature

**Examples:**
- Velcro (burrs)
- Streamlined shapes (fish, birds)
- Water-repellent surfaces (lotus leaf)
- Strong structures (honeycomb, spider web)

**Process:**
- Research biomimicry examples
- Identify natural solution to study
- Design human application
- Build model or prototype
- Test functionality

**Learning:** Nature as inspiration, adaptations, structure and function

**4. Sustainable Shelter Design**

**Discipline:** Civil/Architectural Engineering

**Problem:** Design emergency shelter for disaster relief

**Criteria:** Portable, quick assembly, weather-resistant, low-cost, sustainable

**Constraints:** Budget limit, size limit, materials available

**Process:**
- Research disaster shelters and needs
- Consider climate and context
- Design shelter with CAD (Tinkercad, SketchUp)
- Build scale model
- Test water resistance, stability
- Calculate costs
- Present design with rationale

**Islamic Connection:** Helping those in need, humanitarian engineering

#### Engineering Challenges and Projects

**1. Compound Machine Design**

**Discipline:** Mechanical Engineering

**Problem:** Design machine that uses multiple simple machines to accomplish complex task

**Requirements:** At least 4 different simple machines, accomplishes useful task

**Process:**
- Review simple machines
- Identify task (e.g., vending machine, conveyor system)
- Design system integrating machines
- Build working model
- Calculate mechanical advantages
- Demonstrate and explain

**2. Electronic Circuit Design**

**Discipline:** Electrical Engineering

**Problem:** Design functional electronic circuit

**Examples:**
- Burglar alarm (sensor triggers buzzer/light)
- Automatic night light (LDR sensor)
- Moisture sensor for plants
- Temperature sensor with display

**Components:** Arduino or breadboard, sensors, LEDs, buzzers, resistors, wires

**Process:**
- Learn circuit basics and components
- Design circuit diagram
- Build on breadboard
- Program (if using Arduino)
- Test and troubleshoot
- Refine and finalize

**Learning:** Circuits, sensors, programming, troubleshooting

**3. Structural Engineering: Tower Challenge**

**Discipline:** Structural/Civil Engineering

**Problem:** Build tallest free-standing tower that holds weight

**Criteria:** Height, holds 500g weight on top

**Constraints:** Spaghetti and marshmallows (or paper and tape), time

**Process:**
- Research tower structures (Eiffel Tower, communication towers)
- Understand geometric shapes and stability
- Design tower structure
- Build
- Test height and load-bearing
- Analyze structural failures
- Iterate

**Learning:** Structural integrity, base width, triangular bracing, center of gravity

**4. Robotics Challenge**

(Detailed in Robotics & Coding Programs document)

**Problem:** Program robot to complete specific task

**Example:** Navigate maze, follow line, sort objects

**Platform:** LEGO Mindstorms EV3

**Process:**
- Understand task and rules
- Design robot with appropriate sensors
- Build robot
- Program logic and commands
- Test and debug
- Optimize for speed and accuracy

## Design Thinking and Innovation

### Design Thinking Methodology

Design thinking is a human-centered approach to innovation that draws from the designer's toolkit to integrate people's needs, technology possibilities, and business requirements.

**Five Stages of Design Thinking:**

1. **Empathize:** Understand users' needs and experiences
2. **Define:** Clearly articulate the problem
3. **Ideate:** Brainstorm creative solutions
4. **Prototype:** Build tangible representations
5. **Test:** Try solutions with users and get feedback

**Overlap with Engineering Design Process:**

Design thinking emphasizes user needs and iterative creativity. Engineering design process emphasizes technical feasibility and systematic testing. Both are iterative and user-focused.

### Innovation at Smart Academy

**Innovation Culture:**
- Encouraging creativity and "thinking outside the box"
- Valuing diverse ideas and perspectives
- Creating safe environment for risk-taking and failure
- Celebrating novel solutions and persistence

**Innovation Activities:**

**Maker Mindset:**

## Assessment and Evaluation

### Assessment Philosophy

Engineering is assessed primarily through performance-based evaluation, focusing on process as well as product.

- All stages of design process
- Sketches, plans, notes
- Test data and results
- Reflections and iterations

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Completeness of documentation
- Clarity and organization
- Evidence of process steps
- Reflections and learning

**Evaluation:**
- Does it meet criteria?
- How well does it function?
- Quality of construction
- Creativity and innovation

**Testing:**
- Objective measures (distance, weight, time, etc.)
- Subjective observations (usability, aesthetics)

**3. Presentations and Communication**

**Formats:**
- Oral presentations
- Display boards
- Videos
- Written reports

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Clarity and organization
- Explanation of design and process
- Use of visuals and evidence
- Communication skills

**4. Collaboration and Teamwork**

**Evaluation:**
- Cooperation and communication
- Contribution to team
- Conflict resolution
- Shared responsibility

**Methods:**
- Teacher observations
- Peer evaluations
- Self-reflection

**5. Problem-Solving and Critical Thinking**

**Evaluation:**
- Identification of problems
- Generation of solutions
- Application of knowledge
- Persistence and flexibility

**Evidence:**
- Observations during work
- Engineering notebooks
- Discussions and explanations

**6. Technical Skills**

**Evaluation:**
- Use of tools safely and correctly
- Accurate measurement
- Quality of construction
- CAD proficiency (secondary)

**Methods:**
- Skills demonstrations
- Practical assessments
- Product quality

### Rubrics for Engineering Projects

**General Engineering Project Rubric:**

**Engineering Design Process (30%)**
- Followed all steps systematically
- Documented process thoroughly
- Multiple iterations based on testing
- Reflection on learning

**Technical Quality (25%)**
- Prototype functionality
- Quality of construction
- Use of tools and materials
- Safety adherence

**Creativity and Innovation (20%)**
- Original ideas and approach
- Creative problem-solving
- Unique features or improvements

**Criteria and Constraints (15%)**
- Meets all specified criteria
- Works within all constraints
- Optimization attempts

**Communication (10%)**
- Clear presentation
- Explanation of design and process
- Visual aids and documentation
- Effective communication

**Specific rubrics** tailored to individual challenges with relevant criteria

### Grading

Engineering activities typically graded on process and effort as much as final product:
- Emphasis on learning through iteration
- Recognizing that "failed" designs teach valuable lessons
- Effort, persistence, and improvement valued
- Innovation and creativity rewarded
