## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

This policy establishes clear, consistent criteria for student promotion from one grade to the next. The policy ensures that:

- **Standards Maintained**: Students promoted only when ready for next level
- **Fair Treatment**: All students evaluated by same criteria
- **Early Intervention**: Struggling students identified and supported early
- **Parent Communication**: Families informed regularly about progress
- **Student Success**: All students given opportunity to achieve

### Philosophy

**At Smart Academy, we believe:**

1. **Success for Every Child**: With appropriate support and time, all children can learn
2. **Social Promotion Limited**: Moving unprepared students forward is not helpful
3. **Retention as Last Resort**: Retention considered only when other options exhausted
4. **Holistic Assessment**: Academic, social, and emotional factors all considered
5. **Islamic Perspective**: Each child is unique, created by Allah with different strengths

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students from PlayGroup to Class 8
- Promotion decisions made at end of academic year
- Consideration for special cases

## Promotion Criteria by Grade Level

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

**Philosophy:** Developmentally appropriate, minimal retention

**Promotion Considered Based On:**
- Social readiness (can separate from parents, interacts with peers)
- Emotional readiness (can manage emotions, follows routines)
- Language development (age-appropriate communication)
- Cognitive development (basic concepts understood)
- Physical development (motor skills appropriate)

**Retention Considered When:**
- Significant developmental delays
- Extensive absences affecting progress
- Parent and teacher agreement that another year would be beneficial

**Note:** Retention in early childhood rare and only with strong justification and parent agreement.

### Class 1-2

**Promotion Criteria:**
- **Overall Achievement:** Minimum 50% aggregate
- **Subject Requirements:** Minimum 40% in English, Bengali, Mathematics
- **Islamic Studies:** Minimum passing grade
- **Attendance:** Minimum 75% attendance (excused absences considered)
- **Teacher Recommendation:** Based on overall readiness

**Automatic Promotion Consideration:**
- Students slightly below criteria but showing consistent improvement
- Students who would benefit from summer program
- Cases where parent provides additional support commitment

### Class 3-5

**Promotion Criteria:**
- **Overall Achievement:** Minimum 50% aggregate
- **Subject Requirements:** Minimum 40% in English, Bengali, Mathematics, Science
- **Islamic Studies:** Minimum passing grade (important for character development)
- **Attendance:** Minimum 75% attendance
- **Work Habits:** Satisfactory completion of assignments

**Conditional Promotion:**
- May be promoted on academic probation if:
  - Overall 45-49% aggregate
  - Strong effort and attitude demonstrated
  - Parents agree to summer tutoring
  - Academic support plan developed

### Class 6-8

**Promotion Criteria:**
- **Overall Achievement:** Minimum 50% aggregate
- **Subject Requirements:** Minimum 40% in each core subject (English, Bengali, Math, Science)
- **Islamic Studies:** Minimum passing grade required
- **Attendance:** Minimum 75% attendance mandatory
- **GPA Equivalent:** Minimum 2.00 overall

**Important Note:**
Students not meeting criteria must attend and pass summer remedial program to be considered for promotion.

## Academic Requirements

### Core Subject Performance

**For promotion, students must demonstrate:**

**Class 1-5:**
- **English and Bengali:** Reading at or near grade level, basic writing skills
- **Mathematics:** Understanding of grade-level concepts
- **Science:** Basic understanding of scientific concepts
- **Islamic Studies:** Demonstrated Islamic knowledge and character

**Class 6-8:**
- **English and Bengali:** Grade-level reading comprehension and writing
- **Mathematics:** Grade-level computational and problem-solving skills
- **Science:** Understanding of scientific principles and methods
- **Islamic Studies:** Islamic knowledge and practice

### Foundation Skills

**Essential skills for promotion:**

**Literacy Skills:**
- Reading comprehension appropriate for grade level
- Written communication skills
- Basic research skills
- Vocabulary development

**Numeracy Skills:**
- Number sense and operations
- Problem-solving ability
- Mathematical reasoning
- Application to real-world situations

**Learning Skills:**
- Completes assignments independently
- Follows instructions
- Works well with others
- Demonstrates effort and persistence

### Year-End Assessment Weight

**Promotion decision based on:**

| Component | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Full year performance | 60% |
| Final examination | 30% |
- Teacher assessment of overall readiness | 10% |

**Total:** 100%

## Attendance Requirements

### Mandatory Attendance

**For Promotion:**
- **Minimum 75% attendance** required for promotion consideration
- **Medical absences** documented may be considered for exemption
- **Religious absences** for Hajj/Umrah considered excused

**Attendance Calculation:**
- Total days present ÷ Total school days × 100
- Both excused and unexcused absences counted
- Suspensions counted as absences

### Attendance Waiver Process

**For medical or exceptional circumstances:**

1. Parent submits written request with documentation
2. Medical certificate from licensed physician required
3.特殊情况 reviewed by Promotion Committee
4. Decision based on:
   - Severity of condition
   - Academic performance when present
   - Ability to complete curriculum
   - Teacher recommendation

**Note:** Waiver not guaranteed — each case considered individually.

## Retention Policy

### When Retention is Considered

**Retention may be recommended when:**
- Student significantly below grade level in key subjects (below 35%)
- Excessive absences (below 60% attendance) without valid reason
- Student would benefit from additional time to mature and develop
- Strong recommendation from teachers and school counselor
- Parent agreement and support for retention

### Retention Decision Process

**Step 1: Early Identification**
- At-risk students identified by mid-year
- Parents notified of concerns
- Interventions implemented

**Step 2: Pre-Retention Review**
- Formal review held in March/April
- Evidence of interventions reviewed
- Options discussed with parents

**Step 3: Final Decision**
- Made by Promotion Committee
- Includes: Principal, Vice Principal, Teachers, Counselor
- Parent input considered
- Final decision communicated by May

### Retention Limitations

**Retention may NOT be recommended:**
- More than once in primary grades (1-5)
- More than once total without exceptional circumstances
- After Class 7 (students proceed with support)

### Retention Support

**If student is retained:**
- Individualized learning plan developed
- Additional support provided
- Differentiated instruction implemented
- Progress monitored closely
- Parents involved in support plan

## Conditional Promotion

### Academic Probation

**Students promoted on academic probation when:**
- Fall slightly below promotion criteria
- Show potential for success with additional support
- Commit to summer school and support plan
- Parents agree to support conditions

**Probation Conditions:**
- Summer school attendance required
- Regular tutoring during next year
- Weekly progress monitoring
- Parent commitment to home support
- Monthly review meetings

### Summer School Program

**Purpose:** Provide opportunity to improve skills and meet promotion requirements

**Program Details:**
- **Duration:** 6 weeks (July-August)
- **Schedule:** 4 days per week, 3 hours per day
- **Focus:** English, Mathematics, Arabic/Islamic Studies
- **Assessment:** Final summer school exam

**Promotion After Summer School:**
- Must attend minimum 80% of sessions
- Must pass summer school assessment (minimum 50%)
- Must complete all assignments
- Must demonstrate improved skills

**Cost:**
- Additional fee applies for summer school
- Scholarships available for families with financial need

## Appeal Process

### Right to Appeal

**Parents may appeal promotion decision when:**
- New evidence available not considered
- Procedural error occurred
- Extenuating circumstances not adequately considered
- Documentation error discovered

### Appeal Process

**Step 1: Informal Discussion**
- Meet with class teacher to discuss concerns
- Review evidence and decision rationale
- Often resolves misunderstandings

**Step 2: Formal Appeal**
- Submit written appeal to Vice Principal within 5 days
- Include: Student name, grade, grounds for appeal, supporting evidence
- Appeal reviewed by Promotion Committee

**Step 3: Appeal Hearing**
- Parents invited to present case
- Committee reviews all information
- Student may be present for part of discussion

**Step 4: Decision**
- Written response within 10 school days
- Decision is final
- Next steps outlined

### Appeal Documentation Required

**For successful appeal, provide:**
- Medical documentation (if health issue)
- Special circumstances documentation
- Evidence of interventions not previously considered
- Independent assessment reports (if applicable)

## Special Considerations

### Students with Special Needs

**Individualized Promotion Criteria:**
- IEP goals considered in promotion decision
- Progress measured against individual goals
- Team decision with parents, teachers, specialists
- Focus on appropriate placement for student's needs

**Not held to same standards** when:
- Documented learning disability exists
- Modifications in place throughout year
- Evidence of appropriate progress shown

### English Language Learners

**Considerations:**
- Language acquisition stage considered
- Bilingual development recognized
- Content understanding prioritized
- Extra time for language development allowed

**Promotion based on:**
- Overall academic progress
- Language development trajectory
- Content knowledge in first language when applicable
- Teacher recommendation

### Transfer Students

**Mid-Year Transfers:**
- Previous school records reviewed
- Placement testing administered
- Probationary period for assessment
- Promotion decision based on available evidence

**Late Joiners:**
- Adjusted expectations for material missed
- Focus on concepts covered while enrolled
- Previous school performance considered

## Parent Communication

**Parents informed about promotion status:**

| Timeline | Communication |
|----------|----------------|
| Mid-Year (January) | Progress report with promotion status |
| Pre-Retention Review (March) | Warning letter if at risk |

**Discussion includes:**
- Current academic performance
- Strengths and areas of concern
- Interventions implemented

**Parents should:**
- Communicate concerns early
- Partner with teachers
- Follow through on recommendations
- Attend all meetings
- Support child's learning at home
