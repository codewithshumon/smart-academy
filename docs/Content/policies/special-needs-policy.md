## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Smart Academy is committed to providing an inclusive education that meets the diverse needs of all learners. This policy ensures:

- **Equal Access**: All students can access the curriculum
- **Appropriate Support**: Students receive needed accommodations and modifications
- **Dignity and Respect**: All students valued for their unique contributions
- **Partnership with Parents**: Families involved in all decisions
- **Islamic Values**: Every child seen as a creation of Allah with inherent worth

### Our Commitment

**We believe that:**
1. Every child can learn given appropriate support and time
2. Diversity enriches our school community
3. Inclusion benefits all students
4. Early identification and intervention is critical
5. Collaboration between home and school is essential

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- Students with learning difficulties
- Students with physical disabilities
- Students with emotional or behavioral needs
- Gifted and talented students
- Students with medical conditions affecting learning
- All students requiring additional support

## Definition of Special Needs

### Categories of Special Educational Needs

**1. Specific Learning Difficulties**
- Dyslexia (reading difficulties)
- Dyscalculia (mathematical difficulties)
- Dysgraphia (writing difficulties)
- Processing difficulties

**2. General Learning Difficulties**
- Mild learning delays
- Moderate learning delays
- Global developmental delay

**3. Physical Disabilities**
- Mobility impairments
- Visual impairments
- Hearing impairments
- Chronic health conditions
- Temporary injuries

**4. Emotional and Behavioral Needs**
- Anxiety disorders
- Attention difficulties (ADD/ADHD)
- Social communication difficulties (including Autism Spectrum)
- Emotional regulation difficulties

**5. Speech and Language Needs**
- Speech delays
- Language disorders
- Communication difficulties

**6. Gifted and Talented**
- Exceptional academic ability
- Specific talents (arts, music, etc.)
- Exceptional creativity

**7. Medical Conditions**
- Epilepsy
- Diabetes
- Asthma
- Other conditions affecting learning

## Identification and Assessment

### Referral Process

**Who Can Refer:**
- Parents/guardians
- Teachers
- School counselor
- Administration
- Healthcare professionals
- Student (self-referral for older students)

**Referral Process:**
1. Concern noted and documented
2. Initial discussion with parents
3. Referral form submitted to SEN coordinator
4. Screening/assessment scheduled
5. Findings shared with parents
6. Action plan developed

### Assessment Methods

**Screening Assessments:**
- Vision and hearing screening
- Developmental screening (early years)
- Academic skills screening
- Behavioral observations

**Comprehensive Assessment:**
- Educational psychologist assessment (external)
- Speech and language assessment (external)
- Occupational therapy assessment (external)
- Medical assessment (when appropriate)

**In-School Assessment:**
- Teacher observations and documentation
- Work sample analysis
- Curriculum-based measurements
- Progress monitoring data

### Confidentiality

**All assessment information:**
- Kept confidential
- Shared only with relevant staff
- Stored securely
- Accessed only with permission
- Shared with external professionals only with parent consent

## Accommodations and Modifications

### Accommodations vs. Modifications

**Accommodations:** Changes HOW students learn
- Same curriculum expectations
- Different ways to access learning
- No change in standards

**Modifications:** Changes WHAT students learn
- Adjusted curriculum expectations
- Different learning goals
- Individualized standards

### Common Accommodations

**Environmental Accommodations:**
- Preferential seating
- Quiet testing location
- Reduced distractions
- Accessible classroom arrangement
- Assistive technology

**Instructional Accommodations:**
- Extended time for assignments
- Visual aids and supports
- Break down of instructions
- Multi-sensory teaching
- Repetition and review

**Assessment Accommodations:**
- Extended time (25-50%)
- Reader for tests
- Scribe for responses
- Oral testing
- Modified test format
- Use of calculator when appropriate

**Assignment Accommodations:**
- Reduced quantity with focus on quality
- Alternative response formats
- Use of technology for writing
- Copies of notes
- Assignment checklists

### Common Modifications

**Curriculum Modifications:**
- Reduced number of objectives
- Alternative materials at appropriate level
- Modified reading materials
- Functional curriculum components
- Life skills focus

**Grading Modifications:**
- Modified grading criteria
- Pass/fail options for some subjects
- Individualized achievement standards
- Progress-based grading

## Individualized Education Plans

### IEP Development

**IEP Team Includes:**
- Parents/guardians (essential members)
- Classroom teacher(s)
- SEN coordinator
- School counselor
- Student (when appropriate)
- External professionals (when involved)

**IEP Components:**
1. **Present Level of Performance:** Current strengths and needs
2. **Annual Goals:** Measurable goals for the year
3. **Short-term Objectives:** Steps toward annual goals
4. **Accommodations/Modifications:** Specific supports needed
5. **Support Services:** Therapy, tutoring, etc.
6. **Evaluation Criteria:** How progress will be measured
7. **Timeline:** When and how often reviewed

### IEP Review

**Review Schedule:**
- **Formal review:** At least twice per year
- **Progress monitoring:** Ongoing
- **Annual review:** Comprehensive evaluation
- **Transition review:** When moving to next level

**IEP Meetings:**
- Scheduled at mutually convenient times
- Parents notified at least 2 weeks in advance
- Student work and progress reviewed
- Goals and supports adjusted as needed
- Parents receive copy of updated IEP

## Working with Parents

### Partnership Approach

**We believe parents are:**
- Experts on their children
- Essential partners in education
- Valued members of the IEP team
- Advocates for their children's needs

### Communication

**Regular Communication Includes:**
- Initial meeting to discuss concerns
- Assessment findings sharing
- IEP development meeting
- Regular progress updates
- Term reviews
- Ongoing communication as needed

**Communication Methods:**
- Face-to-face meetings
- Phone conversations
- Email updates
- Communication notebook
- Portal messages

### Parent Rights

**Parents have the right to:**
- Request evaluation for special needs
- Review all assessment records
- Participate in IEP development
- Request IEP review
- Appeal decisions
- Access independent evaluation
- Withdraw from program at any time

### Parent Responsibilities

**Parents are responsible for:**
- Providing relevant information
- Attending meetings
- Supporting IEP at home
- Communicating concerns
- Following through on recommendations
- Partnering with school
