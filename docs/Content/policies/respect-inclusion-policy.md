## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Smart Academy is committed to creating a respectful, inclusive community where every member feels valued, welcomed, and able to participate fully. This policy ensures:

- **Respect for All**: Every person treated with dignity
- **Inclusive Environment**: Everyone can participate fully
- **Celebration of Diversity**: Differences valued and celebrated
- **Equitable Treatment**: Fair access to opportunities
- **Belonging**: All community members feel they belong

### Our Vision

We envision a school community where:
- Every student feels safe, valued, and respected
- Differences are celebrated, not just tolerated
- All students have equitable access to opportunities
- Every student can achieve their full potential
- Islamic values of respect, justice, and compassion guide all interactions

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students, staff, and parents
- All school activities and programs
- All aspects of school life
- Online communications related to school
- Visitors and volunteers

## Respect Framework

### Respect for Self

**Students will learn to:**
- Value themselves as creation of Allah
- Take pride in their identity
- Develop healthy self-esteem
- Practice self-care
- Maintain personal hygiene and appearance

### Respect for Others

**Students will demonstrate:**

**Teachers and Staff:**
- Follow directions respectfully
- Use appropriate titles and language
- Listen when spoken to
- Accept feedback graciously

**Peers:**
- Treat others as you want to be treated
- Respect personal space and boundaries
- Include others in activities
- Speak kindly and respectfully
- Value different opinions and backgrounds

**Visitors:**
- Greet visitors politely
- Assist visitors when appropriate
- Represent school well

### Respect for Environment

**Students will:**
- Keep school clean and tidy
- Care for school property
- Respect displays and others' work
- Use resources responsibly
- Protect the natural environment

### Respect for Learning

**Students will:**
- Allow others to learn without disruption
- Participate respectfully in discussions
- Value others' contributions
- Complete work honestly
- Take learning seriously

## Inclusion Commitment

### Definition of Inclusion

Inclusion means **valuing and involving every person** regardless of differences. At Smart Academy, inclusion ensures:

- **Full Participation**: Everyone can participate in all aspects of school life
- **Belonging**: Everyone feels they are part of the community
- **Access**: Barriers to participation are identified and removed
- **Support**: Appropriate support provided to ensure success
- **Voice**: All perspectives valued and heard

### Our Inclusion Commitment

Smart Academy is committed to including and supporting:

**1. Students of All Abilities**
- Students with special educational needs
- Students with physical disabilities
- Students with learning differences
- Gifted and talented students

**2. Students from All Backgrounds**
- Different socioeconomic backgrounds
- Different family structures
- Different cultural backgrounds
- Different geographic origins (rural, urban)

**3. Students at All Stages**
- New students to the school
- Students at different academic levels
- Students with different language proficiency
- Students with different interests and talents

**4. Students of All Character**
- Different personalities and temperaments
- Different strengths and challenges
- Different learning styles
- Different social comfort levels

## Protected Characteristics

While we value all aspects of human diversity, we specifically prohibit discrimination based on:

**Protected Characteristics:**
- Race, ethnicity, or national origin
- Religion or belief (within Islamic framework)
- Gender
- Disability
- Socioeconomic status
- Family structure
- Age
- Academic ability
- Physical appearance

**Note:** As an Islamic institution, our framework is guided by Islamic teachings. Within this framework, we respect diversity of opinion, practice, and understanding.

## Building Inclusive Community

### Inclusive Practices in the Classroom

**Teachers will:**
- Use diverse examples and perspectives in teaching
- Ensure all students can participate
- Provide multiple ways to demonstrate learning
- Address different learning styles
- Create supportive classroom environments
- Challenge stereotypes and biases
- Facilitate positive peer interactions

### Inclusive Practices in Activities

**All Activities Will:**
- Welcome all students to participate
- Provide accommodations as needed
- Consider diverse needs in planning
- Celebrate participation and effort
- Ensure safety and belonging for all
- Avoid exclusionary practices

### Inclusive Language

**Our Community Will Use:**
- Respectful language about all groups
- Person-first language (e.g., "student with autism" not "autistic student")
- Inclusive greetings and language
- Avoid stereotypes and generalizations
- Words that build up, not tear down

### Celebrating Diversity

**We Celebrate:**
- Islamic heritage and values
- Bengali culture and traditions
- Different family traditions (within Islamic framework)
- Individual talents and abilities
- Different perspectives and experiences
- Cultural events and celebrations

**Appropriate Celebrations:**
- Islamic events (Eid, Ramadan, etc.)
- Bengali cultural events (Pohela Boishakh, etc.)
- International Day celebrations
- Achievement celebrations
- Talent showcases
- Sports and arts events

## Respect for Differences

### Socioeconomic Differences

**We Address by:**
- Uniform policy reducing visible differences
- Confidential financial assistance
- Avoiding class-based language
- Equal treatment of all students
- Focus on character, not wealth

### Academic Differences

**We Address by:**
- Valuing all types of intelligence
- Multiple ways to succeed
- Recognition for effort and improvement
- Support for struggling students
- Challenge for advanced students
- Avoiding academic elitism

### Physical Differences

**We Address by:**
- Teaching respect for all bodies
- Addressing teasing about appearance
- Emphasizing inner character
- Adapted participation when needed
- Accessibility for all

### Personality Differences

**We Address by:**
- Valuing introverts and extroverts
- Different ways of participating
- Respect for quiet students
- Respect for energetic students
- Understanding of differences

## Responding to Incidents

### Disrespectful or Exclusionary Behavior

**When incidents occur:**
1. **Address immediately** — Stop the behavior
2. **Educate** — Teach why it's wrong
3. **Restore** — Make things right
4. **Support** — Help all involved
5. **Follow up** — Monitor and support

### Discrimination or Harassment

**Response to serious incidents:**
- Immediate investigation
- Support for target
- Consequences for perpetrator
- Education for community
- Review of policies and practices
- Report to authorities if required

### Reporting Procedures

**Who Can Report:**
- Students who experience or witness incidents
- Parents
- Staff members
- Any concerned community member

**How to Report:**
- Direct report to teacher or administrator
- Written report to office
- Anonymous report option
- Phone call to administration
