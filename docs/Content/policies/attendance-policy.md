## Policy Overview

### Philosophy of Attendance

Smart Academy believes that regular attendance is fundamental to academic success and character development. Consistent attendance enables students to:

- Benefit fully from our integrated Islamic and academic curriculum
- Participate in daily prayers and spiritual activities
- Build strong relationships with teachers and peers
- Develop habits of punctuality and responsibility
- Achieve their full learning potential

### Islamic Foundation

**On the Value of Time:**
> "By time, indeed, mankind is in loss, except for those who have believed and done righteous deeds and advised each other to truth and advised each other to patience." (Quran 103:1-3)

**On Fulfilling Commitments:**
> "O you who have believed, fulfill [all] contracts." (Quran 5:1)

**The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said:**
> "There are two blessings that many people lose: health and free time." (Sahih Bukhari)

### Policy Scope

This policy applies to:
- All enrolled students from PlayGroup to Class 9
- All school days as defined in the academic calendar
- All scheduled classes, activities, and events
- Co-curricular and extracurricular activities when required

## Attendance Requirements

### Minimum Attendance Standards

**As per Bangladesh Education Board regulations:**

| Grade Level | Minimum Required Attendance | Note |
|-------------|---------------------------|------|
| PlayGroup - Class 5 | 85% | Required for promotion and eligibility for honors |
| Class 6 - 9 | 75% | Required to sit for final examinations |

### Daily Attendance Recording

**Attendance is recorded:**
- **Morning Assembly**: 8:00 AM - First attendance check
- **Each Period**: Secondary students checked each class period
- **After Prayer**: Dhuhr prayer attendance also recorded
- **After Lunch**: Post-lunch attendance check

**Attendance Status Categories:**
- **Present (P)**: Student is in class on time
- **Late (L)**: Student arrives after 8:15 AM
- **Absent (A)**: Student is not present
- **Excused (E)**: Absence with valid justification
- **Medical (M)**: Absence due to illness with documentation

### Important Note

> **Students who fall below the minimum attendance requirement will not be promoted to the next grade and will not be eligible for academic awards, scholarships, or leadership positions.**

## Reporting Absences

### Parent Responsibility

Parents/guardians must report all student absences. Notification is required:

1. **Before 8:00 AM** on the day of absence
2. **By written note** upon student's return
3. **With documentation** for extended or medical absences

### Methods of Reporting

**1. Phone Call:**
- Call: +8801709-651168 (Ext. 105 - Attendance Office)
- Leave message with: Student name, class, reason for absence

**2. Email:**
- Send to: attendance@smartacademybd.com
- Include: Student name, class, dates, reason

**3. Parent Portal:**
- Submit absence notification through online portal
- Upload supporting documents if applicable

**4. Written Note:**
- Send with sibling or submit in person
- Must be signed by parent/guardian

### Absence Notification Format

```
Absence Notification

Student Name: ______________________________________
Class: _________________ Section: _________
Date(s) of Absence: ______________________________
Reason: __________________________________________
Parent Signature: _________________________________
Date: ____________________________________________
Contact Number: _________________________________
```

### Return to School Procedure

**After any absence, student must:**
1. Submit absence note to class teacher
2. Submit medical certificate if applicable
3. Collect missed work from teachers
4. Complete missed assignments within agreed timeline
5. Attend any required makeup sessions

## Types of Absences

### Excused Absences

Absences are considered excused when:

**1. Illness or Medical Emergency**
- Student is too ill to attend school
- Doctor's appointment that cannot be scheduled outside school hours
- Medical emergency in immediate family
- Documentation: Doctor's note or medical certificate required for absences exceeding 3 days

**2. Religious Observance**
- Islamic religious holidays (Eain, Eid, etc.)
- Important religious events (e.g., Hajj, Umrah with family)
- Religious observances of other faiths (for non-Muslim students)
- Documentation: Parent notification in advance

**3. Family Emergency**
- Death in the immediate family
- Serious family emergency requiring student presence
- Documentation: Parent explanation, discretion of administration

**4. School-Sponsored Activities**
- Sports competitions
- Academic competitions
- Field trips
- School performances
- Not counted as absence

**5. Special Circumstances**
- Court appearances
- Immigration appointments
- Other exceptional situations approved by administration

### Unexcused Absences

Absences that are not excused include:

- Family vacations during school days
- Oversleeping
- Missing the bus
- Babitting siblings
- Weather (unless school closed)
- Transportation problems (unless school transport)
- Birthday celebrations
- Shopping trips
- Visiting relatives

**Consequences:**
- First offense: Warning to parents
- Second offense: Parent conference
- Third offense: Disciplinary action
- Continued: May affect promotion

### Chronic Absentism

**Definition:** Missing 10% or more of school days (approximately 18+ days per year)

**Intervention:**
1. Parent conference with Vice Principal
2. Attendance improvement plan developed
3. Regular monitoring and check-ins
4. Home visit if needed
5. Possible report to education authorities

## Late Arrival Policy

### Tardiness Definition

A student is considered late if they arrive:
- **After 8:15 AM** but before 9:00 AM: Tardy
- **After 9:00 AM**: Half-day absent
- **After 11:00 AM**: Full-day absent

### Late Arrival Procedures

**When a student arrives late:**
1. Report to Attendance Office
2. Parent must provide reason for lateness
3. Obtain late slip
4. Present to teacher before entering class
5. Teacher marks student as Late (L)

### Excused Tardiness

Tardiness may be excused for:
- Medical appointments with documentation
- Family emergency
- School transportation delay
- Inclement weather
- Other exceptional circumstances

### Unexcused Tardiness

**Consequences:**
- **1-3 times per term**: Warning
- **4-6 times per term**: Parent notification
- **7-9 times per term**: Parent conference, detention
- **10+ times per term**: Disciplinary action

### Tardy Consequences Summary

| Tardies per Term | Consequence |
|------------------|-------------|
| 1-3 | Warning |
| 4-6 | Parent notification |
| 7-9 | Parent conference, loss of break time |
| 10+ | Disciplinary probation, behavior contract |

## Early Dismissal

### Early Pickup Procedure

**When student needs to leave early:**
1. Parent sends note or emails in advance
2. Approved by Administration
3. Parent comes to Main Office (not classroom)
4. Student called to office
5. Parent signs early dismissal log
6. Student released to parent/guardian

**Note:** Students will NOT be released to anyone except authorized parents/guardians without prior written authorization.

### Early Dismissal Impact

**Time Away from School:**
- **Before 11:00 AM**: Counts as full-day absence
- **11:00 AM - 1:00 PM**: Counts as half-day absence
- **After 1:00 PM**: Counts as present (subject to attendance in classes)

### Valid Reasons for Early Dismissal

- Medical appointments (with documentation preferred)
- Family emergency
- Special religious observance
- Other exceptional circumstances approved by administration

## Consequences of Poor Attendance

### Academic Consequences

**Below Minimum Attendance:**
- **Not promoted** to next grade
- **Not eligible** to sit for final examinations
- **Not eligible** for academic awards or honors
- **Not eligible** for scholarships

**Missed Work:**
- Student responsible for all missed assignments
- Makeup work must be completed within timeline set by teacher
- Teachers not required to reteach missed lessons
- Missed tests/quizzes may be made up at teacher discretion

### Disciplinary Consequences

**Unexcused Absences:**
- First offense: Warning
- Second offense: Parent conference
- Third offense: Detention
- Continued: Suspension consideration

**Chronic Truancy:**
- Attendance contract required
- Loss of privileges
- Possible suspension
- Possible expulsion
- Report to education authorities

### Activities and Awards Ineligibility

Students with poor attendance are ineligible for:
- Student council positions
- Leadership roles in clubs/teams
- School honors and awards
- Perfect attendance recognition
- Field trips (except educational requirements)
- Sports team participation (at coach discretion)

## Attendance Recognition

### Perfect Attendance Award

**Criteria:**
- Zero absences (excused or unexcused) for entire academic year
- Zero tardies for entire academic year
- Full day attendance every day

**Recognition:**
- Certificate at year-end assembly
- Special recognition in school newsletter
- Privilege card (extra privileges for following year)
- Name on Perfect Attendance plaque

### Excellent Attendance Award

**Criteria:**
- 97% or above attendance
- No unexcused absences
- No more than 3 tardies

**Recognition:**
- Certificate at year-end assembly
- Recognition in school newsletter

### Most Improved Attendance

**Criteria:**
- Significant improvement from previous term
- Attendance above 90% in improvement term
- No unexcused absences

**Recognition:**
- Certificate
- Recognition in class

## Special Circumstances

### Extended Medical Absence

**For medical absences exceeding 10 consecutive days:**

1. **Notify administration** as soon as possible
2. **Provide medical documentation** from attending physician
3. **Meeting with Administration** to plan for continued learning
4. **Homebound services** may be arranged if appropriate
5. **Modified attendance requirements** may be considered with medical documentation

### Chronic Health Conditions

**For students with ongoing health issues:**
- Submit medical documentation to school office
- Meet with school nurse and administration
- Develop individual health plan
- Accommodations made as appropriate
- Attendance requirements may be modified with documentation

### Family Crisis/Situations

**For serious family situations:**
- Contact administration immediately
- Each situation handled individually
- Support and accommodations provided
- Confidentiality maintained
- Attendance requirements may be modified temporarily

### Religious Pilgrimage

**For Hajj/Umrah:**
- Provide advance notice to school
- Religious travel considered excused absence
- Work assigned in advance when possible
- Extended deadline for completion
- May affect attendance percentage but not promotion if all work completed

## Attendance Monitoring and Communication

### Parent Notification

**Parents will be notified when:**
- Student reaches 3 absences in a term (warning)
- Student reaches 5 absences in a term (concern)
- Student reaches 10 absences in a term (serious concern)
- Student falls below minimum attendance requirement

**Notification Methods:**
- Automated phone call
- Email notification
- Portal message
- Letter sent home
- Phone call from attendance office

### Attendance Reports

**Report Frequency:**
- **Daily**: Teacher attendance records
- **Weekly**: Class attendance summary to teachers
- **Monthly**: Attendance report sent to parents
- **Term**: Full attendance record with report card
- **Year**: Summary attendance report

**How to Check Attendance:**
- Parent/Student Portal: Real-time attendance
- Mobile App: Daily attendance notifications
- Report Card: Term attendance summary
- Request: Attendance report from office

## Special Considerations

### Prayer Attendance

**As an Islamic institution, prayer attendance is monitored separately:**

- Dhuhr prayer attendance required for all students
- Prayer attendance recorded but not counted in academic attendance
- Repeated failure to attend prayer addressed separately
- Prayer attendance considered for character/religious awards

### Co-Curricular Activities

**Attendance required for:**
- Sports practices and competitions when committed
- Club meetings when member
- School performances when participant
- Academic competitions when selected

**These absences are NOT counted in attendance percentage.**

### Before/After School Programs

- Attendance taken separately
- Not counted in regular school attendance
- Important for student development

## Make-Up Work Policy

### Student Responsibility

**When absent, student must:**
1. Check portal for missed assignments
2. Contact classmates for notes
3. Request makeup work from teachers
4. Complete work within timeline:
   - **1-2 days absent**: 3 days to complete
   - **3-5 days absent**: 5 days to complete
   - **1+ weeks absent**: Timeline determined by administration

### Teacher Responsibility

**Teachers will:**
- Maintain updated assignment information on portal
- Provide makeup work upon request
- Be reasonable in deadlines
- Provide necessary materials
- Assist students who genuinely need help

### Tests and Quizzes

**Makeup policy:**
- Excused absence: Makeup test allowed
- Unexcused absence: Makeup at teacher discretion
- Advance notice absences: Test may be taken early
- Extended absence: Alternative assessment may be given
