## Policy Overview

### Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for all examinations conducted at Smart Academy. It ensures:

- **Fair Assessment**: All students assessed under consistent conditions
- **Academic Integrity**: Examinations conducted honestly and ethically
- **Islamic Values**: Fear of Allah (Taqwa) guides all conduct
- **Preparation**: Students develop skills for formal examinations
- **Standards**: Alignment with national and international best practices

### Examination Philosophy

At Smart Academy, examinations serve multiple purposes:

1. **Assessment of Learning**: Measuring student understanding and skills
2. **Assessment for Learning**: Identifying areas for improvement
3. **Quality Assurance**: Ensuring academic standards are met
4. **Feedback**: Providing information to students, parents, and teachers
5. **Preparation**: Building skills for external examinations (SSC, HSC)

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All formal written examinations
- All practical examinations
- All oral examinations
- All scheduled assessments
- Internal and external examinations

## Examination Rules and Conduct

### Before Examination

**Preparation Requirements:**

1. **Attendance Requirement:**
   - Minimum 75% attendance required to sit for final examinations
   - Attendance verified before examination begins

2. **Fee Clearance:**
   - All school fees must be paid
   - No dues to library, laboratory, or other departments
   - Clearance certificate obtained from office

3. **Admit Card:**
   - Admit card issued before examinations
   - Must bring admit card to each examination
   - Replacement fee if lost

4. **Examination Materials:**
   - Bring own pens, pencils, erasers, geometry box
   - Only clear pencil cases allowed
   - No unauthorized materials

### Examination Hall Rules

**Upon Entry:**
1. Arrive **15 minutes before** examination time
2. Line up quietly outside examination room
3. Enter only when permitted by invigilator
4. Find assigned seat (seat numbers posted)
5. Place admit card on desk for verification
6. Place all materials on desk

**During Examination:**

**PERMITTED:**
- Writing materials (pens, pencils, erasers)
- Mathematical instruments (for math/science)
- Calculator (if specified for subject)
- Water bottle (clear, label removed)
- Watch (analog only, no smart watches)

**PROHIBITED:**
- Mobile phones, smart watches, or any electronic devices
- Books, notes, or any reference materials
- Unauthorized calculator models
- Correction fluid/tape (use crossing out)
- Communication with other students
- Leaving seat without permission

**Conduct Requirements:**
1. Complete silence at all times
2. Remain seated unless permission given
3. Raise hand for invigilator assistance
4. Write only on answer paper provided
5. Follow all invigilator instructions

### Answer Book Guidelines

**Instructions:**
1. Write name, roll number, subject on cover page
2. Write all answers in blue or black pen
3. Pencils only for diagrams and graphs
4. Number all questions clearly
5. Cross out mistakes (do not use correction fluid)
6. Do not write in margins
7. Use both sides of paper
8. Handwriting must be legible

### End of Examination

**Procedure:**
1. Stop writing when instructed
2. Ensure all questions answered
3. Arrange answer sheets properly
4. Wait at seat until papers collected
5. Leave examination room quietly
6. Do not take any examination material

## Types of Examinations

### Class-Level Examinations

**PlayGroup - KG:**
- **Format**: Oral and practical assessment
- **Duration**: 20-30 minutes per subject
- **Focus:** Understanding through activities and interaction
- **No formal written examination**

**Class 1-2:**
- **Format:** Written, oral, and practical
- **Duration**: 1 hour per paper
- **Focus:** Basic concepts and skills

**Class 3-5:**
- **Format:** Written papers
- **Duration**: 1.5-2 hours per paper
- **Sections:** Objective, short answer, long answer

**Class 6-8:**
- **Format:** Formal written papers
- **Duration**: 2-3 hours per paper
- **Sections:** Multiple choice, short answer, structured questions

**Class 9:**
- **Format:** SSC preparation format
- **Duration**: 3 hours per paper
- **Sections:** Complete examination pattern

### Examination Types

**1. Unit Tests**
- Conducted after completing each unit
- Duration: 30-45 minutes (Primary), 1 hour (Secondary)
- Focus: Specific topic assessment

**2. Mid-Term Examinations**
- Conducted twice per year
- Duration: Varies by grade
- Focus: Term syllabus

**3. Terminal Examinations**
- Final examinations each term
- Duration: As per grade
- Focus: Full term syllabus

**4. Preparatory Examinations (Class 9)**
- Before SSC examinations
- Full examination format
- Timed practice

**5. Practical Examinations**
- Science subjects (Class 6-9)
- Computer studies
- Physical education assessment
- Islamic studies oral assessment

## Examination Day Procedures

### For Students

**Day Before Examination:**
- Review notes and key concepts
- Organize examination materials
- Get adequate sleep (8+ hours recommended)
- Prepare uniform and admit card
- Relax and avoid stress

**Examination Day:**
- Wake up early enough
- Eat healthy breakfast
- Arrive at school 30 minutes early
- Bring admit card and materials
- Use restroom before examination
- Find examination room

**During Examination:**
- Read all instructions carefully
- Manage time effectively
- Answer easier questions first
- Review answers if time permits
- Stay calm and focused

### For Parents

**Before Examinations:**
- Ensure adequate sleep and nutrition
- Provide quiet study environment
- Offer encouragement, not pressure
- Avoid adding stress
- Pray for child's success

**During Examinations:**
- Ensure punctual arrival
- Provide healthy meals
- Avoid scheduling conflicts
- Be supportive regardless of outcome

### For Invigilators

**Duties:**
1. Distribute answer papers and question papers
2. Verify student identity and admit cards
3. Explain examination rules
4. Monitor continuously and fairly
5. Prevent malpractice
6. Assist students when needed
7. Collect answer papers properly
8. Submit papers to examination office

## Absence and Make-up Examinations

### Absence Policy

**No Make-Up For:**
- Unexcused absence
- Absence without valid reason
- Absence due to family vacation
- Absence due to social events

**Make-Up Available For:**
- Medical emergency with certificate
- Serious family emergency
- School-sponsored activity conflict
- Other exceptional circumstances (approved by administration)

### Make-Up Examination Procedure

**Request Process:**
1. Parent notifies school immediately of absence
2. Documentation provided (medical certificate, etc.)
3. Application for make-up examination submitted
4. Administration reviews request
5. Decision communicated to parent

**If Approved:**
- Make-up examination scheduled
- May be different format but equivalent content
- Conducted within 1 week of return
- Fair assessment maintained

**If Not Approved:**
- No marks awarded for that examination
- Grade calculated based on other assessments
- May affect promotion and awards

## Malpractice and Disciplinary Action

### Definition of Malpractice

**Examination malpractice includes:**

1. **Cheating:**
   - Copying from another student
   - Allowing another student to copy
   - Using unauthorized notes/materials
   - Communicating with other students

2. **Impersonation:**
   - Another person taking examination for student
   - Student taking examination for another

3. **Unfair Means:**
   - Having unauthorized materials in possession
   - Writing on desk/body for cheating
   - Using hidden devices

4. **Disruptive Behavior:**
   - Creating disturbance in examination hall
   - Disrespecting invigilator
   - Refusing to follow instructions

5. **Forgery:**
   - Forging admit card
   - Forging medical certificate
   - Altering examination documents

### Consequences of Malpractice

**First Offense:**
- Zero on that examination paper
- Parent conference immediately
- Warning letter in student file
- Suspension for remainder of examination period

**Second Offense:**
- Zero on all papers for that examination
- Suspension from school
- Disciplinary probation
- Possible expulsion

**Severe Offenses:**
- Immediate expulsion
- Report to education board
- Permanent record
- No refund of fees

### Appeals Process

**If accused of malpractice:**
1. Student given opportunity to explain
2. Evidence reviewed by committee
3. Fair hearing conducted
4. Decision based on facts
5. Parents may appeal to Principal

## Special Accommodations

### Students with Special Needs

**Accommodations available for:**
- Learning disabilities (with documentation)
- Physical disabilities
- Medical conditions
- Temporary injuries

**Types of Accommodations:**
- Extra time (up to 50% additional)
- Reader/scribe assistance
- Separate examination room
- Modified question paper
- Use of assistive technology
- Rest breaks during examination

**Procedure:**
1. Parent submits documentation
2. Assessment by school counselor
3. Accommodation plan developed
4. Teachers informed
5. Accommodations implemented

### Extended Time

**Eligibility for extended time:**
- Documented learning disability
- Physical disability affecting writing speed
- Medical condition requiring breaks
- Other verified need

**Extended time provisions:**
- 25% extra time: Common for most accommodations
- 50% extra time: Significant needs
- Breaks: Additional pauses during examination

## Examination Security

### Question Paper Security

**Procedures:**
1. Papers prepared by subject teachers
2. Reviewed by Department Head
3. Printed securely
4. Stored in locked cupboard
5. Distributed only on examination day
6. Unused papers collected and destroyed

### Answer Book Security

**Procedures:**
1. Answer books distributed at start
2. Serially numbered
3. Collected with attendance sheet
4. Sent for checking
5. Stored securely after checking
6. Available for re-evaluation request

### Result Security

**Procedures:**
1. Checking done by subject teachers
2. Moderation by Department Head
3. Entry into system by office
4. Verification before publication
5. Secure storage of records
