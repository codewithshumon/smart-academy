## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Smart Academy is committed to supporting students in fulfilling their religious obligation of Salah (prayer). This policy establishes:

- **Support for Prayer**: Facilities and time for prayer
- **Prayer Education**: Teaching proper prayer
- **Respect for Prayer**: Creating prayer-friendly environment
- **Accommodation**: Understanding individual needs
- **Balance**: Prayer and academic responsibilities

### Our Commitment

**We Support Students By:**
- Providing clean prayer facilities
- Allowing time for Dhuhr prayer at school
- Teaching proper performance of Salah
- Creating a respectful prayer environment
- Accommodating individual prayer needs

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All Muslim students
- Prayer facilities use
- Prayer scheduling
- Prayer education
- Staff supervision

## Islamic Foundation

### Quranic Command for Prayer

**The Obligation of Salah:**
> "Indeed, prayer is obligatory upon the believers at specified times." (Quran 4:103)

**Success Through Prayer:**
> "Successful indeed are the believers who are humble in their prayers." (Quran 23:1-2)

**The Purpose of Prayer:**
> "Establish prayer for My remembrance." (Quran 20:14)

**Warning Against Neglect:**
> "But there came after them successors who neglected prayer and pursued desires; so they are going to meet destruction." (Quran 19:59)

### Hadith on Prayer

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said:

> "The first matter that the slave will be brought to account for on the Day of Judgment is the prayer. If it is sound, then the rest of his deeds will be sound. And if it is bad, then the rest of his deeds will be bad." (Tirmidhi)

> "Between a man and polytheism and disbelief is abandoning the prayer." (Sahih Muslim)

> "Pray in the manner you saw me praying." (Sahih Bukhari)

### Importance of Prayer in Islam

**Prayer is the:**
- Second pillar of Islam
- Most important obligatory act after Shahada
- Connection between servant and Lord
- Daily reminder of faith
- Source of spiritual strength

## Prayer Requirements

### Five Daily Prayers

**The Five Prayers:**

| Prayer | Arabic | Time Period | School Impact |
|--------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Fajr | فجر | Dawn to sunrise | Before school |
| Dhuhr | ظهر | After noon until mid-afternoon | At school |
| Asr | عصر | Mid-afternoon to sunset | After school |
| Maghreb | مغرب | After sunset to twilight | After school |
| Isha | عشاء | Twilight to midnight | After school |

### Age of Responsibility

**Islamic Perspective:**
- Children are not accountable until puberty (Bulugh)
- Parents commanded to instruct children to pray by age 7
- Parents commanded to discipline for prayer by age 10
- Training begins early

**At Smart Academy:**
**Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG):**
- Introduction to prayer concept
- Learning basic positions
- Praying together as activity
- No obligation, training only

**Primary (Class 1-5):**
- Regular prayer instruction
- Learning complete prayer
- Praying at school as training
- Encouraged but not enforced

**Secondary (Class 6-9):**
- Prayer expected of Muslim students
- Regular Dhuhr prayer at school
- Preparation for adulthood responsibility
- Gradual transition to full obligation

## Prayer During School Day

### Dhuhr Prayer at School

**Scheduled Prayer Time:**
- Daily Dhuhr prayer scheduled
- Approximately 1:00 - 1:20 PM (varies with season)
- After lunch break
- Before afternoon classes resume

**Who Prays:**
- Muslim students from Class 1 and above
- Staff who wish to pray
- Voluntary for early childhood
- Expected for secondary students

**Prayer Format:**
- Congregational prayer (Jama'ah) when possible
- Led by student or staff Imam
- Boys and girls pray separately
- 2 Rak'ahs Dhuhr prayer
- Additional sunnah prayers optional

### Prayer Procedure

**Before Prayer:**
1. Make intention (Niyyah)
2. Perform wudu (ablution)
3. Proceed to prayer hall quietly
4. Line up in rows (Saff)
5. Wait for iqamah

**During Prayer:**
1. Follow Imam in congregation
2. Pray with focus and humility
3. Maintain proper positions
4. Complete prayer properly
5. Make dua after prayer

**After Prayer:**
1. Make dhikr (remembrance of Allah)
2. Roll up prayer mat
3. Return to class quietly
4. Resume academic activities

### Prayer Etiquettes (Adab)

**In Prayer Hall:**
- Enter with right foot
- Leave with left foot
- Maintain silence
- No running or playing
- Respect those praying
- Keep space clean

**During Prayer:**
- Focus and humility (Khushu)
- No talking
- Proper alignment in rows
- Follow Imam properly
- Complete all positions

## Jumu'ah (Friday) Prayer

### Friday Prayer

**Importance of Jumu'ah:**
> "O you who have believed, when [the adhan] is called for the prayer on the day of Jumu'ah [Friday], then proceed to the remembrance of Allah and leave trade." (Quran 62:9)

### Jumu'ah at Smart Academy

**For Secondary Students (Class 6-9):**
- Jumu'ah prayer arranged for male students
- Held at nearby mosque or school facility
- Students dismissed with permission
- Parent consent required
- Transportation arranged if needed

**For Female Students:**
- Dhuhr prayer offered at school
- Jumu'ah not obligatory for women
- Educational session about Jumu'ah

**For Primary Students:**
- Special Friday assembly
- Islamic reminders
- Educational activities
- Dhuhr prayer at school

### Friday Special Features

**At School on Friday:**
- Special Islamic assembly
- Islamic reminders and stories
- Clean Friday emphasized
- Surah Al-Kahf recitation encouraged
- Increased Islamic atmosphere

## Prayer Education

### Learning Salah

**By Grade Level:**

**Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG):**
- Introduction to prayer concept
- Learning Allah loves when we pray
- Learning basic positions (standing, bowing, prostrating)
- Saying "Allahu Akbar"
- Praying together as fun activity

**Lower Primary (Class 1-2):**
- Learning complete prayer sequence
- Memorizing Al-Fatihah
- Learning short surahs
- Learning wudu
- Practicing daily

**Upper Primary (Class 3-5):**
- Perfecting prayer performance
- Memorizing more surahs
- Learning prayer meanings
- Understanding importance
- Regular congregational prayer

**Secondary (Class 6-9):**
- Proficient in complete prayer
- Memorizing required surahs
- Understanding prayer meanings
- Leading prayer as Imam
- Regular obligatory prayer

### Prayer Curriculum

**Topics Taught:**
1. Importance and obligation of prayer
2. Prayer times and how to determine them
3. Wudu (ablution) and Tayammum
4. Prayer performance (all positions and words)
5. Required surahs and recitations
6. Congregational prayer
7. Friday prayer (for boys)
8. Missed prayers (Qada)
9. Prayer in special situations

### Required Surahs

**By Grade Level:**

**Class 1-2:**
- Al-Fatihah
- Al-Ikhlas
- Al-Falaq
- An-Nas

**Class 3-5:**
- All above plus:
- Al-Kafirun
- Al-Lahab
- An-Nasr
- Al-Asr

**Class 6-9:**
- All above plus:
- Al-Baqarah 1-5 (Ayatul Kursi)
- Ar-Rahman (partial or complete)
- Al-Mulk (partial or complete)
- Other selected surahs

## Special Situations

### Missed Prayers

**If Prayer Missed:**
- Makeup prayer (Qada) should be performed
- Students learn importance of not missing prayer
- Parent guidance on makeup prayers
- Not treated as punishment at school
- Encouragement to establish regular prayer

### Prayer During Exams

**During Testing:**
- Prayer time accommodated
- Short break allowed for prayer
- Student can pray before or after exam
- No penalty for prayer time
- Respect for religious obligation

### Prayer During Activities

**Special Events:**
- Prayer time considered in scheduling
- Breaks provided for prayer when needed
- Field trips: prayer arrangements made
- Sports events: prayer breaks allowed
- School events: prayer scheduled

### Prayer During Illness

**If Student Unwell:**
- Prayer can be adapted based on ability
- Sitting or lying down allowed if needed
- Medical exemption respected
- Parent should advise school
- Educational support for understanding

## Parent Partnership

### Shared Responsibility

**Home and School:**
- Both support prayer development
- Consistency in expectations
- Regular communication
- Mutual support and encouragement

### Parent Responsibilities

**At Home:**
- Teach children about prayer importance
- Pray with children at home
- Ensure Fajr and evening prayers

- Encourage regular prayer

**Supporting School:**
- Ensure children know prayer basics

- Communicate concerns to school

### School Communication

**School Will:**
- Inform parents of prayer education topics
- Share prayer progress and concerns
- Provide prayer resources
- Answer prayer-related questions
- Consult parents on prayer matters

## Exemptions and Accommodations

### Medical Exemptions

**If Student Cannot Pray:**
- Parent should notify school
- Medical certificate may be required
- Exemption respected
- No penalty for not praying
- Alternative quiet activity

### Special Needs

**Accommodations Available:**
- Physical accommodations as needed
- Extended time for wudu
- Assistance with movement
- Adapted prayer positions
- Support from staff

### Personal Circumstances

**Individual Consideration:**
- Personal situations respected
- Confidential discussion with parent
- Appropriate accommodations made
- No forced participation
- Respect for personal choices

## Monitoring and Assessment

### Prayer Monitoring

**Not For Academic Assessment:**
- Prayer participation not graded
