## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Social media has become an integral part of modern communication. This policy provides guidance for:

- **Responsible Use**: Using social media in accordance with Islamic values
- **Safety**: Protecting students from online dangers
- **Reputation Management**: Understanding digital footprint
- **Balance**: Maintaining healthy technology habits
- **Community**: Building positive online community

### Philosophy

At Smart Academy, we recognize that:
- Social media can be used for good and harm
- Students need guidance in navigating social media
- Online behavior has real-world consequences
- Digital footprint is permanent
- Islamic values apply to online interactions
- Parent involvement is essential

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students and their social media use
- All official school social media accounts
- Social media use related to school community
- Online behavior affecting school environment

## Student Guidelines

### Age-Appropriate Use

**Minimum Age Requirements:**
- Most social media platforms require users to be 13+
- Smart Academy recommends delaying social media until at least Class 6
- Parent decision before age 13, following platform rules

**Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG):**
- Should not have personal social media accounts
- May appear only with parent permission and supervision
- Privacy strictly maintained

**Primary (Class 1-5):**
- Generally should not have personal social media accounts
- Parent decision and oversight essential
- Family accounts may be appropriate
- Focus on learning digital citizenship skills

**Secondary (Class 6-9):**
- May have social media with parent permission
- Parent monitoring strongly recommended
- Account passwords shared with parents
- Responsible use emphasized

### Responsible Posting Guidelines

**Before Posting, Ask:**
1. Is this true and honest?
2. Is this kind and necessary?
3. Is this consistent with Islamic values?
4. Would I say this in person?
5. Would I want my parents/teachers to see this?
6. Could this harm someone now or in the future?

### What NOT to Post

**Students Should NOT Post:**
- Inappropriate photos (revealing, suggestive)
- Photos of others without permission
- Location information that could compromise safety
- Negative comments about school or classmates
- False information or rumors
- Anything that could harm reputation
- Anything showing inappropriate behavior
- Complaints that should be handled privately

### Privacy Settings

**Students Should:**
- Use strict privacy settings
- Accept only people they know personally
- Keep personal information private
- Regularly review privacy settings
- Understand that nothing is truly private online

### Time Management

**Healthy Social Media Habits:**
- Limit social media time
- No social media during prayer times
- No social media during homework/study time
- No social media after bedtime
- Balance online and offline activities
- Real-world interaction prioritized

### Online Behavior

**Treat Others Online As You Would In Person:**
- Be kind and respectful
- Don't say anything online you wouldn't say in person
- Stand up for others if you see bullying
- Report inappropriate content
- Include others, don't exclude
- Think before posting or commenting

## Parent Guidelines

### Parental Responsibility

**Parents Are Responsible For:**
- Deciding when children can have social media
- Monitoring children's social media use
- Teaching appropriate social media behavior
- Setting and enforcing limits
- Modeling healthy social media use
- Staying informed about platforms and trends

### Monitoring Strategies

**Recommended Approaches:**
- Know your child's passwords
- Follow your child's accounts
- Set family rules for social media use
- Use parental controls and monitoring apps
- Regularly review posts and messages
- Keep devices in public areas at home
- Establish phone-free times and zones

### Red Flags

**Warning Signs of Problematic Use:**
- Excessive time on social media
- Hiding activity or deleting history
- Changes in mood or behavior
- Withdrawal from family and friends
- Declining grades
- Sleep problems
- Inappropriate content encountered

### Communication

**Talk With Your Children About:**
- What they're doing online
- Who they're communicating with
- Privacy settings and safety
- Islamic values online
- Digital footprint and permanence
- Social media pros and cons
- Cyberbullying and what to do

## School Social Media Use

### Official School Accounts

**Smart Academy Maintains:**
- Facebook page
- Instagram account
- YouTube channel
- Website news section

**Purpose:**
- Share news and announcements
- Celebrate student achievements
- Communicate with families
- Share educational resources
- Build community

### Student Photos and Information

**School Will:**
- Obtain parental consent before posting
- Use only appropriate photos
- Protect student privacy
- Never share identifying information
- Remove content upon parent request
- Respect families who opt out

### Parent Posting About School

**When Posting About School:**
- Respect privacy of other students
- Get permission before posting others' photos
- Represent school accurately
- Bring concerns directly to school, not social media
- Follow school policies when sharing photos

### School Events

**At School Events:**
- Photos allowed for personal use
- Do not post photos of other students without permission
- Respect requests not to photograph
- Focus on your own children
- Follow photography guidelines

## Privacy and Safety

### Personal Information

**Never Share Online:**
- Full name (use first name or nickname)
- Address or location
- Phone number
- Birth date
- School name with location
- Routine or schedule
- Family information
- Photos that reveal location

### Stranger Danger

**Online Safety Rules:**
- Don't accept friend requests from strangers
- Don't meet online friends without parent approval
- Don't share personal information
- Block anyone who makes you uncomfortable
- Tell parent about suspicious interactions
- Remember people may not be who they say they are

### Digital Footprint

**Understanding Permanence:**
- Everything posted is potentially permanent
- Even deleted posts may have been copied
- Screenshots can be taken
- Nothing online is truly private
- Posts can affect future opportunities
- Colleges and employers may check social media

## Inappropriate Content and Reporting

### Cyberbullying

**Recognizing Cyberbullying:**
- Mean comments or messages
- Spreading rumors online
- Excluding others online
- Sharing embarrassing photos or information
- Creating fake accounts
- Impersonating others

**If You Are Cyberbullied:**
- Don't respond to the bully
- Save evidence (screenshots)
- Block the person
- Tell an adult immediately
- Report to the platform
- Don't retaliate

**If You See Cyberbullying:**
- Don't join in or like mean posts
- Support the person being bullied
- Report the content
- Tell an adult
- Be an upstander, not a bystander

### Inappropriate Content

**If You Accidentally Encounter Inappropriate Content:**
- Close it immediately
- Tell a parent or teacher
- Don't show it to others
- Clear your history if needed
- Don't worry about getting in trouble

### Reporting to School

**Report to School When:**
- Cyberbullying involves school community members
- Online behavior affects school environment
- Safety concerns arise
- You need help or guidance
- You're unsure what to do

**How to Report:**
- Tell a trusted teacher
- Contact school counselor
- Email administration
- Use anonymous reporting option if preferred

## Consequences for Policy Violations

### School Response

**When social media use affects school:**

**Level I: Minor Issues (first offense):**
- Warning and education
- Parent notification
- Reflection activity
- Restorative conversation

**Level II: Moderate Issues:**
- Parent conference
- Behavior contract
- Loss of technology privileges
- Counseling required
- Disciplinary action

**Level III: Serious Issues:**
- Suspension
- Expulsion consideration
- Report to authorities if illegal
- Permanent disciplinary record

### Affecting School Environment

**School May Address:**
- Cyberbullying involving students
- Posts that disrupt learning
- Threats or harassment
- Inappropriate content affecting school
- Misrepresentation of school
