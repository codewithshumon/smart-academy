## Policy Overview

### Purpose

Smart Academy maintains comprehensive emergency procedures to ensure the safety of all students, staff, and visitors. This plan provides:

- **Clear Protocols**: Specific procedures for each emergency type
- **Regular Practice**: Drills to prepare for emergencies
- **Trained Staff**: Staff prepared to respond effectively
- **Parent Communication**: Clear communication during emergencies
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regular review and updates

### Our Approach

We follow these principles in all emergency procedures:
1. **Life Safety First**: Student and staff safety is the priority
2. **Clear Communication**: Accurate, timely information
3. **Practice and Preparation**: Regular drills and training
4. **Calm Response**: Maintaining order and reducing panic
5. **Partnership**: Working with emergency services

### Scope

This plan covers:
- All emergency situations that may affect the school
- All school facilities and grounds
- All school activities and events
- All students, staff, and visitors

## Emergency Categories

### Level 1 Emergencies (Minor)

**Definition:** Incidents that can be handled with school resources

**Examples:**
- Minor injuries requiring first aid
- Small spills or messes
- Minor facility issues
- Small fire (extinguished quickly)
- Weather delays

### Level 2 Emergencies (Moderate)

**Definition:** Incidents requiring coordination but not immediate evacuation

**Examples:**
- Serious injuries
- Power outage
- Bomb threat (non-immediate)
- Severe weather watch
- Medical emergency

### Level 3 Emergencies (Major)

**Definition:** Life-threatening situations requiring immediate response

**Examples:**
- Fire
- Violent intruder
- Natural disaster
- Chemical spill
- Terrorist threat
- Any immediate danger to life

## Specific Emergency Protocols

### Fire Emergency

**IF FIRE IS DISCOVERED:**
1. Pull the nearest fire alarm
2. Call "Fire!" loudly
3. Evacuate immediately (do not collect belongings)
4. Close doors behind you (do not lock)
5. Use stairs, NOT elevators
6. Assist those who need help
7. Report to assembly point
8. Account for all students

**DO NOT:**
- Try to fight the fire (unless trained and small)
- Open doors that feel hot
- Use elevators
- Return for belongings
- Delay evacuation

### Fire Drills

**Conducted:**
- Monthly during academic year
- Different times of day
- Varied exit routes practiced
- Proper documentation

### Earthquake

**DURING SHAKING:**
1. **DROP** to hands and knees
2. **COVER** head and neck with arms
3. **HOLD ON** to something sturdy
4. Stay away from windows
5. Stay indoors until shaking stops
6. If outside, stay away from buildings

**AFTER SHAKING:**
1. Evacuate if building damaged
2. Watch for hazards
3. Assemble at safe location
4. Account for all students
5. Do not re-enter until cleared

### Severe Weather

**Thunderstorm/Lightning:**
- All outdoor activities halted
- Students moved indoors
- Windows and doors closed
- Stay away from windows

**Tornado Warning:**
- Move to designated safe areas
- Drop and cover
- Protect head
- Stay away from windows
- Remain until all-clear

**Cyclone Preparedness:**
- Monitor weather reports
- Secure loose items
- Prepare for early dismissal if needed
- Communicate with parents

### Bomb Threat

**RECEIVING A THREAT:**
1. Keep caller on line if possible
2. Note caller details
3. Note exact words
4. Listen for background sounds
5. Do not hang up

**AFTER CALL:**
1. Notify administration immediately
2. Do not use two-way radio (may trigger)
3. Search without alarming (if time)
4. Evacuate if threat believed credible
5. Follow bomb squad instructions

### Armed Intruder / Lockdown

**IF INTRUDER PRESENT:**
1. **LOCKDOWN** immediately
2. Lock all doors
3. Turn off lights
4. Stay away from doors/windows
5. Hide silently
6. Silence phones
7. Wait for law enforcement

**IF IN HALLWAY:**
1. Run to nearest classroom
2. If can't reach room, hide
3. Stay out of sight
4. Remain silent

**IF CONFRONTED:**
- Run if escape possible
- Hide if can't escape
- Fight only as last resort

### Medical Emergency

**IF MEDICAL EMERGENCY:**
1. Assess situation
2. Call for help
3. Call school nurse
4. Call emergency services if needed
5. Provide first aid if trained
6. Clear area
7. Stay with student
8. Contact parent

## Evacuation Procedures

### Primary Evacuation Routes

**Posted in Each Classroom:**
- Primary exit route
- Secondary exit route
- Assembly point location
- Map with route marked

### Evacuation Process

**Teachers Will:**
1. Signal evacuation
2. Take attendance sheet
3. Lead students to exit
4. Close door (unlocked)
5. Account for all students
6. Report missing students
7. Maintain order and calm

**Students Will:**
1. Line up quickly and quietly
2. Follow teacher directions
3. Walk, not run
4. Stay in line
5. No talking
6. Stay together

**Special Considerations:**
- Students with mobility issues pre-identified
- Evacuation assistance planned
- Staff assigned to help
- Equipment available if needed

### Assembly Points

**Designated Locations:**
- Primary assembly point: Main playground
- Secondary assembly point: Side field
- Each class has assigned area
- Stay with class
- Quiet and orderly

### All Clear Signal

**Return to Building:**
- Only when all-clear given
- By emergency services or administration
- Enter in organized manner
- Return to classes
- Account for students again

## Lockdown Procedures

### Types of Lockdown

**Level 1 Lockdown (Precautionary):**
- External threat nearby
- School secures perimeter
- No outdoor activities
- Normal indoor activities continue
- Prepared for potential escalation

**Level 2 Lockdown (Full):**
- Direct threat to school
- All doors locked
- Lights off
- Silence required
- Hide if needed
- Wait for law enforcement

### Lockdown Process

**When Lockdown Announced:**
1. Lock all doors immediately
2. Turn off lights
3. Close windows and blinds
4. Move students out of sight
5. Everyone remains silent
6. Silently lock door (no sound)
7. Hide if in hallway
8. Wait for all-clear

**During Lockdown:**
- No talking or noise
- Phones silent (no calls/texts)
- Stay hidden
- Wait for police to open door
- Follow police instructions exactly

**All Clear:**
- Given by law enforcement
- Follow police instructions
- Check for injuries
- Account for all students
- Return to normal when directed

## Reunification Procedures

### Parent Reunification

**If Students Cannot Return to School:**
1. Alternative reunification site activated
2. Parents notified of location
3. Bring identification
4. Check in at designated area
5. Only authorized adults can pick up
6. Sign out student
7. Follow exit procedures

### Reunification Site

**Primary Site:** School grounds (if safe)
**Secondary Site:** [Designated nearby location]

### What Parents Should Do

**DURING EMERGENCY:**
1. **WAIT** for communication from school
2. **DO NOT** come to school immediately (may block emergency response)
3. **CHECK** communication channels
4. **FOLLOW** instructions given
5. **BRING** identification to reunification site

### Reunification Process

**To Pick Up Child:**
1. Go to designated reunification area
2. Present photo ID
3. Be listed as authorized pick-up
4. Verify child's information
5. Sign out child
6. Follow exit procedures

## Communication During Emergencies

### Communication Channels

**School Will Use:**
- SMS/text message alerts
- Email notifications
- School website updates
- Social media (Facebook)
- Automated phone calls
- Media releases (for major events)

### Parent Responsibility

**Ensure School Has:**
- Current phone numbers
- Emergency contacts
- Updated email addresses
- Authorized pick-up list

**Parents Should:**
- Keep contact information updated
- Check communication channels regularly
- Follow school instructions
- Avoid calling school during emergency (lines needed for response)

### Communication Timing

**Initial Notification:** Within 15 minutes of emergency
**Updates:** Every 30 minutes or as information available
**Resolution Notification:** When situation resolved

## Post-Emergency Procedures

### Immediate Aftermath

**After Emergency Resolved:**
1. Account for all students and staff
2. Assess for injuries
3. Provide first aid/medical care
4. Secure the area
5. Document the incident
6. Debrief staff
7. Communicate with families
8. Arrange counseling if needed

### Return to Normal Operations

**When Safe:**
- Assess facilities
- Clean up if needed
- Return to classes
- Provide support to students
- Resume normal routines
- Review and improve procedures

### Emotional Support

**Providing Support:**
- Counselor available
- Age-appropriate explanations
- Opportunities to discuss
- Normalization of feelings
- Additional support for those most affected
