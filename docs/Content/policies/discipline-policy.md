## Policy Overview

### Purpose

This policy establishes a fair, consistent, and Islamic approach to student discipline. It ensures:

- **Safe Environment**: All students can learn without disruption
- **Clear Expectations**: Students understand behavioral standards
- **Fair Process**: All students treated with justice and due process
- **Growth Focus**: Discipline as an opportunity for learning and growth
- **Parent Partnership**: Families involved in supporting positive behavior

### Goals of Discipline

At Smart Academy, discipline aims to:

1. **Teach** appropriate behavior, not just punish misbehavior
2. **Maintain** a safe, orderly learning environment
3. **Develop** self-discipline and responsibility
4. **Protect** the rights of all students to learn
5. **Restore** relationships when harm occurs
6. **Prepare** students for responsible citizenship

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All behavioral incidents on campus
- School-sponsored activities and events
- School transportation
- Online behavior related to school
- Off-campus behavior affecting school environment

- Focus on improvement, not labeling

## Levels of Misconduct

### Level I: Minor Misconduct

**Definition:** Behaviors that interrupt orderly operation or violate minor rules but do not seriously harm others.

**Examples:**
- Minor disruption of class
- Tardiness (first few occurrences)
- Minor dress code violations
- Late homework (few instances)
- Minor inappropriate language
- Failure to follow directions
- Minor misuse of technology

**Response:**
- Handled by teacher
- Verbal warning or reminder
- Loss of minor privilege
- Parent contact for repeated offenses

### Level II: Moderate Misconduct

**Definition:** Behaviors that disrupt the learning environment or show disrespect but do not cause serious harm.

**Examples:**
- Repeated Level I offenses
- Disrespectful language or behavior
- Cheating on minor assignments
- Minor vandalism
- Repeated dress code violations
- Inappropriate physical contact
- Misuse of school property
- Repeated failure to follow rules

**Response:**
- Teacher and administration involvement
- Parent conference required
- Detention or loss of privileges
- Behavior contract
- Documentation in student file

### Level III: Serious Misconduct

**Definition:** Behaviors that seriously disrupt the school environment or show significant disrespect for rules and authority.

**Examples:**
- Fighting or physical aggression
- Bullying or harassment
- Serious cheating/plagiarism
- Significant vandalism or theft
- Insubordination or defiance
- Repeated Level II offenses
- Leaving campus without permission
- Serious technology misuse (cyberbullying)
- Possession of inappropriate materials

**Response:**
- Immediate parent contact
- Suspension (in-school or out-of-school)
- Formal behavior plan
- Possible expulsion consideration
- Report to authorities if required by law

### Level IV: Severe Misconduct

**Definition:** Behaviors that endanger safety, violate law, or show complete disregard for school community.

**Examples:**
- Possession or use of weapons
- Possession, use, or distribution of drugs/alcohol
- Serious physical assault
- Serious theft or vandalism
- Repeated Level III offenses
- Criminal behavior
- Threats to safety

**Response:**
- Immediate suspension
- Expulsion consideration
- Report to law enforcement
- Parent conference required
- Permanent record

## Disciplinary Actions and Consequences

### Range of Consequences

**Minimum Consequences (Level I):**
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Temporary loss of privilege (recess, etc.)
- Reflection activity
- Parent notification

**Moderate Consequences (Level II):**
- Detention (lunch or after school)
- Loss of privileges (field trips, events, positions)
- Behavior contract
- Community service at school
- Parent-teacher conference
- Documentation in student file

**Serious Consequences (Level III):**
- In-school suspension (1-3 days)
- Out-of-school suspension (1-5 days)
- Loss of all privileges and positions
- Referral to school counselor
- Behavior improvement plan
- Probationary status

**Severe Consequences (Level IV):**
- Extended suspension (up to 10 days)
- Expulsion from school
- Permanent disciplinary record
- Report to authorities
- Loss of credit for term/year

### Specific Consequences Explained

**Detention:**
- **Purpose:** Time for reflection and making amends
- **Duration:** 30 minutes to 1 hour
- **Location:** Designated area under supervision
- **Activities:** Reflection, study, community service, reading
- **Notification:** Parents notified at least one day in advance
- **Transportation:** Parent responsibility for after-school detention

**In-School Suspension:**
- **Purpose:** Removal from class while continuing education
- **Duration:** 1-3 days
- **Location:** Separate designated area
- **Expectations:** Complete school work, silent study, reflection
- **Credit:** Full credit for work completed
- **Communication:** Parents notified daily

**Out-of-School Suspension:**
- **Purpose:** Removal from school for serious offenses
- **Duration:** 1-10 days depending on offense
- **Expectations:** Student not on campus during suspension
- **Work:** May complete work for partial credit
- **Re-entry:** Meeting with administration and parents required
- **Record:** Documented in student file

**Expulsion:**
- **Purpose:** Permanent removal for severe or repeated offenses
- **Process:** Formal hearing with Discipline Committee
- **Appeal:** Parents may appeal to Board of Directors
- **Record:** Permanent notation on school record
- **Transfer:** School will assist with transfer if appropriate

## Disciplinary Procedures

### For Minor Misconduct (Level I)

**1. Teacher Response:**
- Addresses behavior immediately
- Reminds student of expectations
- May assign minor consequence
- Documents incident if repeated

**2. Parent Contact (if repeated):**
- Phone call or note home
- Description of behavior
- Request for support
- Plan for improvement

### For Moderate Misconduct (Level II)

**1. Teacher Referral:**
- Documents incident in writing
- Refers to Administration
- Notifies parents

**2. Administrative Response:**
- Investigates incident
- Meets with student
- Contacts parents
- Assigns consequence
- Documents action

**3. Follow-up:**
- Monitor behavior improvement
- Contact parents with progress
- Adjust plan as needed

### For Serious Misconduct (Level III)

**1. Immediate Action:**
- Student removed from situation
- Administration notified immediately
- Parents contacted immediately
- Incident documented

**2. Investigation:**
- Statements from witnesses
- Student's perspective heard
- Evidence reviewed
- Facts determined

**3. Disciplinary Action:**
- Due process followed
- Consequence determined
- Parents informed in writing
- Plan for re-entry developed

**4. Re-entry Meeting:**
- Student, parents, administration
- Review of expectations
- Behavior plan established
- Support systems identified

### For Severe Misconduct (Level IV)

**1. Emergency Response:**
- Immediate safety ensured
- Emergency contacts made
- Law enforcement contacted if needed
- Campus secured

**2. Suspension Pending Hearing:**
- Student sent home immediately
- Parents notified
- Hearing scheduled

**3. Formal Hearing:**
- Discipline Committee convened
- Evidence presented
- Student/parents may present case
- Decision made

**4. Final Action:**
- Expulsion or long-term suspension
- Written decision provided
- Appeal rights explained
- Records processed

## Due Process and Appeals

### Student Rights

**Every student has the right to:**
- Know the charges/allegations
- Present their version of events
- Have fair consideration of their case
- Have parents present in serious matters
- Appeal decisions for serious consequences

### Due Process

**For Minor Consequences:**
- Informal process with teacher
- Opportunity to explain
- Fair and consistent application

**For Major Consequences (suspension/expulsion):**
- Formal process with administration
- Notice of charges and evidence
- Opportunity to respond
- Parent involvement
- Written decision
- Right to appeal

### Appeals Process

**Grounds for Appeal:**
- Procedural error occurred
- New evidence not considered
- Consequence disproportionate to offense
- Discrimination or bias

**Appeal Steps:**

**Step 1: Review with Administration**
- Discuss concern with Vice Principal within 3 days
- Many issues resolved at this level

**Step 2: Formal Appeal**
- Submit written appeal to Principal
- Appeal reviewed by Disciplinary Committee
- Decision within 5 school days

**Step 3: Board Appeal**
- For expulsion cases only
- Appeal to Board of Directors
- Final decision

## Restorative Practices

### Restorative Approach

**When harm occurs, we focus on:**
1. **What happened?** (Understanding the incident)
2. **Who was affected and how?** (Recognizing impact)
3. **What needs to happen to make things right?** (Repairing harm)

### Restorative Questions

**Asking the person who caused harm:**
- What happened?
- What were you thinking at the time?
- What have you thought about since?
- Who has been affected by what you have done?
- What do you think you need to do to make things right?

**Asking the person who was harmed:**
- What did you think when you realized what had happened?
- What impact has this incident had on you and others?
- What has been the hardest thing for you?
- What do you think needs to happen to make things right?

### Restorative Actions

**Making Amends:**
- Apology (genuine and specific)
- Repair or replacement of damaged property
- Community service
- Restitution
- Rebuilding trust

### Mediation

**When Appropriate:**
- Facilitated meeting between parties
- Safe, structured conversation
- Focus on understanding and repair
- Agreement on how to move forward

### Discipline Records

**Maintained for:**
- Due process documentation
- Pattern identification
- Intervention planning

**Access to Records:**

- Records maintained in confidence
- Records follow student through school

**Minor offenses** may be removed from record:
- After one year of good behavior
- Upon graduation
- At administration discretion

- Fighting, bullying
- Substance offenses
- Criminal behavior
- Expulsion
