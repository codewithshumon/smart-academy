## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Smart Academy is committed to providing a safe, healthy environment for all students, staff, and visitors. This policy ensures:

- **Physical Safety**: Protection from physical harm
- **Health Promotion**: Wellness and healthy habits
- **Risk Management**: Identification and mitigation of hazards
- **Emergency Preparedness**: Readiness for emergencies
- **Islamic Values: Health as trust (Amanah) from Allah**

### Our Commitment

Smart Academy pledges to:
1. Maintain safe facilities and equipment
2. Promote physical and mental health
3. Prepare for emergencies
4. Educate about safety
5. Respond promptly to safety concerns
6. Continuously improve safety practices

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All school facilities and grounds
- All school activities and events
- School transportation
- All students and staff
- Visitors and volunteers

## General Safety Standards

### Safety Rules for Students

**All Students Must:**
- Walk, not run, in hallways and stairs
- Keep hands to themselves
- Follow all safety instructions
- Report safety concerns immediately
- Use equipment properly and safely
- Follow playground rules
- Wear appropriate footwear

### Prohibited Items

**Not Allowed on Campus:**
- Weapons or weapon-like objects
- Dangerous items (knives, tools, etc.)
- Fireworks or flammable materials
- Toxic or harmful substances
- Alcohol, tobacco, or drugs
- Any item that could cause harm

### Playground Safety

**Rules:**
- Use equipment only as intended
- Take turns on equipment
- No pushing or shoving
- Stay in designated areas
- Wear appropriate shoes
- Report broken equipment
- Supervision by adults required

### Laboratory Safety (Class 6-9)

**Science Lab Rules:**
- Follow teacher instructions exactly
- Wear safety equipment when required
- No eating or drinking in lab
- Handle materials carefully
- Report accidents immediately
- Clean up after activities
- Know emergency procedures

## Health Services

### School Nurse

**Available Services:**
- Health assessments
- First aid for minor injuries
- Medication administration
- Health counseling
- Illness assessment
- Health education

### Medication Policy

**School Administering Medication:**

**Prescription Medication:**
- Written authorization from parent required
- Written instructions from physician
- Original container with label
- Stored securely in health office
- Administered by designated staff
- Documentation of administration

**Over-the-Counter Medication:**
- Written parent authorization required
- Provided by parents in original container
- Stored in health office
- Administered according to label

**Student Self-Carry:**
- Only with doctor's order and parent request
- School approval required
- Proper documentation
- For conditions like asthma, diabetes, etc.

### Illness at School

**If Student Becomes Ill:**
- Referred to school nurse
- Assessed and appropriate care given
- Parents contacted if needed
- May rest in health office briefly
- Sent home if necessary

### Communicable Diseases

**Exclusion from School:**
- Fever (above 100°F/37.8°C)
- Vomiting or diarrhea
- Uncontrollable cough
- Undiagnosed rash
- Contagious condition
- Until no longer contagious or with doctor's note

### Health Screenings

**Conducted:**
- Vision screening
- Hearing screening
- Growth monitoring (height/weight)
- Scoliosis screening (appropriate age)
- Dental health education
- General health observations

## Incident Reporting

### Reporting Procedures

**Students Should Report:**
- Any injury or illness
- Unsafe conditions
- Broken equipment
- Safety concerns
- Bullying or harassment
- Any other concern

**How to Report:**
- Tell any teacher or staff member
- Go to the main office
- Tell parent to contact school
- Use anonymous reporting option if needed

### Incident Documentation

**For All Injuries:**
- Incident report completed
- Parent notified
- Appropriate action taken
- Follow-up as needed
- Records maintained

### Serious Incidents

**Immediate Response:**
- Emergency services contacted if needed
- Parents notified immediately
- First aid administered
- Student safety prioritized
- Investigation conducted
- Report filed
