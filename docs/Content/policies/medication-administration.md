## Policy Overview

### Purpose

Smart Academy recognizes that some students require medication during school hours. This policy ensures:

- **Safety**: Medications administered safely and correctly
- **Security**: Proper storage and handling of medications
- **Documentation**: Clear records of all medications given
- **Communication**: Proper communication with parents
- **Training**: Staff appropriately trained

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All prescription medications
- All over-the-counter medications
- Herbal and alternative remedies
- Emergency medications (EpiPens, inhalers, etc.)

### General Principles

**Medication at School Should Be:**
- Necessary for the student's health
- Prescribed by a physician (for Rx)
- Documented with proper authorization
- Stored securely
- Administered by trained staff
- Given according to label/doctor's orders

## Parent Responsibilities

### Authorization Required

**Before Any Medication Can Be Given:**
1. **Medication Authorization Form** completed
2. **Prescriber's Authorization** for prescription meds
3. **Original Container** with label
4. **Written Instructions** for administration
5. **Parent Permission** for over-the-counter meds

### Providing Medication

**Parents Must:**
- Deliver medication directly to school nurse/designee
- NOT send medication with student
- Provide in original container
- Update information as changes occur
- Pick up expired/unused medication
- NOT ask staff to administer first dose

### Communication

**Keep School Informed Of:**
- New medications
- Dose changes
- Medication discontinuation
- Side effects to watch for
- Emergency procedures

## Prescription Medication

### Requirements

**For Prescription Medication to be Given at School:**

1. **Written Order from Prescriber**
   - Student's name
   - Medication name and strength
   - Dosage and route
   - Time to be given
   - Duration of treatment
   - Possible side effects
   - Prescriber's signature and contact

2. **Parent Authorization**
   - Permission to administer
   - Consent to contact prescriber if needed
   - Understanding of risks and benefits

3. **Original Container**
   - Pharmacy label with student's name
   - Current prescription
   - Clear dosing instructions

### Administration

**School Will:**
- Give medication exactly as prescribed
- Document each dose
- Monitor for side effects
- Communicate concerns to parent
- Store medication securely
- Return medication when completed

### Long-Term Medications

**For Medications Taken Daily:**
- Monthly supply provided
- Authorization renewed annually
- Regular review of effectiveness
- Documentation maintained

## Over-the-Counter Medication

### Available OTC Medications

**School May Have Available:**
- Acetaminophen (paracetamol)
- Ibuprofen (for older students)
- Antihistamines
- Cough drops
- Antacids
- Topical anti-itch cream

**Parent Authorization Required:**
- Written permission for each medication
- Specific symptoms allowed
- Dosage and frequency limits
- Duration limit
- Contact if symptoms persist

### Bringing OTC Medication

**If Parent Wants Specific OTC Given:**
- Written authorization required
- Provided in original container
- Clear dosing instructions
- Age-appropriate dosage only
- Limited duration (5 days without doctor's note)

### What School Will NOT Do

**School Will Not:**
- Administer aspirin (due to Reye's syndrome risk)
- Give medication beyond recommended dosage
- Administer expired medication
- Give first dose of any medication
- Administer medication contrary to label without doctor's order

## Self-Carry Medications

### Conditions for Self-Carry

**Students May Carry Medication When:**
- Written order from physician stating necessity
- Written permission from parent
- Demonstrated ability to self-administer
- Appropriate maturity and responsibility
- Emergency medication (asthma inhaler, EpiPen, etc.)

### Self-Carry Agreement

**Required Understanding:**
- Medication is for student's use only
- Will NOT share with others
- Will use only as directed
- Will report when used
- Will inform adult if needing help
- Will keep medication secure

### Monitoring

**School Will Monitor:**
- Appropriate use
- Storage (not left unattended)
- No sharing with others
- Reporting of use
- Refill needs

## Storage and Security

### Storage Location

**Medications Stored:**
- In designated locked cabinet
- In health office
- Temperature appropriate (not too hot/cold)
- Away from children's access
- Securely locked when not supervised

### Special Storage

**Refrigeration Required:**
- Some medications require refrigeration
- Refrigerator in health office
- Secured and monitored
- Separated from food

### Security Measures

**Medication Security:**
- Locked storage when not in use
- Only authorized staff access
- Key controlled by school nurse
- Regular inventory
- Access log maintained

### Expired Medication

**Expired Meds:**
- NOT administered
- Parent contacted to pick up
- Disposed of if not picked up
- Disposal documented

## Administration Procedures

### Daily Medication Schedule

**For Regularly Scheduled Medications:**
- Medication Administration Record maintained
- Each dose documented
- Time given recorded
- Initials of person giving medication
- Any notes or observations

### As-Needed Medications

**For PRN Medications:**
- Clear criteria for giving
- Parent contacted before giving (unless emergency)
- Documentation of reason given
- Maximum frequency specified
- Contact parent if limit reached

### Administration Process

**When Giving Medication:**
1. **Verify** student identity
2. **Check** authorization
3. **Read** label/instructions
4. **Measure** correct dose
5. **Give** medication appropriately
6. **Document** immediately
7. **Monitor** for effects
8. **Wash hands** before and after

### Documentation

**Each Dose Recorded:**
- Date and time
- Medication and dose
- Route of administration
- Person administering
- Any observations
- Parent notification if needed

## Emergency Medication

### Rescue Medications

**Examples:**
- EpiPen for severe allergies
- Rescue inhaler for asthma
- Glucagon for diabetes
- Seizure rescue medication

**Special Procedures:**
- Rapid access maintained
- Multiple staff trained in use
- Emergency plan in place
- Immediate parent notification
- Emergency services called if used

### Emergency Use

**When Rescue Medication Used:**
1. Administer immediately per protocol
2. Call emergency services (999)
3. Call parent immediately
4. Monitor student
5. Document thoroughly
6. Prepare information for EMS

## Errors and Omissions

### Medication Errors

**What Constitutes an Error:**
- Wrong medication given
- Wrong dose given
- Wrong time given
- Wrong route (oral vs injected, etc.)
- Missed dose
- Given to wrong student

**Response to Error:**
1. Student assessed immediately
2. Poison control consulted if needed
3. Parent notified immediately
4. Emergency services if needed
5. Documentation completed
6. Root cause analysis
7. Procedures reviewed

### Prevention

**Error Prevention Measures:**
- Clear labeling
- Double-checking doses
- Verification of student identity
- Proper storage
- Trained staff only
- Clear documentation
- Regular reviews

---

## Refusal of Medication Administration

### School Discretion

**School May Refuse to Administer If:**
- Outside staff's training/scope
- Inadequate or unclear instructions
- Expired medication
- Damaged medication
- Inappropriate container
- Concern about safety or appropriateness
- Lack of proper authorization

**If Refused:**
- Parent notified immediately
- Student may need to go home
- Alternative arrangements discussed
- Documentation completed
