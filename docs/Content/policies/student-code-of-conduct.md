## Policy Overview

### Purpose

Smart Academy's Code of Conduct establishes clear expectations for student behavior. It is designed to:

- Create a safe, nurturing learning environment
- Develop Islamic character (Akhlaq) in all students
- Promote respect, responsibility, and integrity
- Ensure all students can learn without disruption
- Prepare students for responsible citizenship

### Our Approach

We believe in:
- **Positive Discipline**: Focus on teaching appropriate behavior
- **Restorative Practices**: repairing harm rather than just punishing
- **Clear Expectations**: Students understand what is expected
- **Fair and Consistent**: All students treated equitably
- **Partnership with Parents**: Families involved in behavioral support

### Scope

This code applies to:
- All students enrolled at Smart Academy
- All school activities, on and off campus
- School transportation
- School-sponsored events and field trips
- Online activities related to school

## General Behavior Expectations

### At All Times, Students Will:

1. **Treat others with respect and kindness**
2. **Use polite and appropriate language**
3. **Follow directions from teachers and staff**
4. **Take responsibility for actions**
5. **Help create a positive learning environment**
6. **Practice Islamic manners (Adab)**

### Specific Expectations

**In the Classroom:**
- Arrive on time, prepared to learn
- Listen attentively when others speak
- Participate positively in discussions
- Complete assignments honestly
- Respect others' right to learn
- Take care of classroom materials

**In the Hallways and Common Areas:**
- Walk quietly and safely
- Keep to the right
- Respect displays and other students' work
- Use appropriate volume
- Keep areas clean

**In the Prayer Area:**
- Maintain appropriate reverence
- Enter with wudu (ablution) if possible
- Listen respectfully during prayer
- Follow prayer etiquette
- Keep area clean and quiet

**During Assembly:**
- Arrive promptly and line up quietly
- Listen respectfully to speakers
- Participate appropriately
- Show respect during Quran recitation

**In the Cafeteria:**
- Wait patiently in line
- Clean up after eating
- Use appropriate table manners
- Recite Bismillah before eating
- Recite Alhamdulillah after eating

**On the Playground/Field:**
- Play safely and fairly
- Include others in games
- Respect game rules
- Use equipment properly
- Demonstrate good sportsmanship

## Respect and Responsibility

### Respect for Others

**Students will show respect for:**

**Teachers and Staff:**
- Follow their instructions
- Address them appropriately (Sir, Brother, Teacher)
- Listen when they speak
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Appreciate their efforts

**Peers:**
- Treat others as you want to be treated
- Include everyone in activities
- Respect personal space
- Avoid name-calling or teasing
- Help others when needed

**Differences:**
- Respect different opinions and backgrounds
- Appreciate diversity
- Learn about other cultures
- Avoid stereotyping or prejudice

### Respect for Property

**School Property:**
- Keep school clean and tidy
- Care for books, equipment, and furniture
- Use resources appropriately
- Report damage or problems
- Take pride in our school

**Others' Property:**
- Respect others' belongings
- Ask before borrowing
- Return borrowed items promptly
- Report lost items found

**Personal Property:**
- Care for own belongings
- Keep valuables at home
- Label personal items
- Store items appropriately

### Personal Responsibility

**Students are Responsible For:**
- Own actions and choices
- Own learning and progress
- Own words and speech
- Own mistakes (honesty about them)
- Making things right when wrong

## Prohibited Behaviors

### Major Offenses

**The following behaviors are strictly prohibited:**

**1. Bullying and Harassment**
- Physical intimidation or harm
- Verbal teasing or name-calling
- Social exclusion
- Cyberbullying
- Any behavior that makes others feel unsafe

**2. Dishonesty**
- Lying to teachers or staff
- Cheating on tests or assignments
- Stealing or taking others' property
- Forgery or falsification

**3. Disrespect**
- Insubordination or defiance
- Rude or inappropriate language
- Disrespect to teachers, staff, or peers
- Disruptive behavior

**4. Physical Aggression**
- Fighting or physical violence
- Pushing, shoving, or hitting
- Throwing objects dangerously
- Any physical harm to others

**5. Property Damage**
- Vandalism or graffiti
- Damaging school property
- Damaging others' property
- Theft or stealing

**6. Prohibited Items**
- Weapons or weapon-like objects
- Tobacco, drugs, or alcohol
- Inappropriate materials
- Fireworks or dangerous items

**7. Technology Misuse**
- Inappropriate internet use
- Cyberbullying or harassment
- Hacking or unauthorized access
- Recording without permission

### Specific Prohibitions by Grade

**Additional Considerations:**

**Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG):**
- Focus on teaching appropriate behavior
- Gentle correction for minor issues
- Parent contact for repeated behaviors
- Emphasis on learning social skills

**Primary (Class 1-5):**
- Clear consequences established
- Loss of privileges for misbehavior
- Parent involvement as needed
- Teaching of appropriate behavior

**Secondary (Class 6-9):**
- Strict adherence to expectations
- More serious consequences for violations
- Student accountability emphasized
- Preparation for adult responsibilities

## Consequences for Misconduct

### Progressive Discipline Approach

**Level 1: Minor Infractions**
- First offense: Verbal warning
- Second offense: Warning noted, loss of privilege
- Third offense: Parent contact, detention
- Continued: Office referral

**Level 2: Moderate Misbehavior**
- First offense: Parent contact, detention
- Second offense: Parent conference, behavior contract
- Third offense: In-school suspension
- Continued: Suspension consideration

**Level 3: Serious Misbehavior**
- Immediate parent contact
- In-school or out-of-school suspension
- Behavior plan required
- Possible expulsion consideration

### Specific Consequences

**Loss of Privileges:**
- May lose recess, field trips, special events
- May lose leadership positions
- May lose awards or recognition

**Detention:**
- Held during lunch or after school
- Parents notified at least one day in advance
- Productive time (reflection, community service, study)

**Suspension:**
- In-school suspension: Completing work in separate setting
- Out-of-school suspension: Not allowed on campus
- Make-up work required but may receive reduced credit

**Expulsion:**
- For severe or repeated offenses
- Permanent removal from school
- Last resort after other interventions tried
