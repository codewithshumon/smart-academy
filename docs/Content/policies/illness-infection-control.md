## Policy Overview

### Purpose

Smart Academy is committed to maintaining a healthy school environment. This policy establishes:

- **Clear Guidelines**: When to keep children home

## When to Keep Children Home

### General Guidelines

**Keep Child Home If They Have:**
- Fever (100°F/37.8°C or higher)
- Vomiting or diarrhea in past 24 hours
- Uncontrollable coughing
- Difficulty breathing
- Suspected contagious disease
- Rash with fever
- Eye infection (conjunctivitis)
- Too sick to participate in activities

### Specific Symptoms

**Fever:**
- Keep home if temperature 100°F/37.8°C or higher
- Must be fever-free for 24 hours without medication before returning
- Fever is sign of infection and contagion

**Vomiting/Diarrhea:**
- Keep home if occurred in past 24 hours
- Must be tolerating normal diet before returning
- Prevents spread of gastrointestinal illness

**Cough:**
- Persistent/severe cough: keep home
- Mild cough: may attend if no fever
- Cover coughs with elbow, not hands

**Sore Throat:**
- With fever or swollen glands: keep home
- Possible strep: see doctor before returning

**Rash:**
- Any rash with fever: keep home and see doctor
- Unknown rash: have evaluated before returning

**Eye Infection:**
- Red, itchy, watery eyes: possible conjunctivitis
- Keep home until evaluated or treated for 24 hours

### General Appearance

**Keep Home If Child:**
- Is very tired, pale, or lethargic
- Is not eating or drinking well
- Has significant pain or discomfort
- Just doesn't seem well
- Needs more care than school can provide

## Illness at School

### Assessment Process

**When Student Appears Ill:**
1. Teacher sends student to health office
2. School nurse assesses student
3. Temperature taken if fever suspected
4. Symptoms evaluated
5. Parent contacted if needed
6. Appropriate action taken

### Temporary Rest in Health Office

**Student May Rest If:**
- Mild symptoms only
- No fever
- Can return to class after short rest
- Parent not contacted immediately unless condition worsens

### Sending Home

**Student Will Be Sent Home For:**
- Fever of 100°F/37.8°C or higher
- Vomiting or diarrhea
- Suspected contagious illness
- Severe symptoms
- In need of medical evaluation
- Too ill to participate

### Parent Pickup

**When Called:**
- Arrive as soon as possible (within 1 hour)
- Come to main office/health office
- Bring identification if unknown to staff
- Sign student out
- Provide transportation

## Exclusion Criteria

### Specific Illness Exclusion

**Chickenpox:**
- Exclude until all blisters have crusted over
- Usually 5-7 days from rash onset
- No new blisters for 24 hours
- Doctor's note may be required

**Measles:**
- Exclude until 4 days after rash appears
- Doctor's note required for return
- Report to health authorities

**Mumps:**
- Exclude for 5 days after swelling begins
- Doctor's note may be required
- Report to health authorities

**Rubella (German Measles):**
- Exclude for 7 days after rash appears
- Doctor's note required
- Report to health authorities

**Whooping Cough (Pertussis):**
- Exclude until 5 days of antibiotics completed
- Or 3 weeks from cough onset if untreated
- Doctor's note required

**Strep Throat:**
- Exclude until 24 hours of antibiotics completed
- No fever for 24 hours
- Feeling well enough to participate

**Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye):**
- Exclude if bacterial (until 24 hours of treatment)
- May attend if viral (doctor confirmation)
- No discharge present

**Impetigo:**
- Exclude until 24 hours of treatment
- Sores covered if returning earlier
- Doctor's note may be required

**Ringworm:**
- Exclude if not covered and on exposed skin
- May attend if covered and receiving treatment
- Doctor's confirmation helpful

**Head Lice:**
- Exclude until first treatment completed
- No live lice present
- Nits may be present but no live lice
- School will conduct checks as needed

**Scabies:**
- Exclude until treatment completed
- May return after treatment if no longer contagious
- Doctor's note recommended

**Influenza (Flu):**
- Exclude until fever-free for 24 hours without medication
- No longer contagious
- Feeling well enough to participate

**COVID-19:**
- Follow current health authority guidelines
- May change based on local regulations
- Contact school for current requirements

## Return to School Guidelines

### General Requirements

**Before Returning, Student Must:**
- Be fever-free for 24 hours without fever-reducing medication
- Have no vomiting or diarrhea for 24 hours
- Be able to participate in normal activities
- Not need medication for symptoms during school day
- No longer contagious (for communicable diseases)

### Doctor's Note Required When:**
- Student had communicable disease
- Student was hospitalized
- Student has been absent for 5+ days
- Special accommodation needed
- School nurse or administration requests

### Gradual Return

**When Returning After Illness:**
- May need modified activity for first day or two
- Teachers will help student catch up
- Special arrangements for PE/sports if needed
- Communicate needs to school nurse

## Infection Control Measures

### Hand Hygiene

**When Students Must Wash Hands:**
- Before eating or handling food
- After using toilet
- After blowing nose, coughing, or sneezing
- After outdoor play
- After touching animals
- Whenever hands are visibly dirty

**Proper Hand Washing:**
1. Wet hands with water
2. Apply soap
3. Scrub for 20 seconds (sing "Happy Birthday" twice)
4. Rinse well
5. Dry with paper towel
6. Use towel to turn off faucet

**Hand Sanitizer:**
- Available when soap and water not available
- Used with supervision for younger students
- Not effective on visibly dirty hands
- Not effective against all germs

### Respiratory Hygiene

**Cough Etiquette:**
- Cough or sneeze into elbow, not hands
- Use tissue when possible
- Dispose of tissue properly
- Wash hands after use
- Turn away from others when coughing/sneezing

### Cleaning and Disinfection

**Regular Cleaning Of:**
- Classrooms daily
- Bathrooms multiple times daily
- High-touch surfaces regularly
- Toys and shared items
- Cafeteria tables
- Door handles and light switches

**When Someone Is Ill:**
- Immediate cleaning of affected area
- Disinfection of surfaces
- Proper handling of contaminated items
- Ventilation of room if possible

### Personal Items

**To Reduce Spread:**
- Do not share water bottles, cups, utensils
- Do not share combs, brushes, hats
- Do not share towels or clothing
- Keep personal items separate

## Communicable Diseases

### Reporting Requirements

**School Must Report:**
- Certain communicable diseases to health authorities
- Outbreaks or clusters of illness
- Unusual illness patterns
- Serious infectious diseases

**Parent Notification**

**School Will Notify Parents When:**
- Communicable disease identified at school
- Exposure risk to other students
- Information about symptoms to watch for
- Steps being taken to prevent spread

**Common School Notifications:**
- Head lice
- Chickenpox
- Influenza outbreak
- Strep throat cluster
- Stomach illness

### Chronic Illnesses

**Students With Chronic Conditions:**
- Asthma
- Diabetes
- Seizure disorders
- Other ongoing health issues

**School Will:**
- Maintain Individual Health Plan
- Train staff on condition
- Implement accommodations
- Communicate with parents as needed
- Support student participation in all activities

## Parent Responsibilities

### Keeping School Informed

**Parents Should:**
- Report communicable diseases immediately
- Update health information annually
- Inform of new diagnoses
- Provide doctor's notes when required
- Keep emergency contacts current

### Communication

**When Student Is Ill:**
- Call school each day of absence
- Describe symptoms if possible
- Mention if doctor visited
- Provide doctor's note if required
- Estimated date of return

### Support at Home

**When Child Is Ill:**
- Keep home when appropriate
- Encourage rest and fluids
- Follow doctor's advice
- Keep school informed
- Plan for make-up work
