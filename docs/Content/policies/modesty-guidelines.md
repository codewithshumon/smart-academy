## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Haya (modesty) is a core Islamic value that guides behavior, dress, and interactions at Smart Academy. These guidelines establish:

- **Islamic Modesty**: Framework rooted in Quran and Sunnah
- **Age-Appropriate Guidelines**: Modesty expectations by developmental stage
- **Holistic Approach**: Modesty in dress, speech, behavior, and interactions
- **Respect and Dignity**: Modesty as respect for self and others
- **Character Development**: Modesty as part of Islamic character

### Our Vision

We aim to develop students who:
- Understand and value Islamic modesty
- Dress and behave with Haya
- Respect themselves and others
- Maintain appropriate boundaries
- Internalize modesty as lifelong practice

### Scope

These guidelines apply to:
- All students, staff, and parents
- All aspects of school life
- All school activities and events
- Online presence related to school
- Communication and interactions

## Islamic Foundation

### Quranic Teachings on Modesty

**On Modesty for Believers:**
> "Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their modesty. That is purer for them. Indeed, Allah is Acquainted with what they do." (Quran 24:30)

> "And tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their modesty and not to display their adornment except that which [necessarily] appears thereof and to wrap [a portion of] their headcovers over their chests and not to display their adornment except to their husbands, their fathers, their husbands' fathers, their sons, their husbands' sons, their brothers, their brothers' sons, their sisters' sons, their women, that which their right hands possess, or those male attendants having no physical desire, or children who are not yet aware of the private aspects of women..." (Quran 24:31)

**On Modesty as General Virtue:**
> "O children of Adam, take your adornment at every masjid and eat and drink, but be not excessive. Indeed, He likes not those who commit excess." (Quran 7:31)

**On Lowering the Gaze:**
> "Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their modesty... And tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their modesty..." (Quran 24:30-31)

### Hadith on Modesty (Haya)

The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said:

> "Modesty (Haya) and faith are inseparable. If one of them is missing, the other is also missing." (Sahih Bukhari)

> "Modesty is part of faith." (Sahih Bukhari)

> "Every religion has a distinct character, and the distinct character of Islam is modesty." (Ibn Majah)

> "Verily, from the words of the earlier prophets that people have retained are: If you feel no shame, then do as you wish." (Sahih Bukhari)

> "Modesty brings nothing but good." (Sahih Bukhari)

### Understanding Awrah

**Definition:** Awrah refers to parts of the body that must be covered

**For Men:**
- From navel to knee (most scholars)
- Some scholars include entire body except face, hands, and feet

**For Women:**
- Entire body except face and hands (most scholars)
- Some scholars include feet as well
- Extra modesty in prayer

## Understanding Haya (Modesty)

### What is Haya?

**Definition:** Haya is a natural shyness, modesty, and sense of shame that prevents a person from behaving in a way that is displeasing to Allah

**Types of Haya:**

**1. Haya before Allah (Taqwa):**
- Knowing Allah sees everything
- Feeling shy to disobey Allah
- Being mindful of Allah's presence

**2. Haya with oneself:**
- Self-respect and dignity
- Not exposing one's faults
- Not engaging in shameful behavior

**3. Haya with others:**
- Respecting others' privacy
- Not looking at others' awrah
- Not engaging in inappropriate behavior

**4. Haya in appearance:**
- Dressing modestly
- Not seeking attention through appearance
- Covering awrah appropriately

### Importance of Haya

**Haya:**
- Is part of faith (Iman)
- Protects from sin
- Reflects good character
- Promotes respect and dignity
- Is valued in Islam
- Leads to Paradise

## Dress Modesty

### General Principles of Modest Dress

**For All Students:**
- Clothing should not be tight or revealing
- Clothing should not be transparent
- Clothing should not draw inappropriate attention
- Clothing should reflect Islamic values
- Clean and neat appearance maintained

**Modest Dress:**
- Covers awrah appropriately
- Not form-fitting
- Not transparent
- Not immodest by design (slogans, images)
- Appropriate for Islamic environment

### Modesty by Gender

**Boys and Men:**
- Cover from navel to knee at minimum
- Loose, non-transparent clothing
- Not wearing tight pants
- Not exposing chest
- Undergarments covered
- Neat and clean appearance

**Girls and Women:**
- Cover entire body except face and hands
- Loose, non-transparent clothing
- Not form-fitting
- Hijab/head covering (Class 3+)
- Not revealing body shape
- Modest lengths (ankle-length preferred)

### Modesty by Grade Level

**Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG):**
- Introduction to concept of modesty
- Age-appropriate clothing
- Learning privacy concepts
- Learning to dress appropriately
- Parental guidance primary

**Lower Primary (Class 1-2):**
- Understanding awrah concept
- Learning modest dress principles
- Beginning hijab practice (optional for girls)
- Privacy awareness
- Modest behavior encouraged

**Upper Primary (Class 3-5):**
- Regular modest dress practice
- Hijab required for girls
- Understanding reasons for modesty
- Modesty in interactions
- Developing sense of Haya

**Secondary (Class 6-9):**
- Full modest dress practice expected
- Hijab required for girls at all times
- Understanding Islamic rulings
- Modesty as personal value
- Responsibility for maintaining modesty

### Specific Dress Guidelines

**For Girls:**

**Tops/Kameez:**
- Long enough to cover waist and hips
- Loose fit
- Not tight across chest
- Not transparent
- Not low neckline
- Sleeves covering arms

**Bottoms/Salwar/ Trousers:**
- Loose fit
- Ankle-length preferred
- Not tight
- Not transparent
- Undergarments not visible

**Hijab/Scarf:**
- Required Class 3 and above
- Covers hair and neck adequately
- Not transparent
- Stays in place
- Neutral colors (white, navy, black preferred)

**For Boys:**

**Shirts/Kameez:**
- Cover torso appropriately
- Not tight
- Not transparent
- Undergarments covered
- Appropriate neckline

**Trousers/Pants:**
- Cover from waist to below knee
- Not tight
- Undergarments not visible
- Belt worn if needed
- Not sagging

## Behavioral Modesty

### Speech Modesty

**Modest Speech:**
- Speaking respectfully
- Not using profanity
- Not shouting or being loud
- Not making inappropriate comments
- Not gossiping or backbiting
- Not making fun of others
- Using Islamic greetings

**We Teach:**
- Think before speaking
- Speak kindly or remain silent
- Avoid hurtful words
- Respectful disagreement
- Appropriate tone and volume

### Manner Modesty

**Modest Behavior:**
- Walking modestly (not strutting)
- Sitting modestly
- Not drawing attention to oneself
- Not being boastful or arrogant
- Being humble
- Respecting personal space
- Not touching others inappropriately

**We Teach:**
- Good manners (Adab)
- Respecting boundaries
- Appropriate behavior in different settings
- Self-control
- Consideration for others

### Interaction Modesty

**Gender Interaction Guidelines:**

**Lowering the Gaze:**
- Not staring at others
- Looking away when inappropriate
- Focusing on face when speaking
- Not looking at awrah of others

**Physical Contact:**
- No unnecessary physical contact between genders
- Shaking hands with opposite gender not required
- Respectful distance maintained
- No playful touching

**Privacy:**
- Respect others' privacy
- Not entering private spaces without permission
- Knocking before entering
- Not spying or eavesdropping

**Communication:**
- Respectful and professional
- No flirting or inappropriate speech
- No private messaging between unrelated genders
- Focus on academic and appropriate topics

### Public Modesty

**In Public Spaces:**
- Appropriate behavior in hallways
- Appropriate behavior in cafeteria
- Appropriate behavior on playground
- Not displaying affection inappropriately
- Maintaining Islamic behavior everywhere

**Changing Areas:**
- Use designated changing areas
- Privacy respected
- Not exposing awrah
- Appropriate supervision

## Social Modesty

### Online Presence

**Social Media and Internet:**
- Modest profile pictures
- Modest posts and comments
- Not posting inappropriate content
- Not engaging in inappropriate conversations
- Privacy settings maintained
- Reporting inappropriate behavior

**We Teach:**
- Internet safety
- Modesty in online communication
- Privacy protection
- Responsible digital citizenship
- Islamic values online

### Media Consumption

**Guidelines for Media:**
- Avoid inappropriate movies, shows, music
- Parental guidance for media choices
- Media should align with Islamic values
- Awareness of media influence
- Critical thinking about media

**School Approach:**
- Media used in education is appropriate
- No inappropriate content in school
- Media literacy taught
- Critical evaluation of media messages

### Family Modesty

**Respect for Family:**
- Respecting family members
- Not discussing private family matters
- Modesty with family members (as appropriate)
- Maintaining family honor
- Not bringing shame to family

## Enforcement and Support

### Addressing Immodesty

**Approach:**
- Gentle correction
- Private discussion
- Educational focus
- Understanding reasons
- Supporting improvement
- Not shaming

**For Dress Violations:**
- Private conversation with student
- Explanation of expectation
- Parent contact if needed
- Solution-oriented approach
- Support for compliance

**For Behavioral Violations:**
- Private conversation
- Understanding context
- Teaching appropriate behavior
- Parent involvement if needed
- Progressive approach

### Support and Encouragement

**Positive Reinforcement:**
- Recognizing modest behavior
- Praising appropriate dress
- Acknowledging improvement
- Encouraging growth
- Celebrating Islamic values
