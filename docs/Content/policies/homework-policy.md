## Policy Overview

### Philosophy

Smart Academy believes that homework should be **meaningful, manageable, and purposeful**. Our homework policy is designed to:

- **Reinforce Learning**: Practice and consolidate skills learned in class
- **Develop Independence**: Build self-discipline and time management
- **Extend Learning**: Apply knowledge to new situations
- **Involve Parents**: Keep families informed about learning
- **Prepare for Future**: Build study habits for secondary and higher education

> "And say, 'My Lord, increase me in knowledge.'" (Quran 20:114)

**The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) said:**

### Our Commitment

We commit to:
- Assigning only meaningful homework
- Respecting family time
- Considering overall student workload
- Providing clear instructions
- Giving timely feedback
- Being flexible when needed

## Purpose of Homework

### Educational Benefits

**1. Reinforcement**
- Practice skills taught in class
- Review and consolidate learning
- Build automaticity in foundational skills

**2. Preparation**
- Preview upcoming topics
- Background reading for future lessons
- Gathering materials for projects

**3. Extension**
- Apply learning to new situations
- Connect learning to real life
- Develop creative projects

**4. Independent Work Habits**
- Build time management skills
- Develop responsibility
- Practice self-discipline

**5. Parent Communication**
- Parents see what children are learning
- Opportunities for home-school connection
- Parents can support and encourage

### What Homework Should NOT Be

- **Busy work** with no clear purpose
- **Ungraded work** that doesn't receive feedback
- **Punishment** assigned for behavior issues
- **New learning** not yet taught in class
- **Excessive** taking too much time

## Homework Guidelines by Grade

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

**Daily Duration:**
- PlayGroup: No formal homework
- Nursery: Maximum 10 minutes (occasional activities)
- KG: Maximum 15-20 minutes

**Focus:**
- **PlayGroup**: Informal activities at home (reading together, counting objects)
- **Nursery**: Occasional simple activities (drawing, letter practice)
- **KG:** Daily reading with parent, simple practice activities

**Types of Activities:**
- Reading together with family
- Finding letters/numbers at home
- Drawing about school day
- Bringing items from home for class
- Occasional worksheets

**Note:** Homework at this level should feel like play, not work.

### Class 1-2

**Daily Duration:**
- **Class 1**: 20-30 minutes
- **Class 2**: 30-40 minutes

**Daily Expectations:**
- **Reading:** 15 minutes (with or to parent)
- **Practice:** 10-15 minutes (math facts, spelling, etc.)
- **Occasional Projects:** Extended assignments spread over days

**Typical Assignments:**
- Daily reading log
- Math practice sheets
- Spelling/word work
- Simple science observations
- Occasional drawing/writing about learning

### Class 3-5

**Daily Duration:**
- **Class 3**: 40-50 minutes
- **Class 4**: 50-60 minutes
- **Class 5**: 60-70 minutes

**Daily Expectations:**
- **Reading:** 20-30 minutes (independent reading)
- **Subject Practice:** 20-30 minutes (math, language, science)
- **Long-term Projects:** Occasional projects worked on over time

**Typical Assignments:**
- Daily reading (book of choice)
- Math problem set or practice
- Language arts (grammar, writing)
- Science/social studies activities
- Quran/Arabic practice
- Occasional projects or presentations

### Class 6-9

**Daily Duration:**
- **Class 6-7**: 70-90 minutes
- **Class 8-9**: 90-120 minutes

**Daily Expectations:**
- **Subject Review/Practice:** All major subjects
- **Reading/Literature:** Regular assignments
- **Long-term Projects:** Ongoing work on papers, projects
- **Exam Preparation:** Regular review for tests

**Typical Assignments:**
- Mathematics problem sets
- English literature reading and analysis
- Bengali language practice
- Science lab reports or review
- Social studies reading/questions
- Islamic Studies/Quran memorization
- Long-term projects managed over weeks

## Types of Homework

### Practice Homework

**Purpose:** Reinforce skills learned in class

**Examples:**
- Math problems practicing today's lesson
- Grammar exercises
- Quran recitation practice
- Spelling/vocabulary review
- Science equation practice

**Guidelines:**
- Should be familiar content
- Clear instructions provided
- Answers can be checked independently
- Time-limited

### Preparation Homework

**Purpose:** Prepare for upcoming lessons

**Examples:**
- Background reading
- Previewing vocabulary
- Gathering materials
- Brainstorming ideas for writing
- Watching assigned video (rare)

**Guidelines:**
- Introduced briefly in class first
- Clear purpose explained
- Connected to next lesson
- Not graded for accuracy

### Extension Homework

**Purpose:** Apply learning to new situations

**Examples:**
- Real-world math problems
- Science observations at home
- Writing about personal experience
- Creative projects
- Research for presentations

**Guidelines:**
- Student choice when possible
- Connected to student interests
- Creative application encouraged
- Shared with class

### Integration Homework

**Purpose:** Connect learning across subjects

**Examples:**
- Projects combining multiple subjects
- Writing about science topics
- Islamic perspective on social studies
- Art projects about literature

**Guidelines:**
- Collaboration between teachers
- Clear expectations
- Extended timeline
- Meaningful connections

## Homework Free Times

### No Homework Periods

**No homework assigned:**
- **Eid Break:** No homework during Eid holidays (families celebrating)
- **Ramadan:** Reduced homework during Ramadan
- **Exam Week:** No new homework during examination weeks
- **Weekends:** No assigned homework (except catch-up or long-term projects)
- **School Holidays:** No homework during school holidays

### Reduced Homework Times

**Homework reduced during:**
- **Ramadan:** 50% reduction during fasting month
- **Final Exam Preparation:** 2 weeks before exams
- **Major Events:** Sports week, cultural programs, etc.

## Parental Involvement

### Appropriate Parent Support

**Parents should:**
1. **Provide Environment:** Quiet, well-lit space for homework
2. **Set Routine:** Consistent homework time each day
3. **Show Interest:** Ask about what they're learning
4. **Offer Help:** When genuinely stuck, guide but don't do the work
5. **Monitor Progress:** Check completion but not obsess over perfection
6. **Communicate:** Contact teacher if problems arise

### Inappropriate Parent Involvement

**Parents should NOT:**
- Do the homework for the child
- Correct every mistake
- Argue with child about homework
- Punish excessively for incomplete homework
- Contact teacher daily for homework
- Ignore chronic homework problems

### Support by Grade Level

**Early Childhood:**
- Read together daily
- Make activities playful
- Praise effort

**Primary:**
- Help establish routine
- Check homework completion
- Guide, don't do

**Secondary:**
- Provide structure and support
- Monitor from a distance
- Teach independence

## Submission and Grading

### Submission Expectations

**General Rules:**
- Submit at beginning of class on due date
- Neat, complete, and on time
- Name, class, date clearly written
- Late submissions handled per Late Submission Policy

### Grading Homework

**Purpose of Grading:**
- **Feedback**: Let students know how they're doing
- **Accountability**: Encourage completion and effort
- **Practice**: Reinforce learning

**Weight in Grades:**
- **Class 1-5**: 15-20% of total grade
- **Class 6-9**: 15% of total grade

**Focus:**
- Completion and effort more important than perfection
- Learning from mistakes
- Improvement over time

### Feedback

**Types of Feedback:**
- **Check marks** for completion
- **Written comments** for improvement
- **Grades** for significant assignments
- **Stickers/stamps** for younger students
- **Verbal feedback** in class

**Timeline:**
- Reviewed within 2 school days
- Returned within 1 week
- Major projects may take longer

## Homework During Special Circumstances

### Absence

**Short Absence (1-2 days):**
- Complete regular homework when possible
- Extended deadline equal to days absent
- Not expected to complete work while very sick

**Extended Absence (3+ days):**
- Modified homework assigned
- Focus on essential skills
- Extended deadlines
- Consider individual circumstances

### Religious Observances

**Eid and Other Islamic Holidays:**
- No homework assigned before/during Eid
- Extended deadlines for work due near Eid
- Religious observance respected

**Friday Prayers (for secondary students):**
- Homework adjusted if needed
- Religious obligation prioritized

### Family Emergency

**During family crisis:**
- Homework excused
- Focus on family needs
- Extended deadlines upon return
- Compassion applied

## Teacher Responsibilities

### Planning Homework

**Teachers will:**
- Assign purposeful homework only
- Consider total daily homework load
- Provide clear instructions
- Ensure homework can be completed independently
- Coordinate with other teachers (secondary)
- Avoid assigning homework that requires special resources

### Communicating Homework

**Teachers will:**
- Write homework assignments clearly
- Post homework on portal/whiteboard
- Explain expectations before assigning
- Provide examples when needed
- Give adequate time for long-term assignments
- Be available for questions

### Monitoring and Feedback

**Teachers will:**
- Check homework regularly
- Provide timely feedback
- Track completion
- Contact parents about chronic problems
- Use homework to inform instruction
- Celebrate good effort

## Student Responsibilities

### Homework Habits

**Students should:**
1. Write down all assignments
2. Bring necessary materials home
3. Complete homework independently
4. Ask for help when needed
5. Submit homework on time
6. Make up missed homework

### Creating Good Habits

**Recommended Routine:**
1. **Unpack**: Take out homework and materials
2. **Prioritize**: Start with hardest or most important
3. **Focus**: Work without distractions
4. **Check**: Review work before packing away
5. **Pack**: Put everything in backpack for next day

### Time Management Tips

**For Students:**
- Use homework planner
- Break big projects into small parts
- Start early, don't procrastinate
- Take short breaks during long homework sessions
- Ask for help early, not the night before due

## Managing Homework Challenges

### When Students Struggle

**Signs of struggle:**
- Homework takes much longer than expected
- Frequent tears or frustration
- Doesn't understand assignments
- Consistently incomplete homework

**Steps to take:**
1. **Communicate** with teacher about concerns
2. **Assess** the problem (difficulty? time management? distractions?)
3. **Develop** plan with teacher
4. **Monitor** progress
5. **Adjust** plan as needed

### When Homework is Too Much

**If homework consistently takes too long:**
- Contact teacher immediately
- Explain time being spent
- Discuss modifications
- Consider overall workload

**Guideline:** If consistently exceeding maximum time by 50%, contact teacher.
