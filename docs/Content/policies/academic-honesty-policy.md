## Policy Overview

### Purpose Statement

Smart Academy is committed to maintaining the highest standards of academic integrity. This policy establishes clear guidelines for honest academic work and defines consequences for violations. Academic honesty is essential for:

- **Valid Assessment**: Ensuring grades reflect true learning and achievement
- **Character Development**: Building integrity (Amanah) as a core value
- **Fairness**: Creating equal opportunities for all students
- **Trust**: Maintaining confidence in our educational system

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students enrolled at Smart Academy (PlayGroup - Class 9)
- All academic work including assignments, homework, projects, quizzes, tests, and examinations
- Work done both on and off campus
- Online and digital submissions

### Policy Statement

> Smart Academy has **zero tolerance** for academic dishonesty. All students must submit original work that accurately represents their own learning and understanding. Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or academic dishonesty will result in serious consequences.

## Academic Integrity Standards

### General Principles

**All students must:**
1. Submit only their own original work
2. Properly acknowledge all sources used
3. Follow all instructions for assignments and assessments
4. Protect their own work from being copied by others
5. Report any observed academic dishonesty

### Authorized and Unauthorized Assistance

**Authorized Assistance includes:**
- Help from teachers during designated help sessions
- Collaborative work when explicitly permitted by the teacher
- Peer tutoring that enhances understanding (not doing the work)
- Use of approved resources during assessments

**Unauthorized Assistance includes:**
- Copying another student's work
- Allowing someone to copy your work
- Using unauthorized materials during tests
- Receiving help on individual assignments
- Sharing test information with students who haven't taken it

### Proper Citation and Acknowledgment

**When to Cite:**
- Direct quotations from any source
- Paraphrased ideas from any source
- Facts that are not common knowledge
- Images, charts, or diagrams created by others
- Code or algorithms from external sources

**How to Cite (age-appropriate):**
- **PlayGroup-Class 2**: Mentioning sources orally or with simple attribution
- **Class 3-5**: Basic written citations (author, title, website)
- **Class 6-9**: Formal citation methods (APA/MLA style as appropriate)

**Consequences for Plagiarism:**
- Unintentional: Grade reduction and required revision
- Intentional: Zero on assignment and disciplinary action

## Types of Academic Dishonesty

### Cheating on Assessments

**Definition:** Using unauthorized methods to gain unfair advantage on tests, quizzes, or examinations.

**Examples include:**
- Copying from another student's paper
- Using unauthorized notes, books, or devices
- Sharing answers during an assessment
- Using hidden cheat sheets
- Communicating with other students during tests
- Looking at another student's work intentionally

**Penalty:** Zero on the assessment, parent conference, possible suspension

### Plagiarism

**Definition:** Presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own without proper acknowledgment.

**Examples include:**
- Copying text from books, websites, or other sources without citation
- Copying another student's homework
- Using online essay mills or paper-writing services
- Copying and pasting from the internet
- Paraphrasing without giving credit
- Using AI tools to generate work without permission

**Penalty:** Zero on assignment, required rewrite for no credit, disciplinary record

### Fabrication and Falsification

**Definition:** Inventing or altering information in academic work.

**Examples include:**
- Inventing data for experiments or projects
- Faking research sources
- Altering graded work and resubmitting
- Forging signatures or documents
- Creating false citations

**Penalty:** Zero on assignment, possible suspension, parent conference

### Collusion

**Definition:** Unauthorized collaboration with others on individual assignments.

**Examples include:**
- Working together on individual homework
- Sharing completed assignments
- Dividing work among friends for individual projects
- Allowing someone to copy your work

**Penalty:** Zero for all involved parties

### Unauthorized Use of Technology

**Definition:** Using technology in ways not permitted for academic work.

**Examples include:**
- Using smartphones during tests
- Using translation tools for language assignments when prohibited
- Using AI chatbots to write essays or solve problems
- Accessing unauthorized websites during online assessments
- Sharing photos of test questions

**Penalty:** Device confiscation, zero on assessment, disciplinary action

### Sabotage

**Definition:** Intentionally damaging or interfering with another student's work.

**Examples include:**
- Destroying or hiding another student's work
- Tampering with laboratory experiments
- Deleting computer files belonging to others
- Intentionally misleading other students about deadlines or requirements

**Penalty:** Suspension, possible expulsion, parent conference

## Prevention Strategies

### School-Level Prevention

**1. Clear Communication**
- Policy reviewed with students at start of each year
- Teachers explain expectations before each assignment
- Written instructions provided for all assessments

**2. Assessment Design**
- Assignments that require original thinking
- In-class writing assignments
- Multiple versions of tests when appropriate
- Process-based assessment (drafts, outlines)

**3. Monitoring and Supervision**
- Active proctoring during examinations
- Plagiarism detection software for written work
- Regular review of student work patterns

**4. Education and Awareness**
- Lessons on academic integrity
- Examples of proper citation methods
- Discussion of why honesty matters
- Recognition of honest effort

### Classroom Practices

**Teachers will:**
1. Clearly define what constitutes cheating for each assignment
2. Specify whether collaboration is allowed
3. Provide examples of proper citation and acknowledgment
4. Monitor student work during assessments
5. Follow up promptly on suspected violations
6. Teach research and citation skills appropriate to grade level

### Parent Support

**Parents should:**
1. Reinforce the value of honesty at home
2. Provide guidance without doing the work for children
3. Communicate concerns about workload to teachers
4. Help children understand that learning > grades
5. Model integrity in their own lives
6. Support school decisions regarding academic honesty

### Student Responsibilities

**Students are expected to:**
1. Understand what constitutes academic dishonesty
2. Ask questions when uncertain about expectations
3. Protect their own work from being copied
4. Decline to help others cheat
5. Report observed violations to teachers
6. Take pride in honest achievement

## Consequences and Disciplinary Actions

### Grading Consequences

**First Offense (Minor):**
- Zero on the assignment or assessment
- Assignment cannot be made up
- Parent notification
- Warning letter in student file

**First Offense (Major):**
- Zero on the assignment or assessment
- Parent conference required
- Disciplinary probation
- Loss of honors/awards eligibility for one term

**Second Offense:**
- Zero on the assignment or assessment
- Suspension from school (1-3 days)
- Loss of all honors/awards for academic year
- Behavior contract required

**Third Offense or Severe Violation:**
- Zero on the assignment or assessment
- Suspension or expulsion considered
- Permanent disciplinary record
- Loss of all privileges and honors
- Possible notification to future schools

### Additional Consequences

**Leadership Positions:**
- Removal from student council
- Loss of team captain roles
- Ineligibility for leadership positions for one year

**Honors and Awards:**
- Loss of eligibility for academic awards
- Removal from honor roll for the term
- Revocation of falsely obtained certificates

**Recommendations:**
- Disciplinary action noted on school records
- May affect transfer applications
- May affect recommendations to other institutions

## Appeals Process

### Right to Appeal

Students and parents have the right to appeal academic honesty decisions within **three school days** of notification.

### Appeals Process

**Step 1: Informal Review**
- Discuss with the teacher who assigned the consequence
- Clarify misunderstanding and provide additional context
- Many issues resolved at this level

**Step 2: Formal Appeal**
- Submit written appeal to the Vice Principal
- Include evidence supporting the appeal
- Appeal reviewed within five school days

**Step 3: Appeal Committee**
- If not resolved, appeal goes to Academic Honesty Committee
- Committee includes: Vice Principal, Department Head, Teacher Representative
- Parents may attend to present their case
- Final decision made within seven school days

### Grounds for Appeal

Valid grounds for appeal include:
- New evidence not available at time of decision
- Procedural error in the investigation
- Misunderstanding of instructions by teacher
- Evidence of work being original despite appearance

**Not valid grounds:**
- Disagreement with the policy
- Claiming "everyone else does it"
- Blaming pressure or stress
- Arguing that the work was "too hard"

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Student Responsibilities

- Understand and follow all academic integrity policies
- Submit only original work
- Cite sources properly
- Protect your work from others
- Ask questions when uncertain
- Report violations you witness

### Teacher Responsibilities

- Clearly communicate expectations for all assignments
- Design assessments that minimize temptation to cheat
- Monitor students during assessments appropriately
- Report violations promptly and fairly
- Teach research and citation skills
- Model academic integrity

### Parent Responsibilities

- Support the school's academic integrity policy
- Encourage honest effort over high grades
- Provide help without doing work for children
- Communicate with teachers about concerns
- Reinforce Islamic values of honesty and integrity

### Administration Responsibilities

- Ensure policy is clearly communicated
- Support teachers in enforcing policy
- Handle appeals fairly and promptly
- Maintain appropriate records
- Provide professional development on academic integrity

## Specific Guidelines by Grade Level

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

**Focus:** Building foundation of honesty

**Expectations:**
- Simple encouragement to do your own work
- Recognition for honest effort
- Parent involvement in explaining concepts

**Violations:**
- Handled gently with educational focus
- Parent conference
- Opportunity to redo work correctly

### Primary (Class 1-5)

**Focus:** Understanding what cheating is and why it's wrong

**Expectations:**
- Individual work on homework
- Simple citations for sources
- Understanding consequences of dishonesty

**Violations:**
- Zero on assignment
- Parent notification
- Loss of recess or privileges
- Educational activity about honesty

### Secondary (Class 6-9)

**Focus:** Taking full responsibility for academic integrity

**Expectations:**
- Proper citation of all sources
- Complete originality in major work
- Understanding serious consequences
- Leadership in modeling integrity

**Violations:**
- Full range of consequences applied
- Permanent disciplinary record
- Possible loss of academic standing
- Impact on future recommendations
