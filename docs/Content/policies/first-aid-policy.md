## Policy Overview

### Purpose

This policy ensures that Smart Academy provides appropriate first aid for injuries and illnesses that occur during school hours. The policy establishes:

- **Trained Responders**: Staff trained in first aid
- **Accessible Facilities**: First aid kits and rooms
- **Clear Procedures**: Standard response protocols
- **Proper Documentation**: Records of incidents and care
- **Parent Communication**: Timely notification of parents

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students during school hours
- All school activities and events
- All staff and visitors on campus
- Off-campus school activities

## First Aid Procedures

### General Approach

**When Injury or Illness Occurs:**
1. **Ensure Scene Safety** — Don't put yourself at risk
2. **Assess the Situation** — What happened? How serious?
3. **Call for Help** — Get trained staff or school nurse
4. **Provide Care** — Within your training and ability
5. **Call Emergency Services** — If serious or life-threatening
6. **Document** — Complete incident report
7. **Notify Parents** — Appropriate communication

### Basic First Aid Steps

**DRABC Protocol:**
1. **D**anger — Check for danger
2. **R**esponse — Check for response
3. **A**irway — Ensure airway is open
4. **B**reathing — Check for breathing
5. **C**irculation — Check for circulation

### Calling Emergency Services

**Call for Ambulance (999) When:**
- Loss of consciousness
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe bleeding
- Head/neck/back injury suspected
- Severe allergic reaction
- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes
- Any life-threatening situation

**Information to Provide:**
- School name and address
- Phone number
- What happened
- Number of patients
- Condition of patient(s)
- Age of patient
- Any special circumstances

## Common Injuries and Illnesses

### Cuts, Scrapes, and Minor Wounds

**Treatment:**
1. Clean hands, put on gloves
2. Clean wound with water/antiseptic
3. Apply antibiotic ointment if available
4. Cover with adhesive bandage
5. Monitor for infection

**Parent Notification:**
- Minor wounds: Note home to parent
- Larger wounds: Phone call to parent

### Bruises and Minor Burns

**Bruises:**
- Apply cold pack
- Elevate if possible
- Monitor for complications

**Minor Burns (small, first degree):**
- Cool running water for 10-15 minutes
- Cover with clean, non-stick dressing
- Do NOT apply ice directly
- Do NOT break blisters
- Parent notification

**Serious Burns:**
- Call emergency services
- Cool with water
- Cover loosely
- Do NOT apply anything else
- Call parent immediately

### Sprains and Strains

**Initial Treatment (R.I.C.E.):**
- **R**est — Stop activity
- **I**ce — Apply cold pack
- **C**ompression — Wrap if appropriate
- **E**levation — Raise injured area
- Parent notification
- Refer to medical care if significant

### Nosebleeds

**Treatment:**
1. Sit leaning slightly forward
2. Pinch soft part of nose for 10 minutes
3. Do NOT tilt head back
4. Apply cold pack to bridge of nose
5. If bleeding continues, seek medical help

**When to Call Parents/Emergency:**
- Bleeding lasts more than 20 minutes
- Injury is serious
- Student is very distressed
- Underlying medical condition

### Asthma Attacks

**If Student Has Asthma:**
1. Help student use inhaler if available
2. Encourage slow breathing
3. Sit in comfortable position
4. Stay calm
5. Call for help if no improvement in 5 minutes
6. Call emergency services if severe

### Allergic Reactions

**Mild Reaction:**
- Rash, itching, mild swelling
- Monitor closely
- Contact parents
- May give antihistamine if authorized

**Severe Reaction (Anaphylaxis):**
- Difficulty breathing
- Swelling of face/lips/tongue
- Wheezing or difficulty swallowing
- Dizziness or fainting

**Immediate Action:**
1. Call emergency services (999)
2. Use EpiPen if available and trained
3. Call parent immediately
4. Monitor closely
5. Prepare to provide CPR if needed

### Seizures

**During Seizure:**
1. Stay calm
2. Clear area of dangerous objects
3. Do NOT restrain student
4. Do NOT put anything in mouth
5. Protect head
6. Note time seizure starts
7. Call for help

**After Seizure:**
1. Place in recovery position
2. Check breathing
3. Keep airway open
4. Do NOT give food/drink until fully alert
5. Call parent
6. Call ambulance if: first seizure, lasts >5 minutes, injury occurred, breathing doesn't return

### Fainting

**Immediate Action:**
1. Lay student flat on back
2. Elevate legs slightly
3. Loosen tight clothing
4. Ensure fresh air
5. Do NOT slap or shake
6. Call school nurse
7. Call parent

**After:**
- Rest before returning to class
- Monitor for additional symptoms
- Parent may be asked to pick up

### Head Injuries

**Any Head Injury:**
1. Assess seriousness
2. Apply cold pack if minor bump
3. Monitor for symptoms
4. Document incident

**Seek Emergency Care If:**
- Loss of consciousness
- Confusion or disorientation
- Vomiting
- Severe headache
- Unequal pupils
- Clear fluid from ears/nose
- Student not acting normally

## Reporting and Documentation

### Incident Report Form

**Required For:**
- All injuries requiring first aid
- All illnesses requiring parent pick-up
- All incidents requiring emergency services
- Any incident with potential future concern

**Information Recorded:**
- Student name, class, age
- Date and time of incident
- Location of incident
- Description of what happened
- Nature of injury/illness
- First aid provided
- Time of parent notification
- Parent response
- Any follow-up needed

### Documentation Storage

**Maintained By:**
- School nurse
- Confidential health records
- Separate from academic records
- Kept until student graduates or leaves school

### Communication to Parents

**For Minor Incidents:**
- Note home explaining incident
- Description of care provided
- Signs to watch for
- Contact information for questions

**For Serious Incidents:**
- Phone call immediately
- Detailed explanation
- Arrangements for student care
- Follow-up communication

## Parent Notification

### When Parents Are Called

**Immediate Call Required:**
- Head injury with any symptoms
- Severe allergic reaction
- Asthma attack not improving
- Seizure
- Any injury needing stitches
- Fracture or suspected fracture
- High fever
- Any situation requiring emergency services

**Same-Day Notification:**
- Any injury requiring more than basic first aid
- Eye injuries
- Possible broken bones
- Severe pain
- Any situation causing concern

### Information Shared

**When Calling Parents:**
- What happened
- What was done
- Current condition
- What parent needs to do
- Ask about allergies/medical conditions

## Hygiene and Infection Control

### Universal Precautions

**All Staff Use:**
- Disposable gloves for any contact with body fluids
- Hand washing before and after care
- Proper disposal of contaminated materials
- Cleaning of contaminated surfaces
- Protection from exposure

### Handling Body Fluids

**Blood or Other Body Fluids:**
- Wear gloves
- Clean up with appropriate disinfectant
- Dispose of materials in biohazard bag
- Wash hands thoroughly after
- Report any exposure immediately
