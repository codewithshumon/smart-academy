## Policy Overview

### Purpose

Fire safety is critical at Smart Academy. This document establishes:

- **Prevention**: Measures to prevent fires
- **Detection**: Early warning systems
- **Preparation**: Regular drills and training
- **Response**: Clear evacuation procedures
- **Equipment**: Proper firefighting equipment

### Fire Safety Goals

1. Prevent all fires through proactive measures
2. Detect fires immediately if they occur
3. Evacuate everyone safely and quickly
4. Minimize property damage
5. Ensure everyone knows what to do

## Fire Prevention Measures

### General Safety

**School Maintains:**
- Clean and orderly premises
- No accumulated combustible materials
- Proper waste disposal
- Regular safety inspections
- Clear evacuation routes
- Proper electrical maintenance

### Electrical Safety

**Precautions:**
- Regular electrical inspections
- No overloaded circuits
- Proper use of extension cords (minimal)
- Equipment turned off when not in use
- No frayed cords or damaged equipment
- Professional repairs only

### Fire Hazards

**Eliminated or Controlled:**
- Flammable materials properly stored
- No smoking anywhere on campus
- Safe use of heat-producing equipment
- Laboratory safety protocols
- Kitchen safety protocols
- Proper storage of chemicals

### Housekeeping

**Fire Prevention Through:**
- Regular cleaning of all areas
- Proper storage of materials
- No blocked exits or corridors
- Clean cooking areas
- Proper disposal of oily rags
- Regular removal of combustible waste

## Evacuation Procedures

### When Fire Alarm Sounds

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **STOP** all activities immediately
2. **LEAVE** everything behind (don't collect belongings)
3. **FOLLOW** teacher instructions
4. **LINE UP** quickly and quietly
5. **WALK** â€” do not run
6. **USE** designated exit route
7. **LEAVE** building by nearest safe exit

### Classroom Evacuation

**Teachers Will:**
1. Close windows (if time permits)
2. Turn off lights (if time permits)
3. Close door (unlocked)
4. Take attendance sheet
5. Lead students to nearest safe exit
6. Account for all students
7. Report any missing students
8. Stay with class at assembly point

**Students Will:**
1. Stand up quickly and quietly
2. Form line at door
3. Follow teacher
4. Walk, don't run
5. No talking
6. Stay together
7. Go directly to assembly point

### Hallway/Stairwell Procedures

**In Hallways:**
- Keep to the right
- Walk briskly but don't run
- No talking
- Follow exit signs
- Don't push or shove
- Help younger students

**In Stairwells:**
- Use handrails
- One step at a time
- Keep moving
- No talking
- Don't skip steps
- No stopping on stairs

### Special Situations

**If Fire Blocks Exit:**
- Use alternate exit
- Teacher will lead to backup route
- Stay calm
- Follow teacher instructions

**If in Restroom:**
- Exit and join nearest class line
- Or go to assembly point if alone
- Report to teacher at assembly point

**If Separated from Class:**
- Leave building by nearest exit
- Go directly to assembly point
- Find teacher or administrator
- Report your location

**Location:** Main Playground (Field Area)

- Principal or designee

- Using clear, loud voice
- With class information

## What To Do In Case of Fire

### If You Discover Fire

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:**
1. Shout "FIRE!" loudly
2. Activate nearest fire alarm
3. Call emergency services (999)
4. Evacuate immediately
5. Do NOT try to fight fire unless:
   - Fire is small
   - You are trained
   - You have escape route
   - You are confident

### If Your Clothing Catches Fire

**STOP, DROP, and ROLL:**
1. **STOP** moving
2. **DROP** to the ground
3. **COVER** face with hands
4. **ROLL** back and forth
5. **ROLL** until flames are out

**Do NOT:**
- Run (this feeds the fire)
- Try to beat out flames with hands
- Panic

### If Trapped in Room

**If You Cannot Evacuate:**
1. Close door between you and fire
2. Seal cracks with cloth if possible
3. Go to window
4. Signal for help
5. Call emergency services (999) if possible
6. DO NOT open door if hot
7. DO NOT jump unless absolutely necessary

### If There Is Smoke

**In Smoke:**
- Stay low (crawl)
- Cover mouth and nose with cloth
- Hold breath briefly when possible
- Move to exit
- Avoid smoke inhalation

## All Clear Signal

### Returning to Building

**When Safe to Return:**
1. Fire department gives all-clear
2. Principal signals return
3. Classes return in orderly manner
4. Teachers account for students again
5. Report any problems
6. Return to normal activities

**Do NOT Return Until:**
- All-clear signal given
- Authorized by principal or fire department
- Building deemed safe

## Post-Fire Procedures

### After Fire Is Out

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Account for all students and staff
2. Provide first aid if needed
3. Contact parents if needed
4. Secure building
5. Cooperate with fire department
6. Document everything
7. Arrange alternative location if needed

### Investigation

**Fire Department Will:**
- Investigate cause
- Determine origin
- Provide report

**School Will:**
- Cooperate fully
- Document damage
- Review procedures
- Make improvements as needed

### Recovery

**If Building Damaged:**
- Alternative arrangements made
- Parents notified of location changes
- Continuity of education maintained
- Counseling provided if needed
