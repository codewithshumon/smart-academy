## Overview

Professional development at Smart Academy is continuous, collaborative, and focused on improving teaching and learning. This guide outlines opportunities, requirements, and pathways for professional growth as an educator at Smart Academy.

## Philosophy of Professional Development

### Islamic Perspective

**Seeking Knowledge:**
- Seeking knowledge is obligation on every Muslim (Hadith)
- Continuous learning is Islamic value
- Excellence (Ihsan) in teaching requires ongoing growth
- Knowledge is blessing to be shared, not hoarded

**Teaching as Worship:**
- Professional development is form of worship when done with right intention
- Improving teaching skills is service to Allah and community
- Humility: always more to learn
- Gratitude for opportunity to learn and grow

**Stewardship of Talent:**
- Teaching abilities are trust (Amanah) from Allah
- Must develop and refine skills
- Responsible for staying current in field
- Accountable for quality of teaching

### Learning Organization

**Smart Academy as Learning Community:**
- Everyone learns: students, teachers, administrators
- Continuous improvement culture
- Learning from research, practice, and each other
- Reflection and feedback valued

## Observation and Feedback

### Purpose of Observation

**Growth and Improvement:**
- Not evaluation, but learning
- Opportunity for reflective practice
- Feedback to inform teaching
- Celebration of strengths and areas for growth

### Types of Observation

**Peer Observation:**
- Teachers observe each other
- Focus on specific practices or strategies
- Non-evaluative, learning-focused
- Pre-observation conference, observation, post-observation discussion

**Administrative Observation:**
- Formal observations by administration
- Part of evaluation process
- Feedback for growth and accountability
- Multiple observations throughout year

**Self-Observation:**
- Video recording of own teaching
- Reflection on practice
- Identification of strengths and areas for growth
- Goal setting

### Observation Protocol

**Pre-Observation:**
- Clarify purpose and focus
- Discuss lesson context and students
- Identify what to look for
- Establish observation norms

**Observation:**
- Observe actively and take notes
- Focus on agreed-upon areas
- Notice specific behaviors and evidence
- Remain non-judgmental

**Post-Observation:**
- Reflective discussion
- Teacher reflects first
- Observer shares observations
- Specific feedback with examples
- Collaborative problem-solving
- Action plan for growth

## Creating Your Professional Development Plan

### Steps

**1. Self-Assessment:**
- Reflect on current practice
- Identify strengths and areas for growth
- Consider feedback from others
- Review student outcomes

**2. Set Goals:**
- SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- 1-3 major goals for year
- Align with school goals and personal interests
- Include both pedagogical and content goals

**3. Plan Activities:**
- Identify professional development opportunities
- Prioritize based on goals and availability
- Plan timeline and resources needed
- Seek approval and support as needed

**4. Implement:**
- Participate actively in learning
- Apply new learning in classroom
- Document implementation and results
- Seek feedback and support

**5. Reflect and Adjust:**
- Regular reflection on progress
- Adjust plan as needed
- Celebrate successes
- Revise goals as appropriate

**Sample Goals:**
- "By January, implement at least three differentiated instruction strategies in my mathematics lessons"
- "This year, complete online course on technology integration and apply at least two new tools"
- "Develop expertise in teaching reading comprehension and share strategies with team"
