# Teaching Guidelines

## Best Practices for Effective Instruction

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 11, 2026

## Overview

These guidelines provide research-informed best practices for teaching at Smart Academy. They are designed to help teachers deliver high-quality instruction that integrates Islamic values with effective pedagogical approaches.

## Core Teaching Principles

### 1. Start With Intention (Niyyah)

**Before Every Class:**
- Make intention to please Allah through teaching
- Seek reward from Allah for educating His creation
- Remember: Teaching is worship when done with proper intention

**Practical Application:**
- Quick dua before entering classroom
- Remind yourself of purpose in difficult moments
- Maintain sincerity in interactions with students

### 2. Know Your Students

**Understanding Learners:**
- Learn names quickly (first week)
- Understand students' backgrounds and experiences
- Recognize prior knowledge and skills
- Identify interests and motivations
- Be aware of learning challenges

**At Smart Academy:**
- Many students from rural backgrounds
- Varying levels of English proficiency
- Diverse prior educational experiences
- 25% scholarship students with unique challenges

### 3. Create Positive Learning Environment

**Physical Environment:**
- Organized, clean classroom
- Student work displayed
- Islamic reminders present
- Resources accessible

**Emotional Environment:**
- Students feel safe and valued
- Mistakes are learning opportunities
- Effort is recognized and celebrated
- Kindness and respect emphasized

**Islamic Environment:**
- Begin with Bismillah
- Islamic reminders and duas
- Modeling of Islamic character
- Respect in all interactions

### 4. Make Learning Active and Engaging

**Active Learning Strategies:**
- Think-pair-share
- Group discussions and projects
- Hands-on activities
- Movement and interaction
- Questioning and inquiry

**Student Engagement:**
- Connect to students' lives and interests
- Use variety in teaching methods
- Incorporate movement and activity
- Provide choices when appropriate
- Make learning meaningful and relevant

### 5. Teach With Clarity

**Clear Explanations:**
- Use simple, direct language
- Break complex ideas into steps
- Provide examples and non-examples
- Check for understanding frequently
- Adjust explanations based on student response

**Clear Instructions:**
- Step-by-step directions
- Model what you expect
- Check that students understand before starting
- Provide written instructions for complex tasks

### 6. Assess Understanding and Adjust

**Continuous Assessment:**
- Ask questions throughout lesson
- Observe student work
- Use quick checks (thumbs up/down, exit tickets)
- Review completed work
- Listen to student discussions

**Responsive Teaching:**
- Reteach if many students don't understand
- Provide additional support for struggling students
- Extend learning for advanced students
- Adjust pace based on student needs
- Be flexible and adaptive

## Lesson Structure

### Effective Lesson Framework

**1. Introduction/Hook (5-10 minutes)**
- Begin with Bismillah and dua
- Activate prior knowledge
- Introduce learning objectives clearly
- Engage interest with question, problem, or connection

**2. Direct Instruction/I Do (10-15 minutes)**
- Model new skill or explain new concept
- Think aloud to show thinking process
- Provide clear examples
- Check for understanding

**3. Guided Practice/We Do (10-15 minutes)**
- Practice together with students
- Scaffold support gradually
- Correct misconceptions immediately
- Build confidence

**4. Independent Practice/You Do (15-20 minutes)**
- Students practice independently or in pairs/groups
- Teacher circulates and provides feedback
- Differentiate support as needed

**5. Closure (5-10 minutes)**
- Review key learning
- Check understanding (quick assessment)
- Connect to Islamic understanding/perspective
- Preview next lesson
- End with dua

**Total lesson time: 40-45 minutes (adjust based on grade level)**

## Teaching Strategies by Subject

### English Language Teaching

**Developing Language Skills:**

**Reading:**
- Teach phonics and decoding systematically
- Build vocabulary through exposure and direct instruction
- Comprehension strategies: predict, question, clarify, summarize
- Read aloud to model fluency and expression
- Provide reading materials at various levels

**Writing:**
- Model writing process: plan, draft, revise, edit, publish
- Teach sentence structure and paragraph organization
- Provide opportunities for authentic writing
- Give specific feedback on content and mechanics
- Celebrate published work

**Speaking and Listening:**
- Provide many opportunities for oral practice
- Teach discussion skills: taking turns, building on ideas
- Use pair and group work frequently
- Teach presentation skills
- Model active listening

**Islamic Integration:**
- Select texts with Islamic themes or moral lessons
- Include Muslim authors and characters
- Teach about communication as trust (Amanah)
- Use Islamic examples in grammar and vocabulary exercises

### Mathematics Teaching

**Developing Mathematical Understanding:**

**Conceptual Understanding:**
- Use concrete manipulatives before abstract symbols
- Teach multiple strategies for solving problems
- Connect mathematical ideas to real life
- Help students see patterns and relationships
- Encourage mathematical thinking, not just calculation

**Procedural Fluency:**
- Practice basic facts for automaticity
- Teach standard algorithms with understanding
- Provide varied practice
- Use games and activities for motivation
- Connect procedures to concepts

**Problem Solving:**
- Teach problem-solving strategies systematically
- Encourage multiple solution methods
- Provide challenging, non-routine problems
- Have students explain their thinking
- Persevere through difficult problems

**Islamic Integration:**
- Highlight Muslim contributions to mathematics
- Show patterns in creation (geometric patterns, symmetry)
- Teach mathematical ethics: accuracy, fairness, justice
- Connect to concepts like Zakat (calculation), inheritance division

### Science Teaching

**Developing Scientific Understanding:**

**Scientific Inquiry:**
- Teach scientific method: observe, question, hypothesize, test, conclude
- Hands-on experiments and investigations
- Emphasize observation and evidence
- Encourage questioning and curiosity
- Connect science to technology and society

**Science Knowledge:**
- Build understanding of key concepts
- Connect concepts across scientific fields
- Use current events and discoveries
- Address misconceptions directly
- Build vocabulary systematically

**Laboratory Work:**
- Teach safety procedures explicitly
- Develop investigation skills
- Emphasize careful observation and recording
- Draw conclusions from evidence
- Communicate findings clearly

**Islamic Integration:**
- Reflect on signs of Allah in creation (Ayat)
- Teach about Muslim scientists and contributions
- Stewardship of creation (Khalifah)
- Ethics in science and technology
- Quranic scientific signs (relevant to topic)

### Social Studies Teaching

**Developing Social Understanding:**

**Historical Thinking:**
- Teach cause and effect
- Multiple perspectives on events
- Use primary sources when appropriate
- Connect past to present
- Historical empathy (understanding people in context)

**Geographic Understanding:**
- Maps and spatial reasoning
- Human-environment interaction
- Cultural awareness and respect
- Global connections and interdependence
- Geographic diversity of Muslim world

**Civics and Citizenship:**
- Rights and responsibilities
- Community and civic participation
- Islamic governance principles (Shura, justice)
- Respect for law and order
- Service to community

**Islamic Integration:**
- Islamic history and civilization
- Geographic spread of Islam
- Islamic contributions to world civilization
- Islamic principles of governance and justice
- Respect for all people (Quranic teaching)

### Islamic Studies Teaching

**Developing Islamic Understanding:**

**Quranic Studies:**
- Proper Tajweed and pronunciation
- Understanding of meaning (not just memorization)
- Connection of Quranic teachings to life
- Reflection and application
- Love and reverence for Quran

**Hadith Studies:**
- Authentic Hadith from reliable sources
- Understanding context and occasion of revelation
- Application to contemporary life
- Love and respect for Prophet (peace be upon him)
- Following Sunnah in daily life

**Fiqh (Islamic Jurisprudence):**
- Practical rulings for daily life
- Understanding wisdom behind rulings
- Ease and facility in Islam (not hardship)
- Respect for different scholarly opinions
- Application to real-life situations

**Islamic History:**
- Story of Prophet and companions
- Golden age of Islamic civilization
- Contributions to world knowledge
- Lessons from historical events
- Pride in Islamic heritage

**Arabic Language:**
- Focus on Quranic Arabic
- Reading with understanding
- Basic speaking and writing
- Grammar and vocabulary
- Connection to Islamic worship

**Teaching Approach:**
- Make it practical and relevant to students' lives
- Use stories and examples
- Model Islamic character
- Create love for Islam, not just fear of punishment
- Connect to students' experiences and questions

## Differentiation Strategies

### For Struggling Learners

**Scaffolding:**
- Break tasks into smaller steps
- Provide graphic organizers
- Model expected performance
- Provide examples and sentence starters
- Use peer support strategically

**Support:**
- Additional practice opportunities
- Visual supports and reminders
- Simplified instructions
- Extended time when appropriate
- One-on-one or small group instruction

### For Advanced Learners

**Extension:**
- More complex tasks and problems
- Open-ended projects and investigations
- Greater depth and abstraction
- Leadership roles in class
- Independent study opportunities

**Acceleration:**
- Introduce advanced concepts
- Skip mastered content
- Faster pacing when appropriate
- Cross-grade grouping for subjects

### For English Language Learners

**Support:**
- Visual aids and gestures
- Simplified language
- Extra processing time
- First language support (Bengali) when helpful
- Vocabulary instruction with visuals

### For Students with Special Needs

**Consult:**
- Learning Support Specialist
- Individual Education Plans (IEPs) when applicable
- Specific accommodations and modifications
- Regular communication with support staff
- Parent collaboration

## Classroom Management

### Positive Behavior Management

**Prevention:**
- Clear expectations established and taught
- Engaging, well-paced lessons
- Positive relationships with students
- Consistent routines
- Respectful, dignified treatment

**Promotion:**
- Praise specific behaviors
- Positive recognition systems
- Islamic character highlighted
- Good choices celebrated
- Role models acknowledged

**Correction:**
- Private whenever possible
- Clear, specific feedback
- Focus on behavior, not person
- Teach replacement behavior
- Logical consequences when appropriate

### Building Relationships

**With Students:**
- Learn and use names
- Show interest in their lives
- Listen to their concerns
- Be fair and consistent
- Show you care about them

**Islamic Approach:**
- Model Prophetic kindness
- Show mercy and compassion
- Treat with dignity and respect
- Forgive mistakes and move forward
- Make dua for them

## Assessment Practices

### Formative Assessment (During Learning)

**Purpose:**
- Guide teaching: adjust based on student understanding
- Guide learning: students know what they know and don't know
- No-stakes: not for grading, for learning

**Strategies:**
- Observation during lessons
- Question and answer
- Quick quizzes and exit tickets
- Peer and self-assessment
- Practice work and homework

### Summative Assessment (End of Learning)

**Purpose:**
- Evaluate learning at end of unit/term
- Assign grades
- Communicate achievement to students and parents
- Inform future teaching

**Types:**
- Unit tests and quizzes
- Projects and presentations
- Written compositions
- Practical demonstrations
- End-of-term examinations

### Islamic Perspective on Assessment

**Principles:**
- Ihsan (excellence) in all work
- Honest evaluation: no cheating
- Effort matters alongside outcome
- Intention (Niyyah) matters
- Self-reflection (Muhasabah)
- Knowledge is blessing from Allah, not personal achievement

## Homework Guidelines

### Purpose of Homework

**Appropriate Goals:**
- Reinforce classroom learning
- Develop independent study skills
- Involve parents in learning
- Prepare for next lesson

### Homework Principles

**Effective Homework:**
- Clear purpose connected to learning
- Reasonable amount for age/grade
- Clear instructions
- Can be completed independently (after early grades)
- Provides feedback, not just grades

**Amount Guidelines:**
- PlayGroup-KG: Minimal, hands-on activities
- Class 1-3: 30-45 minutes per day
- Class 4-6: 60-75 minutes per day
- Class 7-9: 75-90 minutes per day

### Communication with Parents

**About Homework:**
- Communicate expectations clearly
- Provide guidance on how to support
- Encourage, don't do the work for child
- Communicate concerns about homework completion

## Technology Integration

### Purposeful Technology Use

**Effective Integration:**
- Technology enhances learning, not replaces teacher
- Used when it adds value to lesson
- Supports learning objectives
- Develops digital literacy skills
- Balanced with non-technology methods

### Examples of Effective Use

**Whole Class:**
- Projector for visual aids and videos
- Interactive simulations
- Virtual field trips
- Demonstrations

**Individual/Group:**
- Research projects
- Educational software and apps
- Creative projects (presentations, videos)
- Skill practice and reinforcement

### Islamic Guidelines for Technology

**Responsible Use:**
- Avoid inappropriate content
- Balance screen time with other activities
- Teach digital citizenship
- Avoid distraction and time-wasting
- Use skills for beneficial purposes

## Working With Parents

### Communication Principles

**Partnership Approach:**
- Parents are primary educators
- School supports parents in education
- Shared goals for child's development
- Regular, honest communication

### Effective Communication

**Best Practices:**
- Share good news, not just problems
- Be specific, not vague
- Focus on solutions, not blame
- Listen to parents' perspectives
- Respond to concerns promptly

### Addressing Concerns

**When Parents Have Concerns:**
- Listen fully before responding
- Acknowledge their feelings
- Explain your perspective calmly
- Focus on child's best interests
- Involve administration if needed

## Professional Growth

### Continuous Improvement

**Reflective Practice:**
- Reflect after each lesson: what worked, what didn't?
- Seek feedback from colleagues and mentors
- Be open to suggestions
- Try new approaches
- Learn from mistakes

**Professional Development:**
- Engage fully in school-based training
- Seek out additional learning opportunities
- Read professional literature
- Collaborate with colleagues
- Stay current in your subject area

## Conclusion

These guidelines provide a foundation for effective teaching at Smart Academy. Remember: teaching is both art and science. Use these as principles, not rigid rules. Adapt them to your context, your students, and your subject area.

**Most Important:**
- Sincere intention to please Allah
- Love and care for your students
- Continuous learning and improvement
- Modeling of Islamic character

**May Allah bless your teaching and make it a source of goodness.**
