# Application Process

## How to Apply for Positions

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** January 11, 2026

---

## Overview

Smart Academy seeks qualified, committed individuals who share our mission of providing quality education integrated with Islamic values. This guide outlines our application and hiring process.

---

## Before You Apply

### Review Requirements

**Check Position Requirements:**
- Read the job posting carefully
- Ensure you meet minimum qualifications
- Understand the responsibilities
- Consider if the position aligns with your skills and interests
- Confirm you are committed to Islamic values and our mission

**Self-Assessment:**
- **Qualifications:** Do you meet educational and experience requirements?
- **Skills:** Do you have the necessary skills and competencies?
- **Values:** Are you committed to Islamic values and character?
- **Interest:** Are you genuinely interested in this type of position?
- **Availability:** Can you commit to the work schedule and responsibilities?

### Research Smart Academy

**Learn About Us:**
- Review our website and materials
- Understand our mission and values
- Familiarize yourself with our approach
- Consider if you align with our educational philosophy

**Location and Context:**
- Rural Bangladesh setting
- Islamic school environment
- English medium education
- 25% scholarship students (serving diverse community)

## Application Materials

### Required Documents

**All Positions Must Include:**

**1. Cover Letter**
- **Purpose:** Introduce yourself and explain your interest
- **Content:**
  - Position applying for
  - Why you want to work at Smart Academy
  - How your qualifications match the position
  - Your understanding of and commitment to Islamic values
  - Availability and start date
- **Format:** Professional, concise (1 page)
- **Addressed to:** "Hiring Committee, Smart Academy"

**2. Curriculum Vitae (CV)/Resume**
- **Content:**
  - Personal information (name, contact, location)
  - Educational background (degrees, institutions, dates)
  - Teaching/administrative experience (most recent first)
  - Certifications and qualifications
  - Skills and competencies
  - Professional development
  - References (or "References available upon request")
- **Format:** Clear, organized, error-free
- **Length:** Typically 2-3 pages

**3. Academic Certificates**
- Copies of all academic degrees
- Copies of transcripts (if applicable)
- Teaching certification documents
- Professional certifications

**4. Experience Documents**
- Experience certificates from previous employers
- Letters of recommendation (if available)
- Employment verification

**5. References**
- Contact information for 2-3 professional references
- Include:
  - Name
  - Position/title
  - Relationship (former supervisor, colleague, etc.)
  - Email and phone number
  - Permission to contact them

### Additional Documents for Specific Positions

**Teaching Positions:**
- **Teaching Philosophy Statement** (1-2 pages)
  - Your beliefs about teaching and learning
  - Your approach to classroom management
  - How you integrate Islamic values
  - Your views on student development

- **Sample Lesson Plan** (optional but recommended)
  - Well-structured lesson plan
  - Shows your teaching approach
  - Demonstrates ability to integrate Islamic values

**Islamic Studies Positions:**
- **Islamic Education Background**
  - Details of Islamic education (Alim, Dawra Hadith, etc.)
  - Hifz certificate (if applicable)
  - Tajweed certification
  - Other Islamic qualifications

- **References from Islamic Scholars/Teachers** (if available)
  - Attestation of Islamic character and knowledge
  - Recommendation from Islamic institution

**Administrative Positions:**
- **Leadership Experience Documentation**
  - Description of previous leadership roles
  - Achievements and initiatives
  - Management philosophy

## Preparing Your Application

### Writing Your Cover Letter

**Structure:**

**Header:**
```
Your Name
Your Address
Your Email
Your Phone Number
Date

Hiring Committee
Smart Academy
[School Address]
```

**Salutation:**
"Assalamu Alaikum," or "Dear Hiring Committee,"

**Opening Paragraph:**
- State the position you're applying for
- Where you heard about the opening
- Brief statement of your interest

**Body Paragraphs (2-3):**
- Your qualifications for the position
- Relevant experience and achievements
- Your understanding of and commitment to Smart Academy's mission
- How you integrate Islamic values in your work
- Specific skills or strengths relevant to the position

**Closing Paragraph:**
- Reiterate your interest
- Mention your availability for interview
- Thank the committee for consideration
- Include your contact information

**Closing:**
"Wa Salaam," or "Sincerely," followed by your name

**Tips:**
- Be specific, not vague
- Provide examples of your achievements
- Show enthusiasm for the position and mission
- Proofread carefully for errors
- Keep to one page

### Preparing Your CV/Resume

**Format:**

**Header:**
- Name (prominent)
- Contact information (email, phone)
- Location (city)

**Education:**
- Degree(s), institution(s), graduation year(s)
- Include relevant certifications
- Mention academic achievements if significant

**Experience:**
- Most recent first
- Job title, institution, dates
- Bullet points describing responsibilities and achievements
- Quantify when possible (e.g., "Taught 25 students," "Improved test scores by 15%")

**Skills:**
- Teaching methods and strategies
- Technology skills
- Language proficiency
- Other relevant skills

**Professional Development:**
- Workshops, courses, certifications
- Conferences attended
- Publications or presentations (if applicable)

**References:**
- List 2-3 professional references
- Or state "References available upon request"

**Tips:**
- Use clear formatting and consistent style
- Be honest (don't exaggerate)
- Tailor to the position
- Proofread multiple times
- Keep to 2-3 pages
- Save as PDF before sending

### How to Apply

**Primary Method: Email**

**Send to:** careers@smartacademy.edu.bd

**Subject Line:**
Format: "Position Title - Your Name"
Example: "English Teacher - Ahmed Rahman"

**Email Body:**
```
Assalamu Alaikum,

Please find attached my application materials for the [Position Title] position at Smart Academy.

Attached:
1. Cover letter
2. CV/Resume
3. Academic certificates
4. Experience certificates
5. References

Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to the opportunity to contribute to Smart Academy's mission.

Wa Salaam,
[Your Full Name]
[Your Phone Number]
```

**Attachments:**
- All documents as PDF files preferred
- Name files clearly: "CoverLetter_[YourName]", "CV_[YourName]", etc.
- Ensure all files are included

**Alternative Methods:**
- **In-Person:** Submit to main office during office hours
- **Mail:** Send to school address (ensure arrives before deadline)

### Application Deadline

**Respect Deadlines:**
- Applications must be received by deadline date
- Late applications typically not considered
- Plan ahead to ensure timely submission

**Deadline Types:**
- **Fixed Deadline:** Specific date, no exceptions
- **Rolling Admission:** Applications reviewed as received, until position filled
- **Until Filled:** No specific deadline, position open until filled

**Before Deadline:**
- Submit well before deadline if possible
- Ensure you have time to gather all documents
- Allow time for email delivery and confirmation

## Selection Process

### Overview

**Smart Academy's Selection Process:**

**Stage 1: Application Screening**
- Applications reviewed by hiring committee
- Candidates meeting basic requirements identified
- Incomplete applications typically not considered

**Stage 2: Initial Assessment**
- **Teaching Positions:** Written demonstration of subject knowledge and teaching approach
- **Administrative Positions:** Written assessment or task
- Some candidates may be eliminated at this stage

**Stage 3: Interview**
- Selected candidates invited for interview
- May include multiple rounds
- Panel interview with several interviewers
- Questions about qualifications, experience, approach, and Islamic values

**Stage 4: Teaching Demonstration** (for teaching positions)
- Candidates prepare and teach a sample lesson
- Observed by panel
- Opportunity to demonstrate teaching skills
- Follow-up questions and discussion

**Stage 5: Final Interview**
- For top candidates
- With Principal or senior administration
- Discuss vision, fit with school culture
- Opportunity for candidate to ask questions

**Stage 6: Reference and Background Checks**
- Professional references contacted
- Educational credentials verified
- Background check as appropriate

**Stage 7: Offer**
- Successful candidate receives offer
- Terms of employment discussed
- Contract provided
- Time given to consider and respond

### Timeline

**Typical Timeline:**

**Application Deadline:** [Date]
**Screening:** 1-2 weeks after deadline
**Initial Assessment:** 2-3 weeks after deadline
**Interviews:** 3-4 weeks after deadline
**Teaching Demonstrations:** 3-5 weeks after deadline
**Final Decision:** Within 1 week of final interviews
**Offer Extended:** Immediately after decision
**Response to Offer:** Typically within 1 week
**Onboarding:** Before academic year begins

**Note:** Timeline may vary based on position, number of applicants, and school needs.

## Interview Preparation

### Types of Interviews

**Initial Interview** (30-45 minutes)
- With department head or coordinator
- Focus on qualifications, experience, and basic fit
- Opportunity for you to ask initial questions

**Panel Interview** (45-60 minutes)
- With 2-3 interviewers
- More in-depth questions
- Various perspectives represented
- May include Islamic character questions

**Final Interview** (45-60 minutes)
- With Principal or senior administrator
- Focus on vision, leadership (if applicable), and cultural fit
- More detailed discussion of role and expectations

**Teaching Demonstration** (30-45 minutes)
- Teach a sample lesson to actual students or panel
- Topic provided in advance or you choose
- Demonstrate teaching skills and approach
- Follow-up discussion

### Common Interview Questions

**General Questions:**
- Tell us about yourself and your background.
- Why do you want to work at Smart Academy?
- What do you know about our school and mission?
- What are your strengths and weaknesses as an educator/professional?
- Describe a challenging situation you faced and how you handled it.

**Teaching-Specific Questions:**
- What is your teaching philosophy?
- How do you approach classroom management?
- How do you differentiate instruction for diverse learners?
- How do you assess student learning?
- How do you integrate technology in your teaching?
- How do you involve parents in their child's education?

**Islamic Values Questions:**
- How do you integrate Islamic values in your teaching/role?
- Describe how you model Islamic character.
- How would you handle a situation where Islamic principles conflict with common practice?
- What does Islamic education mean to you?
- How do you approach character development?

**Scenario Questions:**
- How would you handle a disruptive student?
- A parent is unhappy with their child's progress. How do you respond?
- You disagree with a colleague's approach. What do you do?
- Describe a lesson that didn't go well and what you learned.

### Interview Tips

**Before the Interview:**
- Research Smart Academy thoroughly
- Review your application materials
- Prepare answers to common questions
- Prepare questions to ask the interviewers
- Plan appropriate attire (professional, modest)

**During the Interview:**
- Arrive early (10-15 minutes)
- Begin with "Assalamu Alaikum"
- Maintain eye contact and good posture
- Listen carefully to questions
- Answer clearly and specifically
- Use examples from your experience
- Be honest (don't exaggerate)
- Show enthusiasm and genuine interest
- Demonstrate Islamic character in your demeanor
- Ask thoughtful questions

**Questions to Ask Interviewers:**
- What does success look like in this position?
- What are the biggest challenges facing this role/department?
- How would you describe the school culture?
- What opportunities are there for professional development?
- What are the next steps in the hiring process?
- When do you expect to make a decision?

**After the Interview:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Reiterate interest in the position
- Keep it brief and professional
- Wa Salaam or Sincerely

## Teaching Demonstration

### Purpose

The teaching demonstration allows us to see your:
- Teaching style and approach
- Ability to engage students
- Subject knowledge
- Classroom management
- Integration of Islamic values
- Ability to reflect and adapt

### Preparation

**Topic Selection:**
- Usually provided in advance
- May be your choice within guidelines
- Age/grade appropriate
- Allows you to demonstrate your strengths

**Lesson Planning:**
- Prepare detailed lesson plan
- Include learning objectives
- Plan engaging activities
- Prepare materials needed
- Include Islamic integration
- Plan for assessment

**Materials:**
- Bring all necessary materials
- Have backup if technology fails
- Prepare handouts if appropriate
- Organize for smooth delivery

### Delivery

**During the Lesson:**
- Begin with Bismillah
- Introduce yourself and lesson clearly
- Engage students/panel actively
- Demonstrate effective teaching strategies
- Show classroom management
- Integrate Islamic values naturally
- Manage time effectively
- Be flexible and adaptive

**After the Lesson:**
- Be prepared to discuss your lesson
- Explain your choices and rationale
- Reflect on what went well
- Identify what you would improve
- Show openness to feedback

## Reference and Background Checks

### References

**What We Check:**
- Confirmation of employment
- Qualifications and performance
- Strengths and areas for development
- Islamic character and values
- Ability to work with children/students
- Reliability and professionalism

**Reference Questions:**
- In what capacity did you know the candidate?
- Describe the candidate's responsibilities and performance.
- What are the candidate's strengths?
- What areas for improvement did you observe?
- How does the candidate interact with children/students/colleagues?
- Would you recommend this candidate for this position? Why or why not?
- Describe the candidate's character and values.

### Background Verification

**What We Verify:**
- Educational credentials
- Employment history
- Professional certifications
- Criminal background check (if required)
- Character references (including Islamic character)

## After the Interview

### Waiting Period

**Timeline:**
- Typically 1-2 weeks for decision
- May be longer for some positions
- You will be contacted regardless of decision

**During the Wait:**
- Continue your regular activities
- Don't contact repeatedly for updates
- Send one follow-up email if no response after 2 weeks
- Keep exploring other opportunities

### Decision Outcomes

**Offer:**
- Contacted by phone or email
- Offer details provided
- Time to consider (typically 1 week)
- Contract to review and sign
- Onboarding process initiated

**Waitlist:**
- Not selected but still being considered
- May be contacted if first choice declines
- No guarantee of offer
- Continue exploring other opportunities

**Not Selected:**
- Notification sent
- Brief feedback may be provided (not always)
- Don't be discouraged
- Consider applying for future positions

## Offer and Acceptance

### Receiving an Offer

**Offer Includes:**
- Position title and responsibilities
- Start date
- Salary and benefits
- Terms of employment
- Contract duration
- Response deadline

**Reviewing the Offer:**
- Read carefully and completely
- Ask questions about anything unclear
- Consider all terms carefully
- Discuss with family if appropriate
- Make informed decision

**Accepting the Offer:**
- Respond by deadline (email or phone)
- Sign and return contract
- Provide requested documentation
- Begin onboarding process

**Declining the Offer:**
- Respond by deadline (email or phone)
- Be professional and courteous
- Provide brief reason (optional)
- Maintain positive relationship for future
