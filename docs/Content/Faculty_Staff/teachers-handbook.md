## Daily Operations

### Daily Schedule

**Before School (7:45 - 8:30 AM):**
- Arrive and prepare classroom
- Review lesson plans and materials
- Greet arriving students
- Morning assembly preparation

**Morning Assembly (8:30 - 8:50 AM):**
- Attendance taken
- Quran recitation
- Short Islamic talk/reminder
- Announcements
- Morning Duas

**Teaching Blocks (8:50 AM - 2:30 PM):**
- Periods of 40-45 minutes
- Break times: 10:30-10:45 AM, 12:15-12:45 PM (lunch and Dhuhr prayer)
- Dismissal: 2:30 PM

**After School (2:30 - 4:00 PM):**
- Lesson planning
- Grading and assessment
- Meetings as scheduled
- Parent conferences (by appointment)
- Extra-curricular activities supervision

### Daily Responsibilities

**For Every Class:**
- Take attendance
- Begin with Bismillah
- Review previous learning
- Present new content clearly
- Engage students actively
- Assess understanding
- Assign homework when appropriate
- End with summary and reminders

**Ongoing Tasks:**
- Maintain classroom displays
- Update student records
- Communicate with parents as needed
- Participate in staff meetings
- Contribute to school improvement

## Teaching and Learning

### Curriculum Framework

**Subject Areas:**
- **Core Subjects:** English, Mathematics, Science, Bengali
- **Islamic Studies:** Quran, Hadith, Fiqh, Islamic History, Arabic
- **Additional Subjects:** Social Studies, ICT, Physical Education, Arts
- **STEAM Integration:** Projects spanning multiple subjects

**Curriculum Sources:**
- Cambridge Primary/Secondary frameworks (adapted)
- Bangladesh national curriculum requirements
- Islamic studies curriculum developed in-house
- Supplementary materials for enhanced learning

### Lesson Planning

**Required Components:**
- Learning objectives (what students will know/do)
- Islamic integration (connection to faith/values)
- Materials and resources needed
- Teaching procedures (step-by-step)
- Differentiation for diverse learners
- Assessment of learning
- Homework assignment (if applicable)

**Planning Resources:**
- Curriculum guides for each subject
- Textbooks and supplementary materials
- Online educational resources
- Collaboration with subject team
- Previous lesson reflections

### Teaching Strategies

**Recommended Approaches:**
- Active learning: Students engaged, not passive
- Questioning: Open-ended, higher-order questions
- Collaboration: Pair and group work
- Real-world connections: Learning applied to life
- Technology integration: Where appropriate and effective
- Islamic integration: Values, examples, connections

**What Works at Smart Academy:**
- Start with students' prior knowledge
- Use concrete examples before abstract concepts
- Check for understanding frequently
- Provide immediate feedback
- Celebrate effort and progress
- Connect learning to students' lives and faith

### Assessment

**Types of Assessment:**

**Formative (Ongoing):**
- Question and answer during lessons
- Observations of student work
- Quick quizzes and exit tickets
- Peer and self-assessment
- Informal checks for understanding

**Summative (End of Unit/Term):**
- Unit tests and quizzes
- Projects and presentations
- Written assignments
- Practical assessments (science, ICT)
- End-of-term examinations

**Recording and Reporting:**
- Maintain gradebook accurately
- Provide specific feedback, not just scores
- Communicate progress to parents regularly
- Use data to inform teaching

### Communication with Parents

- Monthly progress reports
- Parent-teacher conferences

- Written notes for important information

## Policies and Procedures

### Attendance and Punctuality

**Student Attendance:**
- Take attendance at start of each class
- Record in attendance register
- Follow up on absences
- Notify administration of pattern of absences
- Award attendance certificates for excellent attendance

**Teacher Attendance:**
- Arrive before students
- Be punctual for all duties
- Planned absences: Submit lesson plans in advance
- Emergency absences: Notify ASAP

### Assessment and Grading

**Grading Scale:**

| Grade | Percentage | Description |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| A+ | 80%+ | Excellent |
| A | 70-79% | Very Good |
| A- | 60-69% | Good |
| B | 50-59% | Satisfactory |
| C | 40-49% | Fair |
| D | 33-39% | Poor (Pass) |
| F | Below 33% | Fail |

**Assessment Schedule:**
- Weekly: Formative assessments
- Monthly: Unit tests/quizzes
- Mid-term: Comprehensive assessments (January)
- Final: End-of-year examinations (May/June)

**Recording:**
- Maintain accurate gradebook
- Enter grades in school system
- Keep samples of student work
- Provide feedback promptly

### Homework Policy

**Purpose:**
- Reinforce classroom learning
- Develop study habits and independence
- Allow for practice and application
- Inform parents about learning

**Guidelines:**
- Appropriate amount for age/grade:
  - PlayGroup-KG: Minimal, hands-on activities
  - Class 1-3: 30-45 minutes
  - Class 4-6: 60-75 minutes
  - Class 7-9: 75-90 minutes
- Meaningful, not busywork
- Clear instructions and expectations
- Feedback provided

### Discipline Policy

**Positive Approach:**
- Clear expectations established
- Positive reinforcement for good behavior
- Islamic character focus
- Restorative rather than purely punitive

**When Misbehavior Occurs:**
1. Reminder/warning (private)
2. Loss of privilege
3. Reflection activity
4. Parent contact
5. Administrator involvement

**Serious Misbehavior:**
- Bullying: Zero tolerance
- Cheating/dishonesty: Serious consequence
- Physical harm: Immediate administrator involvement
- Disrespect to Islam: Serious consequence and parent meeting

### Health and Safety

**Emergency Procedures:**
- Fire drills: [Schedule and procedures]
- Lockdown drills: [Procedures]
- Medical emergencies: Contact school nurse/admin
- Natural disasters: Follow school protocol

**Student Safety:**
- Never leave students unsupervised
- Report safety concerns immediately
- Follow proper dismissal procedures
- Field trip safety protocols

**Child Protection:**
- Mandatory reporter for suspected abuse
- Follow child protection policy
- Maintain appropriate boundaries
- Report concerns to administration
