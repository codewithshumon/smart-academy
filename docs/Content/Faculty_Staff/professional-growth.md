## Individual Growth Planning

### Creating Your Professional Development Plan

**Step 1: Self-Reflection**
- Assess current strengths and areas for growth
- Consider career goals
- Reflect on feedback from evaluations and observations
- Identify interests and passions

**Step 2: Set Goals**
- **Short-term (1 year):** Specific skills or knowledge to develop
- **Medium-term (2-3 years):** Certifications, advanced roles
- **Long-term (5+ years):** Career advancement, advanced degrees

**SMART Goals:**
- **Specific:** Clear and focused
- **Measurable:** Can assess achievement
- **Achievable:** Realistic given resources and time
- **Relevant:** Aligned with role and interests
- **Time-bound:** Specific timeline

**Examples:**
- "This year, I will implement at least three differentiated instruction strategies in my mathematics lessons and assess their impact on student engagement and learning."
- "Within two years, I will complete a Master's degree in Education with focus on curriculum development."
- "This year, I will develop expertise in teaching reading comprehension and present strategies to my team."

**Step 3: Identify Resources and Activities**
- Professional development opportunities available
- Mentors and collaborators
- Books and resources to read
- Courses or workshops to attend
- Projects to lead or participate in

**Step 4: Implement and Document**
- Participate in learning activities
- Apply learning to practice
- Document activities and reflections
- Collect evidence of growth

**Step 5: Reflect and Revise**
- Regular reflection on progress
- Adjust plan as needed
- Celebrate successes
- Revise goals for next cycle

- Small groups of staff
- Regular meetings
- Focused on specific topics or questions
- Data-driven and action-oriented

**Activities:**
- Analyzing student work
- Lesson study

- Sharing strategies and resources

### Peer Observation

**Purpose:** Learning from and with colleagues

**Process:**
- Pre-observation conference: Purpose and focus
- Observation: Classroom visit with specific focus
- Post-observation conference: Reflection and feedback

**Benefits:**
- See different teaching styles
- Get new ideas
- Receive feedback
- Build collaboration
- Develop shared understanding of effective practice

### Collaborative Planning

**Team Planning:**
