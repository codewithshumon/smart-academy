## Executive Summary

Arabic, the language of the Quran and Islamic civilization, holds special significance at Smart Academy. Our Arabic program prioritizes Quranic understanding while developing comprehensive language skills for reading classical Islamic texts and communicating in Modern Standard Arabic.

### Key Features

**Quranic Arabic Priority**
- Alphabet and pronunciation (Tajweed basics)
- Quranic vocabulary (most common 500 words)
- Understanding Quranic verses
- Translation and interpretation basics
- Memorization with comprehension

**Classical Arabic Foundation**
- Grammar: Nahw (النحو) - syntax
- Morphology: Sarf (الصرف) - word patterns
- Reading classical Islamic texts
- Hadith understanding
- Islamic literature

**Communication Skills**
- Modern Standard Arabic (MSA)
- Everyday vocabulary
- Conversation practice
- Reading and writing
- Listening comprehension

**Cultural and Artistic**
- Arabic calligraphy (Khatt)
- Islamic art through Arabic
- Arabic poetry appreciation
- Historical context
- Contemporary Arab world

**Progressive Development**
- PlayGroup-KG2: Letters, sounds, simple words
- Class 1-5: Reading fluency, basic grammar, Quranic vocabulary
- Class 6-9: Grammar mastery, classical texts, communication

## Vision and Philosophy

### Vision Statement

To develop students who can read and understand the Quran in its original Arabic language, appreciate Islamic classical literature, and communicate effectively in Arabic, thereby deepening their connection to Islam and its rich intellectual heritage.

### Educational Philosophy

**Arabic as Sacred Language**

Arabic holds unique status as the language chosen by Allah for His final revelation. Learning Arabic is therefore both a linguistic endeavor and an act of worship, bringing students closer to understanding Allah's words directly.

> "Indeed, We have sent it down as an Arabic Quran that you might understand." (Quran 12:2)

**Core Principles**

1. **Quranic Understanding as Primary Goal**
   - Direct access to Quran without translation
   - Understanding prayer (Salah) meanings
   - Comprehending du'as and adhkar
   - Deepening spiritual connection

2. **Classical Islamic Heritage**
   - Reading Hadith in Arabic
   - Understanding fiqh texts
   - Accessing Islamic scholarship
   - Connecting to 1400+ years of knowledge

3. **Practical Communication**
   - Conversational Arabic for hajj/umrah

   - Modern Standard Arabic literacy
   - Professional opportunities

4. **Cultural Appreciation**
   - Islamic civilization achievements
   - Arabic poetry and literature
   - Calligraphy as art form
   - Arab-Islamic culture

5. **Systematic and Progressive**
   - Building from fundamentals
   - Age-appropriate instruction
   - Scaffolded learning
   - Continuous review and practice

### Methodology

**Grammar-Translation Method** (for classical texts)

**Integrated Skills**

## Arabic: Language of the Quran

### Divine Selection

Allah chose Arabic as the language of the final revelation:

> "And indeed, the Quran is the revelation of the Lord of the worlds. The Trustworthy Spirit has brought it down upon your heart, [O Muhammad] - that you may be of the warners - in a clear Arabic language." (Quran 26:192-195)

### Quranic Arabic Characteristics

**Eloquence and Beauty**
- Unmatched linguistic beauty
- Rhythmic and poetic
- Precise and profound
- Miraculous (I'jaz)

**Linguistic Perfection**
- Every word chosen carefully
- Multiple layers of meaning
- Grammatically flawless
- Rhetorical excellence

**Preservation**
- Quran preserved in original language
- No changes in 1400+ years
- Arabic grammar preserved through Quran
- Living connection to Prophet's time

### Understanding the Quran in Arabic

**Why Learn Quranic Arabic?**

1. **Direct Connection to Allah's Words**
   - No intermediary translation
   - Original meanings and nuances
   - Emotional and spiritual impact
   - Personal relationship with Quran

2. **Prayer Understanding**
   - Know what you're saying in Salah
   - Conscious worship
   - Greater khushu (concentration)
   - Deeper connection to Allah

3. **Islamic Knowledge**
   - Access to authentic sources
   - Read Hadith in Arabic
   - Understand Islamic rulings
   - Independent learning ability

4. **Reward and Blessings**
   - Prophetic encouragement to learn Arabic
   - Reward for reciting Quran with understanding
   - Following Sahaba's example
   - Preserving Islamic knowledge

### Quranic Vocabulary Focus

**Most Common Quranic Words**

The Quran uses approximately 1,800 unique root words. Knowing the most common 500 words enables understanding of about 70% of the Quran.

**High-Frequency Words**
- الله (Allah) - 2,699 times
- رب (Rabb - Lord) - 962 times
- قال (Qala - said) - 1,722 times
- الناس (an-Nas - people) - 241 times
- كتاب (Kitab - book) - 261 times

**Curriculum Priority**
Our curriculum systematically teaches high-frequency Quranic vocabulary, enabling students to recognize and understand the most common words in their Quranic reading.

**KG1 (Age 4)**

**Learning Objectives**
- Recognize all Arabic letters
- Pronounce letters correctly
- Learn Islamic phrases and meanings
- Begin simple word reading
- Write letters (with assistance)

**Arabic Alphabet Complete**
All 28 letters with correct pronunciation:
ا ب ت ث ج ح خ د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ع غ ف ق ك ل م ن هـ و ي

**Letter Forms**
- Initial form (beginning of word)
- Medial form (middle of word)
- Final form (end of word)
- Isolated form (standalone)

**Letter Sounds**
- Short vowels: fatha (َ), kasra (ِ), damma (ُ)
- Sukun (ْ) - no vowel
- Shadda (ّ) - doubled letter
- Tanween: fathatain, kasratain, dammatain

**Simple Words**
- بيت (Bayt) - House
- يد (Yad) - Hand
- نور (Nur) - Light
- كلب (Kalb) - Dog
- قمر (Qamar) - Moon

**Short Duas**
- الحمد لله (Alhamdulillah)
- سبحان الله (SubhanAllah)
- لا إله إلا الله (La ilaha illallah)
- الله أكبر (Allahu Akbar)

**KG2 (Age 5)**

**Learning Objectives**
- Read simple Arabic words fluently
- Write letters and simple words
- Understand basic Islamic phrases

- Develop proper pronunciation (Tajweed basics)

**Reading Skills**
- Combining letters to form words
- Reading with harakat (vowel marks)
- Simple sentence reading
- Quranic words recognition

**Writing Skills**
- Letter writing independently
- Connecting letters
- Simple word writing
- Name in Arabic

**Vocabulary Expansion** (200 words)
- Family members
- Body parts (Arabic names)
- Numbers 1-10 (واحد، اثنان، ثلاثة...)
- Colors in Arabic
- Animals
- Daily objects

**Short Surahs with Meanings**
- Surah Al-Fatihah (meanings explained)
- Surah Al-Ikhlas (with translation)
- Surah Al-Falaq (with translation)
- Surah An-Nas (with translation)

**Tajweed Basics**
- Correct letter pronunciation
- Clear articulation points (Makharij)
- Proper vowel sounds

## Arabic Grammar (Nahw and Sarf)

### Nahw (النحو) - Syntax

**I'rab (إعراب) - Grammatical Analysis**

**Three Cases**
1. **Marfu' (مرفوع)** - Nominative (indicated by damma ُ)
   - Subject (فاعل)
   - Mubtada and Khabar (مبتدأ وخبر)

2. **Mansub (منصوب)** - Accusative (indicated by fatha َ)
   - Direct object (مفعول به)
   - Circumstantial (حال)

3. **Majrur (مجرور)** - Genitive (indicated by kasra ِ)
   - After prepositions
   - Second part of idafah (مضاف إليه)

**Sentence Types**
1. **Nominal Sentence (الجملة الاسمية)**
   - Begins with noun
   - Structure: Mubtada + Khabar
   - Example: البيت كبير (The house is big)

2. **Verbal Sentence (الجملة الفعلية)**
   - Begins with verb
   - Structure: Verb + Subject (+ Object if transitive)
   - Example: كتب الطالب الدرس (The student wrote the lesson)

### Sarf (الصرف) - Morphology

**Root System**
- Most Arabic words built on 3-letter roots
- Roots carry core meaning
- Patterns (أوزان) modify meaning

**Example Root: ك ت ب (k-t-b) - related to writing**
- كَتَبَ (kataba) - he wrote
- كِتَاب (kitab) - book
- كَاتِب (katib) - writer
- مَكْتَب (maktab) - office, desk
- مَكْتُوب (maktub) - written, letter
- مَكْتَبَة (maktaba) - library

**Verb Forms (أوزان)**
Ten major forms, each with specific meaning patterns:
- Form I: Basic meaning
- Forms II-X: Modified meanings (causation, reciprocation, seeking, etc.)

## Arabic Calligraphy

### Islamic Art Through Arabic

Calligraphy (خط - Khatt) is the highest art form in Islamic culture, as it beautifies the words of Allah and Islamic expressions.

### Major Scripts Taught

**1. Naskh (نسخ)**
- Most common script for Quran printing
- Clear, readable
- Standard for beginners
- Used in everyday writing

**2. Thuluth (ثلث)**
- Ornamental script
- Large, decorative letters
- Mosque decorations
- Certificates and titles

**3. Diwani (ديواني)**
- Ottoman imperial court script
- Flowing, decorative
- Certificates and official documents

**4. Ruq'ah (رقعة)**
- Modern handwriting script
- Fast, simple
- Everyday use

**5. Kufi (كوفي)**
- Angular, geometric
- Oldest script
- Architectural inscriptions
- Early Qurans

### Calligraphy Curriculum
