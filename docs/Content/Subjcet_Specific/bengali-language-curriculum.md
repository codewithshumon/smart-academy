## Executive Summary

Bengali, the national language of Bangladesh and one of the world's most spoken languages, forms the cornerstone of our students' cultural identity and national pride. Smart Academy's Bengali Language Curriculum honors the Language Movement martyrs while developing comprehensive language proficiency within an Islamic educational framework.

### Key Features

**Comprehensive Language Skills**
- Reading (পড়া): Decoding, fluency, comprehension
- Writing (লেখা): Handwriting, composition, creative writing
- Speaking (বলা): Articulation, conversation, presentation
- Listening (শোনা): Comprehension, attention, critical listening

**Cultural and Historical Integration**
- Language Movement history (ভাষা আন্দোলন)
- Bengali literature classics
- National identity and pride
- Cultural traditions and celebrations
- Liberation War connection

**Islamic Framework**
- Bengali Islamic literature
- Moral values through Bengali texts
- Respectful language use (আদব)
- Bengali in Islamic context
- Muslim Bengali writers

**Literary Exposure**
- Rabindranath Tagore (রবীন্দ্রনাথ ঠাকুর)
- Kazi Nazrul Islam (কাজী নজরুল ইসলাম)
- Begum Rokeya (বেগম রোকেয়া)
- Jibanananda Das (জীবনানন্দ দাশ)
- Contemporary Bengali writers
- Children's literature

## Vision and Philosophy

### Vision Statement

To develop eloquent, literate Bengali speakers who cherish their mother tongue, appreciate Bengali literary heritage, honor Language Movement martyrs, and express Islamic values through beautiful Bengali language.

### Educational Philosophy

**Mother Tongue Foundation**

Bengali is not just a subject but the foundation of our students' identity, thinking, and cultural heritage. As the language for which martyrs sacrificed their lives in 1952, Bengali holds special significance in Bangladesh's history and national consciousness.

**Core Principles**

1. **National Pride and Identity**
   - Bengali as symbol of Bangladesh
   - Language Movement (Ekushey February)
   - Cultural heritage preservation
   - National unity through language

2. **Comprehensive Language Development**
   - Four skills integration (reading, writing, speaking, listening)
   - Grammar and composition
   - Literature appreciation
   - Critical thinking through language

3. **Cultural Literacy**
   - Bengali literature classics
   - Traditional stories and folklore
   - National celebrations
   - Regional diversity

4. **Islamic Values in Bengali**
   - Islamic concepts in Bengali
   - Muslim Bengali writers
   - Respectful language (Adab)
   - Moral literature

5. **Functional Proficiency**
   - Everyday communication
   - Academic language
   - Formal and informal registers
   - Digital Bengali literacy

### Language Philosophy

**Additive Bilingualism**
Smart Academy promotes additive bilingualism where Bengali proficiency strengthens rather than competes with English. Students develop high proficiency in both languages for different contexts.

**Linguistic Diversity**
While standard Bengali (শুদ্ধ বাংলা) is taught, we respect regional dialects and variations, recognizing linguistic diversity as cultural richness.

**Living Language**
Bengali is taught as a living, evolving language relevant to contemporary contexts while honoring classical forms.

## Importance of Bengali Language

### Global Significance

**Demographics**
- 7th most spoken language globally
- Over 265 million speakers worldwide
- Official language: Bangladesh, West Bengal (India)
- Significant diaspora communities

**Cultural Contribution**
- Rich literary tradition spanning centuries
- First South Asian language to receive Nobel Prize in Literature (Tagore, 1913)
- Vibrant contemporary literature
- Thriving film and music industry

### National Significance

**Constitutional Status**
- State language of Bangladesh (Article 3)
- Medium of instruction in government schools
- Official language for all government business
- Symbol of national sovereignty

**Historical Significance**
- Language Movement 1952 (ভাষা আন্দোলন)
- Foundation for Bangladesh independence
- International Mother Language Day (UNESCO, February 21)
- Shaheed Minar as symbol

**Cultural Identity**
- Unifying force for Bengali people
- Medium for expressing Bangladeshi culture
- Connection to heritage and ancestors
- Pride and self-respect

## Language Movement and National Identity

### Historical Background

**British Colonial Period**
- Bengali renaissance in 19th century
- Development of modern Bengali literature
- Bengali as language of education and culture

**Pakistan Period (1947-1971)**

**Language Issue**
- Urdu declared sole state language of Pakistan (1948)
- Bengali speakers majority (56%) of Pakistan population
- Language as political and cultural issue
- Growing discontent in East Pakistan

**Language Movement (ভাষা আন্দোলন)**

**Key Events**

**1948**
- March 11: First Language Movement Day
- Students protest Urdu-only policy
- Muhammad Ali Jinnah insists "Urdu and only Urdu"

**1952**
- February 21: Police fire on student demonstrators
- Martyrs: Rafiq, Barkat, Salam, Jabbar, Shafiur
- Birth of Bengali nationalism
- Annual commemoration begins

**Impact**
- Bengali recognized as state language (1956)
- Foundation for independence movement
- Symbol of linguistic and cultural rights
- Inspiration for other language movements worldwide

**International Recognition**
- UNESCO declares February 21 as International Mother Language Day (1999)
- Recognition of linguistic rights globally
- Bangladesh's contribution to linguistic diversity

### Integration in Curriculum

**Every Grade Level**
- Age-appropriate Language Movement education
- Shaheed Minar significance
- February 21 activities
- Martyrs' sacrifice appreciation
- National pride cultivation

**Activities**
- Probhat Feri (morning procession) - February 21
- Ekushey poetry recitation
- Essay writing on Language Movement
- Art depicting Shaheed Minar
- Interviews with elderly about Language Movement

## Bengali Within Islamic Framework

### Islamic Perspective on Mother Tongue

**Prophetic Guidance**
The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) emphasized respecting all languages while encouraging eloquence in one's mother tongue.

**Linguistic Diversity in Islam**
- Quran 30:22 - "And among His signs is the creation of the heavens and earth, and the diversity of your languages and colors"
- All languages honored in Islam
- Mother tongue as gift from Allah
- Using language for good purposes

### Islamic Concepts in Bengali

**Religious Terminology**
Students learn Islamic concepts in both Arabic (original) and Bengali (translation/explanation):
- আল্লাহ (Allah)
- ইসলাম (Islam)
- ঈমান (Iman - faith)
- ইবাদত (Ibadah - worship)
- নামাজ (Namaz/Salah - prayer)
- রোজা (Roza/Sawm - fasting)
- হজ্জ (Hajj)
- জাকাত (Zakat)

**Islamic Literature in Bengali**
- Translated Quran with Bengali meanings
- Hadith collections in Bengali
- Islamic stories for children
- Bengali Islamic poetry
- Contemporary Islamic writings

### Muslim Bengali Writers

**Historical Figures**
- Kazi Nazrul Islam (কাজী নজরুল ইসলাম): National poet, Islamic themes
- Golam Mostafa (গোলাম মোস্তফা): Islamic poet
- Jasimuddin (জসীমউদ্দীন): Folk tradition
- Sufia Kamal (সুফিয়া কামাল): Social justice, women's rights

**Contemporary Writers**
- Syed Mujtaba Ali
- Humayun Ahmed
- Islamic children's book authors

### Islamic Values in Language Use

**Adab (Etiquette) in Bengali**
- Respectful forms of address (আপনি vs. তুমি)
- Greeting: আসসালামু আলাইকুম
- Gratitude: আলহামদুলিল্লাহ
- Inshallah: ইনশাআল্লাহ
- Bismillah: বিসমিল্লাহ

**Moral Language**
- Truthfulness (সত্যবাদিতা)
- Kindness in speech (নম্র ভাষা)
- Avoiding gossip (পরনিন্দা পরিহার)
- Speaking good or remaining silent
- Using language for benefit

**Rhymes and Songs**

- Action songs
- Counting rhymes
- Seasonal songs

Examples:
- আম পাতা জোড়া জোড়া...

- আয় আয় চাঁদ মামা...

**Writing**

**Literature**

## Bengali Literature

### Literary Periods

**Ancient Period (650-1200 CE)**
- Charjapada (চর্যাপদ): Earliest Bengali texts
- Buddhist songs

**Medieval Period (1200-1800)**
- Vaishnava Literature: Devotional poetry
- Mangal Kavya: Narrative poems
- Translated texts: Ramayana, Mahabharata

**Modern Period (1800-Present)**

**Bengal Renaissance (1800-1900)**
- Ram Mohan Roy: Social reform
- Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar: Education reform
- Michael Madhusudan Dutt: Blank verse introduction
- Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay: Novel development

**Tagore Era (1880-1941)**
- Rabindranath Tagore: Transformed Bengali literature
- Nobel Prize (1913) - first Asian laureate
- Poetry, songs, stories, novels, dramas, essays

**Nazrul Era (1920s-1940s)**
- Kazi Nazrul Islam: Rebel poet
- Egalitarian themes
- Islamic devotional songs

**Contemporary Period (1947-Present)**
- Partition literature
- Language Movement literature
- Liberation War literature (1971)
- Modern and postmodern trends

### Major Literary Figures

**Rabindranath Tagore (রবীন্দ্রনাথ ঠাকুর, 1861-1941)**
- First non-European Nobel laureate
- "Gitanjali" (Song Offerings)
- National anthems: Bangladesh and India
- Over 2,000 songs, numerous poems, stories, novels, dramas

Studied Works:
- Poems: "সোনার তরী", "আমার সোনার বাংলা"
- Stories: "কাবুলিওয়ালা", "ছুটি", "পোস্টমাস্টার"
- Songs: "আমার সোনার বাংলা" (Bangladesh National Anthem)

**Kazi Nazrul Islam (কাজী নজরুল ইসলাম, 1899-1976)**
- National poet of Bangladesh
- Revolutionary poetry
- Islamic devotional songs (hamd, naat, ghazal)
- Advocate for equality and justice

Studied Works:
- Poems: "বিদ্রোহী", "কামাল পাশা"
- Songs: Islamic devotional music, patriotic songs

**Begum Rokeya (বেগম রোকেয়া, 1880-1932)**
- Pioneer feminist writer
- Advocate for women's education
- "Sultana's Dream" (feminist utopia)

**Jibanananda Das (জীবনানন্দ দাশ, 1899-1954)**
- Modern poetry pioneer
- Nature and Bengal landscape
- "Banalata Sen" - most famous poem

**Other Important Writers**
- Jasimuddin (জসীমউদ্দীন): Folk poetry
- Sufia Kamal (সুফিয়া কামাল): Women's rights, social justice
- Humayun Ahmed (হুমায়ূন আহমেদ): Contemporary fiction
- Syed Mujtaba Ali (সৈয়দ মুজতবা আলী): Travel writing, humor
