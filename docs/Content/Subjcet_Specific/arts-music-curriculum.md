## Program Overview

### Introduction to Arts and Music at Smart Academy

> "Indeed, Allah is Beautiful and He loves beauty." - Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), Sahih Muslim

Arts and Music education at Smart Academy represents our commitment to developing the whole child - intellectually, emotionally, spiritually, and creatively. Our comprehensive program nurtures creative expression while honoring Islamic guidelines and values, recognizing that artistic ability is a gift from Allah that should be developed and used for beneficial purposes.

Our arts curriculum embraces the rich tradition of Islamic arts, including calligraphy, geometric patterns, and architectural appreciation, alongside contemporary visual arts education. Our music program focuses on permissible forms of musical expression within Islamic guidelines, including nasheeds (Islamic songs), Quranic recitation, percussion, and vocal training.

Smart Academy's arts and music program serves 500+ students across all grade levels, providing:
- **Visual Arts Education**: Drawing, painting, sculpture, and design
- **Islamic Arts**: Calligraphy, geometric patterns, and arabesque designs
- **Music within Islamic Guidelines**: Nasheeds, percussion, and vocal training
- **Drama and Performance**: Theater, storytelling, and public speaking
- **STEAM Integration**: Arts as the "A" in STEAM education

### Program Vision

To develop creative, confident, and culturally aware students who appreciate beauty as a reflection of Allah's creation, express themselves through various artistic mediums, and contribute to the preservation and advancement of Islamic artistic traditions.

### Program Mission

## Islamic Perspective on Arts

### The Foundation of Islamic Arts

> "Say: Who has forbidden the adornment of Allah which He has brought forth for His servants?" - Quran 7:32

Islam has a rich artistic tradition spanning 1400 years across diverse cultures and regions. Understanding Islamic perspectives on arts provides the foundation for our program.

### Principles of Islamic Arts

**1. Tawhid (Unity of God) in Art**

Islamic art reflects the oneness of Allah:

- **Unity in Design**: Repeating patterns reflecting divine unity
- **Infinity Expression**: Patterns extending beyond borders
- **Balance and Harmony**: Reflecting cosmic order
- **Mathematical Precision**: Order reflecting divine wisdom
- **Beauty Without Idol**: Abstract rather than representational
- **Spiritual Focus**: Art directing to the Creator

**2. Permitted and Prohibited Elements**

Smart Academy follows scholarly guidance on:

**Generally Encouraged**
- Arabic calligraphy (especially Quran)
- Geometric and arabesque patterns
- Floral and vegetal designs
- Architectural decoration
- Abstract and non-figurative art
- Nature and landscape depiction
- Craft arts (pottery, weaving, etc.)
- Illumination and manuscript decoration

**Approached with Scholarly Guidance**
- Representational art (for education, documentation)
- Photography (within modest guidelines)
- Animation (for educational purposes)
- Digital art and design
- Scientific illustration

**Avoided in Our Program**
- Sculpture of complete animate beings for worship or decoration
- Music with prohibited instruments
- Immoral or inappropriate content
- Images that could lead to veneration
- Wasteful or extravagant art
- Art serving harmful purposes

**3. Beauty and Excellence (Ihsan)**

The Prophet (PBUH) said: "Allah has prescribed excellence in all things."

- Striving for beauty in all work
- Quality and craftsmanship valued
- Excellence as worship
- Careful and deliberate creation
- Attention to detail
- Finishing work properly

### Music in Islamic Guidelines

Our music program follows Islamic scholarly guidance:

**Permissible Elements**
- Nasheeds (Islamic songs without prohibited instruments)
- Vocal music with appropriate content
- Duff/tambourine (explicitly permitted in hadith)
- Simple percussion instruments
- Quranic recitation with tajweed
- Cultural songs with clean content
- Poetry recitation
- Chanting and group vocals

**Our Music Program Includes**
- Nasheed learning and performance
- Vocal training and technique
- Rhythm and percussion skills
- Quranic melodics (maqamat in recitation)
- Cultural Bangladeshi songs (appropriate)
- Choir and group performances
- Poetry and spoken word

**What We Do Not Include**
- Instruments considered prohibited by majority scholars
- Music with inappropriate lyrics
- Music promoting harmful behaviors
- Excessive focus on entertainment
- Performance contexts against Islamic values
- Immodest presentation

### Historical Context of Islamic Arts

**The Golden Age of Islamic Art (7th-17th century)**

- Calligraphy development across dynasties
- Mosque architecture innovations
- Manuscript illumination
- Textile and carpet arts
- Ceramic and pottery traditions
- Metalwork and woodwork
- Garden design

**Regional Traditions**

- **Arab World**: Foundational calligraphy and geometric art
- **Persian/Iranian**: Miniature painting, carpet weaving
- **Turkish/Ottoman**: Tile work, mosque architecture
- **Mughal/South Asian**: Blend of Islamic and local traditions
- **Moroccan/Andalusian**: Geometric zellige, stucco carving
- **Central Asian**: Architecture, textile arts
- **Southeast Asian**: Calligraphy, mosque architecture

**Contemporary Islamic Arts**

- Modern calligraphy artists
- Contemporary geometric art
- Islamic architecture today
- Digital Islamic art
- Fusion and innovation
- Global Muslim artists

### Famous Islamic Artists and Works

**Historical Masters**

- **Ibn Muqla** (886-940): Standardized Arabic scripts
- **Ibn al-Bawwab** (d. 1022): Perfected proportional calligraphy
- **Yaqut al-Musta'simi** (d. 1298): Master calligrapher of Baghdad
- **Bihzad** (c. 1450-1535): Persian miniature painting
- **Sinan the Architect** (1489-1588): Ottoman mosque architecture

**Notable Works**

- The Great Mosque of Cordoba, Spain
- The Blue Mosque, Istanbul, Turkey

### Assessment Rubrics

**1. Visual Arts Rubric (General)**

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Technique | Exceptional skill mastery | Strong technique | Developing skills | Emerging skills |
| Creativity | Highly original, innovative | Creative approach | Some originality | Limited creativity |
| Effort | Outstanding commitment | Strong effort | Adequate effort | Minimal effort |
| Composition | Masterful design | Effective composition | Basic organization | Limited organization |
| Growth | Exceptional improvement | Clear growth | Some development | Little visible growth |

**2. Calligraphy Rubric**

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Letter Form | Perfect proportions | Good accuracy | Recognizable forms | Needs work |
| Consistency | Perfectly consistent | Generally consistent | Some variation | Inconsistent |
| Composition | Balanced, beautiful | Well organized | Adequate layout | Needs improvement |
| Precision | Meticulous | Careful | Adequate | Rushed |
| Presentation | Exhibition quality | Clean presentation | Acceptable | Needs improvement |

**3. Nasheed/Vocal Rubric**

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Pitch | Perfect accuracy | Good accuracy | Mostly accurate | Needs work |
| Rhythm | Excellent precision | Good precision | Mostly correct | Needs improvement |
| Expression | Moving, emotional | Expressive | Some expression | Little expression |
| Memorization | Perfect recall | Strong recall | Mostly memorized | Needs more work |
| Participation | Outstanding contribution | Strong participation | Adequate participation | Minimal participation |
