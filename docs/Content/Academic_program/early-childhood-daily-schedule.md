# Early Childhood Daily Schedule Guide

**Smart Academy | Structuring the Day for Optimal Learning**

---

## Introduction

This comprehensive guide outlines the daily schedules for Smart Academy's Early Childhood Education program across all three levels: PlayGroup (ages 3-4), Nursery (ages 4-5), and Kindergarten (ages 5-6). Our schedules balance structure with flexibility, active learning with rest, and academic content with play, all while maintaining a strong Islamic foundation throughout the day.

---

## Scheduling Principles

### Foundation for Daily Structure

**1. Predictability with Flexibility**
- Consistent daily routines
- Expected sequence of activities
- Flexibility for emergent interests
- Adaptability to children's needs
- Weather and seasonal adjustments

**2. Balance of Activities**
- Active and quiet times
- Individual, small group, large group
- Indoor and outdoor experiences
- Teacher-directed and child-choice
- Academic and play-based learning

**3. Attention to Developmental Needs**
- Age-appropriate activity lengths
- Frequent transitions (younger children)
- Longer focus time (older children)
- Rest and movement balance
- Social interaction opportunities

**4. Islamic Rhythm**
- Prayer times integrated
- Quran throughout the day
- Islamic greetings and manners
- Du'as at key moments
- Islamic character modeling

**5. Time Allocations**
- **Islamic Studies**: 15-20% of day
- **Academic Instruction**: 30-35% of day
- **Centers/Play**: 25-30% of day
- **Outdoor/Physical**: 10-15% of day
- **Routines/Transitions**: 10-15% of day
- **Specials (Art/Music/PE)**: 10-15% of day (rotating)

## Schedule Components Explained

### Circle Time (Morning Meeting)

**Purpose:**
- Build community
- Set tone for day
- Islamic reminders
- Calendar and weather
- Introduce daily theme

**Typical Components:**
1. Islamic greeting (Assalamu Alaikum)
2. Opening du'a
3. Attendance (counting friends)
4. Calendar activities
5. Weather observation
6. Theme introduction
7. Day's schedule preview
8. News/show and share

**Duration:**
- PlayGroup: 15-20 minutes
- Nursery: 20-25 minutes
- Kindergarten: 25-30 minutes

### Islamic Studies Block

**Components:**
- Quran recitation (whole group or individual)
- Arabic letter/word study
- Islamic story or concept
- Character focus (Akhlaq)
- Memorization practice
- Du'a learning

**Approach:**
- Age-appropriate methodology
- Individual pacing for Quran
- Interactive and engaging
- Connection to daily life
- Positive and joyful

### Academic Instruction

**Language Arts:**
- Phonics lessons
- Shared reading
- Guided reading (small groups)
- Word work
- Writing activities
- Independent reading

**Mathematics:**
- Number talks/warm-ups
- Concept introduction
- Guided practice
- Math games
- Problem-solving
- Centers with manipulatives

**Science/Social Studies:**
- Hands-on exploration
- Investigations
- Experiments
- Thematic studies
- Projects
- Discovery activities

### Learning Centers/Free Choice

**Available Centers:**
1. Literacy (reading, writing, letters)
2. Math (manipulatives, games)
3. Science/Discovery (exploration, experiments)
4. Art (creative expression)
5. Blocks/Construction (building, spatial)
6. Dramatic Play (role-play, imagination)
7. Quran/Islamic corner
8. Sensory/Fine Motor

**Teacher Role:**
- Observe and document
- Facilitate learning
- Ask guiding questions
- Provide mini-lessons
- Assess understanding

**Duration:**

- Nursery: 40-50 minutes

### Outdoor Play

**Benefits:**
- Gross motor development
- Vitamin D and fresh air
- Stress release
- Social interaction
- Exploration of nature

**Activities:**
- Free play on equipment
- Organized games
- Sports skills practice
- Nature walks

**Implementation:**

- Conversation skills

**Lunch:**
- Balanced meal
- Family-style when possible
- Independence in eating

### Prayer Times

**Dhuhr Prayer:**
- Integrated into daily schedule
- Wudu preparation and instruction
- Congregational prayer
- Teacher-led for young children
- Individual practice for older children

**Islamic Reminders:**
- Brief, age-appropriate
- Connection to daily life
- Character emphasis
- Love for Allah and Prophet

### Specials/Enrichment

**Art:**
- Creative projects
- Islamic art exploration
- Skill development
- Process-focused

**Music:**
- Nasheeds
- Rhythm and movement

**Physical Education:**
- Structured activities
- Sports skills
- Fitness and health
- Teamwork

**Technology:**
- Educational apps/programs
- Coding introduction (KG)

### Transition Times

## Detailed Daily Schedules

### PlayGroup Schedule (Ages 3-4)

#### HALF-DAY PROGRAM (8:00 AM - 11:30 AM)

**8:00-8:15 AM: Arrival & Morning Activities**
- Health check at entrance
- Handwashing
- Self-selected quiet activities (puzzles, books, coloring)
- Teacher greets each child individually
- *Islamic Connection:* Morning greetings (Sabah al-Khair)

**8:15-8:45 AM: Morning Circle**
- Sitting in circle on carpet
- Islamic greeting: Assalamu Alaikum
- Morning du'a
- Attendance song (counting friends)
- Calendar and weather
- Day of week song
- Theme introduction
- Schedule preview with pictures

**8:45-9:15 AM: Islamic Studies**
- Quran listening (beautiful recitation playing)
- Arabic letter of the week
- Simple Islamic concept (Allah's love, kindness)
- Prophet story (very simple, engaging)
- Action songs for Arabic letters
- Short Surah practice (Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas)

**9:15-9:45 AM: English Language Arts**
- Story time (engaging picture book)
- Interactive reading
- Letter sound of the week
- Vocabulary building
- Songs and rhymes
- Movement with letters/sounds

**9:45-10:30 AM: Learning Centers (Free Play)**
- Children choose centers
- Teacher circulates, observes, interacts
- Small group pull-out for individual attention
- Documentation photos/notes
- *Available Centers:* Blocks, Dramatic Play, Art, Books, Manipulatives, Sensory, Islamic Corner

**10:30-10:45 AM: Snack Time**
- Handwashing song and routine
- Bismillah before eating
- Healthy snack (fruit, crackers, juice)
- Table manners practice
- Conversation time
- Du'a after eating
- Clean-up help

**10:45-11:15 AM: Outdoor Play**
- Gross motor activities
- Playground equipment
- Running, jumping, climbing
- Group games (simple)
- Sand play
- Nature exploration

**11:15-11:30 AM: Closing Circle & Dismissal**
- Bathroom
- Return to circle
- Review what we learned
- Goodbye song
- Closing du'a
- Gather belongings
- Line up for dismissal

#### FULL-DAY PROGRAM (8:00 AM - 2:00 PM)

*Morning follows Half-Day schedule through lunch*

**11:30 AM-11:45 AM: Dhuhr Prayer Time**
- Bathroom break
- Wudu demonstration/observation
- Prayer area
- Children observe and some participate
- Brief Islamic reminder

**11:45 AM-12:15 PM: Lunch**
- Handwashing
- Bismillah
- Lunch time
- Social interaction
- Clean-up

**12:15-1:00 PM: Rest/Quiet Time**
- Bathroom
- Rest mats prepared
- Soft music
- Audio story
- Quiet rest (some children may sleep)
- Quiet toys for those who don't sleep

**1:00-1:30 PM: Math & Science Activities**
- Number games
- Counting activities
- Shape exploration
- Sensory science
- Simple experiments
- Pattern activities

**1:30-1:55 PM: Creative Time**
- Art projects
- Music and movement
- Dramatic play
- Building activities
- Nasheed songs
- Creative expression

**1:55-2:00 PM: Closing & Dismissal**
- Quick bathroom
- Gather belongings
- Closing du'a
- Parent pick-up

## Flexible Scheduling Considerations

### Adjustments for Individual Needs

**Pacing:**
- Some activities shorter/longer based on engagement
- Attention span variations accommodated
- Rest breaks as needed
- Acceleration for advanced learners

**Special Needs:**
- Sensory breaks
- Movement opportunities
- Quiet spaces available
- Visual schedules
- Transition warnings

**Weather:**
- Indoor gross motor alternatives
- Flexible outdoor time
- Extended outdoor time in pleasant weather
- Rainy day activities planned

### Special Event Schedules

**Field Trips:**
- Modified schedule
- Safety protocols
- Parent volunteers
- Learning objectives clear

**Celebrations (Eid, etc.):**
- Special activities
- Cultural sharing
- Community building
- Islamic significance emphasis

**Guest Speakers/Visitors:**
- Integrated into regular schedule
- Age-appropriate presentations
- Interactive components
- Follow-up activities

**Assessment Days:**
- Individual assessments during center time
- No disruption to routine
- Stress-free environment
- Alternative activities for others

## Visual Schedules

### Importance for Young Children

**Benefits:**
- Predictability and security
- Independence (knowing what's next)
- Reduced anxiety
- Understanding time sequence
- Following routines

**Implementation:**
- Picture cards for each activity
- Displayed at child's eye level
- Reviewed during morning meeting
- Children can "read" their day
- Move completed activities

**Types:**
- Whole class schedule (posted)
- Individual schedules (when needed)
- Activity sequence within centers
- Procedure charts (handwashing, etc.)

## Smooth Transitions

### Strategies for Success

**Preparation:**
- 5-minute warning
- Transition songs
- Visual/auditory signals
- Clear expectations
- Practice and routine

**During Transitions:**
- Calm, organized process
- Teacher models
- Positive reinforcement
- Assistance as needed
- Efficient and smooth

**Common Transition Times:**
- Arrival to circle
- Circle to centers
- Centers to snack
- Outdoor to indoor
- Lunch to quiet time
- Dismissal preparation

---

## Time Management Tips

**For Teachers:**
- Follow schedule but remain flexible
- Know when to extend or shorten
- Observe children's engagement
- Balance structure and spontaneity
- Plan for transitions
- Have backup activities ready

**For Parents:**
- Arrive on time for drop-off/pick-up
- Consistent schedule at home
- Preview day's activities with child
- Support transitions (gentle separation)
- Respect pickup times
