## Overview

Smart Academy uses a comprehensive, holistic approach to admissions. We consider multiple factors to ensure each student admitted will thrive in our unique educational environment.

**Our Approach:**
- Holistic review of each applicant
- Fair and transparent evaluation
- Islamic values as foundation
- Academic potential assessment
- Character and conduct importance
- Family commitment consideration

---

## Our Selection Philosophy

### Islamic Principles Guide Us

**1. Justice (Adl)**
- Equal opportunity for all applicants
- Fair evaluation without bias
- Consideration of individual circumstances
- No discrimination except on merit

**2. Excellence (Ihsan)**
- Seeking students committed to learning
- Maintaining high academic standards
- Valuing character and conduct
- Preparing students for success

**3. Compassion (Rahma)**
- Understanding family challenges
- Considering individual needs
- Supporting diverse learners
- Flexibility in genuine hardship

**4. Wisdom (Hikmah)**
- Balanced consideration of factors
- Looking beyond test scores
- Recognizing different strengths
- Making decisions in students' best interests

### What We Seek

We seek students who:
- **Value Islamic education** and want to strengthen their faith
- **Are eager to learn** and demonstrate academic potential
- **Show good character** and respectful behavior
- **Contribute positively** to school community
- **Have supportive families** committed to their education
- **Will benefit from** and contribute to Smart Academy

## Primary Admission Criteria

### 1. Academic Potential and Readiness

**What We Evaluate:**
- Previous academic performance
- Assessment test results
- Learning aptitude
- Grade-level readiness

**Expectations by Grade Level:**

**PlayGroup-KG:**
- Developmental readiness
- Age-appropriate skills
- Curiosity and eagerness to learn
- Ability to separate from parents

**Class 1-5:**
- Solid foundation in basic skills
- Age-appropriate academic achievement
- Ability to handle grade-level work
- Previous school performance (minimum 60% average)

**Class 6-9:**
- Strong academic record
- Ability to handle rigorous curriculum
- Subject proficiency for grade level
- Preparation for secondary education

**Weight:** 40% of overall decision

### 2. Islamic Character and Values

**What We Evaluate:**
- Respect for Islamic practices
- Understanding of basic Islamic teachings
- Demonstrated good character (Akhlaq)
- Willingness to participate in Islamic programs

**Indicators We Look For:**
- Respectful behavior and manners
- Honesty and integrity
- Cooperation with others
- Regular prayer habits (where age-appropriate)
- Interest in Quran and Islamic learning

**For Non-Muslim Applicants:**
- Respect for Islamic practices
- Willingness to participate in Islamic studies
- Understanding of school's Islamic identity
- Commitment to Islamic values and environment

**Weight:** 30% of overall decision

### 3. Conduct and Behavior

**What We Evaluate:**
- Previous school conduct record
- Behavior during assessment/visit
- Social skills and interactions
- Disciplinary history

**Positive Indicators:**
- Good conduct certificate from previous school
- Respectful interaction with peers and adults
- Cooperative attitude
- No history of serious behavioral issues
- Positive teacher recommendations

**Concerns:**
- History of bullying or violence
- Chronic disciplinary problems
- Disrespectful behavior
- Expulsion from previous school

**Weight:** 15% of overall decision

### 4. Family Commitment and Partnership

**What We Evaluate:**
- Parent understanding of Smart Academy's mission
- Willingness to partner with school
- Support for Islamic education
- Communication with school

**Indicators:**
- Parent interview participation
- Understanding of school policies
- Commitment to homework support
- Agreement with Islamic approach
- Willingness to participate in school activities

**Weight:** 10% of overall decision

### 5. Special Talents and Contributions

**What We Evaluate:**
- Unique talents or abilities
- Leadership potential
- Extracurricular involvement
- Diverse perspectives and backgrounds

**Considerations:**
- Academic competition achievements
- Sports or artistic talents
- Community service involvement
- Leadership experience
- Diverse background that enriches community

**Weight:** 5% of overall decision

## Grade-Specific Criteria

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

**Key Criteria:**
- Developmental readiness (social, emotional, cognitive)
- Age appropriateness
- Ability to separate from parents
- Language development (Bengali and/or English)
- Toilet training (for PlayGroup/Nursery)

**Less Emphasis On:**
- Formal academic skills
- Previous school experience
- Advanced abilities

**Most Important:**
- Readiness for school environment
- Social development
- Behavioral readiness
- Family support

### Primary (Class 1-5)

**Key Criteria:**
- Academic foundation for grade level
- Reading and writing ability (English and Bengali)
- Basic mathematical concepts
- Arabic alphabet recognition
- Behavior and conduct
- Attendance record

**Important Factors:**
- Previous school performance
- Teacher recommendations
- Assessment results
- Social skills

**Balance Between:**
- Academic readiness
- Character development
- Family involvement

### Secondary (Class 6-9)

**Key Criteria:**
- Strong academic record
- Subject proficiency
- Preparation for rigorous curriculum
- Islamic character development
- Leadership potential
- Extracurricular involvement

**Important Factors:**
- Previous 2-3 years performance
- Conduct certificate
- Assessment test performance
- Interview quality
- Recommendations

**Competitive Considerations:**
- Limited seats available
- Higher academic expectations
- Leadership and service valued
- Role model potential for younger students

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Document Review

**What We Check:**
- Completeness of application
- Previous academic records
- Conduct certificates
- Attendance records
- Required documents submitted

**Red Flags:**
- Incomplete applications
- Poor academic performance without explanation
- Serious disciplinary issues
- Falsified information

### Step 2: Assessment

**Age-Appropriate Testing:**
- PlayGroup-KG: Play-based observation
- Class 1-3: Basic skills assessment
- Class 4-5: Subject-based assessment
- Class 6-9: Comprehensive subject testing

**What We Assess:**
- Academic readiness and knowledge
- Problem-solving abilities
- Language comprehension
- Mathematical reasoning
- Arabic and Islamic knowledge
- Learning aptitude

### Step 3: Interview

**Student Interview:**
- Assess communication skills
- Evaluate Islamic character
- Gauge interest in learning
- Understand personality and interests
- Observe behavior and manners

**Parent Interview:**
- Discuss expectations
- Explain school policies
- Assess family commitment
- Answer parent questions
- Ensure understanding of Smart Academy's approach

### Step 4: Comprehensive Review

**Admissions Committee Reviews:**
- Academic potential
- Character and conduct
- Family fit with school
- Special circumstances
- Overall contribution to community

**Decision Factors:**
- Meeting minimum criteria
- Comparison with other applicants
- Seat availability
- Special considerations
- Mission alignment

## Special Considerations

### Priority Categories

**1. Siblings of Current Students**
- Priority consideration given
- Must still meet admission requirements
- Not automatic admission
- Demonstrated success of current sibling considered

**2. Children of Alumni**
- Some priority consideration
- Must meet all admission criteria
- Alumni connection valued but not decisive

**3. Early Applicants**
- Consideration for early applications
- More time for thorough evaluation
- Not automatic advantage over qualified later applicants

### Special Circumstances

**1. Scholarship Applicants**
- Academic potential strongly weighted
- Character and Islamic commitment valued
- Family circumstances considered
- Financial need assessed separately

**2. Transfer Students**
- Previous school quality evaluated
- Curriculum compatibility assessed
- Placement testing for appropriate grade
- Integration support considered

**3. Special Needs Students**
- Individual assessment of needs
- Ability to provide support evaluated
- Honest discussion about capabilities
- Alternative placement recommended if needed

**4. Late Applicants**
- Considered if space available
- No disadvantage if equally qualified
- Late application fee may apply

### Factors That May Disqualify

**Automatic Disqualification:**
- Falsified documents or information
- Serious disciplinary history (violence, bullying)
- Expulsion from previous school for serious cause
- Inability to meet grade-level requirements without extensive support

**Possible Disqualification:**
- Severe behavioral issues
- Extreme academic deficiency
- Family unwillingness to support school policies
- Inability to benefit from our program

## Scoring System

### How Decisions Are Made

**Qualitative Evaluation:**
- No strict numerical cutoff
- Holistic review of each applicant
- Professional judgment of admissions committee
- Consideration of multiple factors

**General Guidelines:**

**Strong Candidate:**
- Meets or exceeds academic expectations
- Demonstrates strong Islamic character
- Excellent conduct record
- Supportive, committed family
- Likely to thrive and contribute

**Qualified Candidate:**
- Meets minimum academic standards
- Good character and conduct
- Family supportive
- Can succeed with support

**Waitlist Candidate:**
- Meets minimum criteria
- Qualified but space limited
- May be admitted if space opens

**Not Admitted:**
- Does not meet minimum criteria
- Serious concerns about fit
- Inability to provide needed support
- Better fit at another institution

## Notification of Results

### Decision Categories

**1. Offer of Admission**
- Congratulations! Your child has been admitted
- Response deadline specified (usually 2 weeks)
- Enrollment instructions provided
- Orientation information included

**2. Waitlist**
- Qualified applicant but space limited
- No guarantee of admission
- Will be contacted if space opens
- Usually notified by May/June

**3. Not Admitted**
- Polite notification sent
- No detailed feedback provided (policy)
- May reapply next year
- Not encouraged to appeal

**4. Conditional Offer**
- Admission offered with conditions
- May include academic probation
- Specific requirements must be met
- Review period specified

### Response Required

**If Admitted:**
- Accept or decline by deadline
- Pay admission and security fees
- Submit enrollment documents
- Attend orientation program

**If Waitlisted:**
- May accept another school and decline waitlist
- Or remain on waitlist and hope for space
- No obligation if offered admission elsewhere

## Appeals Policy

**Limited Appeals:**
- Appeals only considered for significant new information
- Must be submitted in writing within 7 days
- Reviewed by appeals committee
- Final decision within 2 weeks

**Grounds for Appeal:**
- Significant error in evaluation
- New information not previously available
- Extenuating circumstances not considered

**Not Grounds for Appeal:**
- Disagreement with decision
- Belief that child should have been admitted
- Comparison with other admitted students

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About Criteria

**Q: What is most important in admission decision?**
A: Academic potential and Islamic character are the two most important factors, followed by conduct and family commitment. We look at the whole child.

**Q: Do you only accept top students?**
A: No, we seek students with potential and commitment, not just top performers. We look at attitude, character, and willingness to learn.

**Q: Does applying early increase chances?**
A: Early application allows more time for review but doesn't guarantee admission. Qualified applicants are considered regardless of timing.

**Q: How important are test scores?**
A: Test scores are one factor among many. We consider the whole picture, including character, conduct, and family commitment.

**Q: Do you give preference to wealthy families?**
A: Absolutely not. In fact, 25% of our students are on scholarship. Economic status is not a factor in admission decisions.

**Q: Can a student with weak academics be admitted?**
A: If potential is demonstrated and other factors are strong, a student may be admitted with academic support. Each case is considered individually.

**Q: What about children with behavioral issues?**
A: We consider each case individually. Minor issues can be addressed. Serious behavioral problems may indicate we're not the right fit.

**Q: Do you accept students who don't speak Arabic?**
A: Yes, we teach Arabic. Previous knowledge is beneficial but not required. Students without Arabic will receive support.

**Q: How competitive is admission?**
A: It varies by grade level. Early childhood grades have more availability. Upper grades fill quickly. Apply early for best consideration.

**Q: Can I appeal an admission decision?**
A: Appeals are only considered for significant new information not previously available. The decision is usually final.
