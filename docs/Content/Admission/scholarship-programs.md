## Types of Scholarships

### 1. Need-Based Scholarships

**Purpose:** Support families with genuine financial constraints

**Coverage:** 25% to 100% of tuition fees

**Awarded Based On:**
- Family income and assets
- Number of dependents
- Special circumstances (medical, unemployment, etc.)
- Genuine financial need
- Commitment to education

**Who Should Apply:**
- Families with limited income
- Unemployed or underemployed parents
- Large families with limited income
- Families facing medical crises
- Single-parent households
- Any family with genuine need

### 2. Merit-Based Scholarships

**Purpose:** Recognize and encourage academic excellence

**Coverage:** 25% to 75% of tuition fees

**Awarded Based On:**
- Outstanding academic performance
- Consistent high achievement (90%+ average)
- Excellent conduct and character
- Positive contributions to school
- Leadership and service

**Who Should Apply:**
- Top performers in previous grade
- Students with exceptional talent
- Competition winners
- Student leaders
- Those with outstanding Islamic character

### 3. Hifz Scholarships

**Purpose:** Support students memorizing the Quran

**Coverage:** 50% to 100% of tuition fees

**Awarded Based On:**
- Enrollment in Hifz program
- Progress in Quran memorization
- Commitment to continue
- Good behavior and conduct

**Requirements:**
- Maintain Hifz enrollment
- Continue memorization progress
- Good Islamic character
- Regular attendance in prayers

### 4. Orphan Scholarships

**Purpose:** Support students who have lost one or both parents

**Coverage:** Up to 100% of tuition fees

**Awarded Based On:**
- Verification of orphan status
- Financial need
- Academic commitment
- Good conduct

**Documentation Required:**
- Death certificate of parent(s)
- Guardian documentation
- Proof of financial situation
- School records

### 5. Special Circumstances Scholarships

**Purpose:** Assist families facing temporary crises

**Coverage:** Varies based on situation

**Considered For:**
- Natural disasters
- Medical emergencies
- Job loss
- Family crises
- Other genuine hardships

**Duration:**
- Usually one academic year
- May be renewed if circumstances continue
- Reviewed annually

### 6. Sibling Discounts

**Purpose:** Support families with multiple children

**Discount:**
- 2nd sibling: 10% off tuition
- 3rd sibling: 15% off tuition
- 4th+ sibling: 20% off tuition

**Automatic:**
- No application required
- Applied automatically
- See Sibling Discounts document

## Eligibility Criteria

### General Eligibility

**All Scholarship Applicants Must:**
- Be enrolled or applying for admission to Smart Academy
- Meet admission requirements
- Demonstrate genuine need or merit (as applicable)
- Maintain good conduct and character
- Commit to regular attendance
- Support Smart Academy's mission

### Need-Based Scholarship Criteria

**Income Thresholds:**
- Family income below 300,000 BDT/year typically eligible
- Above threshold may still qualify if special circumstances
- Each case considered individually

**Considerations:**
- Total family income (all sources)
- Number of dependents
- Assets and property
- Cost of living in area
- Special expenses (medical, debts, etc.)
- Number of children in school

**Documentation Required:**
- Income verification (salary certificate, tax returns)
- Bank statements (3-6 months)
- Proof of assets/property
- Documentation of special circumstances
- Number of dependents verification

### Merit-Based Scholarship Criteria

**Academic Requirements:**
- Previous year average: 90% or above
- Consistent performance over multiple years
- Excellent conduct record
- No disciplinary issues
- Positive contributions to school

**Other Considerations:**
- Leadership roles
- Competition achievements
- Community service
- Islamic character
- Special talents

## Scholarship Terms

### Award Duration

**Annual Awards:**
- Scholarships awarded for one academic year
- Must reapply each year for continued support
- Not automatically renewed

**Review Process:**
- Financial situation reassessed annually
- Academic performance reviewed
- Conduct evaluated
- May be increased, decreased, or discontinued

### Scholarship Conditions

**Recipients Must:**
- Maintain good conduct and behavior
- Attend classes regularly (minimum 90% attendance)
- Make satisfactory academic progress
- Participate fully in school programs
- Not engage in disciplinary issues
- Report any changes in financial situation

**Academic Progress:**
- Maintain passing grades in all subjects
- No failing grades
- Positive attitude toward learning
- Good effort and participation

**Conduct:**
- Excellent Islamic character
- Respect for teachers and peers
- No disciplinary infractions
- Positive contributions to school

### Scholarship Termination

**May Be Terminated If:**
- Academic performance not maintained
- Serious disciplinary infractions
- Poor attendance without valid reason
- False information provided on application
- Family financial situation improves significantly
- Student withdraws from school

**Notice:**
- Written notice of termination
- Reason for termination explained
- Right to appeal (except for false information)
- One-month grace period to adjust payment

## Renewal Process

### Annual Renewal Required

**Scholarships Not Automatic:**
- Must reapply each year
- Current financial situation reassessed
- Academic progress reviewed
- Decision made for upcoming year

### Renewal Application

**Timeline:**
- Application period: December 1-31
- Submit updated financial information
- Current academic record reviewed
- Decision: Announced by February

**Documentation Needed:**
- Updated income verification
- Current bank statements
- Changes in circumstances
- Academic progress report

### Changes in Award

**May Be:**
- **Increased:** If financial situation worsened
- **Decreased:** If financial situation improved
- **Continued:** At same level if unchanged
- **Discontinued:** If no longer eligible

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About Scholarships

**Q: How do I apply for a scholarship?**
A: Complete the scholarship application form (available online or at admission office) and submit with required documentation by January 31 deadline.

**Q: Who is eligible for scholarships?**
A: Families with genuine financial need, students with outstanding academic merit, Hifz students, orphans, and those facing special circumstances may be eligible.

**Q: What percentage of students receive scholarships?**
A: Approximately 25% of our students receive some form of financial assistance.

**Q: How much financial assistance can I receive?**
A: Scholarships range from 25% to 100% of tuition fees, depending on need, merit, or category.

**Q: What is the income limit for need-based scholarships?**
A: There's no strict cutoff, but families with income below 300,000 BDT/year typically qualify. Each case is considered individually based on complete circumstances.

**Q: Do I have to repay scholarships?**
A: No, scholarships are grants, not loans. However, if false information is provided, repayment may be required.

**Q: Can I get both scholarship and sibling discount?**
A: Need-based scholarships and sibling discounts are mutually exclusive. You'll receive whichever is greater. Merit scholarships can be combined with sibling discount.

**Q: Are scholarships available for new students only?**
A: No, both new and current students can apply. Current students apply in December, new students apply with admission application.

**Q: What if my financial situation changes during the year?**
A: Contact the finance office immediately. We can review your situation and adjust scholarship if appropriate.

**Q: How long does a scholarship last?**
A: Scholarships are awarded for one academic year and must be renewed annually. Renewal is not automatic.

**Q: Will anyone know my child is on scholarship?**
A: No, all scholarship information is kept confidential. Only necessary finance and administrative staff are aware. Other students and parents will not know.

**Q: Does scholarship cover all fees?**
A: Scholarships typically cover tuition only. Additional fees (library, lab, etc.) and admission/security fees are normally not covered.

**Q: What if I'm denied a scholarship but still can't afford fees?**
A: Contact the finance office to discuss payment plans or other options. We're committed to working with families to make education accessible.
