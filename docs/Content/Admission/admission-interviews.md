## Interview Overview

### Purpose of Interviews

Admission interviews help us:
- Understand the student beyond paper application
- Assess character and Islamic values
- Evaluate communication skills
- Gauge family commitment to Smart Academy's mission
- Ensure mutual understanding and fit
- Answer family questions

**Important:**
- Interviews are conversations, not interrogations
- No trick questions or hidden agendas
- Opportunity for mutual understanding
- Part of holistic admission process

### Interview Format

**Two Separate Interviews:**

1. **Student Interview**
   - One-on-one with admission staff
   - Age-appropriate conversation
   - Assesses student's personality and readiness

2. **Parent Interview**
   - Both parents encouraged to attend
   - Discussion about family and expectations
   - Opportunity to ask questions

**Duration:**
- Student interview: 15-30 minutes depending on grade
- Parent interview: 20-30 minutes

**Timing:**
- Usually same day as assessment
- Student interviewed first, then parents
- Break between if needed

## Student Interview

### What to Expect

**Format:**
- Friendly, informal conversation
- Age-appropriate questions
- Comfortable environment
- Not about testing knowledge

**Goals:**
- Assess communication skills
- Evaluate Islamic character and manners
- Understand student's personality
- Gauge interest in learning
- Observe confidence and attitude

### Interview by Grade Level

#### Early Childhood (PlayGroup-KG)

**Duration:** 10-15 minutes

**Approach:**
- Very informal, friendly conversation
- May include play or simple activities
- Focus on comfort and natural behavior

**Sample Interactions:**
- "What's your name?"
- "How old are you?"
- "What toys do you like to play with?"
- "Do you have brothers and sisters?"
- "What's your favorite color/food/game?"
- "Can you count for me?"
- "Show me this picture/letter/number"

**What We Observe:**
- Language development
- Social comfort
- Ability to separate from parents
- Following simple directions
- Basic knowledge (colors, numbers, etc.)
- Politeness and manners
- Confidence and curiosity

#### Primary (Class 1-5)

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes

**Approach:**
- Semi-structured conversation
- Mix of personal and academic questions
- Assessment of maturity and readiness

**Sample Questions:**
- "Tell me about yourself."
- "What do you like about your current school?"
- "What are your favorite subjects? Why?"
- "What do you find challenging?"
- "What do you like to do in your free time?"
- "Do you have any hobbies or special interests?"
- "Do you like reading? What books do you like?"
- "Why do you want to come to Smart Academy?"
- "What do you know about Smart Academy?"
- "Do you pray? Tell me about it."
- "What makes a good student?"
- "How do you help others?"

**What We Assess:**
- Communication skills
- Confidence and self-expression
- Interest in learning
- Islamic awareness
- Manners and respect
- Honesty and sincerity
- Critical thinking (basic)
- Goals and aspirations (age-appropriate)

#### Secondary (Class 6-9)

**Duration:** 20-30 minutes

**Approach:**
- More structured conversation
- Deeper questions about goals and values
- Assessment of maturity and readiness for secondary level

**Sample Questions:**
- "Tell me about yourself—your background, interests, strengths."
- "What do you enjoy most about school? Least?"
- "Describe your favorite teacher and what made them special."
- "What are your academic strengths and weaknesses?"
- "How do you handle challenging subjects?"
- "What are your hobbies and interests outside school?"
- "Have you participated in any clubs, sports, or activities?"
- "What does being a good Muslim mean to you?"
- "How do you balance deen (religion) and dunya (worldly life)?"
- "Why are you interested in Smart Academy specifically?"
- "What do you know about our Islamic approach?"
- "How do you contribute to your family and community?"
- "What are your goals for the next few years?"
- "How do you handle conflicts or disagreements?"
- "Describe a situation where you showed leadership."
- "What book has influenced you and why?"
- "How do you use technology responsibly?"
- "What does 'character' mean to you?"

**What We Assess:**
- Communication and articulation
- Critical thinking and reasoning
- Islamic identity and values
- Self-awareness and maturity
- Goals and aspirations
- Leadership potential
- Social responsibility
- Academic motivation
- Honesty and integrity
- Fit with Smart Academy community

### Student Interview Tips

**Before the Interview:**
- Get good sleep the night before
- Eat a healthy meal
- Dress neatly and modestly
- Be yourself—not someone else
- Think about your interests and goals

**During the Interview:**
- Be honest and sincere
- Speak clearly and confidently
- Make eye contact
- Listen carefully to questions
- Take time to think before answering
- Show enthusiasm and interest
- Use good manners (say Assalamu Alaikum, thank you)
- Ask questions if you have them
- Don't worry about being perfect

**What Impresses:**
- Authenticity and honesty
- Respect and good manners
- Genuine interest in learning
- Islamic character and values
- Self-awareness and maturity
- Positive attitude
- Curiosity and questions

**What Doesn't Impress:**
- Rehearsed or scripted answers
- Arrogance or showing off
- Dishonesty or exaggeration
- Rudeness or disrespect
- Disinterest or lack of effort
- Parent-coached responses

## Parent Interview

### What to Expect

**Format:**
- Discussion with admission staff
- Two-way conversation
- Opportunity for parents to learn about Smart Academy
- Usually with both parents together

**Goals:**
- Understand family expectations
- Assess commitment to Smart Academy's mission
- Explain school policies and approach
- Answer parent questions
- Ensure mutual understanding

### Parent Interview Topics

#### Understanding Your Child

**Questions May Include:**
- "Tell us about your child—their strengths, challenges, personality."
- "What motivates your child to learn?"
- "How does your child handle challenges or setbacks?"
- "What are your concerns about your child's education?"
- "How do you support your child's learning at home?"
- "What are your hopes for your child's education?"

#### Educational Philosophy and Expectations

**Discussion Topics:**
- Your educational goals for your child
- Understanding of Smart Academy's Islamic approach
- Expectations from the school
- Views on homework and discipline
- Thoughts on technology use
- Approach to Islamic education

#### Family Commitment

**Questions May Include:**
- Why do you want your child at Smart Academy?
- What attracts you to our Islamic approach?
- How will you support your child's education?
- How will you partner with teachers and school?
- Can you attend parent-teacher meetings and school events?
- How do you reinforce Islamic values at home?
- Are you comfortable with our policies and expectations?

#### Practical Considerations

**Topics Discussed:**
- Transportation arrangements
- Financial commitment
- Communication preferences
- Special circumstances or needs
- Health or learning considerations

#### Opportunity for Parents to Ask Questions

**Parents Should Ask:**
- Details about academic programs
- Islamic education approach
- Extracurricular activities
- School policies and procedures
- Communication methods
- Parent involvement opportunities
- Any concerns or questions

### Parent Interview Tips

**Before the Interview:**
- Review Smart Academy's website and materials
- Prepare questions to ask
- Discuss your educational goals as parents
- Be ready to discuss your child honestly
- Think about how you can support the school

**During the Interview:**
- Be honest and open
- Share concerns freely
- Ask questions freely
- Listen carefully to information
- Take notes if helpful
- Show enthusiasm and commitment
- Demonstrate partnership attitude
- Be respectful and courteous

**What Impresses:**
- Genuine interest in Smart Academy's mission
- Commitment to supporting child's education
- Understanding of Islamic approach
- Willingness to partner with school
- Honesty about child's needs
- Thoughtful questions
- Respectful communication

**What Doesn't Impress:**
- Focus only on academics, not character
- Unwillingness to support school policies
- Demanding or entitled attitude
- Dishonesty about child's needs
- Lack of interest in Islamic education
- Reluctance to partner with school

## Interview Day Tips

### Practical Preparation

**What to Bring:**
- Application acknowledgment letter
- List of questions to ask
- Notebook and pen (for parents)
- Water bottle

**What to Wear:**
- Modest, neat dress
- Clean and presentable
- Comfortable clothing
- Business casual appropriate
- Islamic dress appreciated but not required

**Timing:**
- Arrive 15 minutes early
- Allow extra time for traffic
- Plan to stay 1-2 hours total
- Don't schedule other appointments immediately after

### During the Interview

**For Students:**
- Greet with "Assalamu Alaikum"
- Make eye contact
- Speak clearly
- Be polite and respectful
- Listen carefully
- Take time to think
- Be honest
- Smile and be friendly
- Show enthusiasm
- Ask questions

**For Parents:**
- Be supportive of your child
- Let your child speak for themselves
- Don't answer for your child
- Demonstrate partnership with school
- Show respect for staff
- Ask thoughtful questions
- Listen actively
- Take notes if needed
- Be honest and open

### Common Concerns

**Nervousness:**
- Normal to be nervous
- We understand and create comfortable environment
- Take deep breaths
- Remember we want to get to know you, not test you

**Language Concerns:**
- English preferred but not required
- We want to understand you, not test grammar
- Speak in language you're comfortable with
- Communication skills matter more than perfection

**Not Knowing Answers:**
- It's okay to say "I don't know"
- Better to be honest than to make up answers
- We're not testing specific knowledge
- We want to understand your thinking

**Shy Children:**
- We're experienced with shy students
- Patient and gentle approach
- Don't worry, we'll make them comfortable
- Shyness doesn't disqualify

## Common Questions

### Student Questions

**Personal:**
- Tell me about yourself.
- What are your strengths and weaknesses?
- What do you like to do in your free time?
- Who is your role model and why?

**Academic:**
- What are your favorite subjects? Why?
- What subjects do you find challenging?
- How do you handle difficult subjects?
- How do you study/prepare for tests?

**Islamic:**
- What does being a good Muslim mean to you?
- How do you practice Islam in daily life?
- What is your favorite Surah/Hadith and why?
- How do you balance Islamic and academic studies?

**About Smart Academy:**
- Why do you want to come to Smart Academy?
- What do you know about our school?
- What are you most excited about?
- Do you have any questions about Smart Academy?

### Parent Questions

**About Child:**
- Tell us about your child's strengths and challenges.
- How does your child learn best?
- What motivates your child?
- What are your concerns?

**About School:**
- What are our academic expectations?
- How do we integrate Islamic values?
- What support is available for struggling students?
- How do we communicate with parents?

**About Partnership:**
- How can parents support the school?
- What are our expectations for parental involvement?
- How will we work together for your child's success?

## After the Interview

### What Happens Next

**Evaluation:**
- Interview combined with assessment results
- Academic records reviewed
- Complete picture considered
- Admission committee makes recommendation

**Timeline:**
- Decision notification by March 15, 2026
- Email, SMS, and letter sent
- Clear explanation of decision

**Possible Outcomes:**

**1. Offer of Admission**
- Congratulations!
- Student and family good fit
- Respond by deadline
- Enrollment begins

**2. Waitlist**
- Qualified but space limited
- May be admitted if space opens
- Usually notified by May-June

**3. Not Admitted**
- Decision is final
- Not detailed feedback provided
- May reapply next year

### Reflection

**For Families:**
- Discuss the interview and visit
- Reflect on whether Smart Academy fits
- Consider child's comfort and interest
- Make informed decision together

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About Interviews

**Q: Are interviews required for all applicants?**
A: Yes, both student and parent interviews are part of the admission process for all grade levels.

**Q: Who conducts the interviews?**
A: Trained admission staff, including the admission officer, principal, or coordinators.

**Q: Can we bring other children to the interview?**
A: If possible, make other arrangements. Younger siblings can be disruptive. If necessary, bring someone to supervise them in waiting area.

**Q: How long is the interview process?**
A: Plan for 1-2 hours total, including both student and parent interviews, plus breaks.

**Q: Should we prepare for the interview?**
A: No special preparation needed. Be honest, be yourself, and come with questions. Authenticity is most important.

**Q: What if we don't speak English well?**
A: English preferred but not required. We want to understand you. Communication skills matter more than language perfection.

**Q: Do both parents need to attend?**
A: Both parents strongly encouraged, but we understand if this isn't possible. At least one parent must attend.

**Q: What if my child is very shy?**
A: We're experienced with shy children and create a comfortable environment. Shyness doesn't disqualify an applicant.

**Q: Can parents sit in on the child's interview?**
A: For PlayGroup-KG, if needed for child's comfort. For older students, no—the child needs to speak for themselves.

**Q: Do you interview every applicant?**
A: Yes, every applicant has an interview as part of the holistic admission process.
