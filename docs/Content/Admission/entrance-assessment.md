## Assessment Overview

### Purpose of Assessment

The entrance assessment helps us:
- Evaluate readiness for the grade level
- Understand each child's strengths and areas for growth
- Determine appropriate placement
- Identify support needs
- Make fair admission decisions

**Important:**
- Assessments are age-appropriate
- No pass/fail - evaluation of readiness
- Not about high scores, but fit and potential
- One part of holistic admission process

### Assessment Philosophy

**Islamic Approach:**
- Every child has unique God-given talents
- Assessment identifies potential, not limits
- Character and attitude valued alongside academics
- Fair and just evaluation for all

**Developmentally Appropriate:**
- Play-based for young children
- Structured testing for older students
- No unnecessary pressure or stress
- Natural abilities observed

## Grade-Specific Assessments

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - KG)

#### PlayGroup Assessment

**Format:** Play-based observation

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**What Happens:**
- Child engages in play activities
- Social interaction observed
- Language development noted
- Motor skills assessed
- Attention span observed

**Activities May Include:**
- Free play with toys
- Simple puzzles
- Drawing or coloring
- Picture identification
- Conversation with assessor
- Separation from parents observed

**Parent Interview:** 15-20 minutes
- Discussion about child's development
- Home environment
- Parent expectations
- Questions answered

#### Nursery Assessment

**Format:** Play-based with some structured activities

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes

**What Happens:**
- Play-based activities
- Basic concept recognition
- Language comprehension check
- Following directions
- Social interaction

**Skills Observed:**
- Color and shape recognition
- Counting (1-10)
- Language expression
- Fine motor skills
- Sharing and cooperation

**Parent Interview:** 15-20 minutes

#### Kindergarten Assessment

**Format:** Structured readiness assessment

**Duration:** 60-75 minutes

**What Happens:**
- Letter recognition (English and Arabic)
- Number concepts (1-20)
- Basic concepts
- Drawing/writing sample
- Reading readiness check

**Skills Assessed:**
- Pre-reading skills
- Pre-math skills
- Language comprehension
- Following instructions
- Social maturity

**Parent Interview:** 20-30 minutes

## Assessment Format

### Written Assessment (Class 1+)

**Format:**
- Paper-based assessment
- Multiple choice, short answer, and extended response
- Age-appropriate length
- Breaks provided for younger students

**Content:**
- Grade-level appropriate material
- Based on Bangladesh curriculum expectations
- Fair and balanced across subjects
- Culturally appropriate content

### Oral Assessment (All Grades)

**Format:**
- One-on-one with assessor
- Conversation and questions
- Age-appropriate interaction

**Purpose:**
- Assess communication skills
- Evaluate understanding
- Observe confidence
- Check language proficiency

### Observation (PlayGroup-KG)

**Format:**
- Naturalistic observation
- Play-based activities
- Note-taking by assessor

**What's Observed:**
- Social interaction
- Language use
- Motor skills
- Attention span
- Creativity
- Problem-solving approaches

## Preparation Guidelines

### What Parents Should Do

**✓ DO:**
- Ensure child gets good sleep before assessment
- Provide healthy breakfast
- Arrive on time (15 minutes early)
- Bring required materials
- Stay calm and positive
- Encourage child to do their best
- Emphasize effort over results
- Follow normal routine

**✗ DON'T:**
- Over-prepare or pressure child
- Create stress or anxiety
- Make assessment seem like high-stakes test
- Compare child to others
- Promise rewards for "good performance"
- Coach answers or content
- Disrupt normal routine significantly
- Show anxiety to child

### What Students Should Know

**For Young Children (PlayGroup-KG):**
- You're going to play some games and activities
- The teacher wants to get to know you
- Just be yourself and have fun
- Listen carefully to instructions
- It's okay to ask questions

**For Primary Students (Class 1-5):**
- The assessment helps teachers understand how you learn
- Do your best but don't worry
- Take your time
- Read questions carefully
- Ask if you don't understand something
- It's not a pass-fail test

**For Secondary Students (Class 6-9):**
- This is one part of the admission process
- Show what you know and can do
- Think through problems carefully
- Manage your time
- Don't rush
- Stay calm and focused

### No Special Preparation Needed

**Important:**
- Smart Academy does not recommend or provide test preparation
- No coaching or tutoring needed
- Previous school learning is sufficient
- We assess natural ability and readiness
- Over-preparation can create unnecessary stress

## Assessment Day

### What to Bring

**Required:**
- Application acknowledgment letter
- Pencils (we provide, but student may bring their own)
- Eraser
- Sharpener
- Water bottle

**Optional:**
- Light sweater (if air-conditioned room)
- Tissue

**Do NOT Bring:**
- Calculators (not needed at this level)
- Smartwatches or electronic devices
- Books or study materials
- Mobile phones (student phones not permitted)

### Arrival and Check-In

**Arrival:**
- Arrive 15 minutes before scheduled time
- Check in at reception
- Present acknowledgment letter
- Wait in designated area

**For Parents:**
- Wait in designated waiting area
- Assessors will call when needed for interview
- May not stay in assessment room (except PlayGroup if needed)

### During Assessment

**Environment:**
- Quiet, comfortable room
- Small group of students (or individual for some grades)
- Breaks provided as needed
- Assessor available to clarify instructions

**For Students:**
- Listen carefully to instructions
- Ask if unclear about anything
- Take your time
- Do your best
- Stay calm and focused

**For Parents (During Interview):**
- Share information about your child openly
- Ask questions about Smart Academy
- Express expectations and concerns
- Discuss your child's strengths and needs

## Results and Decisions

### How Results Are Used

**Part of Holistic Review:**
- Assessment results: ~40% of decision
- Combined with other factors:
  - Previous academic record
  - Character and conduct
  - Family commitment
  - Special circumstances

**Not Pass/Fail:**
- No minimum passing score
- Overall readiness evaluated
- Strengths and needs considered
- Individual context matters

### Decision Categories

**1. Offer of Admission**
- Student demonstrates readiness
- Likely to succeed at Smart Academy
- Contributes positively to community
- Family commitment evident

**2. Waitlist**
- Student qualified and capable
- Space limitations
- May be admitted if space opens
- Usually notified by May-June

**3. Not Admitted**
- Concerns about readiness or fit
- Inability to provide needed support
- Better fit at another institution
- Decision is final

**4. Conditional Offer**
- Admission with conditions
- Usually academic support required
- Specific requirements outlined
- Review after specified period

### Notification Timeline

**Decision Notification:**
- Sent by March 15, 2026
- Email, SMS, and formal letter
- Clear explanation of decision
- Next steps provided

**Individual Results:**
- Detailed scores not shared
- No appeals on assessment results
- Decision is comprehensive, not based solely on assessment

## Special Considerations

### Students with Special Needs

**Accommodations:**
- Discuss needs before assessment day
- Appropriate accommodations provided when possible
- Assessment may be modified
- Honest discussion about fit

**Before Assessment:**
- Meet with special education coordinator
- Provide documentation of needs
- Discuss appropriate accommodations
- Understand what we can provide

### Late Applicants

**Assessment:**
- May be scheduled individually
- Different format or timing
- Process may be compressed
- Decision timeline may be shorter

### Distance Applicants

**Options:**
- In-person assessment strongly preferred
- May arrange assessment at current school (in some cases)
- Video assessment interview (secondary only, when necessary)
- Additional documentation may be required

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About Assessment

**Q: Does my child need special tutoring for the assessment?**
A: No, we assess natural readiness and learning, not prepared knowledge. Regular schooling is sufficient preparation.

**Q: What if my child is sick on assessment day?**
A: Contact us immediately. We'll reschedule if illness is genuine. Don't bring sick children.

**Q: Can parents stay in the room during assessment?**
A: For PlayGroup, if needed for child's comfort. For older grades, parents wait outside.

**Q: How are children with different educational backgrounds evaluated fairly?**
A: We assess potential and readiness, not specific curriculum knowledge. Context is considered in evaluation.

**Q: Do you share assessment scores with parents?**
A: No, individual scores are not shared. We provide admission decision based on holistic review.

**Q: What if my child performs poorly on assessment but has good grades?**
A: We consider the complete picture. One off day doesn't necessarily disqualify a qualified applicant.

**Q: Can assessments be retaken?**
A: No, assessments are conducted once per admission cycle. We get a complete picture in one session.

**Q: How much does assessment count toward admission?**
A: Approximately 40% of the decision. We also consider previous record, character, family commitment, and other factors.

**Q: Do you assess Islamic knowledge for non-Muslim applicants?**
A: We assess respect for Islamic practices and willingness to participate, not prior knowledge.
