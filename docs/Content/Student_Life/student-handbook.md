### Health Guidelines

**Daily Health**:
- Eat nutritious breakfast

- Get adequate sleep (8-10 hours)
- Practice good hygiene
- Stay home when sick

**Communicable Illness**:
- Stay home when contagious
- Report illness to school

- Complete medication before returning

**Mental Health**:
- Talk to trusted adult when struggling
- Utilize counseling services
- Practice stress management
- Maintain balance in activities
- Seek help when needed

### Bullying Prevention

**Definition**: Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance.

**Types**:
- Verbal: Teasing, name-calling, inappropriate comments
- Social: Excluding, spreading rumors, embarrassing
- Physical: Hitting, kicking, damaging property
- Cyber: Online harassment, social media bullying

**Smart Academy Has Zero Tolerance for Bullying**

**If You Are Bullied**:
1. Tell the bully to stop (clearly, confidently)
2. Walk away safely
3. Report immediately to teacher/counselor
4. Document incidents (what, when, where, who)
5. Know that you will be supported

**If You Witness Bullying**:
1. Don't join in or laugh
2. Support the victim
3. Report what you saw
4. Be a friend, not a bystander

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Academic Questions

**Q: What if I don't understand a lesson?**
A: Ask your teacher during class, stay after class for clarification, or come during extra help time. Don't stay confused!

**Q: How can I improve my grades?**
A: Attend every class, complete all homework, study regularly (not just before tests), ask for help, participate actively, and get enough sleep.

**Q: What if I fail a test?**
A: Don't panic. Talk to your teacher about what went wrong, get extra help, and focus on doing better next time. One test doesn't define you.

**Q: Can I change my class/section?**
A: Changes are rarely made and only for valid reasons. Talk to your teacher and the Principal if you have serious concerns.

### Social Questions

**Q: What if I don't have friends?**
A: Join clubs, participate in activities, be friendly to classmates, give it time. Making friends takes effort. Counselors can help too.

**Q: What if someone is mean to me?**
A: Tell them clearly to stop, avoid them when possible, report to teacher/counselor if it continues. You don't deserve to be treated poorly.

**Q: How do I handle conflict with a friend?**
A: Talk to them privately, use "I" statements, listen to their side, apologize if you're wrong, seek help from counselor if needed.

### Practical Questions

**Q: What if I forget my lunch?**
A: Tell your teacher. The school will help arrange something. Call parents if needed.

**Q: What if I lose something?**
A: Check the Lost & Found (in main office). Ask around where you last had it. Don't wait—look immediately.

**Q: Can I leave school early?**
A: Only for valid reasons and with parent permission. Parent must call or come to school.

**Q: What if I feel sick at school?**
A: Tell your teacher immediately. You'll be sent to the medical room. Parents will be contacted if you need to go home.
