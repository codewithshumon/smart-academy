## Philosophy of Service

### Islamic Foundation of Service

**Service as Worship**:
> *"The best among you are those who bring greatest benefit to humanity."* — Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

**Serving Others = Serving Allah**:
> *"And worship Allah and associate nothing with Him, and to parents do good, and to relatives, orphans, the needy, the near neighbor, the neighbor farther away, the companion at your side, the traveler, and those whom your right hands possess. Indeed, Allah does not like those who are self-deluding and boastful."* — Quran 4:36

**Key Principles**:
- **Khidma (Service)**: Helping others is obligatory
- **Akhlaq**: Character shown through service
- **Rahma**: Compassion for those in need
- **Adl**: Justice in serving all
- **Ihsan**: Excellence in service
- **Sadaqah**: Charity through action, not just wealth

### Our Commitment

**Every Smart Academy student will**:
- Participate in meaningful service
- Develop leadership skills
- Understand social responsibility
- Practice Islamic character through action
- Make a positive impact on community

## Community Service Programs

### Service Requirements

**Graduation Requirement**:
- Class 5: 10 hours minimum per year
- Class 6-8: 20 hours minimum per year
- Class 9: 30 hours minimum per year
- Encouraged: Exceed minimum significantly

**Tracking System**:
- Hours logged through student portal
- Verified by supervisor
- Annual service report card
- Recognition for outstanding service

### Service Opportunities

#### On-Campus Service

**Peer Tutoring Program**:
- Help classmates with academic subjects
- One-on-one or small group tutoring

- Training provided

**Campus Improvement**:
- Gardening and landscaping
- Recycling program
- Cleaning and beautification
- Library assistance
- Office help

**Event Support**:
- Set up and clean up for events
- Ushering and guiding guests
- Supporting school programs
- Assisting with ceremonies

#### Off-Campus Service

**Elderly Care**:

- Share talents and skills

**Education Support**:
- Tutoring at local schools
- Teaching Quran to neighborhood children
- Reading support programs
- Homework help clubs

**Environmental Initiatives**:
- Community clean-up days
- Tree planting campaigns
- Environmental education
- Recycling drives

**Social Services**:
- Food distribution
- Clothing drives
- Charity fundraising
- Emergency relief support

**Health Initiatives**:
- Blood donation campaigns (for eligible)
- Health education outreach
- First aid awareness
- Mental health advocacy

**Class Representatives**:

- Communicate council decisions to class

**Committees**:
- Events Committee
- Service Committee
- Spirit Committee
- Academic Committee
- Islamic Activities Committee

### Responsibilities

**Represent Student Voice**:
- Bring student concerns to administration
- Suggest improvements
- Gather input on decisions
- Communicate effectively

**Plan Activities**:
- Monthly student activities
- Spirit weeks and special days
- Service projects
- Educational programs

**Promote School Spirit**:
- Organize spirit activities
- Support school teams and programs
- Create positive school culture
- Include all students

**Serve the School**:
- Help with school functions
- Welcome new students
- Support school initiatives
- Model Islamic character

### Elections

**Election Process**:
- Nominations in February
- Campaign week (speeches, posters)
- Voting in March
- Winners announced before spring break
- Term: April-March (one year)

**Eligibility**:
- Good academic standing (2.5+ GPA)
- Good behavior record
- Demonstrated leadership potential
- Commitment to Islamic character
- Teacher recommendations

**Voting**:
- All students in Class 3-9 vote
- Secret ballot
- Vote for Head Boy/Girl and class representative
- Islamic character considered in voting

## Mentoring Programs

### Peer Mentoring

**Purpose**: Older students help younger students succeed

**Big Brother/Big Sister Program**:
- Class 7-9 students mentor Class 1-5 students
- Regular meetings and check-ins
- Academic support
- Social and emotional support
- Islamic character development

**Mentor Responsibilities**:
- Meet weekly with mentee
- Help with homework
- Be a positive role model
- Listen and support
- Report concerns to teachers
- Encourage Islamic practices

**Mentee Benefits**:
- Academic support
- Social connection
- Guidance from older student
- Role model relationship
- Increased confidence

### Academic Mentoring

**Subject-Specific Tutoring**:
- Strong students tutor those needing help
- Scheduled during lunch or after school
- Peer-to-peer teaching
- Builds both tutors' and tutees' skills

**Training Provided**:
- How to explain concepts
- Patience and encouragement
- When to ask for teacher help
- Building positive relationships

## Service Learning Model
