## Eid Celebrations Overview

### Significance of Eid

**Eid in Islam**:
- Days of celebration and gratitude
- Marks completion of blessed times (Ramadan, Hajj)
- Expression of joy and community
- Reminder of Allah's blessings
- Opportunity for unity and charity

**Eid at Smart Academy**:
- Celebrate Islamic festivals joyfully
- Educate about significance
- Create festive atmosphere
- Maintain Islamic boundaries
- Build community through celebration

### Celebration Philosophy

**Within Islamic Boundaries**:
- Joy without extravagance
- Celebration within Islamic limits
- No un-Islamic practices
- Focus on gratitude, worship, community
- Charity and remembering those less fortunate

## Eid ul-Fitr Celebration

### School-Based Celebration

**Timing**:
- Before or after Eid (depending on school calendar)
- During school day
- Special assembly and programs

**Date**: Last school day before Eid break (March 19, 2026 anticipated)

### Program Components

#### Special Assembly (8:00-9:30 AM)

**Opening**:
- Quran recitation (Quran competition winners)
- Nasheed performances
- Eid takbirat

**Educational Component**:
- Explanation of Eid ul-Fitr significance
- Ramadan reflection
- Importance of Zakat ul-Fitr
- Islamic reminder about celebration

**Student Performances**:
- Nasheed performances by Nasheed Group
- Class presentations about Ramadan and Eid
- Short skits about Eid practices
- Islamic poetry recitation

#### Classroom Activities (9:30 AM - 12:00 PM)

**Age-Appropriate Activities**:

**PlayGroup-KG**:
- Eid coloring activities
- Simple nasheeds
- Eid treats
- Stories about Eid

**Class 1-5**:
- Eid card making
- Islamic art projects
- Nasheed practice
- Class party (modest, halal treats)

**Class 6-9**:
- Eid presentations
- Islamic discussions
- Community service planning
- Reflection on Ramadan growth

#### Eid Treats and Food

**Halal Treats**:
- School-provided modest refreshments
- Students may bring treats to share
- All food halal
- Emphasis on sharing (not excess)
- No food waste

### Dress Guidelines

**Eid Dress Options**:
- Students may wear Eid clothes to school
- Must follow Islamic guidelines (modest)
- Cultural dress encouraged (Bengali traditional)
- School uniform also perfectly acceptable
- No pressure or competition for fancy clothes

**Guidelines**:
- Modest and simple (not extravagant)
- Comfortable for school activities
- Respectful of school environment
- No competition or showing off

### Gift-Giving (Optional)

**Modest Exchange**:
- Small, meaningful gifts optional
- No expectation or pressure
- Focus on sharing, not materialism
- Class gift exchange may be organized
- Charity emphasized over gifts

## Eid ul-Adha Observance

### School-Based Activities

**Timing**:
- Before Eid (typically)
- Educational focus
- Hajj simulation
- Eid celebration

**Date**: Last school day before Eid break (June 11, 2026 anticipated)

### Educational Programs

#### Special Assembly (8:00-9:30 AM)

**Hajj Focus**:
- Explanation of Hajj rituals
- Significance of Eid ul-Adha
- Story of Prophet Ibrahim (AS)
- Importance of sacrifice and submission to Allah

**Activities**:
- Hajj simulation activity
- Visual presentations on Makkah
- Student presentations on Hajj
- Islamic songs about Hajj

#### Hajj Simulation

**Educational Activity**:
- Walk through Hajj rituals symbolically
- Wear ihram (white cloth for boys who've reached puberty)
- Perform symbolic tawaf (circumambulation)
- Sa'i simulation
- Understand Rami al-Jamarat (stoning of devil)
- Learn about sacrifice

#### Educational Value**:
- Understand Hajj rites
- Prepare for future Hajj
- Appreciate Hajj obligation
- Connect to Islamic history
- Visualize sacred journey

## Ramadan Activities

### Special Ramadan Schedule

**Modified School Day**:
- Shortened periods during Ramadan
- School day: 8:00 AM - 1:30 PM (or 2:00 PM depending on year)
- Reduced homework
- Physical activities minimized
- Emphasis on spiritual activities

**For Fasting Students**:
- No PE or sports during fasting hours
- Quran recitation instead of vigorous activity
- Adjusted expectations for physical participation
- Quiet activities during breaks

**For Non-Fasting Students**:
- Regular activities continued
- Respectful of fasting students
- Quiet during lunch time
- Understanding of Ramadan significance

### Ramadan Programs

**Daily Activities**:
- Extra emphasis on Quran recitation
- Islamic reminders throughout day
- Dua and Dhikr opportunities
- Increased focus on Islamic Studies
- Charity drives and collections

**Weekly Activities**:
- Islamic lectures during lunch
- Quran competitions
- Iftar programs (for students staying late)
- Taraweeh prayer information
- Ramadan reflection activities

**Special Events**:
- Ramadan assembly (beginning of Ramadan)
- Quran recitation emphasis
- Last 10 days special programs
- Laylatul Qadr observance
- Eid preparation

## Islamic Holiday Policies

### School Closure

**Eid Breaks**:
- School closed for Eid ul-Fitr (typically 3-6 days)
- School closed for Eid ul-Adha (typically 2-3 days)
- Exact dates based on moon sighting
- Parents notified via SMS/email

**Academic Considerations**:
- No major tests or exams scheduled near Eid breaks
- Homework minimized before Eid
- Make-up work provided for extended absences
- Flexibility for families traveling

### Religious Observances

**Other Islamic Occasions**:
- Special assemblies for important Islamic events
- Educational programs about Islamic history
- Milad un-Nabi (Prophet's birthday) - educational focus on Seerah
- Islamic New Year - assemblies and education
- Day of Aashura - educational programs
- Isra and Mi'raj - special programs

**Policy**:
- Schools remain open for most Islamic days
- Special assemblies or programs acknowledge significance
- Educational focus over celebration
- Islamic values reinforced
