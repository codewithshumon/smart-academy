## Overview

At Smart Academy, our daily schedule is thoughtfully designed to balance academic rigor, spiritual development, physical activity, and social interaction. The structured routine provides students with predictability while allowing for engaging, varied activities throughout the day.

### Schedule Principles

**✓ Balance**: Academic work, physical activity, spiritual practices, and social time
**✓ Age-Appropriate**: Different schedules for different grade levels
**✓ Islamic Integration**: Prayer times and Islamic practices built into the day
**✓ Efficiency**: Minimal wasted time, smooth transitions
**✓ Flexibility**: Adjustments for special events, seasons, and needs

---

## Daily Schedule Structure

### Standard School Day: 8:00 AM - 3:45 PM

**Total Instructional Time**: 6 hours per day
**Breaks**: 2 breaks (mid-morning and lunch)
**Prayer**: 2 congregational prayers (Dhuhr, Asr)
**Physical Activity**: Daily physical education or sports

## Break Times & Activities

### Mid-Morning Break (10:15-10:35 AM / 9:50-10:10 AM)

**Purpose**: Physical activity, nutrition, social interaction

**Activities Available**:
- Healthy snack consumption
- Socializing with peers
- Quick outdoor play (younger students)
- Restroom breaks
- Preparation for next classes

**Guidelines**:
- Students must remain in designated areas
- Healthy snacks encouraged
- No running in hallways
- Respectful interaction with peers
- Return to class on time

### Lunch Break (11:40 AM - 12:30 PM)

**Components**:
- **Dhuhr Prayer** (first 15 minutes): Congregational prayer for all
- **Lunch** (25 minutes): Nutritious meal in cafeteria or brought from home
- **Social Time** (remaining time): Interaction with friends

**Lunch Options**:
- School cafeteria (halal meals)
- Packed lunch from home
- Combination of both

**Lunchtime Guidelines**:
- Begin with "Bismillah"
- Eat in moderation (Sunnah practice)
- Clean up after eating
- Socialize respectfully
- Include everyone in conversation
- Respect food and avoid waste

## Prayer Times

### Dhuhr Prayer (12:30 PM)

**Procedure**:
1. Wudu preparation (beginning 12:15 PM)
2. Call to prayer (Adhan)
3. Congregational prayer (separate for boys and girls)
4. Brief Islamic reminder (2-3 minutes)
5. Dismissal for lunch

**Attendance**: Required for all Muslim students

**Accommodations**: Non-Muslim students have quiet reading time

### Asr Prayer (2:45 PM)

**Procedure**:
1. Wudu preparation
2. Call to prayer
3. Congregational prayer
4. End-of-day announcements (2-3 minutes)
5. Dismissal

**Timing**: Adjusted seasonally based on actual Asr time

### Friday Special Schedule

**Jumu'ah Prayer**:
- Secondary boys (Class 6-9): Attend Jumu'ah at nearby mosque
- Modified schedule for Friday
- Early dismissal for those attending Jumu'ah
- Islamic assembly during prayer time for others

## Schedule Guidelines

### Arrival & Departure

**Arrival**:
- Students should arrive by 7:50 AM
- Late arrivals after 8:00 AM marked as late
- Three lates equal one absence
- Parents should notify school of anticipated lateness

**Departure**:
- Students depart after Asr prayer (3:00 PM)
- Early departure requires parent permission
- Students must be picked up promptly
- Late pickup students wait in designated area

### Attendance Expectations

**Minimum Attendance**: 90% required for promotion

**Reporting Absence**:
- Parent calls school before 8:00 AM
- Written note upon return
- Medical certificate for absences over 3 days
- Make-up work arranged by teachers

### Schedule Changes

**Weather-Related**:
- Heavy rain: Modified indoor activities
- Extreme heat: Reduced outdoor activities
- Natural disasters: School closure announced via SMS/app

**Special Events**:
- Exam schedules modify regular timetable
- Event days may have special schedules
- Parent notification in advance of changes

**Ramadan Schedule**:
- Shortened periods during Ramadan
- Modified lunch break (for fasting students)
- Earlier dismissal during Ramadan
- Iftar arrangements for extended-day students

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Schedule-Related Questions

**Q: What if my child misses the bus?**
A: Call the transport office immediately at +880170-9651170. Alternative arrangements will be made.

**Q: Can I pick up my child early?**
A: Yes, for valid reasons. Send a note in advance or call the office. Early pickup should be minimized to avoid missing important instruction.

**Q: How is lunchtime supervised?**
A: Teachers on duty supervise lunch. Islamic Studies teachers lead prayers. Staff ensures all students eat appropriately and safely.

**Q: What happens during Ramadan?**
A: Schedule is modified for fasting students with earlier dismissal. Non-fasting students follow regular schedule with accommodations.

**Q: Are there tutoring sessions after school?**
A: Yes, teachers offer extra help after school. Check with individual teachers for their availability.

**Q: How are prayer times managed?**
A: Dhuhr prayer is during lunch break, Asr before dismissal. All Muslim students participate. Beautiful prayer facilities are provided.
