## Introduction

### As-Salamu Alaikum!

At Smart Academy, education extends far beyond textbooks and classrooms. We believe that **true learning happens through doing**, and our comprehensive co-curricular program provides diverse opportunities for students to discover their passions, develop new skills, and grow into well-rounded individuals.

### What Are Co-Curricular Activities?

**Co-curricular activities** are learning experiences that complement the formal curriculum. They are not "extra"—they are **essential components** of holistic education that:

- Apply classroom learning to real-world situations
- Develop skills not easily taught in traditional classrooms
- Provide opportunities for leadership and collaboration
- Nurture talents and interests beyond academics
- Build character and confidence
- Create joyful, memorable school experiences

### Our Commitment

Smart Academy is committed to providing:
- **Diverse opportunities** for every interest and ability
- **Quality instruction** from qualified mentors
- **Islamic environment** in all activities
- **Inclusive access** for all students
- **Balance** between academics and activities

## Time Commitment & Balance

### Typical Time Requirements

**Clubs**:
- **Meeting Frequency**: Once or twice weekly
- **Duration**: 45-60 minutes per meeting
- **Additional Time**: Some preparation or practice outside meetings

**Sports Teams**:
- **Practice Frequency**: 2-3 times weekly
- **Duration**: 60-90 minutes per practice
- **Additional Time**: Matches, tournaments, some weekend activities

**Competitive Activities**:
- **Time Commitment**: Varies by competition level
- **Additional Practice**: Often requires extra individual practice
- **Events**: Some competitions occur on weekends

### Finding Balance

**Guidelines for Healthy Balance**:

**PlayGroup-KG**:
- Focus on play-based activities
- Minimal scheduled commitments
- Exposure to various activities through school day
- Parent-child activities at home

**Class 1-3**:
- 1-2 activities maximum
- Focus on exposure and enjoyment
- Not more than 2 afternoons per week
- Plenty of free play time

**Class 4-5**:
- 2-3 activities maximum
- Begin developing some specialization
- Not more than 3 afternoons per week
- Balance between active and less active activities

**Class 6-9**:
- 3-4 activities possible
- More specialization based on interests
- Careful time management required
- Must maintain academic performance

### Signs of Over-Commitment

**Watch For**:
- Declining grades
- Increased stress or anxiety
- Difficulty sleeping
- Little free time or relaxation
- Loss of enjoyment in activities
- Frequently rushing or exhausted

**If Over-Committed**:
- Reevaluate priorities
- Consider reducing one activity
- Ensure adequate sleep and rest
- Maintain family time
- Don't sacrifice academics for activities

## Enrollment & Participation

### How to Join Activities

**Annual Activity Fair** (January):
- All clubs and activities represented
- Students and parents can learn about options
- Sign-up opportunities
- Meet coaches and club leaders

**Ongoing Enrollment**:
- Contact club sponsor or coach
- Attend a trial meeting/practice
- Complete enrollment form
- Pay any applicable fees (some activities have fees)

### Eligibility Requirements

**Academic Standing**:
- Maintaining minimum GPA (varies by activity level)
- Passing all subjects
- Good attendance record
- Teacher recommendations for some activities

**Behavioral Expectations**:
- Good behavior record
- No major disciplinary issues
- Commitment to representing school well
- Following Islamic code of conduct

### Attendance Policy

**Regular Attendance Required**:
- Students must attend regularly
- Unexcused absences affect participation
- Communication with sponsor/coach for conflicts
- Commitment for full term/activity season expected

**Academic Conflicts**:
- Academics take priority
- Communicate with teachers and activity sponsors
- Find balance, don't abandon academics
- Extra help available if needed

## Getting Started

### Steps to Get Involved

1. **Explore Options**: Review available activities, talk to teachers and peers
2. **Consider Interests**: What do you enjoy? What are you curious about?
3. **Check Requirements**: Is there eligibility criteria? Time commitment?
4. **Attend Activity Fair**: January - learn about all options firsthand
5. **Sign Up**: Complete enrollment process
6. **Commit**: Attend regularly, participate fully, do your best
7. **Evaluate**: After a term, reflect - is this activity right for you?
