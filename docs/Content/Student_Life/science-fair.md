## Project Guidelines

### Scientific Method

**Required Steps**:

1. **Question**: What do you want to find out?
2. **Research**: Background information
3. **Hypothesis**: What do you think will happen?
4. **Experiment**: Test your hypothesis
5. **Data**: Collect and record information
6. **Analysis**: What do the data show?
7. **Conclusion**: Was your hypothesis correct?
8. **Application**: How does this help?

### Project Requirements

**Original Investigation**:
- Student-conducted experiment
- Not just demonstration or model
- Data collected and analyzed
- Clear variables identified

**Display Board**:
- Tri-fold display (standard size)
- All required sections included
- Clear and organized
- Visually appealing

**Research Paper** (Class 6-9):
- 3-5 pages (Class 6-7) or 5-8 pages (Class 8-9)
- Background research
- Methodology
- Data and analysis
- Conclusion and bibliography

**Presentation**:
- 3-5 minute explanation
- Answer judges' questions
- Demonstrate understanding

## Judging Criteria

### Evaluation Categories

**Scientific Thought** (30 points):
- Clear question and hypothesis
- Well-designed experiment
- Variables properly controlled
- Data accurately collected and analyzed
- Conclusion supported by data

**Creativity and Innovation** (20 points):
- Original project idea
- Creative approach
- Novel application
- Innovative solution

**Presentation and Display** (20 points):
- Clear, organized display
- Thorough understanding shown
- Good communication skills
- Professional appearance

**Research and Understanding** (15 points):
- Background research evident
- Literature review included
- Scientific understanding
- Context provided

**Effort and Learning** (15 points):
- Student work evident
- Learning demonstrated
- Challenges addressed
- Growth shown

## Day of Event

### Thursday - Judging Day

**Schedule**:
- **7:30 AM**: Project setup
- **8:30 AM**: Judges arrive, briefing
- **9:00 AM**: Judging begins
- **12:00 PM**: Lunch break
- **1:00 PM**: Judging continues
- **3:00 PM**: Judging ends, projects stay in place

**For Students**:
- Arrive on time (7:30 AM)
- Wear school uniform
- Stand by project during assigned judging times
- Be prepared to explain project multiple times
- Answer questions clearly and honestly

**What Judges Ask**:
- Tell me about your project
- What was your question/hypothesis?
- How did you conduct your experiment?
- What were your results?
- What would you do differently?
- Why did you choose this topic?
- What did you learn?

### Friday - Public Viewing and Awards

**Public Viewing** (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):
- Students, parents, and visitors welcome
- Students stand by projects
- Explain projects to visitors
- No formal evaluation

**Awards Ceremony** (2:00-3:00 PM):
- All students and parents attend
- Awards presented
- Guest speaker (scientist or engineer)
- Recognition of participation

## Parent Involvement

### Supporting Your Student

**Encourage**:
- Interest and curiosity
- Independent work (student does project)
- Problem-solving over perfect results
- Learning from mistakes

**Provide**:
- Materials and supplies (within reason)
- Transportation to library for research
- Space for project work
- Time for project completion

**Avoid**:
- Doing the work for student
- Focusing only on winning
- Over-criticizing
- Comparing to other students

### Attending the Fair

**Thursday**:
- Public viewing not available (judging only)

**Friday**:
- Attend public viewing (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
- Attend awards ceremony (2:00-3:00 PM)
- Visit multiple projects
- Ask students questions
- Celebrate all participants

## Safety Guidelines

### Experiment Safety

**Required**:
- Teacher approval of experimental design
- Safety equipment when needed
- Adult supervision for hazardous materials
- No dangerous chemicals or procedures
- No harm to animals

**Prohibited**:
- Growth of bacteria/mold without teacher supervision
- Dangerous chemicals
- Experiments that could cause harm
- Projects involving vertebrate animals
- Fire hazards
