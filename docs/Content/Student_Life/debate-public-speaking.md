## Debate Program

### What is Debate?

**Structured Argumentation**:
- Two sides argue opposing positions
- Topic announced in advance
- Research and preparation required
- Judges evaluate arguments
- Focus on logic and evidence, not emotion

### Debate Formats

**Modified Parliamentary Debate**:
- Teams of 2-3 students
- Prepared topics
- 15-20 minute rounds
- Points of Information allowed
- Emphasis on logic and rhetoric

**Policy Debate**:
- Focus on specific policy proposals
- Evidence-based arguments
- Detailed research required
- Longer preparation time
- Advanced debaters

**Public Forum Debate**:
- Audience-friendly format
- Accessible to observers
- Contemporary topics
- Balances logic and persuasion

### Debate Topics

**Categories**:
- **Education Policy**: School uniforms, homework, testing
- **Social Issues**: Technology use, media influence, privacy
- **Environmental Policy**: Climate change, conservation, development
- **International Relations**: Foreign policy, global issues
- **Islamic Topics**: (When appropriate) Islamic perspectives on issues
- **Ethics and Values**: Moral dilemmas, character questions

**Guidelines for Topics**:
- Age-appropriate complexity
- Balance between sides
- Educational value
- Islamic considerations
- Student interest

## Public Speaking Program

### Types of Speaking

**Informative Speaking**:
- Teach audience about topics
- Research-based presentations
- Clear organization
- Educational focus

**Persuasive Speaking**:
- Convince audience of position
- Emotional appeal combined with logic
- Call to action
- Evidence-based arguments

**Extemporaneous Speaking**:
- Limited preparation time
- Current events topics
- Quick thinking required
- Organized under pressure

**Original Oratory**:
- Student-written speeches
- Personal experiences and insights
- 7-10 minute speeches
- Memorable and engaging

**Impromptu Speaking**:
- No preparation time
- 2-3 minute speeches
- Topics drawn randomly
- Tests quick thinking

### Speech Structure

**Introduction**:
- Hook to gain attention
- Statement of topic
- Thesis or main point
- Preview of speech

**Body**:
- 2-3 main points
- Evidence and examples
- Transitions between points
- Logical organization

**Conclusion**:
- Summary of main points
- Memorable closing statement
- Call to action if appropriate
- Thank audience
