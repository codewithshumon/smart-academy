## Types of Support

### Classroom Differentiation

**What Teachers Do**:
- Adapt teaching methods to student needs
- Vary instructional strategies
- Provide multiple ways to learn
- Adjust pace and complexity
- Use multi-sensory instruction

**Examples**:
- Visual aids and graphic organizers
- Hands-on activities
- Group work and peer support
- Technology integration
- Flexible grouping

### Small Group Instruction

**Pull-Out Support**:
- Students work in small groups outside regular class
- Targeted skill instruction
- Reinforcement of classroom learning
- Intensive support in specific areas

**Push-In Support**:
- Support teacher works in classroom
- Small group within class
- Helps students access curriculum
- Collaborates with classroom teacher

### Peer Tutoring

**Student Tutors**:
- Older students tutor younger students
- Same-age peer tutoring
- Trained in tutoring strategies
- Supervised by teachers

**Benefits**:
- Reinforces learning for tutors
- Builds relationships
- Provides role models
- Alternative explanation methods

## Monitoring Progress

### Regular Assessment

**Ongoing Monitoring**:
- Daily classroom assessment
- Weekly skill checks
- Monthly progress monitoring
- Term evaluations

**Progress Measurement**:
- Compare student to self, not others
- Monitor growth over time
- Adjust interventions based on data
- Communicate progress to parents

### Response to Intervention (RTI)

**Tiered Support**:
- **Tier 1**: Quality classroom instruction for all
- **Tier 2**: Small group interventions for some
- **Tier 3**: Intensive individual support for few

**Process**:
- Screen all students
- Monitor progress
- Provide increasing support as needed
- Refer for evaluation if not responsive
