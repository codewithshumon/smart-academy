## Introduction

### As-Salamu Alaikum, Future Club Members!

Clubs and societies at Smart Academy are where **passions come alive**, **friendships are formed**, and **skills are developed** beyond the classroom. Whether you want to explore a new interest, develop a talent, or make a difference, there's a club for you!

### What Are Clubs?

Clubs are **student-led groups** that meet regularly to explore shared interests. They provide:

- Hands-on learning experiences
- Leadership opportunities
- Social connections with like-minded peers
- Skill development in specialized areas
- Opportunities to represent Smart Academy
- Fun and meaningful activities

### Club Philosophy

**Student-Centered**: Clubs are driven by student interests and leadership

**Learning-Focused**: Every club teaches valuable skills

**Inclusive**: All students are welcome to participate

**Islamic**: All club activities align with Islamic values

**Service-Oriented**: Clubs contribute to school and community

## Starting a New Club

### Proposal Process

**1. Develop Your Idea**:
- What is the club's purpose?
- Who would be interested in joining?
- What activities would the club do?
- How often would you meet?

**2. Find a Sponsor**:
- Approach a teacher who shares your interest
- Explain your vision
- Confirm their willingness to support

**3. Write a Proposal**:
- Club name and mission
- Purpose and goals
- Target membership
- Meeting schedule
- Resource needs
- Proposed activities

**4. Submit Proposal**:
- Submit to Student Activities Coordinator
- Present to Student Council or Administration
- Be prepared to answer questions

**5. Approval Process**:
- Review by administration
- Consideration of resources and appropriateness
- Decision within 2 weeks
- If approved, help with startup

### Requirements for New Clubs

**Minimum Requirements**:
- At least 10 interested students
- Faculty sponsor willing to support
- Clear purpose aligned with school values
- Regular meeting schedule
- Beneficial activities planned

**Not Approved**:
- Clubs that conflict with Islamic values
- Activities that are unsafe
- Clubs that duplicate existing clubs without clear differentiation
- Clubs that school cannot support (resources, supervision)

## Club Policies

### Membership

**Open Membership**:
- Most clubs are open to all students in appropriate grade range
- No discrimination based on ability
- Tryouts only for competitive teams/performance groups
- Everyone welcome to explore

**Joining a Club**:
- Attend first meeting of interest
- Complete club enrollment form
- Commit to regular attendance
- Pay any applicable fees (some clubs have small fees for materials)

### Attendance

**Expected Attendance**:
- Regular attendance required
- Notify sponsor if unable to attend
- Three unexcused absences may result in removal
- Active participation expected

**Academic Priority**:
- Academics come first
- Students may be asked to limit club activities if grades suffer
- Support available to balance academics and activities

### Leadership

**Club Officer Positions**:
- President: Leads meetings, coordinates activities
- Vice President: Assists president, leads when president absent
- Secretary: Takes notes, handles communications
- Treasurer: Manages any funds (with sponsor oversight)
- Event Coordinators: Plan specific activities

**Elections**:
- Held annually (usually March)
- All members vote
- Terms: One academic year
- Can run for re-election

### Conduct

**All Club Members Must**:
- Follow Smart Academy Code of Conduct
- Represent school positively
- Respect sponsor and fellow members
- Take care of club materials and equipment
- Participate actively and positively

**Consequences for Misconduct**:
- Warning from sponsor
- Meeting with sponsor and parent
- Removal from club
- May affect eligibility for other clubs

### Funding

**Club Dues**:
- Some clubs have small dues (BDT 100-300 per year)
- Covers materials, supplies, activities
- Scholarships available for financial need
- All money handled through school office

**Fundraising**:
- Clubs may fundraise for specific purposes
- Must follow school fundraising guidelines
- Approval required from administration
- Funds held in school account
