## Peer Mentoring Program

### Big Brother/Big Sister Program

**Structure**:
- Older students mentor younger students
- Class 7-9 students mentor Class 1-5 students
- Regular meetings throughout year
- Supervised by school counselor

**Matching Process**:
- Mentors selected based on character and maturity
- Mentees identified who would benefit
- Careful consideration of compatibility
- Parent consent required
- Trial period to assess fit

**Activities**:
- Academic help (homework, studying)
- Social support (friendship skills)
- Adjustment support (new students)
- Islamic character development
- Special projects together

**Training for Mentors**:
- Communication skills
- Boundaries and ethics
- When to ask for help
- How to support effectively
- Islamic character in mentoring

**Monitoring**:
- Regular check-ins with counselor
- Mentor meetings (monthly)
- Parent feedback
- Ongoing support

## Policies and Guidelines

### Mentoring Boundaries

**Appropriate Boundaries**:
- Public meetings only (private spaces not appropriate)
- School-sponsored activities
- Parent awareness and consent
- Supervised by staff
- Clear guidelines for interaction

### Selection and Training

**Mentor Qualities**:
- Strong Islamic character
- Responsible and mature
- Good academic standing
- Kindness and patience
- Willingness to serve
- Commitment to program

**Training Requirements**:
- Attend mentor training
- Understand guidelines and boundaries
- Learn communication skills
- Know when to seek help
- Understand reporting requirements
