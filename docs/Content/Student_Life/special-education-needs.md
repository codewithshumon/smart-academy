## SEN Overview

### What is Special Education?

**Definition**: Educational support for students with diverse learning needs that require individualized instruction and accommodations.

**Diverse Learning Needs**:
- Learning disabilities
- Attention challenges (ADHD)
- Autism spectrum
- Physical disabilities
- Sensory impairments
- Emotional and behavioral difficulties
- Gifted and talented students
- Other learning differences

### Smart Academy's Commitment

**Inclusive Education**:
- Every student can learn
- Diversity is valued and respected
- Individual needs addressed
- Accessible education for all
- Islamic commitment to serve all children

**Services Provided**:
- Individualized support plans
- Accommodations and modifications
- Specialized instruction
- Collaborative teaching
- Resource room support
- External referrals when needed

## Accommodations and Modifications

### Accommodations vs. Modifications

**Accommodations**:
- Changes in how student learns
- Same content, different presentation
- Same expectation, different access
- Examples: Extended time, preferential seating

**Modifications**:
- Changes in what student learns
- Different content or expectations
- Individualized goals
- Examples: Reduced assignments, alternative curriculum

### Common Accommodations

**Instructional**:
- Extended time for assignments and tests
- Written and verbal instructions
- Graphic organizers and outlines
- Checklists for multi-step tasks
- Note-taking assistance
- Breaks during work periods

**Environmental**:
- Seat near teacher
- Quiet work area
- Minimal distractions
- Natural lighting
- Accessible materials

**Assessment**:
- Extended test time (up to 50% or as determined)
- Tests read aloud (unless reading is being tested)
- Scribe for written responses
- Frequent breaks
- Alternative setting

**Assignment**:
- Reduced number of problems
- Alternative response formats
- chunking assignments
- Clear rubrics and expectations
- Checklist for completion

## Process and Procedures

### Referral Process

1. **Concern Expressed** by parent, teacher, or student
2. **Documentation** of concerns and interventions tried
3. **Screening** to determine if evaluation needed
4. **Evaluation** with parent consent
5. **Eligibility Determination** by team
6. **IEP Development** if eligible
7. **Service Delivery** and monitoring
8. **Annual Review** of progress and planning

### Documentation

**Records Maintained**:
- Referral forms
- Evaluation reports
- IEPs and progress reports
- Communication logs
- Parent consent forms
- Confidential and protected
