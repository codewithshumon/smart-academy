## About Science Club

### Mission

> "To inspire wonder about Allah's creation, develop scientific thinking skills, and nurture the next generation of Muslim scientists who will use knowledge to serve humanity."

### Philosophy

**Science as Islamic Pursuit**:
- Studying Allah's creation is an act of worship
- Scientific thinking strengthens faith
- Knowledge leads to appreciation of divine wisdom
- Science serves humanity when used ethically
- Muslim scientists contributed greatly to human knowledge

**The Quran and Science**:
> *"Indeed, in the creation of the heavens and the earth and the alternation of the night and day are signs for people of understanding."* — Quran 3:190

**Key Principles**:
- **Curiosity**: Wonder about creation
- **Inquiry**: Ask questions and seek answers
- **Evidence**: Base conclusions on observation and data
- **Ethics**: Use science for good, not harm
- **Tawheed**: See unity in natural laws (Allah's sunnah)

### Membership

**Meeting**: Every Tuesday, 3:30-4:30 PM

## Projects and Experiments

### Primary Projects (Class 3-5)

**Sample Projects**:
- Growing plants from seeds
- Simple circuits
- Water filtration experiments
- Magnet exploration
- Weather observation
- Kitchen chemistry

**Skills Developed**:
- Observation
- Data recording
- Basic experimental design
- Drawing conclusions
- Presentation skills

### Secondary Projects (Class 6-9)

**Advanced Projects**:
- Renewable energy models
- Microscopic studies
- Chemical analysis
- Physics investigations
- Environmental impact studies
- Technology innovations

**Scientific Process**:
1. **Question**: What do we want to know?
2. **Research**: Background information
3. **Hypothesis**: Predicted outcome
4. **Experiment**: Test the hypothesis
5. **Data**: Collect and record
6. **Analysis**: Interpret results
7. **Conclusion**: What did we learn?
8. **Communication**: Share findings
