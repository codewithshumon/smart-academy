## Cultural Programs Overview

### Philosophy

**Islamic Cultural Identity**:
- Celebrate culture within Islamic boundaries
- Appreciation for diversity
- Respect for all cultures
- No blind imitation of un-Islamic practices
- Balance between tradition and faith

**Cultural Expression**:
- Art, music, drama, literature
- Language and poetry
- Traditional dress and customs
- Festivals and celebrations
- Culinary traditions

### Goals

**For Students**:
- Appreciate Bengali culture and heritage
- Respect diverse cultural backgrounds
- Express creativity appropriately
- Develop cultural knowledge and pride
- Share culture with others

## Annual Cultural Program

### Event Details

**Date**: Third Friday of September (September 18, 2026)
**Time**: 3:00 PM - 8:00 PM
**Venue**: School Auditorium
**Participants**: All students
**Audience**: Parents, families, community members

### Program Components

#### 1. Drama Production

**Major Theatrical Performance**:
- 45-60 minute play
- Class 6-9 performers
- Student-directed where appropriate
- Professional staging and lighting

**Recent/Future Productions**:
- Historical plays featuring Muslim heroes
- Stories with moral lessons
- Bengali folk tales adapted for stage
- Original student-written plays

#### 2. Nasheed Performance

**Islamic Vocal Music**:
- Nasheed Group performances
- Group and solo performances
- Arabic, Bengali, and English nasheeds
- Focus on vocal skill and Islamic message

#### 3. Dance and Movement

**Cultural Dance** (Islamic Guidelines):
- Bengali folk dance
- No inappropriate movements
- Modest costumes required
- Cultural expression within Islamic boundaries

#### 4. Art Exhibition

**Display of Student Art**:
- Visual arts from all grades
- Islamic art and calligraphy
- Cultural themes
- Sculpture and crafts

#### 5. Talent Show

**Student Talents Showcase**:
- Various performances
- Auditions required
- Acts screened for appropriateness
- Opportunity for hidden talents

#### 6. Awards and Recognition

**Cultural Achievement Awards**:
- Best Performer (various categories)
- Best Art Display
- Cultural Contribution Award
- Most Improved Performer

## Guidelines for Cultural Activities

### Islamic Principles

**Permissible Cultural Expression**:
- Art and music within Islamic boundaries
- Modest dress and behavior
- No inappropriate content
- Respect for Islamic values
- Avoid imitation of un-Islamic cultural practices

**Music Guidelines**:
- Nasheeds and Islamic songs only
- No musical instruments (except daff/drum)
- Focus on vocal music
- Islamic lyrics and themes
- Modest performance style

**Dance Guidelines**:
- Folk dances with modest movements
- No inappropriate touching or movements
- Separate performances for males and females when appropriate
- Modest costumes
- Cultural, not immodest, expression

**Drama Guidelines**:
- Islamic moral content
- No inappropriate scenes
- Modest costumes and behavior
- Separate gender roles when appropriate
- Educational and moral value

## Logistics

### Event Preparation

**Rehearsals**:
- Drama: After school, August-September
- Nasheed: Weekly throughout year
- Talent show: September rehearsals
- All practices: 3:30-5:00 PM typically

**Costume and Set Preparation**:
- School provides basic items
- Parents may need to provide some costume elements
- Volunteers help with sets and props

### Day of Event

**Schedule**:
- Setup: Morning/early afternoon
- Performer call time: 2 hours before show
- Audience seating: 30 minutes before show
- Program start: As scheduled

**Food and Refreshments**:
- Halal snacks available for purchase
- Water provided
- No food in auditorium during performance
