## Counseling Services

### Individual Counseling

**What It Is**:
- One-on-one meetings with counselor
- Safe space to discuss concerns
- Confidential conversations
- Support and guidance
- Problem-solving and skill-building

**Common Topics**:
- Academic stress and pressure
- Friendship issues
- Family concerns
- Emotional challenges
- Anxiety and worry
- Sadness and grief
- Self-esteem
- Goal setting
- Decision making

**How It Works**:
1. **Request**: Student, parent, or teacher requests counseling
2. **Intake**: Initial meeting to understand concerns
3. **Planning**: Set goals for counseling
4. **Sessions**: Regular meetings (usually 30-45 minutes)
5. **Progress**: Work toward goals
6. **Closure**: Successful resolution or referral

**Frequency**:
- Short-term: 1-4 sessions
- Medium-term: 5-10 sessions
- Long-term: As needed, with parent consent

### Group Counseling

**What It Is**:
- Small groups (4-8 students)
- Meet regularly for 6-8 sessions
- Common concern or topic
- Peer support and learning
- Facilitated by counselor

**Group Topics**:
- **Friendship Skills**: Making and keeping friends
- **Study Skills**: Academic strategies
- **Anxiety Management**: Coping with worry
- **Grief Support**: Dealing with loss
- **Social Skills**: Interpersonal effectiveness
- **Transitions**: Adjusting to change
- **Divorce/Changes**: Family changes support

**Benefits**:
- Not alone in concerns
- Learn from peers
- Practice skills in group
- Supportive environment
- Different perspectives

### Academic Counseling

**Services**:
- Study skills development
- Time management
- Test anxiety reduction
- Motivation strategies
- Academic goal setting
- Learning challenges support
- Transition support (new students, grade changes)

### College and Career Counseling

**For Secondary Students** (Class 6-9):
- Career exploration
- Interest inventories
- High school preparation
- Goal setting for future
- Skills development for career readiness

### Crisis Intervention

**Immediate Support For**:
- Academic crisis (failing grades)
- Emotional crisis (overwhelming stress)
- Family emergency
- Loss or grief
- Peer relationship crisis
- Any immediate concern

**Response**:
- Same-day support when possible
- Safety assessment
- Parent notification as appropriate
- Referral to external resources if needed
- Follow-up support

## Confidentiality

### What It Means

**Confidential**: What you share stays private

**Limits of Confidentiality**:
- **Safety first**: If you might hurt yourself or others
- **Abuse**: If someone is hurting you
- **Legal**: If required by law

**Otherwise**:
- Information stays with counselor
- Not shared without permission
- Respected and protected

**Parent Communication**:
- Generally informed of counseling participation
- Specifics shared only with student's permission
- Safety concerns always communicated
- Collaboration for student's benefit

## When to Seek Counseling

### Signs a Student Might Need Counseling

**Academic Signs**:
- Sudden grade drop
- Lack of motivation
- Test anxiety
- Homework struggles
- Difficulty concentrating

**Emotional Signs**:
- Mood changes
- Increased anxiety or worry
- Sadness or irritability
- Withdrawal from friends
- Loss of interest in activities

**Behavioral Signs**:
- Acting out
- Difficulty following rules
- Change in friends
- Risk-taking behaviors
- Regression to earlier behaviors

**Social Signs**:
- Friendship problems
- Social withdrawal
- Bullying (being bullied or bullying)
- Conflict with peers
- Difficulty getting along

### How to Access Services

**Students**:
- Drop by counseling office
- Send email to counselor
- Ask teacher to help arrange meeting
- Tell parent and ask them to contact

**Parents**:
- Call or email counselor
- Stop by counseling office
- Request through teacher
- Complete referral form (available from office)

**Teachers**:
- Refer student with concerns
- Complete referral form
- Consult with counselor about strategies
- Request classroom observation
