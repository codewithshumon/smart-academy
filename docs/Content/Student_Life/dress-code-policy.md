## Policy Overview

### Purpose of Uniform Policy

**1. Islamic Modesty (Haya)**
- Promote Islamic standards of dress
- Maintain modest appearance for all students
- Reduce focus on materialism and appearance

**2. Equality**
- Eliminate visible economic differences
- Create sense of belonging and equality
- Prevent competition based on clothing

**3. School Identity**
- Foster pride and belonging
- Promote school spirit
- Represent Smart Academy professionally

**4. Practicality**
- Simplify morning routine
- Reduce clothing costs for families
- Ensure appropriate dress for all activities

### Policy Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students enrolled at Smart Academy (PlayGroup - Class 9)
- All school hours (8:00 AM - 3:45 PM)
- All school-sponsored activities and field trips
- School transportation
- Representing school at external events

## Islamic Foundation

### Modesty Requirements

**Quranic Guidance**:
> "Tell the believing men to lower their gaze and guard their modesty. That is purer for them." (Quran 24:30)

> "And tell the believing women to lower their gaze and guard their modesty..." (Quran 24:31)

### Islamic Dress Principles

**For Boys**:
- Cover awrah (from navel to knee) at all times
- Wear loose, non-transparent clothing
- Avoid tight or revealing clothing
- Maintain neat, clean appearance
- Avoid imitating non-Muslim styles that conflict with Islamic values

**For Girls**:
- Cover entire body except face and hands
- Wear loose, non-transparent clothing
- Hijab/head covering (required Class 3+)
- Avoid tight or form-fitting clothing
- Avoid excessive adornment

### Our Commitment

Smart Academy is committed to maintaining an Islamic environment where students can dress modestly while participating fully in all educational activities.

## Uniform Requirements

### Early Childhood (PlayGroup - Kindergarten)

#### PlayGroup & Nursery

**Purpose**: Comfort, ease of movement, independence

**Boys**:
- Colored polo shirt with school logo (light blue, yellow, or green)
- Navy blue shorts or elastic-waist trousers
- Comfortable closed-toe shoes (velcro preferred)
- White socks

**Girls**:
- Colored polo shirt with school logo (light blue, pink, or yellow)
- Navy blue shorts, leggings, or easy salwar-kameez
- Comfortable closed-toe shoes (velcro preferred)
- White socks
- Optional: Small hair clip or headband (simple)

**Notes**:
- Colors may be rotated daily
- Easy for children to manage independently
- Comfortable for active play
- Washable and durable

#### Kindergarten

**Transition to More Structured Uniform**:

**Boys**:
- White polo shirt with school logo
- Navy blue elastic-waist shorts or trousers
- Black belt (if trousers with belt loops)
- Black sneakers or shoes
- White socks

**Girls**:
- Simple navy blue kameez with white trim
- White salwar (comfortable elastic waist)
- White scarf/dupatta (optional, encouraged)
- Black sneakers or shoes
- White socks
- Simple hair accessories (navy, white, or black)

## Grooming Standards

### Hair

**Boys**:
- Clean, neatly groomed
- Trimmed regularly
- Cut in simple, modest style
- Not extending below collar
- Sideburns not below earlobe
- No extreme styles or colors
- No artificial hair dye

**Girls**:
- Clean, neatly groomed
- Tied back neatly if long
- Simple hair accessories (navy, white, black, or brown)
- No extreme styles or colors
- No artificial hair dye

**Hijab Styling**:
- Should cover all hair
- Should be secure and neat
- Should not require frequent adjustment
- Pins should be safe and minimal

### Personal Hygiene

**Daily Practices**:
- Regular bathing/showers
- Use of deodorant/antiperspirant
- Clean nails (short and simple)
- Brushed teeth
- Clean, pressed uniform
- Good general hygiene

### Face and Body

**Makeup Policy**:
- **PlayGroup-Class 8**: No makeup allowed
- **Class 9**: Minimal, natural makeup permitted (light foundation only)
- No eye makeup (eyeliner, mascara, eye shadow)
- No lipstick or colored lip gloss (clear gloss allowed)
- No fake nails or nail polish (clear only)

**Jewelry Policy**:
- **Allowed**: Simple wrist watch (no smart watches)
- **Girls**: Small stud earrings (one per ear, simple design)
- **Not Allowed**: Multiple earrings, hoops, dangling earrings
- **Not Allowed**: Necklaces, bracelets, rings (except simple band)
- **Not Allowed**: Anklets, toe rings
- **No visible piercings** other than earlobes

**Temporary Decorations**:
- No henna (mehendi) on hands during exam periods
- No temporary tattoos
- No face or body art

### Accessories

**Allowed**:
- Simple hair clips and bands (navy, white, black, brown)
- School badge on uniform
- House badge on PE uniform
- Achievement pins (awarded by school)

**Not Allowed**:
- Excessive or decorative hair accessories
- Bandanas (except for medical/religious reasons)
- Sunglasses indoors
- Chains or decorative items
- Flashy or distracting accessories

### Bags and School Items

**School Bag**:
- Should be navy blue or black
- Simple design, no excessive decorations
- Appropriate size for student's grade
- Backpack style recommended for comfort

**Water Bottle**:
- Should be simple, durable
- Navy blue, white, or clear preferred
- Should have student's name
- No inappropriate images or text

**Lunch Box**:
- Should be clean and appropriate size
- Simple design preferred
- No inappropriate images or text

### Casual Dress Days

**Occasional Non-Uniform Days**:
- Announced in advance
- Purpose: Special events, rewards, fundraising
- Still must follow Islamic modesty guidelines

**Guidelines for Casual Dress**:
- Modest tops covering shoulders and chest

**Eid Celebrations**:

## Enforcement

### Daily Checks

**Morning Arrival**:
- Homeroom teachers check uniform compliance
- Students with issues noted and addressed
- Minor problems corrected quickly
- Parents contacted for repeated issues

### Levels of Non-Compliance

**Minor Issues**:
- Untucked shirt
- Missing belt
- Wrong color socks
- Shoes not properly tied
- Jewelry violations

**Response**:
- First offense: Verbal warning, immediate correction
- Second offense: Note home to parents
- Third offense: Parent contact, warning letter
- Continued: Office referral, disciplinary action

**Major Issues**:
- Missing significant uniform items
- Non-uniform clothing without permission
- Repeated violations after warnings
- Extreme non-compliance

**Response**:
- Parent called to bring proper uniform
- Student waits in office until uniform arrives
- Conference with parent and administration
- Behavior contract if pattern continues
- Disciplinary probation for repeated major issues

### Special Circumstances

**Temporary Exemptions**:
- **Medical**: Doctor's note required (e.g., foot injury allowing different shoes)
- **Financial**: Contact administration for confidential assistance
- **Family Emergency**: Communication from parent required
- **Religious Observance**: Appropriate accommodations made

**Process**:
- Parent/guardian contacts school in advance
- Written explanation provided
- Temporary exemption approved by administration
- Clear timeline for return to regular uniform

## Uniform Care

### Daily Care

**Wearing**:
- Uniform should be clean and pressed
- Stains treated promptly
- Tears or loose buttons repaired quickly
- Proper storage to maintain condition

### Washing Guidelines

**General**:
- Follow care instructions on labels
- Wash dark colors separately
- Use appropriate water temperature
- Air dry or low heat to prevent shrinking
- Iron when slightly damp for best results

**White Shirts**:
- May require bleaching for stains
- Iron while damp for crisp appearance
- Starch optional for neat look

**Dark Uniforms**:
- Wash in cold water to prevent fading
- Use color-safe detergent
- Turn inside out before washing
- Air dry away from direct sunlight

### Long-Term Maintenance

**Regular Checks**:
- Inspect for wear and tear monthly
- Replace items when outgrown or worn
- Have backup uniform items available
- Label all items with student name

**Storage**:
- Proper hanging of shirts and blazers
- Fold trousers and knits appropriately
- Store in clean, dry place
- Moth protection for wool items

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: Can students wear uniforms from previous years?**
A: Yes, as long as they still fit and meet uniform requirements. Quality does not change significantly year to year.

**Q: What if my child grows out of uniform mid-year?**
A: Replace items as needed. Uniform shop stocks all sizes. Consider buying slightly larger at start of year for growing room.

**Q: Can my child wear a sweater instead of the school sweater?**
A: No. Only school sweaters with the school logo are allowed as outerwear during the school day.

**Q: Are uniforms required on field trips?**
A: Yes, unless otherwise specified. Uniforms help identify students and maintain school representation.

**Q: What happens if uniform gets dirty or damaged during the day?**
A: Contact the school office. We have spare uniform items for emergencies. Student can change and continue their day.

### Religious and Cultural Questions

**Q: Can my child wear additional religious clothing?**
A: Yes, within reason. Additional religious garments must be navy, white, or black and not interfere with learning. Contact administration to discuss specific needs.

**Q: Are there accommodations for non-Muslim students?**
A: Yes. While uniform is required for all, modesty requirements may be adapted for students of other faiths while maintaining overall appearance standards.

**Q: Can boys wear topi/kufi?**
A: Yes, plain white or navy topi may be worn in class and during prayers. Should not be distracting.

### Financial Questions

**Q: What if I cannot afford uniforms?**
A: Contact administration confidentially. We have financial assistance and exchange programs to ensure every student can wear proper uniform.

**Q: Is there a discount for buying multiple uniforms?**
A: Yes. Bundle pricing available when purchasing complete sets. Contact uniform shop for details.

**Q: What if my child loses uniform items?**
A: Check Lost & Found first. Replacement items can be purchased at uniform shop. Label all items with student name to prevent loss.

### Weather and Seasonal Questions

**Q: Is there a warm weather uniform option?**
A: Students may wear short sleeves during hot months (April-October). Ties become optional during peak heat (May-September).

**Q: What about rain protection?**
A: Students may wear raincoats over their uniform to school. Raincoats should be navy or black if possible. Umbrellas allowed.

**Q: Can students wear boots in winter?**
A: For commuting to school, yes. Students should change into school shoes upon arrival. Boots should be navy or black.
