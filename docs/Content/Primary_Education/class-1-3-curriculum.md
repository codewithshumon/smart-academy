# Class 1-3 Curriculum: Foundation Building Phase

**Smart Academy | Establishing Strong Foundations for Lifelong Learning**

---

## Document Overview

This comprehensive curriculum guide details the educational program for Classes 1, 2, and 3 at Smart Academy - the critical foundation-building years where young learners develop essential literacy, numeracy, and Islamic knowledge that will support all future learning. This three-year phase (ages 6-9) transforms curious children into confident, capable students ready for the skill development phase of Classes 4-5.

---

## Phase Introduction

### The Foundation Building Years

Classes 1-3 represent the most critical period in a child's academic journey. During these formative years, students transition from the play-based learning of early childhood to structured academic study while maintaining the joy and wonder of discovery. These are the years when:

- **Reading skills emerge and solidify** - from letter recognition to fluent reading
- **Mathematical thinking develops** - from counting objects to solving multi-step problems
- **Islamic identity strengthens** - from basic du'as to understanding Allah's guidance
- **Learning habits form** - from teacher-directed to increasingly independent work
- **Social skills mature** - from parallel play to collaborative teamwork
- **Character develops** - from impulsive reactions to thoughtful choices

At Smart Academy, we approach these foundation years with the reverence they deserve. We understand that the skills, habits, attitudes, and knowledge established in Classes 1-3 will either enable or constrain all future academic success. Therefore, we invest tremendous expertise, resources, and care into ensuring every student builds a rock-solid foundation.

### Our Foundation Building Philosophy

> **"The foundation determines the height of the building. Strong foundations in the early years enable towering achievements throughout life."**

Our approach to Classes 1-3 is guided by several core principles:

**1. Mastery Over Speed**
We prioritize deep understanding and true mastery over rushing through content. Students progress when ready, receive support when struggling, and advance when truly prepared. We never sacrifice foundational skills for the sake of pacing.

**2. Islamic Integration from the Start**
Islamic values, knowledge, and character development are not add-ons but are woven into the fabric of every subject. Students see Allah's creation in science, use Islamic examples in math, read Islamic stories in English, and understand that all knowledge comes from and leads back to the Creator.

**3. Explicit Instruction with Gradual Release**
We teach skills explicitly and systematically, then gradually release responsibility to students as they demonstrate mastery. This "I do, we do, you do" approach ensures students have the support they need while building toward independence.

**4. Joy in Learning**
While our academic standards are high, we maintain the joy and wonder of learning. Students are naturally curious, and we channel that curiosity into academic excellence through engaging, meaningful, developmentally appropriate instruction.

**5. Home-School Partnership**
Parents are essential partners in building strong foundations. We communicate extensively, provide resources for home support, and work collaboratively to ensure every child succeeds.

## Class 1 Curriculum (Age 6-7 Years)

### Class 1 Overview

Class 1 marks the formal beginning of academic education. Students arrive with diverse experiences from early childhood education and must be systematically introduced to the structure, expectations, and content of primary school. This year focuses on:

- Establishing classroom routines and learning behaviors
- Developing phonemic awareness and beginning reading skills
- Building number sense and basic computation
- Learning foundational Islamic knowledge and Quranic reading
- Developing fine motor skills for writing
- Cultivating curiosity about the world
- Building positive relationships with peers and teachers

### Class 1: Islamic Studies (90 minutes daily)

**Year Goals:**
By the end of Class 1, students will demonstrate basic Islamic knowledge, regular prayer habits, and good character (Akhlaq) appropriate for their age.

#### Quran and Tajweed

**Noorani Qaida Completion:**
- Recognition of all Arabic letters in different positions
- Pronunciation of letters with correct Makharij (articulation points)
- Understanding of short and long vowels (Harakat)
- Introduction to Tanween and Shaddah
- Simple two and three-letter word reading
- Transition to Quran reading by year end

**Quranic Recitation:**
- Begin reading from Juz 30 (last section of Quran)
- Focus on clear, slow reading with teacher support
- Emphasis on correct pronunciation over speed
- Daily practice under teacher supervision
- Individual and group recitation

**Memorization (Hifz):**
Students memorize the following with meaning and application:
- Surah Al-Fatihah (The Opening)
- Surah Al-Ikhlas (Sincerity)
- Surah Al-Falaq (The Daybreak)
- Surah An-Nas (Mankind)
- Short daily du'as (morning, evening, eating, sleeping, bathroom)

#### Islamic Knowledge

**Basic Aqidah (Beliefs):**
- Allah is our Creator and the Creator of everything
- Allah sees and hears everything
- Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) is Allah's final messenger
- Angels are Allah's creation who obey Him perfectly
- The Quran is Allah's word sent to guide us
- We will all meet Allah on the Day of Judgment
- Good deeds are rewarded, bad deeds have consequences

**Prophets and Stories:**
Students learn basic stories with moral lessons:
- Prophet Adam (AS) - First human and prophet
- Prophet Nuh (AS) - The great flood and obedience
- Prophet Ibrahim (AS) - Trust in Allah
- Prophet Musa (AS) - Standing up for truth
- Prophet Isa (AS) - Miracles and mercy
- Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) - Birth and early life

**Fiqh (Islamic Practices):**
- Five Pillars of Islam (introduction)
- Wudu (ablution) - steps and importance
- Salah (prayer) - basic positions and movements
- Learning to pray at designated times
- Importance of cleanliness in Islam
- Halal and Haram basics (food, behavior)

**Akhlaq (Character Development):**
Students learn and practice:
- Truthfulness (Sidq) - always telling the truth
- Respect for parents and elders (Birr al-Walidayn)
- Kindness to all creatures (Rahma)
- Sharing and generosity (Itha'ar)
- Patience and self-control (Sabr)
- Good manners and etiquette (Adab)
- Islamic greetings (As-salamu alaikum)

**Islamic Habits:**
Daily practice of:
- Five daily prayers (at school: Fajr encouraged, Dhuhr and Asr performed)
- Bismillah before all activities
- Alhamdulillah after eating, drinking, achievements
- Du'a before and after learning
- Helping others and being kind
- Following Islamic etiquette in all situations

#### Arabic Language

**Letter Recognition and Writing:**
- All 28 Arabic letters
- Letter shapes in beginning, middle, end positions
- Letter sounds and pronunciation
- Writing practice for each letter
- Connection between letters

**Basic Vocabulary:**
Simple words related to:
- Family members (Abi, Ummi, Akhi, Ukhti)
- Body parts (Yad, Rijl, Ayn, Udhn)
- Numbers 1-20 in Arabic
- Colors in Arabic
- Common objects in classroom and home
- Greetings and polite expressions

**Simple Sentences:**
- Ana Muslim/Muslimah (I am a Muslim)
- Bismillah (In the name of Allah)
- Alhamdulillah (Praise be to Allah)
- SubhanAllah (Glory be to Allah)
- Simple subject-verb sentences

### Class 1: Mathematics (60 minutes daily)

**Year Goals:**
Students will develop strong number sense, master basic addition and subtraction, and solve simple word problems.

#### Number Sense

**Counting:**
- Count forward and backward to 100
- Count by 2s, 5s, and 10s
- Identify numbers to 100
- Understand place value (tens and ones)
- Compare numbers using more than, less than, equal to
- Order numbers from least to greatest

**Number Recognition and Writing:**
- Read and write numbers 0-100
- Understand number words (one, two, three, etc.)
- Represent numbers using objects, pictures, and symbols
- Understand ordinal numbers (first, second, third, etc.)

#### Operations

**Addition:**
- Addition as combining groups
- Addition facts to 10 (fluency)
- Addition within 20
- Using manipulatives, pictures, and symbols
- Understanding commutative property (3+4 = 4+3)
- Simple word problems

**Subtraction:**
- Subtraction as taking away or comparing
- Subtraction facts to 10 (fluency)
- Subtraction within 20
- Relationship between addition and subtraction
- Using manipulatives, pictures, and symbols
- Simple word problems

**Problem Solving:**
- Solve one-step addition and subtraction word problems
- Draw pictures to represent problems
- Use objects to model situations
- Explain thinking and solutions
- Check answers for reasonableness

#### Geometry

**Shapes:**
- Identify and name 2D shapes (circle, square, rectangle, triangle)
- Identify and name 3D shapes (sphere, cube, cylinder, cone)
- Describe attributes (sides, corners, faces)
- Create and extend patterns
- Recognize shapes in the environment

**Spatial Reasoning:**
- Positional words (above, below, beside, between, etc.)
- Symmetry basics
- Combining shapes to make new shapes
- Understanding part-whole relationships

#### Measurement

**Length:**
- Compare lengths (longer, shorter, same)
- Measure using non-standard units (paper clips, blocks)
- Introduction to standard units (centimeters)
- Estimate and measure objects

**Time:**
- Sequence daily activities
- Read time to the hour and half-hour
- Understand concepts of morning, afternoon, evening, night
- Days of the week and months of the year
- Islamic calendar introduction (Ramadan, Eid)

**Money:**
- Identify Bangladesh currency (Taka coins and notes)
- Value of coins and notes
- Count money
- Simple transactions (buying and selling)
- Understanding saving and charity (Sadaqah)

#### Data and Probability

**Data Collection:**
- Ask questions and collect data
- Organize data using tallies and simple charts
- Create pictographs and bar graphs
- Interpret data from graphs

**Probability:**
- Understand concepts of likely, unlikely, certain, impossible
- Make predictions
- Simple experiments (coin toss, spinner)

## Teaching Methodology for Classes 1-3

### Instructional Approaches

**Explicit Instruction:**
Teachers clearly explain new concepts, model skills, and provide guided practice before independent work. This is especially important for foundational skills in reading, math, and Quran.

**Gradual Release of Responsibility:**
"I do, we do, you do" - teachers model, students practice together, then work independently. This builds confidence and competence.

**Differentiated Instruction:**
- Content differentiation (same concept, different complexity)
- Process differentiation (different ways to learn)
- Product differentiation (different ways to show learning)
- Learning centers and stations
- Flexible grouping
- Tiered assignments

**Active Learning:**
- Hands-on activities
- Cooperative learning
- Projects and investigations
- Games and simulations
- Role-playing
- Real-world applications

**Technology Integration:**
- Smart boards for interactive lessons
- Educational apps and software
- Digital research
- Multimedia creation
- Virtual field trips
- Online practice and assessment

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my child struggles in Class 1-3?**
A: We provide extensive support including tutoring, small group instruction, differentiation, and partnerships with parents. Early intervention is key.

**Q: Is the curriculum too rigorous for young children?**
A: Our curriculum is challenging but developmentally appropriate. We balance rigor with joy in learning.

**Q: How much homework is assigned?**
A:
- Class 1: 15-20 minutes daily
- Class 2: 20-30 minutes daily
- Class 3: 30-45 minutes daily

**Q: How is Islamic education integrated?**
A: Islamic values, knowledge, and perspectives are woven throughout every subject, not just in Islamic Studies period.

**Q: What if my child is advanced?**
A: We provide enrichment, acceleration, and advanced challenges for students who are ready.

**Q: How do you handle behavioral issues?**
A: Through Islamic character education, positive behavior support, restorative practices, and parent partnership.

**Q: Will my child be ready for Class 4?**
A: Our comprehensive curriculum and support ensure every child builds strong foundations for success in Classes 4-5 and beyond.
