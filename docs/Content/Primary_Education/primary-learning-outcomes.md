# Primary Learning Outcomes by Grade

**Smart Academy | Clear Expectations for Student Achievement**

---

## Document Purpose

This comprehensive guide details the expected learning outcomes for students at each grade level (Class 1-5) at Smart Academy. These outcomes articulate what students should know, understand, and be able to do by the end of each grade in all subject areas. They serve as targets for teaching, standards for assessment, and communication tools for parents about grade-level expectations.

---

## Learning Outcomes Philosophy

> **"Clear learning outcomes guide teaching, focus student effort, enable assessment, and ensure every child achieves their full potential."**

**Principles:**
1. **Clear and Specific** - Outcomes are observable and measurable
2. **Standards-Based** - Aligned with national and international standards
3. **Developmentally Appropriate** - Suitable for age and stage
4. **Comprehensive** - Cover all domains (cognitive, affective, psychomotor)
5. **Progressive** - Build systematically across grades
6. **Islamic Integration** - Include Islamic values and knowledge
7. **Achievable** - High expectations but attainable with effort

---

## How to Read Learning Outcomes

Learning outcomes are written as statements beginning with "Students will..." or "By the end of Class X, students will..."

They are organized by:
1. **Subject Area**
2. **Domain or Strand**
3. **Specific Outcomes**

**Verbs indicate levels of learning:**
- Remember: List, name, identify, recall
- Understand: Explain, describe, summarize, interpret
- Apply: Use, demonstrate, solve, apply
- Analyze: Analyze, compare, categorize, examine
- Evaluate: Evaluate, critique, justify, assess
- Create: Create, design, construct, compose

## Class 1 Learning Outcomes (Age 6-7)

### Islamic Studies

**Quran:**
- Read Noorani Qaida correctly
- Recognize all Arabic letters in different positions
- Begin reading simple Quranic words
- Recite Surah Al-Fatihah from memory
- Recite 3-4 short Surahs from memory (Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas, Al-Kawthar)
- Recite daily du'as (morning, evening, eating, sleeping)

**Islamic Knowledge:**
- State that Allah is our Creator
- State that Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) is Allah's messenger
- List the five pillars of Islam
- Describe basic story of 3-4 prophets
- Demonstrate how to make Wudu
- Demonstrate basic prayer positions

**Akhlaq (Character):**
- Demonstrate Islamic greetings (As-salamu alaikum)
- Show respect for parents and teachers
- Share toys and materials with others
- Tell the truth in simple situations
- Help others when asked
- Use polite words (please, thank you, sorry)

**Arabic Language:**
- Recognize and write all 28 Arabic letters
- Pronounce Arabic letters correctly
- Know 50-100 basic Arabic words
- Use simple Arabic greetings and phrases

### English Language Arts

**Reading:**
- Recognize all uppercase and lowercase letters
- Produce sounds for all letters
- Blend sounds to read CVC words (cat, dog, sun)
- Read simple sentences with support
- Identify main characters in a story
- Sequence events (first, next, last)
- Automatically recognize 50-100 sight words
- Answer literal questions about texts read

**Writing:**
- Form all uppercase and lowercase letters legibly
- Write first and last name
- Write simple sentences with capital letters and periods
- Use finger spaces between words
- Spell CVC words phonetically
- Spell 20-30 sight words correctly

**Speaking and Listening:**
- Speak in complete sentences
- Follow 1-2 step oral directions
- Participate in class discussions
- Recite poems and rhymes
- Describe objects and pictures

### Mathematics

**Number and Operations:**
- Count to 100 forward and backward
- Read and write numbers 0-100
- Identify place value (tens and ones)
- Compare numbers using <, >, =
- Add and subtract within 10 (fluency)
- Add and subtract within 20
- Solve simple word problems

**Geometry:**
- Identify and name 2D shapes (circle, square, rectangle, triangle)
- Identify and name 3D shapes (sphere, cube, cylinder, cone)
- Create and extend patterns

**Measurement:**
- Compare lengths (longer, shorter)
- Measure using non-standard units
- Tell time to hour and half-hour
- Identify coins and notes (Bangladesh currency)
- Sequence days of week

**Data:**
- Collect and organize data
- Create and interpret simple pictographs

### Science

**Life Science:**
- Distinguish living from non-living things
- Identify basic needs of living things (food, water, air, shelter)
- Name parts of plants (root, stem, leaf, flower)
- Group animals (mammals, birds, fish, insects)
- Identify five senses and their functions

**Physical Science:**
- Identify three states of matter (solid, liquid, gas)
- Describe properties of objects (color, size, shape, texture)
- Demonstrate push and pull
- Identify sources of light and sound

**Earth and Space:**
- Identify land and water on Earth
- Describe types of weather
- Explain day and night
- Recognize sun, moon, and stars

**Scientific Inquiry:**
- Make observations using senses
- Ask questions about the world
- Make predictions
- Record observations through drawings

### Social Studies

**Self and Family:**
- Describe self (name, age, family)
- Identify family members and relationships
- Describe family traditions

**School and Community:**
- Identify school staff and their roles
- Follow classroom rules
- Name community helpers (doctor, teacher, police, imam)
- Identify community places (masjid, market, hospital, school)

**Bangladesh:**
- Recognize national flag
- State that Dhaka is the capital
- Name major cities (2-3)

**Islamic Civilization:**
- State that Makkah and Madinah are holy cities
- Recognize Kaaba

**Map Skills:**
- Understand simple maps
- Use directional words (left, right, up, down)

### Bengali Language

**Reading and Writing:**
- Recognize and write all Bengali vowels (স্বরবর্ণ)
- Recognize and write all Bengali consonants (ব্যঞ্জনবর্ণ)
- Read simple Bengali words
- Write simple Bengali sentences
- Understand kar (কার)

**Vocabulary:**
- Know 200-300 Bengali words
- Use Bengali for daily communication

**Culture:**
- Recite National Anthem
- Recognize Language Movement importance (basic)

### Physical Education

- Perform locomotor skills (run, jump, hop, skip)
- Throw and catch soft objects
- Kick a ball
- Understand importance of exercise
- Follow game rules
- Demonstrate good sportsmanship

### Arts

- Use drawing tools properly
- Identify basic colors
- Create simple art projects
- Sing simple nasheeds
- Participate in creative movement

- Celebrate successes
