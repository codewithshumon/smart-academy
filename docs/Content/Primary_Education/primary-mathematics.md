# Primary Mathematics Curriculum

**Smart Academy | Building Mathematical Excellence Through Islamic Values**

---

## Document Overview

This comprehensive mathematics curriculum guide details the systematic development of mathematical understanding and proficiency across all five primary classes (Class 1-5) at Smart Academy. Mathematics is fundamental to academic success, critical thinking, and problem-solving. This document outlines the progression from basic number sense and operations in Class 1 to advanced mathematical thinking including pre-algebra concepts in Class 5, preparing students for secondary mathematics and real-world applications.

---

## Mathematics Philosophy

### Our Approach to Mathematics Education

> **"Mathematics is the language of Allah's creation. Understanding mathematical patterns and relationships helps us appreciate the order, beauty, and wisdom in Allah's design."**

**Core Principles:**

**1. Conceptual Understanding**
We prioritize deep understanding of mathematical concepts over memorization of procedures. Students learn why mathematical relationships work, not just how to get answers.

**2. Procedural Fluency**
Students develop efficiency and accuracy in calculations through practice and understanding of underlying concepts. Fluency frees cognitive resources for problem-solving.

**3. Problem-Solving**
Mathematical thinking and problem-solving are central. Students learn multiple strategies, apply mathematics to real situations, and persevere through challenging problems.

**4. Mathematical Reasoning**
Students explain their thinking, justify solutions, critique reasoning, and communicate mathematically. They develop logical thinking applicable beyond mathematics.

**5. Islamic Integration**
Mathematics is connected to Islamic practices (prayer times, Qibla direction, Zakah calculations, inheritance) and viewed as a means to understand Allah's creation.

**6. Real-World Applications**
Students see mathematics as useful and relevant, applying skills to authentic situations from daily life, Islamic practices, and future careers.

**7. Growth Mindset**
All students can learn mathematics with effort. Mistakes are learning opportunities. We celebrate progress and persistence.

## Curriculum Framework

### The Five Mathematical Domains

Our curriculum is organized around five domains that develop progressively:

**1. Number and Operations**
- Number sense
- Place value
- Addition and subtraction
- Multiplication and division
- Fractions and decimals

**2. Algebra**
- Patterns and relationships
- Variables and expressions
- Equations and inequalities

**3. Geometry**
- 2D and 3D shapes
- Properties and relationships
- Measurement (perimeter, area, volume)

- Unit conversion
- Estimation
- Precision and accuracy

**5. Data and Probability**
- Data collection and organization
- Graphical representations
- Statistical measures
- Probability concepts
- Making predictions

## Class 1 Mathematics

### Overview
Class 1 establishes mathematical foundations. Students develop number sense to 100, master basic addition and subtraction, recognize shapes, measure using non-standard units, and collect simple data.

### Number and Operations

**Counting:**
- Count forward and backward to 100
- Count by 1s, 2s, 5s, and 10s
- Identify numbers 0-100
- Write numbers 0-100
- Number words (one through twenty)
- Ordinal numbers (first through tenth)

**Place Value:**
- Understand tens and ones
- Represent numbers using base-10 blocks
- Compare numbers (greater than, less than, equal to)
- Order numbers from least to greatest

**Addition:**
- Addition as combining groups
- Addition facts to 10 (automatic fluency by year end)
- Addition within 20
- Commutative property (3+4 = 4+3)
- Strategies: counting on, making ten, doubles
- Using manipulatives, pictures, and symbols
- Simple word problems

**Subtraction:**
- Subtraction as taking away
- Subtraction as comparing
- Subtraction facts to 10 (fluency)
- Subtraction within 20
- Relationship between addition and subtraction
- Strategies: counting back, using addition
- Simple word problems

### Geometry

**2D Shapes:**
- Identify and name: circle, square, rectangle, triangle
- Describe attributes (sides, corners)
- Create and extend patterns with shapes
- Find shapes in environment

**3D Shapes:**
- Identify and name: sphere, cube, cylinder, cone
- Describe attributes (faces, edges, vertices)
- Find shapes in environment

**Spatial Relationships:**
- Positional words (above, below, beside, between, in front of, behind)

### Measurement

**Length:**
- Compare lengths (longer, shorter, same)
- Measure using non-standard units (paper clips, blocks)
- Estimate lengths

**Time:**
- Sequence daily events
- Read time to hour and half-hour
- Days of week, months of year
- Morning, afternoon, evening, night
- Prayer times (introduction)

**Money:**
- Identify Bangladeshi coins and notes
- Value of coins and notes
- Count money (simple)

### Data

**Data Collection:**
- Ask questions and collect data
- Use tallies
- Create pictographs (one symbol = one object)
- Interpret simple graphs

**Probability:**
- Likely, unlikely, certain, impossible

### Problem-Solving

**Strategies:**
- Use objects and pictures
- Act it out
- Guess and check
- Look for patterns
- Draw a picture

**Word Problems:**
- One-step addition and subtraction
- Join, separate, compare, part-whole problems
- Check if answer makes sense

### Islamic Integration

- Counting blessings (Ni'mah)
- Sharing fairly (Islamic justice)
- Time for prayers
- Zakah introduction (giving to those in need)
- Patterns in nature (Allah's creation)

## Class 2 Mathematics

### Overview
Class 2 builds fluency and extends understanding. Students master addition and subtraction within 100, begin multiplication and division, work with fractions, measure with standard units, and create various graphs.

### Number and Operations

**Place Value:**
- Hundreds, tens, and ones to 1000
- Read and write numbers to 1000
- Expanded form (346 = 300 + 40 + 6)
- Compare and order numbers
- Round to nearest 10 and 100
- Even and odd numbers

**Addition and Subtraction:**
- Addition and subtraction facts to 20 (automatic fluency)
- Add and subtract within 100 (with and without regrouping)
- Mental math strategies
- Estimation
- Multi-step word problems

**Multiplication:**
- Multiplication as repeated addition
- Arrays and equal groups
- Multiplication facts for 2, 5, 10 (fluency by year end)
- Commutative property
- Word problems

**Division:**
- Division as sharing equally
- Division as repeated subtraction
- Relationship to multiplication
- Division facts related to 2, 5, 10
- Word problems

**Fractions:**
- Fractions as parts of a whole
- Halves, thirds, fourths
- Numerator and denominator
- Compare fractions with same denominator
- Simple equivalent fractions (1/2 = 2/4)

### Geometry

**2D Shapes:**
- Properties of shapes (number of sides, vertices, angles)
- Polygons and non-polygons
- Quadrilaterals (square, rectangle, rhombus, trapezoid)
- Triangles
- Circles (center, radius, diameter)
- Line symmetry

**3D Shapes:**
- Properties (faces, edges, vertices)
- Relationship between 2D and 3D shapes

**Transformations:**
- Slides, flips, turns
- Congruent shapes

### Measurement

**Length:**
- Measure with rulers (centimeters, meters)
- Estimate lengths
- Add and subtract lengths
- Perimeter of rectangles and squares

**Time:**
- Tell time to 5 minutes
- AM and PM
- Elapsed time (simple)
- Calendar skills
- Prayer times throughout day

**Money:**
- Count mixed coins and notes
- Making change
- Simple money word problems
- Saving and charity (Sadaqah)

**Mass and Capacity:**
- Grams, kilograms
- Liters, milliliters
- Comparing and ordering

### Data and Probability

**Data:**
- Tally charts and frequency tables
- Pictographs (one symbol = multiple units)
- Bar graphs (vertical and horizontal)
- Line plots
- Interpret and draw conclusions

**Probability:**
- Certain, likely, unlikely, impossible
- Simple experiments
- Predictions

### Problem-Solving

**Strategies:**
- Multiple strategies for same problem
- Multi-step problems
- Checking reasonableness
- Explaining thinking

### Islamic Integration

- Multiplication in Islamic context (distributing food, charity)
- Division and fairness (sharing equally)
- Fractions (portions, inheritance introduction)
- Time management (balancing prayer, study, play)
- Zakah calculations (introduction)

**Time:**

**Money:**

### Data and Probability

**Data:**
- Frequency tables
- Bar graphs, line graphs, pictographs
- Mean, median, mode, range (introduction)
- Interpret and analyze
- Make predictions

**Probability:**

### Problem-Solving

**Strategies:**

## Mathematical Practices

Throughout all classes, students engage in eight Mathematical Practices:

**1. Make sense of problems and persevere in solving them**
**2. Reason abstractly and quantitatively**
**3. Construct viable arguments and critique reasoning**
**4. Model with mathematics**
**5. Use appropriate tools strategically**
**6. Attend to precision**
**7. Look for and make use of structure**
**8. Look for and express regularity in repeated reasoning**
