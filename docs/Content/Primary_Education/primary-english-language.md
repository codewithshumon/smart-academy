# Primary English Language Arts Curriculum

**Smart Academy | Developing Trilingual Excellence Through English Mastery**

---

## Document Overview

This comprehensive English Language Arts curriculum guide details the systematic development of English language proficiency across all five primary classes (Class 1-5) at Smart Academy. As the primary medium of instruction, English proficiency is essential for academic success, global communication, and accessing knowledge. This document outlines the progression from emergent reading and writing in Class 1 to sophisticated literacy skills in Class 5, preparing students for advanced English study in secondary school and beyond.

---

## English Language Arts Philosophy

### Our Approach to English Education

> **"Language is the gateway to knowledge, communication, and understanding. Mastery of English opens doors to academic excellence and global citizenship while maintaining Islamic values."**

**Core Principles:**

**1. Comprehensive Literacy Development**
We develop all aspects of literacy - reading, writing, speaking, listening, and viewing - in an integrated, balanced approach that recognizes their interdependence.

**2. Islamic Integration**
English instruction includes Islamic content, values, and perspectives. Students read Islamic literature, write about Islamic topics, and use English to express and strengthen their Islamic identity.

**3. Academic English**
We explicitly teach academic language and literacy skills needed for success across all subjects, preparing students for increasingly complex texts and tasks.

**4. Authentic Communication**
Students use English for real purposes and audiences, developing genuine communication skills beyond mere grammatical correctness.

**5. Critical Literacy**
We teach students not just to read and write but to analyze, evaluate, and create texts critically and thoughtfully.

**6. Differentiation**
We recognize diverse learning needs and provide support for struggling learners and enrichment for advanced students.

## Class 1 English Language Arts

### Overview
Class 1 establishes the foundation for English literacy. Students learn to decode words, read simple texts, write sentences, and communicate in English.

### Reading - Class 1

**Phonemic Awareness:**
- Rhyme recognition and production
- Syllable counting and segmentation
- Onset and rime
- Phoneme isolation, identification, categorization
- Blending phonemes to form words
- Segmenting words into phonemes
- Phoneme addition, deletion, substitution

**Phonics:**
Systematic, explicit phonics instruction:
- Alphabet recognition (uppercase and lowercase)
- Letter-sound correspondences (all consonants and vowels)
- Short vowels (CVC words: cat, bed, pig, hot, cup)
- Long vowels and silent e (CVCe: cake, bike, rope)
- Consonant blends (bl, cr, st, tr, etc.)
- Consonant digraphs (sh, ch, th, wh, ph)
- Word families (-at, -an, -ig, -op, -ug, etc.)

**Sight Words:**
Automatic recognition of high-frequency words:
- 50-100 sight words by year end
- Dolch and Fry high-frequency words
- Common words not easily decoded

**Fluency:**
- Accuracy in decoding
- Appropriate reading rate
- Expression and intonation
- Tracking print left to right, top to bottom

**Comprehension:**
- Understanding simple stories
- Main idea identification
- Sequencing (first, next, last)
- Recalling details
- Making predictions
- Answering literal questions (who, what, where, when)
- Connecting text to personal experience
- Using illustrations to support understanding

**Texts:**
- Decodable books
- Leveled readers (Levels A-D)
- Simple picture books
- Islamic stories for children
- Pattern books
- Simple non-fiction

### Writing - Class 1

**Handwriting:**
- Proper pencil grip
- Correct letter formation
- Writing on lines
- Spacing between words
- Legibility

**Spelling:**
- CVC words
- High-frequency sight words
- Word families
- Weekly spelling lists

**Composition:**
- Complete sentences
- Capital letters and periods
- Writing about personal experiences
- Describing pictures
- Simple story writing (beginning, middle, end)
- Lists and labels

**Grammar:**
- Nouns (person, place, thing)
- Action verbs
- Descriptive words (adjectives)
- Singular and plural
- Pronouns (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
- Basic sentence structure

### Speaking and Listening - Class 1

**Speaking:**
- Speaking in complete sentences
- Using appropriate vocabulary
- Describing objects and experiences
- Asking and answering questions
- Participating in discussions
- Reciting poems and rhymes
- Show and tell presentations

**Listening:**
- Following one and two-step directions
- Listening to stories
- Responding to questions
- Taking turns in conversation

## Class 2 English Language Arts

### Overview
Class 2 builds fluency in reading and writing. Students read increasingly complex texts, write organized paragraphs, and communicate more confidently.

### Reading - Class 2

**Phonics and Word Study:**
- Review and mastery of Class 1 skills
- Advanced consonant blends (scr, spl, spr, str)
- Vowel teams (ai, ay, ee, ea, oa, ow, etc.)
- R-controlled vowels (ar, er, ir, or, ur)
- Diphthongs (oi, oy, ou, ow)
- Silent letters (kn, wr, gn, mb)
- Multisyllabic word decoding

**Sight Words:**
- 150-200+ sight words
- Automatic recognition

**Fluency:**
- Reading rate: 60-90 words per minute
- Appropriate expression
- Phrasing and pausing at punctuation
- Self-correction
- Independent reading: 20-30 minutes

**Comprehension:**
- Main idea and supporting details
- Sequencing events
- Cause and effect
- Compare and contrast
- Making inferences
- Predicting outcomes
- Character analysis (traits, feelings)
- Story elements (characters, setting, problem, solution)
- Author's purpose
- Fact vs. opinion

**Vocabulary:**
- Context clues
- Prefixes and suffixes
- Root words
- Synonyms and antonyms
- Multiple-meaning words
- Content vocabulary

**Texts:**
- Leveled readers (Levels E-J)
- Chapter books (read-aloud)
- Islamic stories and biographies
- Folktales and fables
- Simple poetry
- Non-fiction texts

### Writing - Class 2

**Composition:**
- Organized paragraphs (topic sentence, details, concluding sentence)
- Narrative writing (personal stories with beginning, middle, end)
- Descriptive writing (sensory details)
- Opinion writing (stating opinion with reasons)
- Informational writing (facts about topics)
- Friendly letters

**Grammar and Mechanics:**
- Sentence types (statement, question, command, exclamation)
- Subject and predicate
- Common and proper nouns
- Singular and plural possessive nouns
- Verb tenses (present, past)
- Adjectives and adverbs
- Pronouns
- Capitalization (first word, names, I, proper nouns)
- Punctuation (periods, question marks, exclamation marks, commas in lists and dates, apostrophes)

**Spelling:**
- Phonics patterns
- High-frequency words
- Weekly spelling lists (10-15 words)

**Writing Process:**
- Prewriting (brainstorming, planning)
- Drafting
- Revising (adding details, rearranging)
- Editing (capitals, punctuation, spelling)
- Publishing

### Speaking and Listening - Class 2

**Speaking:**
- Speaking in complete, detailed sentences
- Oral presentations
- Reciting poems and speeches
- Retelling stories
- Describing in detail
- Asking clarifying questions

**Listening:**
- Following multi-step directions
- Listening for main ideas and details
- Active listening in discussions
- Note-taking (simple)

## Class 3 English Language Arts

### Overview
Class 3 develops deeper comprehension and more sophisticated writing. Students read chapter books independently, write multi-paragraph compositions, and think critically about texts.

### Reading - Class 3

**Word Study:**
- Advanced phonics patterns
- Greek and Latin roots (introduction)
- Prefixes (un-, re-, pre-, mis-, dis-)
- Suffixes (-ly, -ful, -less, -ness, -ment)
- Homophones and homographs
- Spelling patterns and rules

**Fluency:**
- Reading rate: 90-120 words per minute
- Expression matches meaning
- Appropriate phrasing
- Self-monitoring and self-correction
- Independent reading: 30-40 minutes

**Comprehension:**
- Theme identification
- Character analysis (development, motivation, relationships)
- Plot structure (exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, resolution)
- Setting's influence on story
- Point of view (first person, third person)
- Making sophisticated inferences
- Drawing conclusions with evidence
- Comparing and contrasting texts
- Author's purpose and perspective
- Evaluating text quality
- Summarizing
- Determining importance
- Asking questions before, during, and after reading

**Literary Elements:**
- Figurative language (similes, metaphors, personification)
- Imagery and sensory details
- Dialogue
- Foreshadowing
- Flashback
- Symbolism (introduction)

**Vocabulary:**
- Context clues (definition, example, contrast, inference)
- Word parts (prefixes, suffixes, roots)
- Academic vocabulary
- Content-area vocabulary
- Connotation vs. denotation
- Idioms and expressions

**Texts:**
- Chapter books (independent)
- Novels
- Islamic literature
- Historical fiction
- Fantasy and science fiction
- Realistic fiction
- Non-fiction (biographies, informational)
- Poetry (various forms)
- Drama

### Writing - Class 3

**Composition:**
- Multi-paragraph essays (3-5 paragraphs)
- Clear introduction, body, conclusion
- Thesis statements (introduction)
- Topic sentences and supporting details
- Transitions between ideas and paragraphs

**Writing Types:**
- **Narrative:** Personal narratives, fictional stories, memoirs
  - Engaging leads
  - Character and setting development
  - Dialogue
  - Sensory details
  - Plot structure
- **Informational/Explanatory:** Reports, articles, how-to texts
  - Introduction of topic
  - Facts, definitions, details, quotations
  - Text features
  - Domain-specific vocabulary
  - Concluding statement
- **Opinion/Persuasive:** Essays, letters, reviews
  - Clear opinion/claim
  - Logical reasons
  - Supporting evidence
  - Counterarguments (introduction)
  - Call to action
- **Poetry:** Various forms (haiku, acrostic, free verse, rhyming)

**Grammar and Mechanics:**
- All sentence types (simple, compound, complex)
- Independent and dependent clauses
- All parts of speech
- Subject-verb agreement
- Pronoun-antecedent agreement
- Verb tenses (all) and consistency
- Active and passive voice (introduction)
- All capitalization rules
- All punctuation (comma rules, quotation marks, apostrophes, colons, semicolons introduction)
- Spelling patterns mastery

**Writing Process:**
- Prewriting (brainstorming, researching, outlining)
- Drafting
- Revising (content, organization, word choice, sentence fluency)
- Editing (grammar, spelling, mechanics)
- Publishing
- Reflection
- Peer and teacher feedback
- Using rubrics

**Research Writing:**
- Formulating questions
- Finding sources
- Note-taking
- Organizing information
- Simple reports
- Citing sources (introduction)

### Speaking and Listening - Class 3

**Speaking:**
- Oral reports with visual aids
- Book talks
- Recitations
- Discussions (building on others' ideas)
- Asking and answering questions
- Clear articulation and appropriate volume

**Listening:**
- Active listening strategies
- Note-taking
- Identifying main ideas and details
- Evaluating arguments

## Class 4 English Language Arts

### Overview
Class 4 develops advanced literacy skills. Students read complex literature critically, write sophisticated compositions for various purposes, conduct research, and communicate persuasively.

### Reading - Class 4

**Word Study:**
- Multisyllabic word decoding
- Greek and Latin roots (expanded)
- Morphology and word families
- Etymology
- Academic and domain-specific vocabulary

**Fluency:**
- Reading rate: 100-130 words per minute
- Expression appropriate to genre
- Adjust rate for purpose
- Extended independent reading: 45-60 minutes

**Comprehension:**
- Deep analysis of complex texts
- Theme and central message
- Character analysis (traits, motivations, development, complexity)
- Plot analysis (structure, conflict types, resolution significance)
- Setting (impact on characters and events)
- Point of view (first, second, third; reliable/unreliable narrator)
- Sophisticated inferencing
- Textual evidence to support claims
- Author's craft analysis (word choice, structure, techniques)
- Author's purpose and perspective
- Comparing across texts and genres
- Evaluating arguments and evidence
- Fact vs. opinion vs. reasoned judgment
- Bias and propaganda recognition
- Synthesizing from multiple sources
- Metacognitive strategies

**Literary Analysis:**
- Figurative language (similes, metaphors, personification, hyperbole, idioms, alliteration, onomatopoeia)
- Imagery and mood
- Tone
- Symbolism
- Irony (introduction)
- Foreshadowing and flashback
- Allusion

**Text Structures:**
- Narrative, expository, persuasive, descriptive
- Chronological, cause-effect, compare-contrast, problem-solution, description
- Using text features effectively

**Texts:**
- Novels with complex themes
- Islamic literature and biographies
- Historical fiction
- Science fiction and fantasy
- Realistic fiction
- Mystery and adventure
- Non-fiction (various types)
- Poetry (analyzing devices and meaning)
- Drama and plays
- Primary and secondary sources

### Writing - Class 4

**Composition:**
- Well-organized multi-paragraph essays (5+ paragraphs)
- Clear thesis/claim
- Strong topic sentences
- Detailed evidence and examples
- Smooth transitions
- Powerful conclusions
- Voice and style for purpose and audience

**Writing Types:**
- **Narrative:** Complex stories with developed characters, plot, setting, theme
- **Informational/Explanatory:** In-depth reports, articles, research papers
- **Opinion/Argumentative:** Essays with claims, evidence, counterarguments
- **Research Writing:** Formal research process, multiple sources, citations
- **Poetry:** Various forms and devices
- **Business Writing:** Formal letters, emails, applications

**Grammar and Mechanics - Mastery:**
- Complex and compound-complex sentences
- Clauses and phrases
- All parts of speech and functions
- Subject-verb agreement (complex)
- Pronoun usage (including who/whom)
- Verb tenses (perfect tenses)
- Active and passive voice
- Direct and indirect objects
- Appositives
- Punctuation mastery
- Spelling patterns and rules

**Writing Process - Advanced:**
- Extensive prewriting and planning
- Multiple drafts
- Substantive revision
- Careful editing
- Polished publication
- Self and peer assessment with rubrics
- Reflection on writing

### Speaking and Listening - Class 4

**Speaking:**
- Research presentations
- Persuasive speeches
- Debates
- Discussions (synthesizing multiple perspectives)
- Multimedia presentations
- Formal speaking skills

**Listening:**
- Note-taking from lectures
- Identifying arguments and evidence
- Evaluating speaker's credibility
- Critical listening

## Class 5 English Language Arts

### Overview
Class 5 is the culminating year of primary English education. Students demonstrate mastery of literacy skills, read and analyze sophisticated texts, write for various academic and real-world purposes, and prepare for secondary English study.

### Reading - Class 5

**Advanced Reading Skills:**
- Reading rate: 120-150+ words per minute
- Fluency across all genres
- Stamina for extended texts
- Self-monitoring and fix-up strategies

**Critical Reading:**
- Analyzing complex themes and central ideas
- Character complexity and development
- Plot and subplot analysis
- Multiple points of view
- Unreliable narrators
- Author's craft (style, structure, techniques)
- Rhetorical devices
- Evaluating arguments, claims, and evidence
- Detecting bias and propaganda
- Comparing across texts, authors, and time periods
- Synthesizing multiple sources
- Reading in content areas
- Primary and secondary source analysis

**Literary Analysis - Advanced:**
- All figurative language and literary devices
- Symbolism and allegory
- Theme development
- Mood and tone
- Satire and irony
- Cultural and historical context
- Intertextuality

**Vocabulary:**
- Academic and content-specific vocabulary
- Greek and Latin roots, prefixes, suffixes
- Etymology and word histories
- Connotation and denotation
- Analogies
- Context clues mastery

**Texts:**
- Classic and contemporary novels
- Islamic literature and scholarship
- Diverse genres and authors
- Complex non-fiction
- Primary sources
- Poetry (analyzing and interpreting)
- Drama
- Digital texts

### Writing - Class 5

**Composition - Advanced:**
- Sophisticated multi-paragraph compositions
- Strong thesis statements with clear position
- Well-developed arguments with evidence
- Logical organization
- Effective transitions
- Compelling introductions and conclusions
- Voice, style, and tone appropriate to purpose and audience
- Sophisticated vocabulary and sentence variety

**Writing Types - Mastery:**
- **Narrative:** Sophisticated stories with complex characters, plots, themes
- **Informational/Explanatory:** Research papers, reports, articles, explanations
- **Argumentative/Persuasive:** Essays with claims, evidence, counterarguments, rebuttals
- **Research Writing:** Formal research with multiple sources, citations, bibliography
- **Literary Analysis:** Analyzing texts with textual evidence
- **Creative Writing:** Poetry, short stories, scripts
- **Professional Writing:** Business letters, applications, proposals

**Grammar and Mechanics - Complete Mastery:**
- All sentence types and structures
- Complex grammatical structures
- Stylistic choices for effect
- All parts of speech
- Advanced punctuation
- Spelling mastery
- Commonly confused words

**Writing Process - Expert:**
- Independent use of writing process
- Extensive revision for all aspects
- Peer and self-evaluation with sophisticated rubrics
- Publishing in various formats
- Reflection and goal-setting

**Research Skills:**
- Formulating research questions
- Finding and evaluating sources (print and digital)
- Assessing credibility and bias
- Note-taking and organizing
- Synthesizing information
- Avoiding plagiarism
- Citations and bibliography (MLA or APA basics)
- Creating research presentations

### Speaking and Listening - Class 5

**Speaking - Advanced:**
- Formal presentations with multimedia
- Persuasive speeches with evidence
- Debates with rebuttals
- Panel discussions
- Literary analysis presentations
- Demonstrating expertise
- Adapting to audience
- Professional speaking skills

**Listening - Advanced:**
- Active listening in all contexts
- Comprehensive note-taking
- Evaluating arguments and evidence
- Identifying rhetorical strategies
- Critical listening

## Home Support

**Parents can support English development by:**
- Reading with child daily (20-30 minutes minimum)
- Discussing books and asking questions
- Providing diverse reading materials
- Limiting screen time, encouraging reading time
- Visiting library regularly
- Playing word games
- Writing together (letters, journals, stories)
- Encouraging speaking in English at home
- Attending school literary events
- Celebrating reading and writing achievements
