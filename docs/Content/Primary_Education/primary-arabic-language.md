# Primary Arabic Language Curriculum

**Smart Academy | Connecting to the Language of the Quran**

---

## Arabic Language Philosophy

> **"We have sent it down as an Arabic Quran so that you may understand." (Quran 12:2)**

Arabic is the language of the Quran, the language of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), and the liturgical language of Islam. At Smart Academy, Arabic instruction aims to enable students to understand the Quran, perform Islamic rituals with understanding, and connect with the rich Islamic heritage. Our approach balances classical Arabic (for Quranic understanding) with Modern Standard Arabic (for communication).

**Core Principles:**
1. **Quranic Connection** - Arabic for understanding Allah's word
2. **Communicative Competence** - Speaking, listening, reading, writing
3. **Grammar in Context** - Grammar to support understanding, not for its own sake
4. **Systematic Progression** - Building skills incrementally
5. **Cultural Integration** - Arabic language and Islamic culture
6. **Functional Use** - Practical application in Islamic practices

---

## Arabic Language Domains

Our curriculum develops:

### Grammar (Nahw) - Detailed
**Parts of Speech:**
- Noun (Ism), Verb (Fi'l), Particle (Harf)
- Types of nouns (proper, common, concrete, abstract)

**Noun Properties:**
- Definite and indefinite (Al and Tanween)
- Masculine and feminine (rules and patterns)
- Singular, Dual, and Plural
  - Sound masculine plural
  - Sound feminine plural
  - Broken plurals (common patterns)

**Verb Properties:**
- Past tense (Fi'l al-Madi)
- Present tense (Fi'l al-Mudari')
- Command (Fi'l al-Amr)
- Conjugation with pronouns (I, you, he, she, we, they)

**Sentence Structure:**
- Nominal sentence (Jumlah Ismiyyah): Subject (Mubtada) + Predicate (Khabar)
- Verbal sentence (Jumlah Fi'liyyah): Verb (Fi'l) + Subject (Fa'il)
- Agreement between subject and verb
- Agreement between noun and adjective

**Other Grammar:**
- Adjectives follow nouns
- Possessive construction (Idafa) - introduction
- Pronouns (attached and detached)
- Demonstrative pronouns (this, that, these, those)
