# Primary Education Program Overview (Class 1-5)

**Smart Academy | Building Excellence Through Islamic Values and Academic Rigor**

---

## Program Introduction

Welcome to Smart Academy's Primary Education Program, where we transform young learners aged 6-11 into confident, capable, and principled students ready to excel in secondary education and beyond. Our Class 1-5 program represents the critical bridge between early childhood exploration and advanced academic study, combining rigorous academics with deep Islamic education to nurture well-rounded individuals.

The primary years are foundational for establishing learning habits, building core competencies, and shaping character. At Smart Academy, we recognize that these five years determine whether students will thrive throughout their educational journey and become productive members of society who contribute positively while maintaining strong Islamic identity.

---

## Our Philosophy

> **"The primary years are not merely about mastering subjects, but about becoming confident learners, ethical citizens, and devoted Muslims."**

### Core Beliefs

Our Primary Education program is grounded in fundamental principles that guide every aspect of our work:

**1. Integration of Faith and Knowledge**
- Islamic values are woven throughout every subject
- Students see Allah's wisdom in all areas of learning
- Academic excellence is viewed as an act of worship (Ibadah)
- Character development (Tarbiyah) is as important as academic achievement
- Quranic guidance informs ethical decision-making

**2. STEAM-Based Experiential Learning**
- Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics integrated
- Hands-on, project-based learning emphasized
- Real-world problem-solving skills developed
- Critical thinking prioritized over rote memorization
- Creativity and innovation encouraged

**3. Trilingual Proficiency**
- English as primary medium of instruction
- Bengali for national identity and cultural connection
- Arabic for Quranic understanding and Islamic studies
- Balanced development across all three languages
- Reading, writing, speaking, listening in each language

**4. Holistic Child Development**
- Academic, spiritual, physical, social, emotional growth
- Character formation through Islamic lens
- Leadership skills cultivated from early years
- Service orientation and social responsibility
- Resilience and growth mindset

**5. Differentiated Instruction**
- Recognition of diverse learning styles and paces
- Support for struggling learners
- Challenge for advanced students
- Inclusive environment for all abilities
- Personalized learning pathways

## Program Structure

### Five Progressive Years

Our Primary Education serves students ages 6-11 across five critical years:

#### **Class 1 (Age 6-7 years)**
- **Focus**: Foundational literacy, numeracy, and Islamic basics
- **Class Size**: 22-25 students per class
- **Teacher Ratio**: 1:22-25 (1 lead teacher)
- **Duration**: Full-day (7 hours including breaks)
- **Emphasis**: Transition from play-based to structured learning

#### **Class 2 (Age 7-8 years)**
- **Focus**: Building reading fluency, computational skills, Quran recitation
- **Class Size**: 22-25 students per class
- **Teacher Ratio**: 1:22-25 (1 lead teacher)
- **Duration**: Full-day (7 hours including breaks)
- **Emphasis**: Independent learning habits formation

#### **Class 3 (Age 8-9 years)**
- **Focus**: Deep comprehension, problem-solving, Islamic studies expansion
- **Class Size**: 22-25 students per class
- **Teacher Ratio**: 1:22-25 (1 lead teacher)
- **Duration**: Full-day (7 hours including breaks)
- **Emphasis**: Critical thinking and analytical skills

#### **Class 4 (Age 9-10 years)**
- **Focus**: Advanced literacy, complex mathematics, scientific inquiry
- **Class Size**: 22-25 students per class
- **Teacher Ratio**: 1:22-25 (1 lead teacher)
- **Duration**: Full-day (7 hours including breaks)
- **Emphasis**: Research skills and academic independence

#### **Class 5 (Age 10-11 years)**
- **Focus**: Secondary preparation, leadership, comprehensive Islamic knowledge
- **Class Size**: 22-25 students per class
- **Teacher Ratio**: 1:22-25 (1 lead teacher)
- **Duration**: Full-day (7 hours including breaks)
- **Emphasis**: Transition readiness for Class 6

## Curriculum Framework

### Core Subject Areas

Our comprehensive curriculum ensures balanced development across all domains:

**1. Islamic Studies** (90 minutes daily)
- Quran: Recitation with Tajweed, memorization (Hifz), Tafseer basics
- Hadith: Selected Ahadith with explanations
- Seerah: Detailed life of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)
- Fiqh: Islamic jurisprudence age-appropriately
- Aqidah: Islamic beliefs and theology
- Akhlaq: Character and moral development
- Arabic Language: For Quranic comprehension

**2. English Language Arts** (60 minutes daily)
- Reading: Phonics, fluency, comprehension
- Writing: Composition, grammar, creative writing
- Speaking: Oral presentations, discussions
- Listening: Active listening skills
- Literature: Diverse texts, critical analysis

**3. Mathematics** (60 minutes daily)
- Number sense and operations
- Algebra readiness
- Geometry and spatial reasoning
- Measurement and data
- Problem-solving and reasoning
- Financial literacy basics

**4. Science** (45 minutes, 4x/week)
- Life science: Living organisms, ecosystems
- Physical science: Matter, energy, forces
- Earth and space science
- Scientific inquiry and method
- Environmental awareness
- Islamic perspective on creation

**5. Social Studies** (45 minutes, 3x/week)
- Bangladesh studies: History, geography, culture
- World geography and cultures
- Civic education and citizenship
- Islamic history and civilization
- Map skills and spatial thinking
- Current events awareness

**6. Bengali Language** (45 minutes, 4x/week)
- Reading and writing in Bengali
- Bengali literature
- National culture and heritage
- Communication skills
- Grammar and composition
- Poetry and prose

**7. Physical Education** (45 minutes, 3x/week)
- Gross motor skill development
- Sports skills and games
- Physical fitness
- Team building
- Health education
- Islamic perspective on physical wellness

**8. Arts Education** (45 minutes, 2x/week)
- Visual arts: Drawing, painting, crafts
- Islamic calligraphy and geometry
- Music: Nasheeds and Islamic songs
- Drama and creative expression
- Art appreciation

**9. Technology Integration** (Embedded across curriculum)
- Computer literacy
- Educational software
- Internet safety and digital citizenship
- Coding basics (Classes 4-5)
- Research and presentation tools
- STEAM projects

## Assessment & Evaluation

### Comprehensive Assessment System

We use multiple methods to monitor and support student learning:

**Formative Assessment** (Ongoing):
- Daily observations and exit tickets
- Class discussions and questioning
- Quick checks and mini-quizzes
- Work samples and portfolios
- Self and peer assessment

**Interim Assessment** (Quarterly):
- Unit tests and projects
- Quarterly examinations
- Performance tasks
- Progress monitoring
- Standardized assessments

**Summative Assessment** (Bi-annual):
- Mid-year examinations (December)
- Final examinations (June)
- Comprehensive subject tests
- Skills demonstrations
- Portfolio presentations

**Assessment Distribution**:
- Continuous Assessment: 40%
- Written Examinations: 50%
- Practical/Oral: 10%

**Grading Scale**:
- A+ (80-100): Outstanding
- A (70-79): Excellent
- A- (60-69): Very Good
- B (50-59): Good
- C (40-49): Satisfactory
- D (33-39): Pass
- F (Below 33): Needs Improvement
